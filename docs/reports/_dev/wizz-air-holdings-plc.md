## Assessment

This claim is **MISLEADING** for six substantiated reasons:

### 1. Substantiation Failure Under EU Law

The claim violates EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) Article 1(a), which requires emissions reduction claims to disclose baseline year, scope, and methodology. Wizz Air provides none of these. The use of aspirational language ("aspire," "committed," "ambition") without quantified targets, baselines, or timelines violates UCPD Article 6(1) substantiation requirements. Under EmpCo, a company cannot claim emissions intensity reductions without defining the metric (e.g., CO₂ per passenger-kilometer, per seat deployed, per revenue-kilometer) and establishing an audited baseline year against which reduction is measured.

**Evidence:** EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), confidence 0.95.

### 2. Intensity Claims Without Absolute Data

The claim uses intensity language ("most carbon-efficient choice," "emissions intensity") without defining numerator or denominator. EmpCo Annex I explicitly warns that per-unit intensity claims can be misleading if they obscure absolute emission increases. For example, if Wizz Air increases its absolute emissions by 20% while claiming a 5% intensity improvement (by deploying larger aircraft or higher-capacity routes), the absolute environmental impact worsens — but the intensity claim remains technically defensible and thereby misleading.

The EU ETS data gap compounds this: we cannot cross-reference the intensity claim against verified absolute emissions to detect this pattern.

**Evidence:** EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), confidence 0.95; EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC, confidence 0.95.

### 3. EU ETS Registration Gap — Unverified Baseline

Wizz Air Holdings Plc operates one of Europe's largest aircraft fleets with substantial commercial aviation activity. EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC mandates verified emissions reporting for all commercial aviation operators. The EUTL query returned: **no installation IDs registered**.

This is not a confirmed finding of zero emissions — it is a data gap that prevents verification. Possible explanations include:
- Registration under a subsidiary entity rather than the parent holding company
- Small emitter exemption (implausible for a carrier of Wizz Air's scale)
- Data retrieval or jurisdictional issue

Critically, this gap means: the company's absolute emissions baseline is unverified and unknowable; any intensity-reduction claim cannot be grounded in the legal gold-standard baseline (EU ETS verified data); and the claim violates UCPD Article 6(1), which requires verifiable factual basis.

**Evidence:** EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC, confidence 0.95; EU ETS EUTL verification data, 2026 query, confidence unavailable due to gap.

### 4. SBTi Target Removal — Failed Prior Commitment

Wizz Air previously submitted a climate target to the Science Based Targets initiative (UNFCCC partner). That target has been removed from SBTi's registry. This signals one of two failures:
- The company voluntarily withdrew a commitment it could not deliver.
- SBTi rejected the target due to insufficient data, flawed methodology, or non-achievement.

Either scenario indicates the company's prior transition plan was not credible or failed independent validation. The current claim appears to replace that failed SBTi target with an unvalidated aspiration. The removal is a red flag for **TARGET_CREDIBILITY**: the company has not maintained a validated baseline against which "radical reduction" can be measured.

**Evidence:** SBTi Registry, confidence 0.75.

### 5. TPI Level 2 ("Acknowledging") — Minimal Transition Progress

The Transition Pathway Initiative, used by $80 trillion in institutional capital, rates Wizz Air at Management Quality Level 2 — the second-lowest substantive tier. "Acknowledging" means the company has basic climate policies but lacks comprehensive transition strategy. The pathway for 2050 is labeled "International Pledges" (future voluntary commitments), not validated science-based targets. TPI's assessment directly contradicts the company's claim of being "committed" to "radically reducing emissions intensity" — TPI's independent evaluation finds the company's climate transition underdeveloped.

**Evidence:** TPI Airlines Benchmark, 2022, confidence 0.70.

### 6. Deteriorating Historical Trend

Prasine Index assessment history for Wizz Air: 4 prior claims, average score 54.2 (MISLEADING band), trend: DETERIORATING. This indicates the company's claims have become less substantiated over time rather than more detailed or credible. The current claim follows this pattern: aspirational language, minimal specificity, and absence of audited baselines — consistent with a company making claims without corresponding internal transition progress.

**Evidence:** Prasine Index historical assessment data, confidence inherent to prior verdicts.

## Key Finding

Wizz Air's claim of commitment to "radically reducing emissions intensity" violates the substantiation standards mandated by the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825): it provides no baseline year, scope definition, target year, or methodology. The company's previous science-based target has been removed from the SBTi registry, and independent investor benchmarking (TPI) rates the company at the lowest substantive climate transition tier. The absence of EU ETS verified baseline data prevents verification that absolute emissions are declining, and the intensity-based framing risks masking rising absolute emissions.

## Data Gaps

**EU ETS EUTL Registry Query:**  
Wizz Air Holdings Plc returned no registered installation IDs in the EU ETS emissions trading system database. For a carrier operating hundreds of aircraft and thousands of flights annually across EU airspace, this represents a material verification gap. The absence means:
- Absolute emissions baseline cannot be verified against legal gold-standard data.
- Intensity-reduction claims cannot be cross-checked against verified absolute emission trends.
- The claim cannot meet UCPD Article 6(1) substantiation requirements (verifiable factual basis).

**Reason for gap:** Wizz Air may be registered under a subsidiary entity, regional exemption, or there may be a data retrieval/jurisdictional issue. This gap reduces confidence from 0.95 to 0.68 because we cannot definitively prove the claim is *false* (i.e., that absolute emissions are rising despite intensity claims) — only that it is *unsubstantiated*.

**LobbyMap Climate Engagement Data:**  
LobbyMap score (C+) is based on incomplete data (confidence 0.50). The neutral classification does not confirm whether the company is obstructing or supporting climate policy — only that engagement direction is not clearly documented. This limits the strength of any lobbying-contradiction finding.

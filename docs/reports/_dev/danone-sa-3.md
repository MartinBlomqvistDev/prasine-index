### Assessment

Danone's claim "reach net zero emissions by 2050" is **neither substantiated nor falsified by the available evidence record**. This verdict reflects three critical findings:

#### 1. **Third-party validation exists but is incomplete**

SBTi has validated Danone's net-zero targets at 1.5°C temperature classification—a strong signal that an underlying transition plan meets scientific credibility standards. CA100+ rates the company's net-zero ambition as genuine, though with only "partial" capex and short-term target alignment. These sources reduce the risk of outright greenwashing but do not fully close the substantiation gap created by the claim's bare formulation.

#### 2. **The claim lacks the specificity required under current EU law**

As of March 2024, EU Directive 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive) mandates that net-zero claims be substantiated by:
- A credible, verifiable transition plan with documented interim milestones
- Explicit specification of how residual emissions will be permanently removed (by certified carbon removals, not offsetting)
- Quantified scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions baselines

The claim text published on Danone's website—"reach net zero emissions by 2050"—provides none of these elements. The SBTi validation record suggests a detailed internal plan exists, but it is not disclosed in the public claim source. Under EmpCo Directive Article 32(4), the absence of published substantiation is a material deficiency.

#### 3. **Critical data sources are absent or insufficient**

- **EU ETS:** Danone has no registered installations in the EU Emissions Trading System. For a company of Danone's scale (global food and beverage manufacturing), this is unusual and suggests either: (a) in-scope facilities operate below the EU ETS threshold; (b) manufacturing emissions are outsourced to non-Danone-owned contract manufacturers; or (c) the company does not operate sufficient high-energy-intensity facilities in the EU to trigger registration. Without EU ETS data—the primary ground-truth regulatory source for verified EU emissions—the claim cannot be independently audited.

- **CSRD Disclosure:** The claim header states "CSRD obligated=No," inconsistent with Danone's global scale and likely EU-regulated market listing. If Danone is CSRD-obligated (as scale suggests), a net-zero claim should cross-reference or be consistent with mandatory CSRD Scope 1, 2, 3 GHG disclosures. The absence of this linkage is a red flag.

- **Historical Emissions Baseline:** E-PRTR holds only a 2008 data point (188.0 tonnes). No audited 2020 or 2021 baseline year is provided; no reduction pathway percentages are disclosed. Danone's scope 3 emissions (supply chain, logistics, end-of-life)—typically material for food/beverage companies—are not quantified in any source.

- **TPI Pathway Assessment:** The Transition Pathway Initiative, used by $80tn in AUM, has not assessed Danone's 2050 credible pathway (CP 2050: N/A). This is a material gap in investor-facing climate transition quality assurance.

#### 4. **Lobbying engagement is opaque**

Danone is registered as an active EU lobbyist, but LobbyMap assigns only a B- rating (mixed engagement). The direction of the company's lobbying on climate policy is not publicly transparent. No contradiction is detected, but this opacity prevents full confidence in claim alignment with stated net-zero commitment.

#### 5. **Historical consistency cannot be assessed**

This is Danone's first net-zero 2050 claim in Prasine assessment history (0 prior equivalent claims detected). However, across 30 prior claims, Danone's average score is 46.5 (MISLEADING category), indicating a pattern of substantiation challenges in sustainability claims. This historical context warrants caution about robustness of the net-zero commitment.

### Key Finding

Danone's net-zero 2050 claim is backed by third-party validation (SBTi 1.5°C, CA100+ net-zero ambition rating) but published without the specific transition plan details, emissions baselines, or carbon removal methodology now mandated by EU Directive 2024/825; simultaneously, critical verification data sources (EU ETS registration, CSRD disclosure, TPI pathway assessment) are absent or inaccessible, preventing independent audit of claim credibility.

### Data Gaps

**Critical gaps limiting confidence:**

1. **EU ETS Installation Data:** Danone has no registered installations in the EU Emissions Trading System database. For a multinational food/beverage manufacturer, this absence prevents verification of scope 1 (direct) emissions via the EU's primary regulatory verification mechanism. *Impact:* Cannot confirm baseline emissions or verify claimed reductions.

2. **CSRD Disclosure Status:** Claim header indicates "CSRD obligated=No," but this is inconsistent with a company of Danone's estimated size (likely >500 employees and/or EU-regulated market listing). If Danone is CSRD-obligated, mandatory scope 1, 2, 3 GHG disclosures should exist; if not obligated, the reason is unclear. *Impact:* Cannot cross-reference net-zero claim against audited baseline emissions.

3. **Detailed Transition Plan:** The claim text contains no published transition plan, interim 2030/2040 milestones, scope 1/2/3 breakdown, or carbon removal methodology. SBTi validation suggests such a plan exists internally, but it is not accessible in the public claim source. *Impact:* Cannot assess EmpCo Directive compliance (requires published plan detail).

4. **Historical Emissions Baseline:** Only one historical E-PRTR data point (2008: 188.0 t) is available. No audited baseline year (e.g., 2020, 2021) is disclosed. Scope 3 emissions (supply chain, logistics) are not quantified for any year. *Impact:* Cannot assess progress trajectory or validate baseline credibility.

5. **TPI 2050 Pathway Assessment:** TPI has not completed a 2050 credible pathway assessment for Danone (CP 2050: N/A). While TPI rates management quality as "Strategic," the absence of a published pathway assessment is a gap in investor-facing transition quality assurance. *Impact:* Reduces confidence in investor-facing credibility of the claim.

6. **Lobbying Direction:** EU Transparency Register confirms Danone's active lobbying registration, but the direction of engagement on climate policy is not disclosed. LobbyMap score (B-) is inconclusive. *Impact:* Cannot assess whether lobbying contradicts stated net-zero commitment.

**Confidence adjustment:** These gaps lower overall confidence to **68%**. The SBTi validation (0.70 confidence) and CA100+ net-zero ambition rating (0.65 confidence) are the primary confidence anchors; without them, the verdict would shift toward MISLEADING due to substantiation failure under EmpCo Directive Article 32. The absence of EU ETS data and CSRD disclosure detail prevents upgrade to "Confidence: High."

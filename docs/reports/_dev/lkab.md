## Assessment

LKAB's claim of "carbon-free production by 2045" fails to meet substantiation standards under EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) and UCPD Article 6. The verdict is driven by substantiation failure, not by contradicted emissions data.

### Emissions Trajectory: Positive Recent Decline, Weak Long-Term Signal

E-PRTR data shows LKAB's regulated Scope 1 emissions declined 25% between 2020 and 2024 (from 1,326,063 t to 993,354 t). This recent trend is positive and is **not contradicted** by verified sources. However, the long-term picture is less favourable: emissions rose 19% from 2007 (834,047 t) to 2024 (993,354 t). For an industrial mining and mineral processing company, a 19% absolute increase over 17 years, even with recent reversal, suggests that reaching "carbon-free production" by 2045 will require substantially more aggressive action than the recent rate of decline would support. The claim provides no interim milestones (e.g., 50% reduction by 2030, 75% by 2040) that would evidence credible pathway design.

### Critical Substantiation Gaps

The claim as stated is vague and lacks material disclosures required by EmpCo Directive:

1. **Baseline year not specified.** Is "carbon-free" relative to 2007, 2020, current, or another year? The claim does not state this.

2. **No quantified interim targets.** The claim references "three strategic innovation areas" but provides no detail of these areas or measurable milestones. No reduction targets for 2030, 2035, or 2040 are disclosed in the IR snippet.

3. **Scope not defined.** The claim does not clarify whether "carbon-free production" means Scope 1 only (direct industrial emissions), Scope 1+2 (including purchased electricity), or Scope 1+2+3 (including supply chain and use-phase). For a mining company, this distinction is material.

4. **Removal methodology not disclosed.** EmpCo Directive requires explicit methodology for handling residual emissions: are they to be permanently removed via certified carbon removals (compliant) or offset via credit schemes (non-compliant under Annex I)? The claim does not state this.

5. **No audited transition plan referenced.** Under EmpCo standards, net-zero-class claims must be accompanied by a verified, audited transition plan. The IR page snippet provides no evidence of such a plan or its availability.

Under amended UCPD Annex I (EmpCo, in force March 2024), generic net-zero pledges lacking these substantive elements are explicitly blacklisted as unfair commercial practices. LKAB's claim meets this blacklisted profile: aspirational, forward-looking, and light on verifiable detail.

### Target Credibility Assessment

A 2045 net-zero target is marginally credible for a large industrial emitter under science-based frameworks (IEA Net Zero Roadmap, SBTi), which typically require 70–80% absolute reductions by 2035 and net-zero by 2050. LKAB's 2045 date is 5 years ahead of this benchmark, which would require more aggressive near-term action. However, without disclosed interim targets, it is impossible to verify whether the 2045 pathway is credible. The recent 2020–2024 decline (25% over 4 years) would need to approximately double in pace to support a net-zero trajectory by 2045 from a 2024 baseline. The claim scores low on target credibility because the necessary verifiable evidence of pathway design is absent.

### CSRD Compliance Note

LKAB is not CSRD-obligated (confirmed in company record). However, voluntary disclosure of a net-zero-class claim on the corporate IR website still falls under UCPD Article 6 and EmpCo Annex I substantiation requirements. The absence of quantified interim targets, baseline year, scope, and transition methodology in the IR snippet is a substantiation failure under the regulatory standard, regardless of CSRD applicability.

### Historical Consistency

This is the first equivalent net-zero-class claim detected in LKAB's assessment history. Prior scores average 55.5 (MISLEADING band), consistent with a pattern of aspirational environmental claims lacking strong substantiation. This claim follows that pattern but is not a repeat claim.

## Key Finding

LKAB's "carbon-free production by 2045" claim is substantively non-compliant with EmpCo Directive requirements: it lacks quantified interim targets, a disclosed baseline year, explicit scope definition, and a verifiable transition plan. Recent E-PRTR emissions data (down 25% since 2020) show positive momentum but do not substantiate a broad 2045 carbon-free pledge without forward-looking evidence of credible pathway design. The claim is MISLEADING under EU 2024/825 and UCPD Article 6 due to substantiation failure, not contradicted emissions.

## Assessment

The claim asserts that fossil-free variants of Strenx® structural steel will match or exceed the performance of conventional Strenx® grades. This is a product-level technical claim, not an emissions claim, but it is tied to sustainability narrative and regulated under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825).

**What the claim lacks:**

The company has provided no published technical validation, third-party performance testing data, or timeline demonstrating that fossil-free Strenx® grades have been tested and certified to deliver equivalent performance. The term "fossil-free" is not defined in the claim — it is unclear whether this refers to 100% renewable electricity, hydrogen-based reduction, biogenic carbon pathways, or a combination. 

Under the EmpCo Directive standard (in force since March 2024), environmental claims must be substantiated by "recognised scientific evidence." A product claim without published test reports, certification, or a credible transition pathway fails this test. The claim reads as aspirational rather than evidenced.

**What supports the claim:**

SSAB AB holds credible strategic climate credentials. CA100+ (2025) rates the company as having genuine net-zero ambition with partial capex alignment. TPI (2022) assessment of SSAB's steel decarbonisation pathway rates all three checkpoints as "1.5 Degrees" compatible — a Paris-aligned trajectory. These benchmarks suggest the company has genuine capability and intent to decarbonise, which makes a fossil-free product claim technically plausible. However, strategic credibility does not substitute for product-level substantiation.

**Critical data gaps:**

No EU ETS installation records are registered for SSAB AB. This is material. SSAB is a major EU steelmaker and should have ETS-monitored installations if currently operating in the EU. The absence of ETS data prevents independent verification of:
- Current scope 1 emissions from existing steel production
- Whether any fossil-free production is operationalised or remains aspirational
- The actual emissions trajectory against the claimed transition

Without ETS baseline data, it cannot be confirmed whether the "fossil-free grades" exist in commercial production or are still in development.

**Mandatory disclosure inconsistency check:**

No CSRD baseline or Strenx®-specific emissions profile has been provided for comparison. If SSAB is CSRD-obligated (likely, given its size and listing status), a "fossil-free" product claim absent from or inconsistent with mandatory scope 3 emissions reporting would constitute a material breach of fair commercial practice under the amended UCPD.

**Lobbying context:**

SSAB is an active EU lobbyist. The LobbyMap score (C+) is inconclusive — neither confirming nor contradicting climate advocacy. No evidence of anti-climate lobbying has been detected. This does not contradict the claim.

**Historical pattern:**

This is the first claim by SSAB assessed in this trace. Prior claims in Prasine Index (n=2, average score 30.0) suggest a pattern of borderline INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE verdicts. This claim follows that pattern.

## Key Finding

SSAB AB has credible strategic net-zero ambitions (confirmed by CA100+ and TPI), but the specific assertion that fossil-free Strenx® grades will deliver equivalent performance lacks any published technical validation, third-party testing, or timeline. Under the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024), such claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence; the absence of test data, a product definition, and operational proof constitutes substantiation failure.

4. **Definition of "fossil-free"**: The claim does not specify whether "fossil-free" means 100% renewable electricity, hydrogen-based reduction, zero biogenic carbon, or a mix. This ambiguity reduces verifiability. Confidence impact: −5 points.

5. **Timeline and availability**: No date, scale, or commercial availability stated. It is unclear whether fossil-free Strenx® is in pilot production, commercial pilot, or pre-commercial development. Confidence impact: −5 points.

**Overall confidence impact of data gaps**: Confidence reduced from ~75% to 55%.

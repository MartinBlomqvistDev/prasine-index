### Evidence

**1. Substantiation Compliance (EmpCo Directive)**
The claim violates EU 2024/825 substantiation requirements. Per the directive, emissions reduction claims must state: baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology. This claim omits all three. The phrase "most carbon-efficient choice" contains no comparative methodology or benchmark. "Radically reducing" is aspirational language without numerical target or time horizon. The claim is presented without audited baseline data or third-party verification.
*Source: EUR_LEX [32024L0825], 2024*

**2. Science-Based Target Validation**
Wizz Air's SBTi commitment has been removed. The company is no longer listed as a validated participant in the Science Based Targets initiative. Any claim about emissions reduction lacks the gold-standard independent verification required under current standards.
*Source: SBTI [5], https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

**3. Independent Transition Assessment**
The Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) rates Wizz Air at Management Quality Level 2 ("Acknowledging") — the lowest meaningful assessment level. The company's carbon pathway is aligned to "International Pledges" (2°C-equivalent) rather than a 1.5°C science-based pathway. TPI Centre data is used by 150+ institutional investors ($80 trillion AUM) as the standard measure of corporate climate transition credibility.
*Source: TPI Benchmark Assessment [6], 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*

**4. Regulatory Verified Emissions Data**
Wizz Air does not appear in the EU Emissions Trading System (EUTL) registry of verified installations. EU ETS covers aviation emissions from all EU-registered operators since 2012 and represents the highest-quality legal ground truth for actual GHG emissions verified by accredited third parties. The absence of EU ETS verified data 14 years after sector entry prevents independent verification of any emissions reduction claim.
*Source: EU ETS EUTL verified data, https://ec.europa.eu/clima/ets; EUR_LEX [32003L0087]*

**5. Active EU Lobbying Registration**
Wizz Air Holdings Plc is actively registered in the EU Transparency Register as a direct corporate lobbyist (registration no. 481429647259-30, Hungary). The company actively engages EU institutions on policy matters. LobbyMap rates the company C+ (neutral/mixed) on climate policy engagement, indicating inconclusive alignment with its climate claims. Confidence in this data is low (50%) due to incomplete assessment coverage.
*Source: EU_TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER [1], https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do; INFLUENCE_MAP [4], https://lobbymap.org/*

**6. Historical Claim Pattern**
Three prior equivalent claims assessed by Prasine Index. Average greenwashing score: 51.7/100 (all in MISLEADING range, 46–60). The removal of SBTi commitment while continuing emissions reduction claims suggests backsliding rather than progress.
*Source: Prasine Index historical assessment records*

### Assessment

Wizz Air's claim is unsubstantiated and violates applicable EU law. The company claims to be "radically reducing emissions intensity" without providing baseline year, scope definition, methodology, or numerical target — all mandatory under EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825). The aspiration to "continue to be the most carbon-efficient choice" contains no comparative framework or independent verification.

**Primary Finding — Substantiation Failure:**
No evidence source marks "supports_claim = True." The SBTi commitment has been removed, eliminating the most credible form of independent validation. The TPI assessment (Level 2, International Pledges pathway) indicates the company is not aligned with 1.5°C science. The phrasing "support the airline industry's goal to decarbonise" deflects accountability to industry-level action rather than committing Wizz Air to a specific reduction target.

**Secondary Finding — Emissions Accuracy Cannot Be Verified:**
Wizz Air does not appear in the EU ETS EUTL registry. This data gap — 14 years after EU ETS sector entry — prevents independent verification of whether emissions intensity has actually declined. Without baseline data or regulatory verified emissions trail, the claim "radically reducing" is inherently unverifiable.

**Tertiary Finding — Weak Transition Credibility:**
TPI rates Wizz Air at Level 2 (Acknowledging) with alignment to International Pledges, not 1.5°C. For an airline to remain on a 1.5°C pathway requires ~5–10% annual intensity reduction. No such target is stated. The removal of SBTi commitment while maintaining emissions reduction claims represents material backsliding: either the company failed interim targets or strategic pressure for climate action has declined.

**Lobbying Alignment — Neutral, Not Contradictory:**
Wizz Air is an active EU lobbyist. LobbyMap rates engagement as C+ (neutral/mixed), not obstructive. This does not confirm greenwashing via anti-climate lobbying but indicates the company's climate claims lack endorsement by its policy actions. Confidence in lobbying data is low (50%) due to incomplete assessment coverage.

**Score Justification — 62/100 (GREENWASHING):**
The claim exceeds the MISLEADING threshold (≤60) because it is material (airline emissions for a major EU carrier) and completely unsubstantiated by regulatory verification, independent validation, or quantified targets. The SBTi removal and TPI Level 2 rating confirm weak transition credibility. However, the score does not reach CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (≥81) because: (a) no absolute emissions data directly contradicts the claim, (b) intensity *may* be improving despite lack of evidence, and (c) no active anti-climate lobbying is confirmed. The rating reflects material misrepresentation without proof of intentional deception.

### Key Finding

Wizz Air claims to be "radically reducing emissions intensity" without providing baseline year, scope definition, numerical target, or independent verification — a violation of EU 2024/825 substantiation law. The removal of its SBTi commitment, TPI Level 2 assessment, and absence from the EU ETS EUTL verified registry mean the claim cannot be independently verified and is contradicted by evidence of recent backsliding on climate commitments.

The gap may reflect: (a) emissions attributed to a parent company or operating subsidiary, or (b) incomplete EUTL coverage. Regardless, the absence of verified baseline data reduces confidence in the emissions accuracy assessment by ~10 percentage points.

This reflects outdated or incomplete data coverage. Inability to definitively confirm whether Wizz Air opposes climate policy reduces lobbying alignment confidence by ~8 percentage points.

**Baseline Emissions and Methodology:** The company provides no baseline year or scope definition in the claim. Historical emissions data for Wizz Air (Scope 1, intensity per available seat-km, etc.) could not be retrieved from public sources. Without baseline disclosure, the claim is unverifiable by definition.

**Final Confidence Adjustment:** Base confidence ~85% (clear substantiation failure, regulatory framework, SBTi removal) adjusted to **72%** after accounting for EU ETS absence, LobbyMap low confidence, and missing baseline data. This reflects moderate-high confidence in the GREENWASHING verdict with acknowledgment of data verification limitations.

The score does not reach CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81+) because no absolute emissions data directly contradicts the claim and no active anti-climate lobbying is confirmed. Confidence (72%) reflects available evidence clarity offset by critical data gaps (EU ETS absence, LobbyMap low confidence, missing baseline methodology).

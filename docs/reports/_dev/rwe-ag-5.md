## Evidence

The company's targets are listed as "Targets set" with temperature classification "1.5°C". This directly validates the core assertion that targets exist and are SBTi-certified. SBTi, 2026, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action

2. **Emissions reduction trajectory** — E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions for RWE fell 47% from 2007 (122.0 Mt) to 2024 (64.7 Mt), with a dramatic 32% reduction from 2023 to 2024 alone (73.4 Mt → 64.7 Mt). This downward trend is consistent with a credible 1.5°C transition pathway. E-PRTR, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

3. **Independent climate pathway assessment** — Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI), used by 150+ institutional investors with $80 trillion in assets under management, rates RWE's 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees" — within Paris-compatible bounds. Management quality is rated as "Integrating," indicating serious climate integration into strategy. TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates

4. **Net-zero ambition confirmation** — CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark (2025) confirms RWE with "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" and "Capex Alignment = Partial" to short-term targets. This assessment reflects credible net-zero alignment among leading climate investor coalitions. CA100+, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/

5. **Coal asset retirement** — Global Coal Plant Tracker data (2026) shows RWE operates only 1 of 5 tracked coal units (57 MW total), with 4 units retired or cancelled. This indicates accelerating coal exit consistent with 1.5°C targets. GCPT, 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-coal-plant-tracker/

6. **Gas asset retirement** — Global Energy Monitor gas tracker (2026) reports RWE has 6 European gas assets, with 3 operating and 3 retiring or cancelled. This demonstrates partial fossil fuel phase-out. EGT, 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/europe-gas-tracker/

## Assessment

RWE AG's claim that its 2030 and 2040 targets are SBTi-certified and support 1.5°C Paris alignment is **substantiated on its core factual elements** but receives a verdict of INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE due to three material gaps:

**Factual Substantiation**

The claim makes three verifiable assertions:
- Targets are certified by SBTi: **Confirmed**. SBTi records show RWE's targets are active with 1.5°C classification.
- 2030 and 2040 targets exist: **Not contradicted**. The claim references these specific milestones without specifying reduction percentages or scope, making it defensible.
- Targets support 1.5°C Paris alignment: **Confirmed by three independent sources**. SBTi validation, TPI assessment, and CA100+ benchmark all confirm 1.5°C alignment for RWE's pathway.

**Operational Credibility**

The claim is grounded in measurable emissions reductions. E-PRTR data shows RWE's regulated industrial emissions fell 47% over 17 years (2007–2024), with an accelerating pace of 32% reduction in the most recent year alone (2023–2024). This trajectory is consistent with a 1.5°C pathway that requires approximately 50% reductions by 2030 relative to 2010 baselines. The company is operationalizing its climate targets, not simply making unsubstantiated promises.

**Why the Score is Suppressed to 35/100**

Three factors prevent a higher verdict:

1. **EU ETS data gap** — The company has no registered installation IDs in the EU Emissions Trading System, the gold-standard regulatory baseline for large industrial emitters. E-PRTR provides a verified proxy (confidence 0.75), but EU ETS is the authoritative source. The absence prevents independent verification that RWE's SBTi methodology baseline matches actual regulated emissions. This is a significant gap because SBTi targets are calculated against a specific baseline year; without EU ETS confirmation, that baseline cannot be independently audited.

2. **Incomplete fossil fuel exit** — RWE still operates 1 coal unit (57 MW) and 1 oil and gas extraction field. For a company claiming 1.5°C alignment, the presence of any fossil fuel assets—even minimal—introduces ambiguity about whether the transition is credibly complete or merely in-progress. The claim does not specify fossil fuel phase-out dates. This is not falsification (the claim does not promise immediate coal closure), but it represents an unspecified risk to target credibility.

3. **Inconclusive lobbying stance** — RWE's climate policy lobbying position is unknown. LobbyMap rates the company as "C" (neutral/mixed), which neither confirms nor denies alignment with climate legislation. If RWE were actively lobbying against EU climate directives (LobbyMap Band D or E), this would elevate the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING. The neutral score prevents escalation but does not fully resolve the ambiguity.

**Historical Pattern**

RWE's prior assessment history (9 claims, average score 47.8, worst score 64.0) indicates a pattern of exaggeration or vagueness in climate communications. The current claim shows no prior equivalent assertions and represents a **narrower, more specific claim** than prior RWE communications — it does not claim net-zero by 2025 or promise immediate coal closure, only that targets are certified and science-based. The improved clarity and verifiability relative to the company's historical pattern is noted positively.

**Why the Score is Not Lower (SUBSTANTIATED)**

The SBTi certification, active target status, and verified E-PRTR emissions reductions directly support the claim. Three independent institutional investors (SBTi, TPI, CA100+) confirm 1.5°C alignment. The claim is not exaggerated relative to available evidence and does not misrepresent its temporal scope (2030 and 2040 are credible interim milestones on a Paris-compatible pathway, not unrealistic near-term targets). The company is operationally delivering on the emissions reductions that underpin the targets.

## Key Finding

RWE AG's claim that its 2030 and 2040 targets are SBTi-certified and Paris-aligned is supported by independent validation from the Science Based Targets initiative, Transition Pathway Initiative, and CA100+ investors, and is grounded in verified emissions reductions of 47% over 17 years. However, the unavailability of EU ETS baseline data prevents independent confirmation that the SBTi methodology baseline is accurate, and the company's continued operation of coal and oil/gas assets without specified phase-out dates leaves ambiguity about the credibility and completeness of the transition pathway. The verdict reflects substantiated core claims but material data gaps that prevent full confidence.

## Data Gaps

**Critical Gap: EU ETS Installation Data**
RWE AG has no registered installation IDs in the EU Emissions Trading System. EU ETS is the authoritative regulatory database for large industrial emitters in Europe and the standard source used to verify baseline emissions for SBTi target-setting. The absence of EU ETS registration means:
- RWE's SBTi baseline year (typically 2015 or 2019) cannot be independently verified against the regulatory gold standard.
- Alternative verification via E-PRTR is available (confidence 0.75) and shows consistent downward trajectory, but E-PRTR is not equivalent to EU ETS in terms of regulatory authority.
- This gap reduces confidence in full substantiation from 0.85+ (SUBSTANTIATED) to 0.72 (INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE).

**Secondary Gap: Lobbying Direction**
EU Transparency Register and LobbyMap both provide incomplete data on the direction of RWE's climate policy lobbying. The company is an active EU lobbyist, but whether it is lobbying for or against climate legislation cannot be determined from available sources. This prevents assessment of whether the company's public climate commitments are contradicted by private lobbying activities. LobbyMap score C (neutral/mixed) is inconclusive.

**Tertiary Gap: Fossil Fuel Exit Timeline**
RWE's claim does not specify completion dates for coal and oil/gas phase-out. Urgewald GCEL data (2026) lists historical coal capacity (5.8 GW), but GCPT data (2026) shows only 57 MW currently operating. The discrepancy suggests significant retirements, but the exact timeline and remaining operational dates for the remaining coal unit and gas assets are not disclosed in the claim or in publicly available sources reviewed.

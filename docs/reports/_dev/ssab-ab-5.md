### Assessment

SSAB's claim is a forward-looking commitment to launch a product that "does not create fossil-fuel CO₂ emissions." The assessment identifies neither clear substantiation nor verified contradiction, warranting the INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE verdict.

**Substantiation Gaps**

The claim lacks quantified interim milestones, a published transition roadmap, or independent certification of the claimed technology. The verb "aims to launch" combined with absence of specified timeline creates material ambiguity about credibility. TPI's 2022 strategic-level rating and CA100+ validation support the company's stated Paris-compatible direction, but neither source verifies this specific product claim or validates the underlying fossil-free steelmaking technology.

The claim references implementation of "a steelmaking process that does not create fossil-fuel CO₂ emissions," suggesting direct scope 1 abatement rather than reliance on carbon offsets. If substantiated, this would represent more ambitious decarbonization than typical net-zero pledges. However, no published technical specification, pilot project data, or capital expenditure timeline supports the technology assertion.

**EU Legislative Compliance**

Under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), claims of zero or near-zero environmental impact must be "substantiated by recognised scientific evidence" and backed by "a credible, verifiable transition plan." The current claim as publicly stated does not meet this threshold: it lacks audited baseline emissions, published interim targets, or independent technical validation. The directive entered force in March 2024 and applies retroactively to commercial claims. SSAB is not CSRD-obligated (per company query metadata), which exempts it from mandatory GHG disclosure but does not exempt it from EmpCo substantiation requirements. Source: EUR-LEX 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive), https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825

### Assessment

**Core Contradiction: FID-Locked Fossil Fuel Expansion vs. Net-Zero Claim**

TotalEnergies' net-zero 2050 claim is materially contradicted by verified evidence of capital-committed fossil fuel asset expansion. GOGET confirms 36 oil and gas extraction fields with Final Investment Decisions (FID) — institutional-grade evidence used by major financial institutions. Each FID-locked field commits decades of production beyond 2050, making simultaneous net-zero and fossil fuel expansion claims irreconcilable on their face. This is not a policy position or forecast uncertainty; it is documented capital commitment to decades of continued upstream fossil fuel production.

EGT adds evidence of ongoing European gas infrastructure expansion (South Hook LNG Terminal), extending fossil gas lock-in through the 2040s–2050s.

**Prior Regulatory Enforcement (ACM 2021)**

The ACM enforcement action in 2021 specifically warned TotalEnergies for making "vague or unsubstantiated environmental claims" on net-zero and sustainability commitments under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. This is not a generic warning to the sector; it is a documented enforcement action against *this company* for *this type of claim*. The current net-zero 2050 claim is a repeat of the same category of assertion flagged by Dutch competition authorities five years prior, establishing a pattern of non-compliance with substantiation requirements.

**Legislative Substantiation Gaps (EmpCo Directive)**

Under the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024), the claim fails multiple mandatory substantiation tests:

1. **No baseline year disclosed** for Scope 3 emissions (customer energy product use).
2. **No interim reduction milestones** provided for 2030, 2035, 2040, or any checkpoint between present and 2050.
3. **No removal methodology specified** — the claim does not distinguish between permanent certified carbon removal and voluntary carbon credits (the latter are blacklisted under EmpCo Annex I).
4. **No explicit phase-out timeline** for fossil fuel extraction or production.
5. **No verifiable transition plan** linked to the net-zero 2050 target.

The claim is a generic aspirational pledge without the quantified, audited substantiation now required under amended UCPD Annex I.

**Scope 3 Credibility Gap**

The claim explicitly covers "energy products used by customers (Scope 3)." For a major oil and gas company, Scope 3 emissions (emissions from oil and gas sold to customers) typically dwarf Scope 1 and 2. With 36 FID-locked extraction fields locked in for production through 2040+, a credible net-zero Scope 3 pathway by 2050 is implausible without immediate and verifiable commitments to:
- Phase out all oil production by a specified date.
- Decline gas production on a verifiable trajectory.
- Provide certified carbon removal for residual customer emissions.

None of these are disclosed.

**Mitigating Evidence: Genuine Operational Reduction**

E-PRTR verified data shows a genuine 30% decline in TotalEnergies' Scope 1 and 2 emissions from 2007 (17.4 Mt) to 2024 (12.2 Mt). This is a 17-year declining trend, not a one-year anomaly. The company has demonstrated real operational progress on Scope 1/2 emission reduction, and TPI rates its 2050 pathway as 1.5°C compatible — institutional-grade validation from investors controlling $80 trillion AUM.

These mitigating factors prevent the verdict from reaching **CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING** (score 81+). However, they do not resolve the core contradiction: simultaneous net-zero claims and FID-locked fossil fuel expansion are fundamentally irreconcilable.

**Lobbying Position (Inconclusive)**

LobbyMap score is C- (neutral/mixed, confidence 0.50), below the threshold required to trigger documented policy obstruction. EU Transparency Register confirms TotalEnergies is an active lobbyist, but the direction of engagement (climate policy support or blocking) is not independently verified. This does not meet the evidentiary standard for CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

**Score Rationale**

- GOGET FID expansion (confidence 0.87) + ACM prior enforcement (confidence 0.80) = strong push into GREENWASHING band (61–80).
- E-PRTR 30% historical Scope 1/2 reduction (confidence 0.75) + TPI 1.5°C rating (confidence 0.75) = material mitigating evidence.
- ACM enforcement alone would elevate the score to 81+ (CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING territory), but genuine operational emissions reduction pulls back to upper GREENWASHING range.
- **Score: 75** — within GREENWASHING band, reflecting the weight of fossil fuel asset expansion and prior enforcement, balanced against documented Scope 1/2 progress.

### Key Finding

TotalEnergies' net-zero 2050 claim is materially contradicted by verified evidence of 36 FID-locked oil and gas extraction fields extending production beyond 2050, combined with a 2021 ACM enforcement action specifically against this company for unsubstantiated net-zero claims. While the company has achieved genuine 30% Scope 1/2 emissions reduction and holds a TPI 1.5°C pathway rating, these do not resolve the fundamental incompatibility of simultaneous net-zero commitments and capital-locked fossil fuel expansion. The claim also lacks the baseline years, interim milestones, and removal methodology required under the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024).

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation IDs: Not available**  
No EU Emissions Trading System installation identifiers were returned for TotalEnergies SE. This prevented cross-reference validation of Scope 1 emissions against regulatory cap-and-trade data. However, E-PRTR data (confidence 0.75) provides comprehensive coverage of regulated industrial emissions to air and serves as an adequate substitute. This gap does not materially reduce confidence in the Scope 1/2 emissions trend (both sources track the same facilities; E-PRTR is more granular by facility type).

**CSRD Disclosure Cross-Check: Not performed**  
The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (in force FY2024 for large companies) requires disclosure of Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1. TotalEnergies must be subject to CSRD (>500 employees, listed on EU-regulated markets). A material gap exists: the company's mandatory CSRD disclosure for FY2024 was not queried to verify whether the net-zero claim is consistent with or contradicted by its regulatory GHG baseline. This would be a high-confidence cross-check but was not available at assessment date. If CSRD baseline and interim targets contradict the claim, confidence would increase to 88%+.

**Scope 3 Baseline and Transition Plan: Not publicly available**  
No document specifying TotalEnergies' Scope 3 baseline year, interim reduction targets, or certified carbon removal methodology was located in public sources. Under EmpCo Directive requirements, this substantiation must exist or the claim is automatically unfair. The absence of public disclosure does not prove absence of internal documentation, but it places the claim in violation of transparency requirements.

**LobbyMap Methodology Reliability: Low (0.50)**  
LobbyMap score direction is inconclusive. The source does not provide granular position-level tracking (e.g., "supported carbon tax" vs. "opposed Article 3 revision"), limiting its utility for detecting policy contradictions. Confidence in the lobbying assessment is constrained to 0.50 by this methodological limitation.

Prasine Index scored this claim by cross-referencing the company's net-zero 2050 assertion against eight independent sources spanning fossil fuel asset tracking (GOGET, EGT), regulatory enforcement records (ACM), verified emissions data (E-PRTR), investor-facing climate benchmarks (TPI), EU legislative requirements (EmpCo Directive, CSRD), and lobbying transparency registers.

Confidence is not higher (≤0.85) because LobbyMap engagement data are unreliable (0.50) and CSRD cross-validation was not available.

Prasine Index's scoring scale: 0–40 = Credible; 41–60 = Overstated; 61–80 = Greenwashing; 81–100 = Confirmed Greenwashing.

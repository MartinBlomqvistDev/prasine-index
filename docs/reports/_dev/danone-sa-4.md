## The Claim

> We plan to permanently remove an equivalent volume of carbon from the atmosphere and store it. Carbon removal projects, unlike carbon avoidance, focus on capturing CO2e through initiatives such as ecosystem restoration and carbon capture technologies. These efforts will be developed in-house or through trusted partnerships, such as our long-standing involvement with the Livelihoods Funds, of which we've been a founding partner since 2011. With these initiatives, we aim to neutralize around 4.5 MtCO2e per year by 2050.

*Source: Corporate website, https://www.danone.com/sustainability/nature/driving-climate-action.html, accessed 2026-05-11*

## Evidence

**1. EmpCo Directive compliance failure (PRIMARY CONCERN)**
EU Directive 2024/825 (EmpCo), in force since March 2024, amends the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive to blacklist environmental claims not substantiated by recognised scientific evidence. The Directive explicitly prohibits net-zero claims based solely on carbon removal or offsetting schemes without "a credible, verifiable transition plan demonstrating real emissions reductions." [32024L0825], 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825

**2. Absence of baseline emissions data**
No installations registered under the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS). E-PRTR records show only one data point: 188 tonnes of regulated air emissions in 2008 — insufficient to establish a current baseline or trend analysis. E-PRTR data, 2008, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/. This represents a critical gap for a multinational food company claiming a 2050 carbon removal target: without disclosed current absolute emissions (Scope 1, 2, and 3), the claim that 4.5 MtCO2e removal will "neutralize" residual emissions cannot be independently assessed.

**3. CSRD disclosure obligation status unclear**
Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD, 2022/2464) requires companies with >500 employees or listed on EU-regulated markets to disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1. Danone SA, as a Euronext-listed company, is likely CSRD-obligated from FY2024 onwards. The absence of a disclosed baseline emissions figure in the claim, combined with this potential obligation, creates a material inconsistency. [32022L2464], 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464

**4. Carbon removal methodology lacks third-party verification**
The claim specifies removal "through trusted partnerships, such as our long-standing involvement with the Livelihoods Funds" but provides no quantified, audited methodology. "Trusted partnerships" is a vague qualifier that carries no binding verification standard. No peer-reviewed science or independent certification (e.g., Gold Standard, Verra, or equivalent) is cited to substantiate the 4.5 MtCO2e figure or to distinguish between permanent geological storage and temporary ecosystem-based sequestration.

**5. Supporting evidence: CA100+ (2025)**
Climate Action 100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark rates Danone as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" with "Partial capex alignment" and "Partial short-term targets." The "partial" classification suggests incomplete transition planning. CA100+, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/. Confidence: 65%. This is directionally supportive but does not independently verify the 4.5 MtCO2e removal claim or validate its credibility under 1.5°C pathways.

**6. Supporting evidence: SBTi (status current)**
Science Based Targets initiative records that Danone SA has "Targets set" with "1.5°C" temperature classification and "net-zero target = Targets set." SBTi, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action. Confidence: 70%. SBTi validation confirms 1.5°C alignment but does not specifically endorse the carbon removal claim or verify that 4.5 MtCO2e removal is quantitatively credible or temporally appropriate for a 1.5°C pathway.

**7. Lobbying contradiction assessment: EU Transparency Register**
Danone SA is actively registered as an EU direct corporate lobbyist (Registration ID: 65744846168-89). EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do. Confidence: 75%. Registration confirms active policy engagement but does not disclose lobbying direction or positions on climate-related legislation.

**8. Lobbying direction: LobbyMap assessment**
LobbyMap rates Danone SA's climate policy engagement as "B-" (neutral/mixed engagement). LobbyMap, https://lobbymap.org/. Confidence: 50%. This classification is inconclusive — it neither confirms nor contradicts the carbon removal claim but indicates ambiguity about whether the company's actual policy advocacy aligns with its public climate commitments.

**9. Investor assessment: TPI (2022)**
Transition Pathway Initiative rates Danone SA at Management Quality Level 4 (Strategic) but has not assessed company policy (CP) alignment for 2025, 2035, or 2050 pathways. TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates. Confidence: 60%. This reflects incomplete transition pathway specification relative to the carbon removal claim.

## Assessment

Danone's claim to "neutralize around 4.5 MtCO2e per year by 2050" through carbon removal projects is **MISLEADING** under the EmpCo Directive and EU consumer protection law due to substantiation failure and material omissions of baseline data.

**Substantiation Failure (Primary Finding)**

The claim violates EmpCo Directive Article 5 (blacklisted claims) because it presents a net-zero objective ("neutralize") dependent on carbon removal without disclosing:

1. **Audited current baseline emissions** — No verified Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions figure is provided. Without a baseline, the claim that 4.5 MtCO2e removal will "neutralize" residual emissions is unquantifiable. The only available data point (E-PRTR 2008: 188 t) is 17 years old and insufficient to establish a valid comparison.

2. **Near-term absolute emissions reduction pathway** — IPCC 1.5°C science requires 40–50% absolute emissions reductions by 2030 before carbon removal becomes meaningful in 2040–2050. The claim does not disclose any Scope 1, 2, or 3 reduction targets for 2025, 2030, or 2040. SBTi's "Partial short-term targets" rating (CA100+) signals that these specifics are missing.

3. **Independent verification of carbon removal methodology** — The reference to "ecosystem restoration and carbon capture technologies" and "trusted partnerships, such as our long-standing involvement with the Livelihoods Funds" provides anecdotal credibility but no quantified, third-party audited methodology. No Gold Standard, Verra, or equivalent certification is cited. The distinction between permanent removal (e.g., direct air capture with geological storage) and temporary sequestration (e.g., reforestation, which is reversible) is not clarified, despite this distinction being central to EmpCo compliance.

**Data Gaps and Emissions Accuracy**

- **EU ETS**: No registered installations. This is a critical gap for a company with multiple manufacturing facilities across Europe. The absence of EU ETS reporting may indicate outsourced production or non-EU manufacturing, but this is not disclosed in the claim.
- **E-PRTR**: Only one year of data (2008: 188 t), insufficient for trend analysis and inconsistent with typical multi-facility emissions for a global food company.
- **CSRD Status**: The assessment record indicates "CSRD obligated: No," but Danone SA is listed on Euronext and has >500 employees, criteria that likely trigger CSRD applicability from FY2024 onwards. If CSRD-obligated and this claim is material, the absence of a disclosed baseline in the CSRD report would constitute a separate compliance violation.

**Target Credibility**

SBTi's 1.5°C classification and CA100+'s "Partial capex alignment" provide moderate support for Danone's climate ambition but do not validate the specific 4.5 MtCO2e removal target. The target is described as a 2050 objective (26 years hence), which is consistent with net-zero science but lacks the near-term reduction specificity (2025, 2030) expected for 1.5°C alignment. The "Partial short-term targets" rating from CA100+ directly contradicts the sufficiency of the climate plan presented.

**Lobbying Contradiction Assessment**

Danone is registered as an active EU lobbyist (EU Transparency Register). The LobbyMap score of "B-" (neutral/mixed) indicates inconclusive policy alignment. No confirmed evidence of active lobbying *against* climate legislation was identified, so the claim does not rise to the level of CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (which would require demonstrated contradictory lobbying positions). However, the non-transparent nature of the lobbying direction, combined with the incomplete disclosure of emissions reduction pathways, creates secondary doubt about the authenticity of the commitment.

**Historical Consistency**

This is the company's first equivalent carbon removal claim in the Prasine Index assessment record (31 prior claims assessed; 0 repeats). No pattern of reliable carbon removal delivery exists, and no prior equivalent claims enable trend analysis.

**Conclusion**

The claim receives 48/100 (MISLEADING, not CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING) because:
- It presents a plausible carbon removal objective that is directionally aligned with net-zero science.
- Two credible sources (CA100+, SBTi) confirm climate ambition, preventing escalation to higher-confidence greenwashing ratings.
- However, the absence of audited baseline emissions, near-term reduction targets, and third-party verified removal methodology violates the EmpCo Directive's substantiation standard.
- The omission of baseline data and reduction pathway is itself a greenwashing signal under EU consumer protection law: the company exaggerates through selective disclosure.

## Key Finding

Danone's claim fails to meet EU Directive 2024/825 (EmpCo) substantiation standards because it presents a 2050 carbon removal target ("neutralize around 4.5 MtCO2e per year") without disclosing audited current emissions, near-term absolute reduction pathways, or third-party verification of the carbon removal methodology. The absence of EU ETS and comprehensive emissions baseline data prevents independent assessment of whether the carbon removal claim is materially credible or proportionate to actual business-scale emissions.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS installation data**: No registered installations found. This is a critical gap because a multinational food company with European operations would typically report emissions to the EU ETS. The absence may indicate outsourced manufacturing or non-EU-based production, but this is not disclosed. This gap undermines the ability to verify current baseline emissions and assess whether the 4.5 MtCO2e removal target is proportionate.

2. **E-PRTR trend data**: Only one year of data (2008: 188 tonnes). Modern emissions data (2020–2025) would be required to assess current baseline, but the most recent available record is 17 years old. This prevents trend analysis and contradicts the plausibility of a 2050 target without disclosed recent emissions.

3. **CSRD disclosure status**: The assessment record states "CSRD obligated: No," but Danone SA meets the criteria for CSRD applicability (>500 employees, Euronext-listed). If CSRD-obligated, the company's mandatory emissions disclosure under ESRS E1 should provide the baseline against which this claim is assessed. The unclear status creates ambiguity about regulatory obligations.

4. **Carbon removal project audit**: No independent verification documentation or audit trail for the "Livelihoods Funds" partnership or in-house carbon removal initiatives. The claim references these partnerships but provides no quantified delivery record, certification standard, or third-party verification.

5. **Lobbying positions on climate policy**: EU Transparency Register confirms Danone is an active lobbyist, but the direction of lobbying on climate-related policies (carbon pricing, CSRD, ETS) is not disclosed in the available records. LobbyMap's "B-" rating indicates mixed engagement, but specific policy positions are not available.

**Impact on Confidence**: Confidence is rated 58% (moderate) due to these data gaps. The absence of EU ETS and current CSRD disclosure data is the primary driver of uncertainty. The supporting evidence from CA100+ and SBTi prevents lower confidence but does not fully compensate for the lack of audited baseline emissions.

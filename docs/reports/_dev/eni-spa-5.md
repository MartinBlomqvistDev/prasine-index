### Evidence

**REGULATORY ENFORCEMENT — Prior Greenwashing Finding**
AGCM ruling, 2023: Italian competition authority fined Eni €5 million for misleading claims in the 'HVOlution' green diesel campaign. AGCM found that Eni claimed HVO diesel was "green" with a "carbon footprint close to zero" without substantiation. The regulator explicitly rejected the lifecycle methodology and feedstock sourcing as insufficient to support the claims. This was Italy's largest greenwashing enforcement at the time. [ENFORCEMENT, Year 2023, https://www.agcm.it/dotcmsdoc/allegati-news/PS12469%20provvedimento.pdf]

**CORE BUSINESS CONTRADICTION — Active Fossil Fuel Expansion**
GOGET, March 2026: Eni SpA operates 184 oil and gas extraction fields globally. Critically, 9 fields with Final Investment Decision (FID) are actively under development, locking in decades of committed fossil fuel production. Named examples include:
- Bahr Es Salam Oil and Gas Field (Libya)
- Dalma Gas Field (United Arab Emirates)
- Ghasha Oil and Gas Field (United Arab Emirates)
- Litchendjili Phase 3 Oil and Gas Field (Republic of the Congo)

An additional 20 pre-FID discovered fields are likely to enter development. This forward-looking capital commitment to expand upstream oil and gas production is directly irreconcilable with a credible net-zero-by-2035 claim, which would require immediate cessation of new project approvals and rapid decommissioning of existing operations. [GOGET, Year 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/]

**LOBBYING CONTRADICTION — Obstructive Climate Policy Engagement**
LobbyMap: Eni SpA assigned a climate policy score of D+, indicating obstructive engagement — the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation. The company simultaneously appears on the EU Transparency Register as an active direct corporate lobbyist (registration no. 99578067285-35). This constitutes a primary greenwashing indicator: the company makes net-zero claims while its documented lobbying activity opposes the legislative framework necessary to deliver such a transition. [INFLUENCE_MAP, https://lobbymap.org/] [EU_TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do]

**COAL PORTFOLIO — Unaddressed Exposure**
Urgewald Global Coal Exit List: Eni SpA is listed with 12 Mtpa coal mining capacity and 2.3 GW coal power generation capacity. Expansion status is marked as unclear. The continued operation of this coal portfolio remains unreconciled with a net-zero-by-2035 claim, absent explicit time-bound coal phase-out commitments. [COAL_EXIT, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list]

**EMISSIONS REDUCTIONS — Genuine Historical Progress (Partial Mitigation)**
E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions: Eni's regulated emissions fell 77% from 2007 (30.4 Mt) to 2024 (7.0 Mt). A sharp 49% drop occurred between 2023 (13.6 Mt) and 2024 (7.0 Mt), suggesting recent operational changes or asset sales. This downward trajectory is consistent with a net-zero pathway and provides material evidence that the company has demonstrated capacity for decarbonization. However, this historical reduction does not resolve the forward-looking contradiction of ongoing new field development. [EPRTR, Year 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/]

**INVESTOR ENDORSEMENT — Partial Support**
CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark (2025): Eni is rated as having Net Zero Ambition = Yes, Capex Alignment = Yes, and Partial short-term targets. This institutional endorsement from 680+ investors ($80+ trillion AUM) carries weight but does not resolve the GOGET contradiction of 9 fields under development with FID. [CA100, Year 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/]

**SCIENCE ALIGNMENT — Mixed Pathway Assessment**
Transition Pathway Initiative (2022): TPI rates Eni's 2050 pathway as '1.5 Degrees' compatible (within Paris-aligned bounds). However, TPI's 2035 pathway assessment is classified as "National Pledges" — explicitly not aligned with 1.5°C climate science. This indicates TPI views the 2035 target as aspirational and policy-dependent, not science-backed. [TPI, Year 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates]

**EUROPEAN GAS ASSETS — Selective Divestment**
Europe Gas Tracker, March 2026: Eni is retiring 2 of 8 European gas assets. This supports a narrative of selective divestment but provides no context on the company's global upstream portfolio trajectory, which remains heavily committed to new oil and gas field development. [EGT, Year 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/europe-gas-tracker/]

**REGULATORY FRAMEWORK — Substantiation Requirements**
EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825): The amended Unfair Commercial Practices Directive, in force since March 2024, blacklists net-zero claims that lack a credible, verifiable transition plan with permanent certified carbon removals (not offset credits) for residual emissions. Generic net-zero pledges without detailed decarbonization timelines are classified as unfair commercial practices. Eni's claim appears to lack explicit fossil fuel phase-out timelines, clear permanent carbon removal methodology, and reconciliation between the 2035 net-zero target and the company's 144 operating fields plus 9 under development. [EUR_LEX 32024L0825, Year 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825]

Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (2022/2464): Large companies must disclose scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1. Material inconsistencies between a net-zero claim and mandatory CSRD disclosures undermine substantiation. [EUR_LEX 32022L2464, Year 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464]

### Assessment

Eni SpA's claim that "the plan aims to achieve the Net Zero Eni target by 2035" receives a greenwashing verdict based on three irreconcilable contradictions between the stated commitment and verified evidence of contradictory corporate activity.

**The Primary Contradiction: Active Fossil Fuel Expansion vs. 2035 Net-Zero Target**

The most significant finding is Eni's documented commitment to developing 9 oil and gas fields with Final Investment Decision, including major projects in Libya, the UAE, the Netherlands, and the Republic of the Congo (GOGET, 2026). Each of these projects locks the company into decades of fossil fuel extraction, making a credible net-zero-by-2035 transition impossible. Net-zero by 2035 would require immediate cessation of all new fossil fuel approvals and rapid decommissioning of existing operations — the opposite of Eni's current capex strategy. This is not an aspirational mismatch; it is a direct operational contradiction verified by independent project tracking data from a source used by the UN Environmental Programme and the International Energy Agency.

**The Secondary Contradiction: Lobbying Obstruction vs. Policy Alignment**

LobbyMap documents that Eni has a D+ climate policy score, indicating obstructive engagement with climate legislation (INFLUENCE_MAP, 2025). The company simultaneously maintains active registration as a direct corporate lobbyist with EU institutions (EU_TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER). This pattern is a canonical greenwashing signal: Eni makes net-zero claims while its own documented lobbying activity opposes the regulatory frameworks necessary to deliver such a transition. The company cannot credibly commit to a 2035 net-zero target while simultaneously lobbying against the climate policies required to achieve it.

**The Regulatory Enforcement Precedent**

The 2023 AGCM ruling demonstrates that Eni has a documented pattern of making unsubstantiated environmental claims. The Italian regulator fined Eni €5 million for falsely claiming that HVO diesel was "green" without verifiable lifecycle evidence — exactly the type of greenwashing behavior the company is now repeating at scale with the 2035 net-zero claim. The absence of explicit timelines for fossil fuel phase-out, measurable interim targets, and permanent carbon removal methodology mirrors the substantiation failures that triggered the AGCM enforcement action.

**Mitigating Evidence: Genuine Historical Emissions Reductions**

Eni has demonstrated a 77% absolute reduction in regulated industrial emissions from 2007 to 2024 (30.4 Mt to 7.0 Mt), with a particularly sharp 49% drop between 2023 and 2024 (EPRTR, 2024). This evidence shows that the company has genuine operational capacity to decarbonize. Additionally, CA100+ investors rate the company as having credible net-zero alignment, and TPI assesses the 2050 pathway as 1.5°C compatible. These institutional endorsements prevent the claim from reaching "confirmed greenwashing" status.

However, these reductions are attributable to operational changes and asset sales, not to a fundamental shift in the company's upstream strategy. The contradictions outlined above are not resolved by historical progress; they represent a current strategic choice to continue fossil fuel expansion while claiming net-zero ambition.

**Substantiation Gaps Under EmpCo Directive**

The claim fails substantiation tests under the EmpCo Directive (2024/825). It lacks:
- Explicit timelines for phasing out oil and gas exploration and production
- Measurable scope-1, 2, and 3 emissions baselines
- Specification of permanent carbon removal methodology (not offset credits)
- Reconciliation between the 2035 target and the company's commitment to develop 9 new fields and operate 144 existing fields

The claim is aspirational language without verifiable, time-bound sub-targets. Under the amended UCPD Annex I, such claims are now blacklisted as unfair commercial practices.

### Key Finding

Eni SpA's 2035 net-zero claim is directly contradicted by the company's documented commitment to develop 9 additional oil and gas fields with Final Investment Decision and its classified D+ score for obstructive climate policy lobbying. The company's prior €5 million greenwashing fine (AGCM, 2023) for unsubstantiated environmental claims establishes a pattern of making unsupported climate commitments. While genuine 77% historical emissions reductions demonstrate operational capacity to decarbonize, they do not resolve the current strategic contradiction between net-zero ambition and active fossil fuel expansion.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation Data**
EU ETS EUTL verified emissions data could not be retrieved for Eni SpA. No installation IDs are registered in the EU ETS database. This does not undermine the verdict — EPRTR provides comprehensive regulated industrial emissions data (2007–2024) from the European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register, which is the authoritative EU source for corporate emissions tracking. The absence of EU ETS data does not create material uncertainty regarding the company's emissions profile.

**Detailed Lobbying Positions**
While LobbyMap confirms D+ classification (obstructive engagement), the specific legislative positions and advocacy campaigns funded by Eni are not itemized in the available data. However, the score itself is sufficient evidence of obstructive alignment; greater detail would strengthen (not weaken) the greenwashing assessment.

**Eni's CSRD Disclosure**
Eni's mandatory Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) disclosures under ESRS E1 (scope 1, 2, 3 emissions) were not retrieved for comparison against the 2035 claim. A cross-reference to CSRD data would clarify whether material inconsistencies exist between the claim and mandatory disclosure. This gap does not alter the verdict but would provide additional substantiation context.

**Capex Allocation by Project**
GOGET confirms 9 fields under development with FID, but does not break down capital allocation percentages across upstream expansion vs. renewable energy. Eni's investor relations materials likely contain this data. A detailed capex breakdown would quantify whether the company is genuinely rebalancing toward renewables or merely making rhetorical commitments while maintaining upstream primacy.

**Confidence Impact**
These gaps reduce confidence from potential 90%+ to 82%. The core contradictions (GOGET field development, AGCM enforcement, LobbyMap D+ score, EPRTR emissions history) are verified to high confidence (0.87–0.95). The remaining 18% uncertainty reflects the inherent limitation of assessing forward-looking claims: whether Eni intends to divest the 9 fields under development or whether the company's 2035 target is contingent on unannounced asset sales. The available evidence does not support either assumption; current capex commitments stand as documented.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index scores greenwashing claims using five equally weighted components: (1) **Emissions Accuracy** (are historical emissions data consistent with the claim?); (2) **Claim Substantiation** (does the claim meet regulatory standards for evidence?); (3) **Historical Consistency** (does this claim align with the company's prior statements?); (4) **Lobbying Alignment** (does the company's advocacy support or contradict the claim?); (5) **Target Credibility** (is the claimed target science-aligned and operationally feasible?). Each component is scored 0–100; the overall score is their arithmetic mean. Verdicts are assigned as follows: 0–40 (Confirmed Greenwashing), 41–80 (Greenwashing), 81–100 (Substantiated Claim). Confidence reflects data quality and source independence; gaps in verification lower confidence proportionally. This assessment used 11 independent sources: regulatory enforcement (AGCM), project tracking (GOGET), emissions registries (EPRTR), lobbying databases (LobbyMap, EU Transparency Register), investor benchmarks (CA100+, TPI), legislative frameworks (EmpCo Directive, CSRD), and asset tracking (EGT, COAL_EXIT). All sources are EU or internationally recognized; no data was obtained from Eni's own disclosures except the claim text itself. The score of 78 reflects material contradictions (fossil fuel expansion, lobbying obstruction, regulatory precedent) offset by genuine emissions reductions and institutional investor confidence, preventing a "confirmed greenwashing" verdict.

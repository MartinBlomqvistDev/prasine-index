### Assessment

The claim asserts two factual elements: (1) Danone was among the first 100 companies in 2015 to commit to net-zero by 2050, and (2) this commitment aligns with the Paris Agreement. The verdict of INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE reflects critical data gaps that prevent full substantiation, despite the absence of material contradicting evidence.

**Positive signals:**
SBTi validation (Targets set, 1.5°C classification) provides the strongest evidentiary support. SBTi validation requires companies to disclose interim reduction targets and scope. This mitigates the risk that the claim is purely rhetorical. No obstructive climate lobbying was detected; Danone's LobbyMap B- score indicates neutral engagement, preventing a downgrade based on lobbying contradiction.

**Critical substantiation gaps:**

1. **Scope and baseline clarity:** The claim on the website states "Net-Zero emissions by 2050" without specifying whether this covers Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (purchased energy), Scope 3 (value chain), or all three. Under EmpCo Directive Article 6 (UCPD Annex I amendment, effective March 2024), net-zero claims without explicit scope and baseline year are automatically deemed unfair commercial practices. The SBTi record indicates an audited framework exists, but the website claim itself violates EmpCo by omitting these details.

2. **Emissions trajectory verification:** E-PRTR data provides only one year (2008, 188.0 t) and shows minimal regulated emissions. EU ETS returns no installations. For a major food and beverage manufacturer, Scope 3 (ingredient sourcing, logistics, distribution) typically accounts for 70–90% of total emissions. Without access to Danone's published full GHG inventory and baseline (required under CSRD for large listed companies), the claim cannot be verified against actual emissions reduction progress from 2015 to present.

3. **CSRD compliance gap:** Danone is a listed multinational with >500 employees and falls within CSRD scope (FY2024 onwards). The directive requires disclosure of baseline Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions under ESRS E1. No publicly available CSRD disclosure with these baselines was located. If Danone has not published CSRD data, the net-zero claim lacks the mandatory evidentiary foundation under EU law.

4. **TPI pathway validation:** TPI, the investor-standard benchmark for corporate climate transition credibility, rates Danone MQ Level 4 (Strategic) but provides no Carbon Performance pathway assessment for 2050. For a 2050 net-zero claim, the absence of independently assessed carbon performance pathways is a material credibility gap. SBTi validation addresses interim targets (typically 2030); TPI would validate the long-term pathway. Neither is complete without the other.

5. **Historical assertion (2015 commitment):** The claim that Danone "was one of the first 100 companies in 2015" to commit to net-zero is plausible given the timeline (We Mean Business Coalition and similar initiatives enrolled major corporates in 2015–2016). However, no primary source documentation (e.g., press release, commitment letter, corporate announcement) is cited in the claim or available in the evidence record. The assertion is neither confirmed nor contradicted by available data.

**Lobbying alignment:**
No contradiction detected. LobbyMap B- indicates neutral engagement; EU Transparency Register confirms active lobbying but no adverse climate policy obstruction. This dimension supports the claim but does not substantiate it.

**Sector context:**
Danone operates in Consumer Goods (Food & Beverage). This sector typically has high Scope 3 emissions. The TPI absence of carbon performance data for this sector and company is concerning: if TPI has not validated Danone's pathway, investor-facing credibility is weakened.

**Why not MISLEADING?**
A MISLEADING verdict (46–60) would require evidence that the claim materially exaggerates or omits key context in a deceptive manner. While the website claim is bare of supporting detail and violates EmpCo requirements, the presence of SBTi validation provides enough of a mitigating audited framework that definitive exaggeration cannot be established. The problem is insufficiency of evidence, not proven falsity.

---

### Key Finding

Danone's net-zero 2050 claim lacks the scope clarity, baseline documentation, and emissions trajectory verification required under EmpCo Directive Article 6 (in force March 2024), rendering the website statement an unfair commercial practice as currently framed. While SBTi validation supports the existence of an audited net-zero framework, full substantiation is blocked by three critical gaps: (1) no published CSRD disclosure with Scope 1/2/3 baseline and interim targets, (2) no TPI carbon performance pathway validation for 2050, and (3) no documented historical emissions trajectory from 2015 to present. Recommend Danone publish detailed transition plan, full GHG inventory by scope, and seek TPI pathway validation to substantiate the claim.

### Data Gaps

- **EU ETS installation IDs:** No installations registered to Danone SA. This does not indicate zero emissions but reflects either outsourced manufacturing or below-threshold direct combustion. Cannot verify Scope 1 direct emissions trajectory from 2015–2026.

- **CSRD disclosure:** No published CSRD baseline or Scope 1/2/3 GHG inventory located. Danone falls within CSRD scope (listed multinational, >500 employees). Mandatory disclosure under ESRS E1 not accessible in evidence set. This is a critical gap for a net-zero 2050 claim.

- **TPI carbon performance pathway:** TPI provides no 2025/2035/2050 Carbon Performance assessment for Danone. Cannot independently verify whether the 2050 net-zero target aligns with 1.5°C science for the Consumer Goods sector.

- **2015 commitment documentation:** No primary source (press release, commitment letter, announcement) verifying Danone's participation in "first 100 companies" 2015 net-zero initiative. Claim is plausible but unconfirmed.

- **Full emissions inventory:** E-PRTR (188 t, 2008 only) and EU ETS (no data) do not capture Scope 2 or Scope 3 emissions. For a major food and beverage manufacturer, these are the dominant emissions sources. Cannot assess total emissions baseline or reduction trajectory.

**Impact on confidence:** Data gaps reduce confidence to 68%. SBTi validation (0.70 confidence) and LobbyMap (0.50 confidence) provide partial support. However, absence of CSRD data, TPI validation, and full emissions trajectory prevents high-confidence substantiation. The claim is plausible and not contradicted by available sources, but insufficient evidence to establish truth. Regulatory sources (EmpCo, CSRD, UCPD) provide strong frameworks for evaluation but the company's compliance disclosure is incomplete.

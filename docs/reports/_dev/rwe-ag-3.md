## Assessment

RWE AG's claim that it is "committed to shaping the Net Zero energy system of the future" receives a score of 42/100 because evidence of real emissions reductions and formal net-zero targets coexists with significant substantiation gaps and remaining coal exposure that are not adequately resolved by the claim text itself.

**Claim Substantiation (48/100)**

The claim is aspirational and lacks specificity. It does not state a target date, interim milestones, or clarify whether "shaping the Net Zero energy system" refers to RWE's own operations, the EU electricity grid, or both. Under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825, in force since March 2024), generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are classified as automatic unfair commercial practices. The IR claim does not anchor to RWE's published SBTi or TPI targets and does not specify how residual emissions will be addressed (certified carbon removals vs. offset credits). A search for published CSRD disclosure did not return a record, limiting independent verification of the transition plan against EmpCo blacklist standards.

**Emissions Accuracy (28/100)**

EPRTR data confirms substantial absolute emissions reductions: 47% since 2007 and 31% since 2020. This is verified by a regulated, high-confidence source (EPRTR, 2024, confidence 0.75). However, absolute emissions remain very high at 64.7 Mt in 2024. Critically, EU ETS installation-level data could not be retrieved—a significant gap for a major energy utility expected to be registered. This absence prevents independent verification of RWE's baseline and trajectory against the institutional gold standard for EU corporate emissions. The score reflects: verified reductions (positive signal) but absolute emissions still substantial and EU ETS verification unavailable (reduces confidence in baseline accuracy).

**Historical Consistency (35/100)**

This is the first equivalent net-zero claim assessed for RWE in the Prasine dataset. No prior broken promises on this specific claim are detected. However, prior assessments show an average greenwashing score of 50.9 across 7 prior claims, indicating a mixed track record on environmental claims. The trend is "improving," which is favorable, but provides limited historical evidence for delivery on this specific net-zero commitment. Absence of prior repeat claims means the company has not been caught making the same claim twice, a positive signal.

**Lobbying Alignment (30/100)**

RWE AG is registered in the EU Transparency Register as an active corporate lobbyist (registration no. 77608353460-77). However, the direction of its lobbying—whether supportive of or opposed to climate legislation—cannot be determined from registration status alone. LobbyMap assigns RWE a score of "C" (neutral/mixed engagement), which is inconclusive (confidence 0.50). No evidence of climate-opposing lobbying (e.g., against EU ETS carbon pricing, phase-out timelines, or carbon border adjustment mechanisms) was detected. The absence of identified anti-climate lobbying, combined with formal SBTi and TPI alignment, suggests this is not a disqualifying factor. The score reflects genuine ambiguity: neither supportive evidence nor confirmed contradiction.

**Target Credibility (20/100)**

SBTi classification (1.5°C aligned) and TPI 2050 pathway rating ('1.5 Degrees') represent the highest-credibility external climate benchmarks available. Both are peer-reviewed, investor-verified standards. This strongly supports the credibility of RWE's underlying net-zero targets. However, the IR claim text itself does not reference or anchor to these verified targets. A reader of the IR page would not know that SBTi or TPI verification exists. The low score reflects the disconnect between the credible external targets and the generic nature of the claim as published.

**Integration and Key Tension**

Three high-confidence sources (EPRTR 0.75, TPI 0.75, SBTi 0.70) confirm that RWE has reduced emissions and holds science-based, Paris-compatible targets. However, two material unresolved issues prevent a "SUBSTANTIATED" verdict:

1. **Coal exposure remains material**: GCEL lists RWE as operating 44 Mtpa coal mining and 5.8 GW coal capacity. While GCPT confirms that 4 of 5 coal power units are retired or cancelled (only 57 MW operating), the coal mining capacity and the timeline for full coal exit are not resolved by available evidence. A "Net Zero energy system" claim is difficult to reconcile with ongoing coal mining operations absent a published, dated exit timeline.

2. **Claim lacks EmpCo-compliant substantiation**: The IR claim is vague ("committed to shaping"). It does not state a net-zero date, specify interim targets, clarify residual emissions treatment (permanent removal vs. offsetting), or reference the published SBTi/TPI targets that would substantiate it. Under EmpCo Directive Article 5a and Annex I, this type of generic net-zero pledge is automatically classified as an unfair commercial practice unless backed by a detailed transition plan. No such plan was retrieved.

3. **EU ETS baseline unavailable**: The absence of EU ETS installation data prevents independent verification of RWE's emissions baseline against the regulatory standard. For a utility of this scale, this is a notable data gap.

**Why Not GREENWASHING or MISLEADING?**

Six sources support net-zero alignment (CA100+, EPRTR, SBTi, TPI, EGT, GCPT). Only one source clearly contradicts (COAL_EXIT on coal mining scale). RWE's demonstrated 47% emissions reduction since 2007 and formal SBTi 1.5°C targets are not false. The company has not been caught in a direct lie or making demonstrably unfounded claims. Greenwashing verdicts require evidence of material misrepresentation; this case shows mixed evidence, not clear falsehood.

**Why Not SUBSTANTIATED?**

The claim text itself is not anchored to quantified targets, lacks a date, and does not meet EmpCo Directive substantiation requirements. Coal mining exposure at 44 Mtpa remains unresolved. EU ETS verification data are absent. These gaps prevent confident assessment that the claim, as published, is adequately supported.

## Key Finding

RWE holds credible, verified net-zero targets (SBTi 1.5°C, TPI Paris-compatible, 47% emissions reduction since 2007), but the IR claim text is vague, lacks a date or interim milestones, and does not disclose that residual coal mining operations (44 Mtpa) continue—making it difficult to determine whether the claim meets EU greenwashing law (EmpCo Directive, in force March 2024) without access to RWE's detailed published transition plan.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS installation-level data**: No EU ETS installation IDs were retrieved for RWE AG. This prevents independent verification of the company's baseline emissions and verification against the institutional gold standard for EU energy sector emissions reporting. For a utility of RWE's size (64.7 Mt annual emissions), this is a significant gap. Confidence in emissions accuracy would increase to 65+ with EU ETS data.

2. **CSRD disclosure record**: No CSRD submission was found. RWE's CSRD obligation status could not be confirmed from available metadata. If RWE is CSRD-obligated, a published disclosure would substantiate or contradict the IR claim through mandatory ESRS E1 scope 1/2/3 emissions reporting. Without this, the claim cannot be cross-checked against formal sustainability reporting.

3. **Coal exit timeline**: GCEL lists RWE as operating 44 Mtpa coal mining capacity. Global Energy Monitor data do not clarify whether RWE has a published coal mine exit date or is expanding coal extraction. This ambiguity prevents definitive assessment of whether coal exposure contradicts the "Net Zero energy system" claim or is compatible with a credible transition plan. Confidence would increase to 75+ with publication of a coal exit timeline.

4. **Lobbying position detail**: LobbyMap score "C" (neutral/mixed, confidence 0.50) is inconclusive. The direction of RWE's EU lobbying on climate policy (supportive or opposed) is not publicly available from the sources queried. A detailed breakdown of RWE's lobbying positions on EU ETS carbon pricing, coal phase-out, or CBAM would clarify alignment between the net-zero claim and actual policy engagement. Confidence in lobbying alignment would increase to 70+ with this information.

5. **Published transition plan against EmpCo Annex I**: No full transition plan document cross-referenced against EmpCo Directive Annex I blacklist was retrieved. Under EmpCo (in force March 2024), the claim must be substantiated by "recognised scientific evidence" and cannot rely on offset credits for residual emissions. A published plan would clarify whether RWE's claim meets this legal standard. This is a legal substantiation gap, not a technical gap.

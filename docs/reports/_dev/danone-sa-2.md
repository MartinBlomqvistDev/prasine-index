### Assessment

Danone's 2050 net-zero target is supported by two credible third-party frameworks: the Science Based Targets initiative (1.5°C classification) and the CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark ("Net Zero Ambition = Yes"). These endorsements provide baseline assurance that the company has submitted credible net-zero commitments aligned with Paris Agreement pathways.

However, the claim cannot be substantiated to the standard required for legal citation for three critical reasons:

**1. Verified Emissions Baseline Unavailable**

The absence of EU ETS data prevents independent verification of Danone's current Scope 1 emissions or emissions trajectory. The only available emissions record is a single datapoint from 2008 (188 tonnes, E-PRTR), now 18 years stale. For a multinational food company with significant agricultural supply chain emissions (Scope 3), this creates a material verification gap. It is impossible to assess whether Danone's current trajectory is consistent with linear decarbonisation toward 2050 net-zero without a recent, audited baseline.

**2. EmpCo Directive Compliance Not Demonstrated**

The claim text itself—"our goal to achieve Net-Zero emissions by 2050"—is generic and does not comply with the specificity requirements of the amended UCPD Annex I (EmpCo Directive, in force March 2024). The claim lacks:
- Explicit scope boundaries (Scope 1, 2, 3)
- Interim reduction milestones (2030, 2035)
- Methodology for permanent carbon removal post-2050
- Distinction between reductions and offset credits

While Danone's SBTi submission may address these elements in technical documentation, the public-facing claim on the IR page does not. Under Article 2, Annex I of the amended UCPD, this constitutes insufficient substantiation for a net-zero claim.

**3. CSRD Disclosure Status Unverified**

If Danone is obligated under CSRD (likely, given its size and EU listing status), the absence of published Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions baselines and transition roadmap would represent a material reporting gap. The claim cannot be evaluated against mandatory regulatory disclosure requirements if those disclosures are not publicly available or their status is unclear.

**Mitigating Factors:**

The claim is not classified as GREENWASHING because:
- No active contradiction between the claim and documented lobbying (LobbyMap "B-" rating indicates neutral engagement, not opposition)
- Two independent investor-facing frameworks (SBTi, CA100+) have validated the net-zero commitment
- TPI rates Danone at the highest management quality tier for climate strategy
- No evidence of reliance on carbon offsets alone (SBTi 1.5°C classification implies credible reduction pathways)

### Key Finding

Danone's 2050 net-zero claim is backed by credible third-party verification (SBTi 1.5°C, CA100+ recognition) and shows no active greenwashing indicators, but cannot be independently substantiated because: (1) no verified Scope 1 emissions baseline or recent trajectory data is available; (2) the claim text lacks the scope boundaries and interim targets required by the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024); and (3) Danone's mandatory CSRD emissions disclosure status, if applicable, could not be verified.

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data**: No EU ETS installation IDs matched to Danone SA. This prevents verification of Scope 1 baseline emissions and trend assessment. Impact: Cannot assess whether current emissions trajectory is consistent with 2050 net-zero.

2. **E-PRTR Time Series**: Only 2008 data available (188 tonnes). Single-year datapoint does not permit trend analysis. Impact: Cannot determine whether emissions have increased, remained stable, or declined since 2008.

3. **CSRD Emissions Disclosure**: Company marked "CSRD obligated: No" in evidence record, but this status is not independently verified. If Danone is CSRD-obligated (likely, given turnover >€500m and EU listing), absence of published Scope 1, 2, 3 baselines and transition roadmap would be a material gap. Impact: Cannot assess claim against mandatory regulatory disclosure.

4. **Lobbying Direction**: EU Transparency Register confirms Danone is an active lobbyist, but the specific policy positions (pro- or anti-climate) are not disclosed in LobbyMap or other accessible sources. Impact: Cannot fully evaluate alignment or contradiction between the net-zero claim and documented advocacy.

5. **Interim Targets and Scope Boundaries**: The claim text does not specify scope boundaries or interim reduction milestones. Danone's SBTi submission may contain this detail, but it is not accessible in the public IR claim. Impact: Cannot assess EmpCo Directive compliance of the specific claim as stated.

**Confidence Adjustment**: The 55% confidence reflects dependence on two supportive third-party sources (SBTi, CA100+) offset by critical gaps in verified emissions data and regulatory disclosure status. Additional data on recent Scope 1/2/3 emissions, interim targets, and CSRD filing status would raise confidence to 75%+.

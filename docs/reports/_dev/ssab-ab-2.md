### Assessment

SSAB AB's claim that its "new process uses green hydrogen instead of coal to remove oxygen from the iron ore… practically eliminating CO2 emissions from our steelmaking" fails to meet the substantiation standards required under the EmpCo Directive and lacks independent operational verification.

**Substantiation Deficit:**
The claim provides no baseline year, no quantified emissions reduction target, no defined scope (Scope 1 direct process emissions vs. Scope 2/3 upstream hydrogen production emissions), no commercial deployment timeline, and no third-party certification. The phrase "practically eliminating CO2 emissions" is hyperbolic and vague—it does not specify whether the claim applies only to the direct reduction process or includes the full life-cycle carbon intensity of the hydrogen feedstock. Under EmpCo Directive (2024/825), this omission constitutes a substantiation failure.

**Emissions Verification Gap:**
No EU ETS verified emissions data is available for SSAB AB's steelmaking installations (EU ETS query returned "no installation IDs registered"). This prevents independent verification of a baseline emissions level against which to measure the claimed reduction. The absence of EUTL data is a critical gap because the claim references "our new process" (implying current or imminent deployment), not a forward-looking R&D commitment. Without verified baseline data, the materiality and realism of the claim cannot be assessed.

**Hydrogen Carbon Intensity Unspecified:**
The claim does not disclose whether the "green hydrogen" is produced from grid electricity, dedicated renewable capacity, or blue hydrogen from natural gas with carbon capture. Grid-sourced hydrogen in most EU regions still embeds 200–400 gCO₂-eq/kWh; blue hydrogen embeds 70–150 gCO₂-eq/kg from upstream natural gas extraction and reformation. The claim's silence on this critical input means embedded Scope 2/3 emissions may be substantial, contradicting the assertion of "practically eliminating" steelmaking emissions.

**Strategic Credibility vs. Operational Verification:**
TPI and CA100+ assessments (confidence 0.65–0.75) confirm that SSAB AB has credible long-term net-zero alignment and strategic management quality rated as "Strategic." This supports the *plausibility* of hydrogen steelmaking investment as part of a Paris-compatible pathway. However, neither TPI nor CA100+ independently verifies the specific hydrogen process claim or confirms that commercial deployment is imminent or on a binding timeline. CA100+ notes "Short-term target = No," indicating the absence of near-term milestones to operationalize the hydrogen transition.

**Lobbying Stance:**
No contradiction between the claim and corporate lobbying position is identified. LobbyMap rates engagement as C+ (inconclusive), and EU Transparency Register records show active lobbying engagement but not the direction of climate policy advocacy. Absence of evidence of climate obstruction does not, however, constitute evidence of support for the hydrogen process claim.

**Historical Pattern:**
This is not a repeat claim, but SSAB AB's prior assessed claim also scored as INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE (prior worst: 28/100). The current claim exhibits the same pattern: aspiration without hard operational data or third-party verification.

### Key Finding

SSAB AB claims a hydrogen-based steelmaking process that "practically eliminates CO2 emissions," but provides no baseline emissions data, deployment timeline, scope definition, or third-party verification. EU ETS verified emissions data cannot be located for the company's steelmaking operations, preventing independent assessment of the claimed reduction's materiality. The claim meets no substantiation requirement under the EmpCo Directive (baseline year, scope, methodology) and does not disclose the carbon intensity of the hydrogen feedstock, which may be substantial if grid or blue-hydrogen sourced. While TPI and CA100+ assessments confirm credible long-term strategic alignment, they do not verify the specific hydrogen process or confirm imminent deployment.

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Verified Emissions (CRITICAL):** Query returned "no installation IDs registered" for SSAB AB's steelmaking installations. This prevents verification of baseline Scope 1 emissions and assessment of the claimed reduction's materiality. The data gap is not resolved by public disclosure; SSAB AB must register steelmaking installations under EU ETS Regulation 601/2012 for independent emissions verification to be possible.

2. **Hydrogen Production Carbon Intensity:** No source specifies whether the "green hydrogen" is produced from grid electricity, dedicated renewable assets, or blue hydrogen. Grid-sourced hydrogen in most EU regions embeds 200–400 gCO₂-eq/kWh; this embedded Scope 2 carbon is not disclosed in the claim and cannot be verified from available sources.

3. **Commercial Deployment Timeline:** No published timeline for roll-out of the hydrogen steelmaking process. TPI, CA100+, and corporate website do not specify when this process will be commercially deployed at scale or to what share of SSAB's steel production.

4. **Product-Level or Process-Level Third-Party Certification:** No ISCC, Steel Platform, SBTi Scope 3, or equivalent certification source found. Independent verification of the process or product carbon content is not available.

5. **Baseline Year and Scope Definition:** The claim does not state a baseline year (required under EmpCo 2024/825), does not distinguish Scope 1 (process carbon) from Scope 2/3 (hydrogen production), and does not provide a quantified reduction target or percentage reduction.

**Impact on Confidence:**
These gaps reduce confidence to 58%. Supporting evidence (TPI, CA100+) is moderate-to-good quality (0.65–0.75 confidence) but does not independently verify the specific hydrogen process. Absence of EU ETS data is neutral (no contradiction) but prevents high-confidence verification. The lack of product certification and timeline prevents escalation of the verdict to MISLEADING (46–60) or GREENWASHING (61+); without multiple contradicting sources, the claim remains in the INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE band.

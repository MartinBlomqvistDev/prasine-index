### The Claim

> "Our commitment to the progressive reduction of emissions from our operations includes cutting methane fugitive emissions and routine or process flaring and venting, and by investing in energy efficiency. We plan to achieve net zero emissions (Scope 1+2) for the upstream business by 2030 and for all Eni businesses by 2035. Remaining emissions will be voluntarily offset through high quality carbon credits."

*Source: Corporate website, https://www.eni.com/en-IT/sustainability/decarbonization/oil-gas.html, accessed 2026-05-11*

### Evidence

**[1] Active Fossil Fuel Expansion Contradicts Net-Zero Timeline**

GOGET Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker, 2026 data shows Eni has 9 oil and gas fields with final investment decisions (FID) currently in active development, including major projects in Libya (Bahr Es Salam Structures A&E), United Arab Emirates (Dalma Gas, Ghasha), Republic of the Congo (Litchendjili Phase 3), and Canada (Bjarni). An additional 20 discovered fields are classified as pre-FID and likely to enter development. Each field locks in 20–40 years of upstream production. GOGET, 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/

**[2] Obstructive Climate Lobbying While Making Green Claims**

LobbyMap rates Eni SpA with a climate policy engagement score of D+, indicating active obstruction or delay of climate legislation. This score directly contradicts the company's stated net-zero commitment. LobbyMap, accessed 2026, https://lobbymap.org/

EU Transparency Register confirms Eni is actively registered as a direct corporate lobbyist (registration no. 99578067285-35). EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do

**[3] Prior Greenwashing Enforcement — Pattern of Unsubstantiated Green Claims**

The Italian AGCM (Autorità Garante della Concorrenza e del Mercato) fined Eni €5 million in 2023 for the 'HVOlution' green diesel campaign. AGCM found that Eni falsely claimed HVO diesel was "green" with a "carbon footprint close to zero" without verifiable lifecycle evidence or appropriate feedstock sourcing documentation. This was the largest greenwashing fine in Italian regulatory history at the time. The enforcement action establishes a documented pattern: prior unsubstantiated green claim → regulatory fine → new net-zero claim without addressing prior substantiation failures.

AGCM Ruling PS12469, 2023, https://www.agcm.it/dotcmsdoc/allegati-news/PS12469%20provvedimento.pdf

**[4] Carbon Offset Mechanism Blacklisted Under EU Law**

The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, explicitly blacklists environmental claims based on voluntary carbon offsetting without certified carbon removals. The directive amends UCPD Annex I to classify such claims as per se unfair commercial practices.

Eni's claim states remaining emissions will be "voluntarily offset through high quality carbon credits." This mechanism is now prohibited under EU law for substantiating net-zero claims.

EmpCo Directive (32024L0825), 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825

**[5] Emissions Reduction in European Operations Only — Not Global Scope 1+2**

E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions data for Eni shows a 77% reduction from 2007 (30.4 Mt CO₂e) to 2024 (7.0 Mt CO₂e). Most recent year (2024): 6.99 Mt. However, E-PRTR captures only Scope 1 emissions from European regulated industrial facilities and does not include:

- Global upstream operations (Eni operates in 27 countries across six continents)
- Scope 2 (indirect energy emissions)
- Scope 3 (value-chain emissions, including combustion of sold gas and oil)

The claim concerns "net zero emissions (Scope 1+2) for the upstream business" — implying global upstream operations. E-PRTR evidence covers only European regulated facilities, not the global Scope 1+2 baseline required to substantiate the claim.

E-PRTR, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

**[6] Institutional Investor Benchmark Assessment (Partial Support)**

CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark (2025) rates Eni as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" and "Capex Alignment = Yes" with partial short-term targets. This assessment by 150+ institutional investors managing trillions in assets supports the credibility of the stated net-zero ambition. However, CA100+ benchmarking is qualitative and self-reported; it does not independently verify fossil fuel expansion, capital allocation to new fields, or lobbying positions.

CA100+, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/

Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) rates Eni's 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees" compatible, with Strategic+ management quality. However, TPI assessment is forward-looking and benchmarked against announced targets, not against actual capital deployment or 2030/2035 interim target feasibility.

TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates

### Assessment

Eni's net-zero claim meets the threshold for **CONFIRMED GREENWASHING** under the Prasine Index standard (criterion a: active lobbying against relevant climate legislation while making green claims).

**Core Contradiction: Lobbying + Expansion**

The most significant finding is the direct contradiction between Eni's stated net-zero commitment and its confirmed obstructive climate lobbying. LobbyMap assigns a D+ rating, indicating active opposition to or delay of climate legislation. This alone satisfies the greenwashing threshold: a company cannot credibly claim net-zero ambition while simultaneously lobbying to obstruct climate policy.

**Fossil Fuel Expansion Incompatible with 2030 Net-Zero Target**

GOGET data confirms Eni has 9 active FID projects (final investment decisions) currently in development and 20 pre-FID discovered fields likely to enter development. These projects lock in decades of upstream production (20–40 years per field). A net-zero target for 2030 (7 years from present) is not compatible with simultaneous capital commitment to new fossil fuel extraction infrastructure. The claim is internally contradictory: new field development requires sustained production revenue through 2050–2070; net-zero by 2030 requires cessation of upstream operations.

The 2035 target for all Eni businesses is theoretically more feasible under 1.5°C science (10-year transition window), but is undermined by the same expansion evidence and the confirmed obstructive lobbying.

**Prior Greenwashing Enforcement Establishes Pattern**

The AGCM €5 million fine (2023) for the HVOlution campaign is not a minor compliance matter. It is evidence of systemic greenwashing: Eni made unsubstantiated claims about a fossil fuel product being "green" without verifiable lifecycle evidence. The current net-zero claim follows the same pattern — ambitious environmental claims without detailed substantiation. Eni has not publicly addressed or remediated the prior enforcement finding.

**Legal Non-Compliance with EmpCo Directive**

The claim explicitly relies on "voluntarily offsetting through high quality carbon credits" to achieve net-zero. The EmpCo Directive (2024/825), in force since March 2024, blacklists this mechanism. Net-zero claims must demonstrate certified carbon removal, not voluntary offsetting. This claim is not compliant with current EU unfair commercial practices law.

**Substantiation Gaps**

The claim provides specific timelines (2030, 2035) and mentions concrete actions (methane reduction, flaring reduction, energy efficiency), which is preferable to vague pledges. However, the claim lacks:

- A quantified baseline (current Scope 1+2 emissions for upstream and total business)
- A detailed transition pathway showing how net-zero will be achieved by 2030/2035 given active fossil fuel expansion
- Independent verification or third-party audit of the claimed reduction measures
- Disclosure of the offset volume, source, and certification standard for "high quality carbon credits"

**Geographic Scope Mismatch**

E-PRTR data shows a genuine 77% reduction in regulated European emissions, a substantial achievement. However, this applies only to European Scope 1 facilities. The claim concerns "net zero emissions (Scope 1+2) for the upstream business" — a global scope. Eni operates in 27 countries; E-PRTR captures only European regulated emissions. The claim cannot be substantiated using European-only data.

**Mitigating Factors**

CA100+ and TPI institutional investor assessments provide some credibility support. These are used by major asset owners to evaluate climate transition quality. However, neither assessment independently verifies capital allocation to fossil fuel expansion or evaluates lobbying positions. Both are based on disclosed targets and announced strategies, not on actual capital deployment or regulatory engagement.

**Dimension-by-Dimension Scoring**

- **Emissions Accuracy (35/100)**: E-PRTR shows genuine European emission reductions, but does not substantiate global Scope 1+2 net-zero claims. GOGET data shows simultaneous fossil fuel expansion, contradicting the 2030 upstream target.
  
- **Claim Substantiation (45/100)**: Claim specifies timelines and actions, but relies on blacklisted offset mechanism (EmpCo violation). No quantified baseline, no detailed transition pathway, no independent audit disclosed.

- **Historical Consistency (65/100)**: AGCM enforcement (2023) establishes pattern of unsubstantiated green claims. Current claim shows no material progress in addressing prior substantiation failures. Repeat pattern identified.

- **Lobbying Alignment (95/100)**: LobbyMap D+ rating confirms active obstructive lobbying while making green claims. This is the primary greenwashing signal and alone justifies CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING verdict.

- **Target Credibility (68/100)**: 2030 upstream net-zero target is not credible given 9 active FID projects in development. 2035 all-business target is theoretically more feasible but undermined by expansion and lobbying evidence. TPI 2050 assessment supports long-term pathway but does not validate interim targets.

Eni is actively lobbying to obstruct climate legislation (LobbyMap D+ rating) while making a prominent net-zero claim, and simultaneously committing capital to 9 new oil and gas field developments that lock in decades of production — a direct contradiction that satisfies the CONFIRMED GREENWASHING threshold. The claim also violates EU 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive) by relying on voluntary carbon offsets rather than certified removals, and follows a 2023 €5 million AGCM greenwashing enforcement action, establishing a pattern of unsubstantiated environmental claims.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Verified Emissions Data**

No installation IDs were registered for Eni in the EU ETS database query. This means direct EU ETS verified emissions data for 2020–2025 is unavailable. This would have provided independently verified Scope 1 emissions for EU-regulated installations and would have allowed direct verification of emission reduction trajectories. 

**Impact on Confidence**: EU_ETS gap is partially mitigated by E-PRTR data, which provides similar regulatory emissions coverage for European facilities (2020–2024), and by GOGET data, which provides comprehensive fossil fuel extraction project pipeline data at global scope. However, the absence of EU ETS data means no independent verification of emissions from EU installations is possible; assessment relies on regulatory E-PRTR data and project-level GOGET data.

**CSRD Scope 3 Emissions Baseline**

CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) mandatory disclosure is not yet in force for Eni (applies from FY2025 reporting cycle for companies >500 employees). No verified Scope 3 emissions baseline (value-chain combustion of sold gas/oil) is available. This limits assessment of total net-zero feasibility but does not affect the core contradiction (lobbying obstruction + fossil fuel expansion).

**Lobbying Detailed Position Data**

LobbyMap provides a D+ rating but does not disclose the specific legislative positions, e.g., which climate bills Eni opposed or delayed. EU Transparency Register confirms Eni is registered but does not indicate lobbying direction. Access to detailed lobbying position records (e.g., EC transparency register amendment submissions, Council working group positions) would strengthen the lobbying finding but is not publicly available in granular form. LobbyMap D+ rating is high-confidence (0.85) and sufficient to establish obstructive engagement.

**Carbon Offset Volume and Certification**

The claim states "remaining emissions will be voluntarily offset through high quality carbon credits" but does not disclose:
- The total volume of offsets required
- The certification standard (Verra, Gold Standard, etc.)
- The source of removals (nature-based, technology-based, etc.)
- The offset price and budget allocation

This information could not be retrieved from public sources. Without this disclosure, the feasibility and integrity of the offset strategy cannot be assessed.

## Assessment

The claim asserts that Danone set a methane reduction target specific to its fresh milk supply in 2023, and that it was "the first company" to do so. This is a forward-looking commitment claim that cannot be evaluated as historical fact.

**Supporting evidence for credibility**: SBTi validation (confidence 0.70) and CA100+ benchmark recognition (confidence 0.65) confirm that Danone maintains independently audited climate targets and investor-recognized net-zero alignment. These two sources provide credibility signals that the company has genuine climate governance structures.

**Critical substantiation gaps**: 

1. **Specific target details absent**: The verbatim claim text provides no baseline year, scope classification (Scope 1/2/3), or reduction methodology. Under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825, in force since March 2024), this constitutes a mandatory substantiation failure. The SBTi record confirms targets exist but does not specify that the methane reduction target applies to fresh milk or disclose the baseline and methodology.

2. **Supply-chain verification unavailable**: The claim targets "fresh milk supply"—a Scope 3 (value-chain) emissions source. Methane from dairy cattle is predominantly biological (enteric fermentation) and falls outside EU ETS mandatory reporting. No independent third-party verified baseline for methane in Danone's milk supply chain appears in the retrieved evidence. This is a data gap, not a contradiction.

3. **"First company" claim unverifiable**: The assertion of primacy—that Danone was "the first company" to set such a target—requires sector-wide competitive analysis across dairy supply chains. No evidence source provides this comparative assessment. This claim cannot be verified or falsified from available data.

4. **No contradicting evidence exists**: Critically, no source states that Danone *did not* set this target or failed to implement it. The absence of verification is not the same as evidence of falsification.

**Lobbying alignment**: Danone's EU Transparency Register entry confirms active lobbying engagement (reg. no. 65744846168-89), but the LobbyMap score (Band B-) indicates neutral or mixed engagement on climate policy—neither supporting nor contradicting the methane reduction claim.

**Historical pattern**: This is not a repeat claim. Danone's prior assessment history (32 prior claims assessed, average score 46.0 in the MISLEADING band) suggests a pattern of aspiration-driven claims without full disclosure of methodological details—a context that supports caution but does not directly contradict the present assertion.

## Key Finding

Danone's SBTi validation and CA100+ recognition confirm credible climate governance, but the specific methane reduction target for fresh milk supply lacks the baseline year, scope, and methodology required by the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825). The "first company" claim cannot be independently verified. The absence of verification is not evidence of greenwashing, but the incompleteness of the claim prevents substantiation.

## Data Gaps

**EU ETS / EUTL Verified Emissions**: Danone holds no installation IDs registered under the EU ETS. This is expected for a food-and-beverage company whose manufacturing facilities may fall below mandatory reporting thresholds or operate outside the EU ETS scope. However, this means the gold-standard regulatory verification of actual emissions reductions is unavailable. The single E-PRTR data point from 2008 (188 tonnes) is insufficient to assess trend or validate methane reduction claims.

**Scope 3 Supply-Chain Baseline**: No independent third-party verified baseline for methane in Danone's fresh milk supply chain is present in retrieved records. Biological methane from dairy cattle is not regulated under EU ETS reporting standards. This gap means the specific claim about "fresh milk supply" cannot be cross-checked against regulatory or third-party verified data.

**Comparative primacy data**: No source addresses whether other food companies set methane reduction targets for dairy supply chains before or after 2023. The "first company" assertion requires sector-wide auditing that is not available in the evidence base.

**SBTi target methodology detail**: While SBTi validation confirms targets are set, the public SBTi record does not disclose the specific baseline year, scope classification, or quantitative reduction percentage for the methane target. Danone's full SBTi submission (which typically contains these details) is not available in the evidence base.

**Confidence reduction**: These gaps reduce confidence to 60% rather than higher, because the claim's specificity (methane, fresh milk, 2023, "first company") is not directly addressed by any single retrieved source.

### Evidence

**[1] ACM Enforcement Ruling (2023)**
The Dutch Authority for Consumers & Markets (ACM) issued a binding ruling against KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' carbon offset campaign, finding it materially misleading. ACM determined that the offsetting schemes promoted by KLM "did not represent real, permanent emission reductions" and required KLM to cease the misleading communications immediately. [ACM/UIT/544682, 2023, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-klm-must-stop-misleading-consumers-about-sustainability]

**[2] Amsterdam District Court Ruling (2023)**
The Amsterdam District Court (Rechtbank Amsterdam) ruled in Fossielvrij NL v. KLM that KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' carbon offset advertising constituted unlawful greenwashing under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD). The court ordered KLM to cease the misleading advertising. This was the first successful climate greenwashing litigation in the Netherlands. [ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615, 2023, https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl/details?id=ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615]

**[3] EU ETS Directive (2003, as amended)**
EU ETS verified emissions data (Article 15, Regulation 601/2012) represents the highest-quality legal ground truth for actual greenhouse gas emissions. Verified figures are produced by accredited independent third-party verifiers and represent mandatory legal disclosure. No installation IDs for KLM are registered in the EU ETS database. [32003L0087, 2003, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087]

**[4] EmpCo Directive (2024)**
The Empowered Consumers Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, amends the UCPD with specific requirements for emissions reduction claims. The Directive mandates that emissions reduction claims state: (i) baseline year, (ii) scope (Scope 1/2/3), and (iii) methodology. Critically, the Directive specifies that "per-unit or intensity-based reduction claims that obscure absolute emission increases are misleading under UCPD Article 6(1)." [32024L0825, 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825]

**[5] EU Transparency Register (active)**
KLM Royal Dutch Airlines is actively registered as a direct corporate lobbyist in the EU Transparency Register (registration no. 76441066192-30). The registration confirms KLM engages EU institutions on policy matters. The direction of lobbying (supportive or obstructive toward climate policy) is not disclosed by registration alone. [EU Transparency Register, active, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do]

### Assessment

KLM's claimed commitment to reduce Scope 1 and 3 jet fuel CO2 emissions by 30% per RTK by 2030 receives a **GREENWASHING verdict** (78/100) due to a combination of regulatory history, structural claim defects, and substantiation failures.

**Critical Signal: Prior Regulatory Findings of Misleading Environmental Claims**

Two independent, binding regulatory rulings (ACM 2023 and Amsterdam District Court 2023, both confidence 0.90) have determined that KLM engaged in unlawful climate greenwashing. Both rulings found KLM's environmental claims materially misleading under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. While these rulings targeted KLM's carbon offset campaign specifically (the 'Fly Responsibly' scheme), they establish a documented pattern that KLM's environmental communication cannot be trusted as substantiated without independent verification. This is not speculative reputational concern — these are binding legal findings against the same company for equivalent environmental misrepresentation. The pattern is material: the company has twice been found by Dutch authorities to have unlawfully misrepresented environmental benefits.

**Structural Defect: Intensity-Based Metric Without Disclosed Methodology**

The claim uses a per-unit metric (per RTK), not an absolute emissions reduction. This is significant under the EmpCo Directive (confidence 0.95, in force since March 2024), which explicitly warns that "per-unit or intensity-based reduction claims that obscure absolute emission increases are misleading under UCPD Article 6(1)." Airlines routinely use per-RTK metrics to claim progress while expanding route networks and passenger volumes — a documented greenwashing pattern in the sector.

The claim discloses the baseline year (2019 ✓) and scope (Scope 1 and 3 ✓) but fails to disclose **methodology**: How will the 30% per-RTK reduction be achieved? Through fleet electrification, sustainable aviation fuels (SAF), operational efficiency, or carbon removal? The absence of this disclosure is a substantiation failure under the EmpCo Directive standard.

**Verification Gap: No EU ETS Data and No Independent Certification**

No installation IDs for KLM are registered in the EU ETS database. While non-ETS airlines may report under CBAM or ICAO CORSIA, the absence of EU ETS data prevents independent verification of the baseline (2019) and trajectory (2030) against mandatory legal disclosure standards. No supporting evidence is present from SBTi (Science Based Targets initiative), CA100+, or other independent target certification bodies. The claim is unsubstantiated by any third-party verification.

**Credibility Assessment: Target Structure and Sector Context**

A 30% per-RTK reduction by 2030 is not a net-zero commitment and falls within Paris Agreement frameworks for accelerated decarbonisation. However, aviation's net-zero pathway requires deep absolute reductions in the 2020s, not just efficiency gains. A per-RTK target allows an airline to claim progress while expanding its fleet and routes — a well-documented greenwashing pattern. Without disclosed methodology and without independent verification, the credibility of this specific target cannot be assessed.

**Why Not CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING?**

The regulatory rulings (ACM and District Court) target KLM's carbon offset campaign ('Fly Responsibly'), which was ruled misleading because offsets did not represent real, permanent reductions. The current claim addresses operational emissions reductions (per RTK), a distinct claim category. While the pattern of misleading environmental communication is established and material, the direct lineage between the 2023 rulings and this operational efficiency claim is not unambiguous — they are different categories of environmental representation, though both part of a broader greenwashing narrative. Accordingly, this assessment reaches GREENWASHING (61–80) rather than CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100).

### Data Gaps

KLM has no registered installation IDs in the EU ETS database. This prevents independent verification of absolute emissions trajectories against the claimed baseline (2019) and target (2030). While non-ETS airlines do exist and report under alternative schemes (CBAM, ICAO CORSIA), the absence of EU ETS data prevents ground-truth verification of whether absolute emissions are rising while per-RTK efficiency improves — the classic airline greenwashing pattern. This gap is significant for confidence calibration: it prevents confirmation of whether the intensity-based claim masks absolute growth.

**No Independent Target Certification**
No SBTi, CA100+, or equivalent independent target certification is on record. This is absence of supporting data, not contradicting data, but it represents a material gap given KLM's regulatory history of misleading environmental claims.

**Claim Publication Date Unknown**

This creates uncertainty about whether the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024) applies, though the directive's substantiation standards are the current legal ground truth regardless.

KLM is registered as an active EU lobbyist, but the EU Transparency Register does not disclose whether lobbying is supportive or obstructive toward climate policy. LobbyMap data was not retrieved. This prevents assessment of whether KLM's policy engagement contradicts its operational emissions claims.

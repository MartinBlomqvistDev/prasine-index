Under Article 15 of the EU Emissions Trading System Directive, verified emissions data from accredited independent verifiers represent the legally mandated ground truth for GHG emissions. Regulation 601/2012 specifies verification requirements. No installation IDs are registered for Securitas AB in the EUTL database.  
Source: EUR-LEX, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087

**[3] EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825)**  
The Empowering Consumers Directive, in force since March 2024, amended UCPD Annex I to require that emissions reduction claims disclose: baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3 breakdown), and methodology. Claims lacking this specificity violate UCPD Article 6(1) substantiation rules. The claim text as published does not state baseline year, scope allocation, or measurable interim targets.  
Source: EUR-LEX, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825

## Assessment

Securitas AB's claim that it "commits to a substantial reduction of its emissions" with "validation" references Science Based Targets initiative approval, which is confirmed and represents a genuine third-party endorsement of 1.5°C alignment. This is the claim's only direct corroborating evidence.

However, the claim cannot be fully assessed due to two separate failures:

**1. Data Verification Gap (Critical)**

The company is not registered under the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS). As a security services company with distributed operations, Securitas may fall below the ETS registration threshold of 20 MW thermal capacity per installation or 25,000 tCO2e/year for relevant activities. This creates an insurmountable verification problem: without EU ETS verified baseline emissions data or access to independently audited Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions baselines, no independent reviewer can:
- Confirm the baseline year against which SBTi targets were calculated
- Verify whether current emissions performance is tracking toward the stated 1.5°C pathway
- Assess whether "substantial reduction" is supported by measurable historical data

The absence of EU ETS data does not contradict the claim—it creates a void. A company setting credible SBTi targets may still lack EU ETS registration, but the absence removes the primary public source for emissions verification.

**2. Substantiation Gap (Under EmpCo Directive)**

The claim as stated on the company website lacks mandatory substantiation elements required since March 2024 under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825):
- **No baseline year disclosed**: "substantial reduction" implies a starting point; it is not stated
- **No scope declaration**: Scope 1, 2, and/or 3 emissions are not specified
- **No interim targets or timeline**: The claim is forward-looking without measurable checkpoints
- **"Validation" is not explained**: The claim references validation (to SBTi) but does not state whether the reduction is absolute, intensity-based, or subject to exclusions

Under UCPD Article 6(1), a claim that lacks these details breaches the substantiation standard because a reasonable consumer cannot assess whether the commitment is genuine or whether the company is on track.

**Scoring Rationale:**

- **Claim Substantiation (25/100)**: SBTi validation exists and is high-confidence (0.90), but the claim text itself is vague and non-compliant with EmpCo requirements. One supporting source partially mitigates but does not resolve the specificity failure.
  
- **Emissions Accuracy (35/100)**: No verified baseline or trajectory data is available. SBTi's methodology implies an audited baseline was used (which provides secondary confidence in target-setting rigor), but actual emissions performance cannot be verified in public registries.
  
- **Target Credibility (15/100)**: SBTi validation confirms 1.5°C alignment with Paris Agreement science. However, target credibility does not verify delivery, baseline accuracy, or scope completeness.

**Historical Consistency:**  
No prior equivalent claims from Securitas AB have been assessed. No track record of broken promises exists.

**Lobbying Signal:**  
Securitas AB is not registered in the EU Transparency Register. No contradicting lobbying activity has been detected.

## Key Finding

Securitas AB has obtained SBTi validation for 1.5°C-aligned targets, which is genuine third-party endorsement. However, the claim as published lacks mandatory disclosure of baseline year and scope under the EmpCo Directive, and EU ETS data are unavailable to verify emissions performance—creating a substantiation and verification gap that prevents conclusive assessment.

## Assessment

RWE AG's claim "Net Zero by 2040" receives a score of **42/100 (INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE)** because the company presents credible independent validation of net-zero alignment and demonstrated emissions reductions, but critical data gaps and an unresolved material contradiction prevent full substantiation.

### Strengths of the Claim

The claim is supported by four credible third-party validators:

1. **SBTi (confidence 0.70)** lists RWE with "Targets set" status and "1.5°C" temperature classification, indicating the target has been submitted to an independent science-based standard and classified as Paris-compatible.

2. **TPI (confidence 0.75)** rates RWE's 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees," the most ambitious Paris alignment category. TPI is used by 150+ institutional investors managing $80 trillion in assets as the standard benchmark for corporate climate transition quality. The assessment also credits RWE with "Level 3: Integrating" management quality, indicating systematic climate governance.

3. **CA100+ (confidence 0.65)** assesses RWE as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes," providing investor-facing validation. However, critically, the benchmark rates both "Capex Alignment" and "Short-term target" as only "Partial," not full — indicating the company has not yet fully aligned capital expenditure or near-term targets with net-zero requirements.

4. **E-PRTR verified emissions data (confidence 0.75)** shows RWE achieved a 47% absolute reduction in regulated industrial emissions from 2007 (122.0 Mt CO₂e) to 2024 (64.7 Mt CO₂e). The recent trajectory is particularly steep: 2023 (73.4 Mt) to 2024 (64.7 Mt) represents a single-year 12% decline. This historical rate of reduction is consistent with a net-zero 2040 pathway (requiring roughly another 50% reduction over the next 16 years) and is not easily dismissed as "efficiency masking" — it represents genuine absolute emissions contraction.

5. **Coal asset retirement (GCPT, confidence 0.60)** shows 4 of 5 coal units are already retired or cancelled, with only 1 unit (57 MW) still operating. This provides concrete evidence of sectoral transition away from coal generation.

### Critical Weaknesses and Data Gaps

Three material gaps prevent a **SUBSTANTIATED** verdict:

#### 1. **EU ETS Data Unavailable (Critical Gap)**

RWE is one of Europe's largest energy producers and should be subject to the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS). However, the primary EU ETS verification database (EUTL) returned "no installation IDs registered" for this assessment. This is a critical absence:

- EU ETS verified emissions are the gold standard for independent monitoring of large European energy companies' Scope 1 emissions.
- Without EU ETS data, it is impossible to verify RWE's baseline emissions, Kyoto Protocol/ETS compliance history, or specific sectoral decarbonisation rates.
- This gap prevents independent verification that the company's stated net-zero trajectory is consistent with its actual historical emissions profile and regulatory compliance.

**Impact on confidence:** Reduces ability to independently verify the claim by approximately 15–20 percentage points.

#### 2. **Unresolved Coal Portfolio Contradiction (Material Substantiation Issue)**

The **Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (GCEL, confidence 0.65)** lists RWE with:
- 44 Mtpa coal mining capacity
- 5.8 GW coal power generation capacity
- Expansion status: "unclear"

This creates a direct credibility tension with the net-zero 2040 claim:

- A company claiming net-zero in 16 years while the largest independent coal tracking database lists it as operating 44 Mtpa of mining and 5.8 GW of generation raises immediate questions about coal phaseout credibility.
- However, **GCPT (confidence 0.60)** shows only 1 coal unit (57 MW) currently operating, with 4 units retired/cancelled. This suggests either: (a) GCEL data is outdated; (b) GCEL capacity figures refer to closed/mothballed facilities; or (c) there is a material discrepancy between the two datasets.

**This discrepancy is unresolved.** The assessment cannot determine whether RWE's coal exposure is actually 5.8 GW (per GCEL) or 57 MW (per GCPT). This ambiguity prevents full confidence in the coal phaseout credibility of the net-zero claim.

**Impact on confidence:** Adds approximately 15–20 points of uncertainty.

#### 3. **EmpCo Directive Compliance Unverifiable (Regulatory Risk)**

The **EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024**, amends the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD) to blacklist environmental claims that lack:

(i) Recognised scientific evidence  
(ii) A credible, detailed transition plan  
(iii) Permanent carbon removals for residual emissions (not carbon offsets)  
(iv) Verifiable monitoring and reporting

The claim "RWE will be Net Zero by 2040" as stated on the company's website **does not disclose:**

- How residual 2040 emissions will be permanently removed (via certified removals, not offsets).
- A detailed, measurable sectoral transition plan for each business unit.
- Interim milestones and verification mechanisms.

Under EmpCo, a generic net-zero pledge without this substantiation constitutes an "unfair commercial practice" — potentially illegal in EU member states from March 2024 onwards.

**Impact on confidence:** Reduces legal compliance confidence to a level requiring immediate corrective disclosure.

#### 4. **CSRD Disclosure Status Unverified**

RWE, as a large multinational energy company with >500 employees and likely EU-listed status, should be **CSRD-obligated from FY2024** under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (2022/2464). CSRD requires mandatory disclosure of Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1.

- No CSRD-verified baseline or scope 3 accounting is referenced in this assessment.
- Absence of CSRD scope 3 disclosure would be material, as Scope 3 (supply chain, customer use) typically represents 50–80% of total emissions for energy companies.
- A net-zero claim that omits scope 3 emissions from regulatory disclosure would be inconsistent and potentially non-compliant.

**Impact on confidence:** Cannot verify that the net-zero claim accounts for full value-chain emissions as required by law.

### Historical Context and Lobbying Alignment

RWE's prior claim history shows **6 previously assessed claims with a trend of IMPROVING score**. This claim is **not a repeat** — the prior claims are not disclosed, but the improving trend suggests the company is refining its environmental communications. This context is moderately supportive of claim credibility but does not overcome the current data gaps.

**Lobbying alignment** is inconclusive:
- RWE is listed in the **EU Transparency Register (confidence 0.75)** as an active EU lobbyist (reg. no. 77608353460-77).
- **LobbyMap score: C** ("Active engagement classification: not assessed"). A C-band score is neutral/mixed — neither strongly supportive of nor actively hostile to climate policy.
- **No confirmed lobbying contradiction** has been identified. A greenwashing verdict requires evidence that the company actively lobbies against the climate policies needed to deliver its stated target; neutral lobbying does not constitute greenwashing.

## Key Finding

**RWE's net-zero 2040 claim is supported by credible third-party validators (SBTi, TPI, CA100+) and demonstrated 47% historical emissions reductions, but cannot be fully substantiated because EU ETS verification data is unavailable, a material contradiction exists between coal portfolio databases (44 Mtpa per GCEL vs. 57 MW per GCPT), and the company has not disclosed the detailed transition plan and permanent carbon removal mechanism required by the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024) — creating regulatory compliance risk.**

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS EUTL (Critical):** No installation IDs registered. This prevents independent verification of RWE's Scope 1 baseline emissions, historical ETS compliance, and sectoral decarbonisation trajectory. For a major European energy company, this is a material absence. *Impact: Reduces confidence by ~15–20 percentage points. If available, would allow verification of claim against verified regulatory baseline.*

2. **COAL_EXIT Dataset Discrepancy (Unresolved):** GCEL lists RWE with 5.8 GW coal power capacity; GCPT shows only 57 MW operating. The discrepancy is not explained by the available data. *Impact: Prevents definitive assessment of remaining coal exposure and credibility of coal phaseout timeline. If GCEL data were reconciled (e.g., clarifying whether 5.8 GW includes mothballed/closed units), confidence would rise to 70%+.*

3. **CSRD-Verified Baseline (Not Provided):** No CSRD scope 1, 2, 3 baseline or transition accounting disclosed in this record. *Impact: Prevents verification that the net-zero claim accounts for full value-chain emissions (Scope 3) as required by mandatory EU reporting. If CSRD disclosure were provided, confidence would rise by ~10 percentage points.*

4. **EmpCo Directive Compliance Documentation (Not Provided):** No detailed transition plan, interim milestones, permanent carbon removal mechanism, or monitoring framework disclosed in the claim text or supporting materials. *Impact: Compliance with EU 2024/825 cannot be verified. The claim as stated may be legally non-compliant. If corrective disclosure were provided, legal risk would be resolved.*

5. **LobbyMap Detailed Assessment:** LobbyMap score "C" is not granular enough to determine lobbying position on specific climate policies. *Impact: Cannot confirm or exclude active anti-climate lobbying. Does not affect current verdict (no lobbying contradiction found) but limits precision of lobbying analysis.*

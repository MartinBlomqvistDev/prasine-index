### The Claim
> "Securitas has committed to reduce absolute scope 1, 2, and 3 greenhouse gas emissions with 42 percent by 2030 from a 2022 base year."

*Source: Corporate website — Investor Relations, https://www.securitas.com/en/sustainability/environmental-policy-reduces-emissions, accessed 2026-05-11*

### Evidence

**[1] EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC (as amended)**
The EU ETS legal framework establishes verified emissions data under Article 15 and Regulation 601/2012 as the highest-quality independent verification mechanism for corporate GHG claims. Securitas AB has no EU ETS-registered installations. For a services company (security and guarding), this is expected and not contradictory, as the ETS applies primarily to energy-intensive industrial and aviation sectors. However, this means no mandatory third-party verified baseline emissions figure is available in the EU Transaction Log (EUTL).
*EUR-LEX, 2003/amended, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087*

**[2] EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825)**
Emissions reduction claims falling within the scope of unfair commercial practices must specify baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology. Securitas's claim meets these structural requirements: baseline year (2022), scope specification (Scope 1, 2, and 3), and reduction magnitude (42% by 2030). The claim complies with the disclosure standard under UCPD Article 6(1) as amended.
*EUR-LEX, 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**[3] Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) — Securitas AB Target Record**
SBTi confirms that Securitas AB has "Targets set" with a temperature classification of 1.5°C. No net-zero target is currently recorded. SBTi's 1.5°C classification indicates institutional assessment that the company's emission reduction pathway is consistent with climate science; however, the SBTi record does not explicitly confirm this specific 42%-by-2030 commitment or provide validation of the baseline emissions figure.
*SBTi target database, year not specified, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

### Assessment

Securitas AB's claim to reduce absolute Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions by 42% by 2030 from a 2022 baseline is structurally compliant with disclosure requirements under EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) and is directionally consistent with science-based climate targets (SBTi 1.5°C classification). However, the claim cannot be independently verified against audited baseline data.

**Claim Substantiation (35/100)**
The claim specifies all required elements: baseline year (2022), emission scope (Scope 1/2/3), and reduction target (42% by 2030). SBTi institutional confirmation that Securitas holds "Targets set" with 1.5°C alignment provides credibility. However, the SBTi record does not explicitly confirm that the specific 42%-by-2030 commitment has been validated as a Science-Based Target. This is a partial substantiation: the target framework is credible, but the exact figure is not independently confirmed.

**Emissions Accuracy (25/100)**
No EU ETS verified baseline data exists for Securitas AB (no installation IDs registered in EUTL). This is not contradictory — services companies are not subject to mandatory EU ETS reporting. However, it creates a critical verification gap: there is no independent regulatory verification mechanism available to audit the 2022 baseline emissions figure or track progress. The company may be using GHG Protocol Corporate Standard methodology with third-party assurance, but this assurance is not cited in available evidence and is not mandated under EmpCo for the services sector. The absence of an audited baseline emissions figure prevents assessment of whether the target is absolute (covering real emissions reductions) or relative (allowing emissions growth offset by efficiency gains).

**Target Credibility (15/100)**
A 42% absolute reduction over 8 years (2022–2030) is consistent with 1.5°C-aligned pathways published by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC). The SBTi 1.5°C classification supports this assessment. However, credibility is contingent on:
1. Verification of the baseline emissions figure
2. Third-party assurance of methodology
3. Available progress data (2023–2025) showing trajectory

None of these are visible in the current assessment. The score reflects this conditional credibility.

**Data Gaps & Confidence Impact**
The primary gap is the absence of independently verified baseline emissions data. The evidence does not include:
- Audited 2022 baseline emissions figure (absolute tonnes CO₂e)
- Third-party assurance statement (e.g., DNV, TÜV) confirming methodology
- Progress data for 2023–2024 (if available as of May 2026)
- Detailed Scope 1, 2, 3 breakdown or identified reduction levers
- SBTi formal validation record explicitly confirming the 42%-by-2030 target

Confidence is set at 62% because SBTi institutional support (70% confidence per evidence record [3]) is offset by the absence of baseline verification and explicit SBTi validation of this specific target. If audited baseline data or an SBTi formal validation letter confirming the 42%-by-2030 commitment become available, confidence would rise to 75%+.

**Regulatory Compliance**
The claim does not violate EmpCo or UCPD. It avoids greenwashing indicators such as: per-unit intensity claims masking absolute growth, aggregation of reductions and increases, or vague environmental language. However, compliance with disclosure standards does not constitute independent verification.

---

### Key Finding

Securitas AB's 42%-by-2030 emissions reduction target is consistent with science-based climate pathways (SBTi 1.5°C classification confirmed) and complies with EU disclosure requirements, but cannot be independently verified against an audited baseline because the company is not subject to mandatory EU ETS reporting and no third-party assurance of baseline emissions is cited. The claim is credible in direction but unverified in fact.

### Evidence

**[1] EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) — Substantiation Standard**
The EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive amendment (in force March 2024) explicitly prohibits net-zero claims lacking: (a) recognised scientific evidence; (b) a credible, verifiable transition plan; (c) certified carbon removal mechanisms for residual emissions. Generic net-zero pledges without measurable pathways are classified as unfair commercial practices under amended Annex I.
*Source: EUR-LEX, 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**[2] CSRD (EU 2022/2464) — Mandatory Emissions Disclosure**
Large EU companies (>500 employees or listed) must disclose scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024. The claim reference provides no link to audited baseline emissions data or independently assured transition metrics.
*Source: EUR-LEX, 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

TPI rates Ørsted's 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees" aligned across checkpoints 2025, 2035, and 2050 (Management Quality Level 4: Strategic). *Source: TPI Benchmark, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*
*Limitation: TPI assesses management quality and strategic intent, not verified emissions baselines or audited reductions. 2022 data predates the 2040 commitment. TPI's 2050 pathway assessment does not automatically substantiate a 2040 net-zero milestone.*

### Assessment

Ørsted's 2040 net-zero claim fails to meet the substantiation standard required under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825). The claim as published is a generic, unquantified pledge with no disclosed pathway, baseline emissions data, or certified carbon removal methodology.

**Substantiation Deficiency (EmpCo Standard):**
The claim text provides no measurable milestones, verification mechanisms, or detailed transition plan. Under EmpCo Annex I, such an unsubstantiated net-zero pledge would be classified as an unfair commercial practice. No link is provided to audited scope 1, 2, or 3 baseline emissions data, and the company does not appear in Prasine Index's CSRD-verified disclosure registry, preventing cross-check against mandatory GHG reporting standards.

**Emissions Baseline Gap:**
The absence of EU ETS installation data is highly material. For a company historically exposed to fossil fuel generation, EU ETS verified emissions form the ground-truth baseline for tracking decarbonisation. The data gap cannot be attributed to a data query error — it reflects either legitimate asset divestment (consistent with net-zero transition) or registration under a different entity (obscuring the actual emissions perimeter). Without resolution, the claim's baseline is unverifiable.

**Weak Supporting Evidence:**
TPI's 2022 assessment of Ørsted as "1.5 Degrees" aligned on a 2050 pathway provides directional credibility but does not substantiate a 2040 net-zero claim. TPI assesses management quality and strategic alignment, not verified emissions reductions or audited pathways. The 2022 vintage predates the 2040 commitment, and different timelines imply different decarbonisation rates — a 2050 "1.5 Degrees" rating does not automatically validate a 2040 net-zero target.

**SBTi Validation Absent:**
The claim has not been validated by the Science-Based Targets initiative, the globally recognised standard used by 5,000+ companies to substantiate climate targets. Without SBTi certification, the claim lacks the third-party verification mechanism expected under EmpCo standards.

**Historical Pattern:**
Prior assessments of Ørsted show a deteriorating trend (average prior score 34.0, current 35.0, worst prior 42). This is not a repeat-pledge, but the deteriorating trend suggests prior claims have moved toward less substantiation — a soft credibility signal.

**No Contradicting Evidence Found:**
Prasine Index found no EU Transparency Register entry, so no active lobbying contradicting the 2040 commitment is on public record. However, absence of evidence is not evidence of credibility.

### Key Finding

Ørsted's 2040 net-zero claim is a generic, unverified pledge that fails the EmpCo Directive substantiation threshold: no audited baseline emissions data, no detailed transition plan, and no SBTi validation. The critical absence of EU ETS emissions data prevents independent verification of the claim's baseline and progress, and TPI's 2022 support (limited to strategic intent assessment) does not substitute for verified decarbonisation metrics. Under EU 2024/825, this claim would be classified as an unfair commercial practice absent published, quantified transition milestones.

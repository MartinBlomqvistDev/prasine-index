## Evidence

**Supporting Evidence**

1. **Historical emissions reduction (verified):** E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions to air fell from 122.0 Mt (2007) to 64.7 Mt (2024)—a verified 47% reduction. The claim that RWE "has already more than halved emissions" is substantiated by this data. [E-PRTR regulated emissions, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/]

2. **Recent emissions trajectory:** E-PRTR data shows a 32% decline between 2020 and 2024 (94.7 Mt → 64.7 Mt), confirming material recent progress. [E-PRTR regulated emissions, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/]

3. **Science-based interim targets:** RWE holds SBTi-validated "Targets set" status with 1.5°C temperature classification, indicating credible interim decarbonization milestones exist. [SBTi target record, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action]

4. **2050 Paris compatibility:** The Transition Pathway Initiative rates RWE's 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees"—within Paris Agreement alignment bounds. [TPI electricity utilities assessment, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates]

5. **Coal asset rationalization:** Global Coal Plant Tracker records 4 of 5 coal units retired or cancelled; only 1 unit (57 MW) currently operating. This indicates active coal retirement, consistent with decarbonization. [GCPT, 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-coal-plant-tracker/]

**Contradicting Evidence**

1. **Substantial unresolved coal exposure:** RWE is listed on Urgewald's Global Coal Exit List with 44 Mtpa coal mining capacity and 5.8 GW coal power capacity. The expansion status is recorded as "unclear." This scale of coal asset base is materially inconsistent with credible 2040 net-zero pathway. [Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list]

2. **Regulatory substantiation gap:** The claim provides no disclosed evidence of compliance with EU 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive) mandatory requirements: (i) residual emissions quantification, (ii) permanent carbon removal strategy (distinct from offsetting), (iii) verifiable interim milestones, or (iv) credible transition plan. Generic net-zero pledges without this substantiation are blacklisted as unfair commercial practices under the Directive's amended Annex I. [EUR-LEX [32024L0825], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825]

3. **CSRD disclosure inconsistency risk:** No evidence that RWE's 2040 net-zero claim is integrated into mandatory scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions reporting under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive. A net-zero target that contradicts or is absent from CSRD disclosure constitutes a material inconsistency. [CSRD (2022/2464), https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464]

4. **Interim target gap relative to claim ambition:** TPI's "1.5 Degrees 2050" assessment does not validate a 2040 net-zero claim—it addresses the 2050 pathway only. A 2040 target (10 years ahead of Paris-compatible industrial utility expectations) requires extraordinary operational evidence of decarbonization, which is not provided. Combined with the 5.8 GW coal base, this suggests reliance on late-stage asset sales or carbon offsetting rather than operational closure.

5. **Lobbying transparency gap:** RWE is registered as an active EU lobbyist (EU Transparency Register, reg. no. 77608353460-77). However, LobbyMap climate engagement score is Band C (neutral/mixed)—providing no clear evidence of pro-climate lobbying alignment or obstruction of EU climate legislation. Lobbying direction remains unconfirmed. [EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/; LobbyMap, https://lobbymap.org/]

## Assessment

RWE's claim receives a **MISLEADING** score because it presents materially incomplete and exaggerated information about the path to 2040 net-zero.

**What is credible:**
The retrospective claim ("more than halved emissions") is verified by E-PRTR data (47% reduction from 2007 to 2024). Recent momentum is real: 32% of reductions occurred between 2020–2024. SBTi validation and TPI's Paris-compatible 2050 rating confirm interim targets and long-term pathway quality exist.

**Why the claim is misleading:**

1. **Coal liability omitted:** The claim presents net-zero ambition without disclosing RWE's 5.8 GW coal power capacity and 44 Mtpa coal mining exposure. For a 2040 net-zero claim to be credible, RWE must demonstrate either: (a) firm exit dates for all coal assets, or (b) operational proof that coal will be fully decarbonized before 2040. Neither is provided. The presence of this coal base—with "unclear" expansion status—creates a forward-emissions liability that contradicts credibility of a 2040 target.

2. **Mandatory EU regulatory non-compliance:** Under EU 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive, in force March 2024), net-zero claims must be substantiated by:
   - Explicit residual emissions quantification
   - Permanent carbon removal strategy (e.g., certified geological sequestration), not offset credits
   - Verifiable interim milestones
   - Credible transition plan disclosed alongside the claim

   The RWE claim provides none of these. It is a generic pledge without the measurable, verified framework required under amended UCPD Annex I. This is an automatic fairness violation.

3. **Scope 2 and 3 integration unknown:** CSRD requires large EU-listed companies to disclose scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions. No evidence is provided that RWE's net-zero claim accounts for full-scope emissions or aligns with mandatory CSRD disclosure. A net-zero claim that excludes scope 2/3 or relies entirely on carbon offset credits violates EmpCo standards.

4. **Interim target ambition without evidence:** A 2040 net-zero target is 10 years earlier than the Paris Agreement's implicit 2050 deadline for developed-economy industrials. TPI's validation addresses the 2050 pathway, not 2040. To claim 2040 net-zero credibly, RWE must provide extraordinary operational evidence (firm coal exit timelines, renewable capacity buildout schedules, carbon removal capacity contracts). This evidence is absent.

5. **Lobbying direction unconfirmed:** EU Transparency Register confirms RWE actively lobbies EU institutions. LobbyMap Band C (neutral/mixed) does not establish whether RWE supports or obstructs EU climate legislation. This creates ambiguity about whether corporate lobbying contradicts the public net-zero claim—a key greenwashing indicator. Without clear evidence, this factor does not elevate the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING but remains a credibility concern.

**Scoring rationale:**
- **Claim Substantiation (52/100):** Verified historical progress pulls the score down, but mandatory EU regulatory gaps (EmpCo, CSRD) and unresolved coal exposure push it up toward MISLEADING.
- **Emissions Accuracy (35/100):** Coal assets and scope 2/3 ambiguity severely undermine the claim's completeness.
- **Target Credibility (62/100):** SBTi interim targets and TPI 2050 rating provide partial support, but 2040 ambition lacks operational evidence.
- **Historical Consistency (30/100):** Recent E-PRTR progress is real, but coal asset base suggests future decarbonization will depend on asset sales or offsets, not operations.
- **Lobbying Alignment (55/100):** Lobbying engagement confirmed but direction unknown; no clear obstruction of climate policy detected.

**Overall: 58/100 (MISLEADING).**

The claim is directionally credible but materially exaggerated through omission of coal liability and lack of mandatory substantiation frameworks. RWE is improving, but this specific claim overstates readiness and hides material uncertainties.

## Key Finding

RWE's 2040 net-zero claim is substantiated only for historical emissions reductions (2007–2024); the forward commitment is **not compliant with EU 2024/825 (EmpCo Directive)** requirements for net-zero claims—it lacks quantified residual emissions, permanent carbon removal strategy, interim milestones, and disclosed transition plan—and it omits disclosure of 5.8 GW coal power capacity and 44 Mtpa coal mining exposure, both of which create unresolved forward-emissions liability inconsistent with 2040 credibility.

### Evidence

**Evidence contradicting the claim:**

1. **GOGET Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker, 2026**: Eni SpA operates 184 oil and gas extraction fields across 27 countries. Nine fields have Final Investment Decision (FID) status with committed capital to new fossil fuel extraction: Bahr Es Salam (Libya), Dalma (UAE), Ghasha (UAE), L10-11 (Netherlands), and Litchendjili Phase 3 (Republic of the Congo), among others. Each FID field locks in decades of upstream production extending well beyond 2030. URL: https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/

2. **LobbyMap Climate Policy Engagement Score, 2026**: Eni SpA scores D+ on climate policy engagement. This classification indicates obstructive engagement — the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation. A company making net-zero commitments while simultaneously engaging in obstructive climate lobbying presents a direct contradiction between stated commitments and demonstrated actions. URL: https://lobbymap.org/

3. **Italian Competition Authority (AGCM) Enforcement Ruling PS12469, 2023**: AGCM fined Eni €5 million for misleading claims in the 'HVOlution' green diesel campaign. The authority found that Eni claimed HVO diesel was "green" with a "carbon footprint close to zero" without supporting evidence. The ruling established that Eni has a documented pattern of making environmental claims without verifiable substantiation. This prior enforcement action is directly relevant to assessing the credibility of the current 2030 net-zero claim. URL: https://www.agcm.it/dotcmsdoc/allegati-news/PS12469%20provvedimento.pdf

**Evidence supporting the claim:**

4. **E-PRTR Regulated Industrial Emissions Data, 2024**: European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register data shows that Eni's regulated industrial emissions to air fell 77% from 2007 (30,446,783.7 tonnes) to 2024 (6,998,034.3 tonnes). This represents a substantial absolute reduction and is consistent with upstream decarbonization. However, E-PRTR covers only regulated facilities within the EU and air emissions, not scope 3 emissions or global upstream operations. The sharp decline from 2023 to 2024 (13.6 Mt to 7.0 Mt) lacks explanatory context and may reflect asset sales, facility closures, or measurement changes rather than operational decarbonization. URL: https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

5. **CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark Assessment, 2025**: CA100+ rates Eni as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes; Capex Alignment = Partial; Short-term target = Partial." This assessment provides qualified support for the company's net-zero positioning. However, the "Partial" capex alignment designation is inconsistent with the GOGET evidence that Eni is simultaneously committing capital to 9 new FID fields. URL: https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/

6. **Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) Assessment, 2022**: TPI rates Eni's 2050 pathway as aligned with 1.5°C Paris targets and assigns "Strategic+" management quality. This supports a long-term (2050) net-zero direction. However, TPI does not validate a 2030 net-zero upstream target. A credible 2030 intermediate target requires a near-term verified pathway, which this assessment does not provide. URL: https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates

**Regulatory context:**

7. **EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), March 2024**: The directive amends the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive to blacklist unsubstantiated environmental claims. Net-zero claims must demonstrate that residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals, not offset schemes. Generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are automatically classified as unfair commercial practices. Eni's 2030 net-zero claim provides no breakdown of emissions reductions versus removals, no certified carbon removal capacity, and no verified transition plan. URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825

8. **Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD, 2022/2464)**: Large companies must disclose scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1. A net-zero claim that contradicts or is absent from mandatory CSRD disclosure constitutes a material inconsistency. URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464

### Assessment

Eni's claim of "Net Zero Upstream by 2030" is directly contradicted by three material lines of verified evidence:

**Capital allocation contradiction (highest weight):** GOGET verified data shows that Eni has committed capital (FID status) to nine new upstream extraction fields. These fields include major developments in Libya, the UAE, the Republic of the Congo, and the Netherlands. Each FID field represents a multi-decade production commitment extending well beyond 2030. A company cannot credibly claim net-zero upstream operations by 2030 while simultaneously locking in capital to new fields that will produce fossil fuels throughout the 2030s and beyond. This is the core greenwashing signal: the stated target is irreconcilable with the company's own capital deployment decisions.

**Lobbying contradiction:** LobbyMap assigns Eni a D+ score for climate policy engagement, indicating obstructive engagement with climate legislation. A company actively opposing climate policy while simultaneously claiming ambitious net-zero targets presents a material contradiction. The company's demonstrated actions (lobbying against climate legislation) directly contradict its stated commitments (net-zero by 2030). This is a primary greenwashing indicator under the Prasine Index framework.

**Pattern of unsupported claims:** The Italian Competition Authority fined Eni €5 million in 2023 for claiming that HVO diesel was "green" with a "carbon footprint close to zero" without supporting evidence. This enforcement ruling establishes a documented pattern of environmental greenwashing by Eni. The current 2030 net-zero claim, made without CSRD baseline disclosures, certified carbon removal capacity, or detailed transition methodology, follows the same pattern: an aspirational pledge unsupported by verifiable evidence.

**Moderating factors:** E-PRTR data documents a genuine 77% reduction in Eni's regulated emissions from 2007 to 2024, indicating real historical decarbonization effort within the EU. CA100+ and TPI both provide qualified support for Eni's long-term net-zero direction (though both explicitly qualify their assessments as "partial" for near-term targets). These factors prevent escalation to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING but do not overcome the material contradictions from GOGET capex data, LobbyMap lobbying score, and prior AGCM enforcement.

**Substantiation gap under EmpCo Directive:** Under EU 2024/825, Eni's net-zero claim fails substantiation tests. The company provides no breakdown of emissions reductions versus certified carbon removals, no verified pathway, and no CSRD baseline disclosures. This constitutes an automatic unfair commercial practice under the amended directive.

The supporting evidence (historical emissions reductions, investor benchmark ratings) is weaker and more temporally limited than the contradicting evidence (active capital commitments, obstructive lobbying, prior enforcement). The company is not on a credible trajectory to achieve 2030 net-zero upstream given verified capital commitments to multi-decade new field development.

### Key Finding

Eni claims "Net Zero Upstream by 2030" while simultaneously committing capital to nine new oil and gas extraction fields with Final Investment Decision status and engaging in obstructive climate policy lobbying — a direct contradiction between the stated target and verified capital allocation. The company was fined €5 million by Italian regulators in 2023 for making unsubstantiated green claims (HVOlution campaign), establishing a pattern of environmental greenwashing that this claim repeats without demonstrated methodological improvement.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation Data:** No EU ETS installation IDs are registered for Eni in the available dataset. This prevents verification of EU ETS baseline emissions or verification of the company's scope 1 emissions under the EU carbon trading system. However, this gap does not undermine the assessment: the GOGET capex data, LobbyMap score, and AGCM enforcement are independent of EU ETS data and constitute sufficient contradictory evidence.

**CSRD Baseline Disclosures:** Eni's 2024 CSRD disclosures (mandatory under the directive for FY2024 reporting) were not available for verification. These disclosures would provide scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions baselines essential to validating a 2030 net-zero target. The absence of these baseline data from the company's own mandatory regulatory filings, combined with the lack of detail in the investor relations claim, increases the risk that the 2030 target lacks internal baseline verification.

**Field-by-field production timeline:** GOGET data confirms FID status for nine fields but does not provide year-by-year production forecasts for each field. This creates uncertainty about whether production from these fields overlaps with 2030 or extends entirely beyond it. However, given that FID fields typically have 5–10 year development timelines and multi-decade production lifespans, the overlap with 2030 is probable. This does not change the contradiction but introduces moderate uncertainty about timing.

**Confidence impact:** The 78% confidence level reflects strong evidence from GOGET (confidence 0.87), LobbyMap (0.85), and AGCM enforcement (0.95), offset by moderate uncertainty about production timeline specifics and unavailable CSRD baseline data. The verdict of GREENWASHING is supported by evidence quality but not certainty approaching CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

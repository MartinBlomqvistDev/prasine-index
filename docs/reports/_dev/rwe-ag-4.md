## Assessment

RWE's claim to phase out coal by 2030 is **plausible but unsubstantiated** against the verification standards required for legal citation.

**Directional Evidence of Progress**

The claim receives support from multiple investor-facing and regulatory datasets. E-PRTR shows a genuine 47-year reduction trend (2007–2024) and a sharp recent decline (31% in four years). Global Coal Plant Tracker confirms active retirement of coal units, with only 1 unit (57 MW) currently operating. CA100+ investors, TPI, and SBTi all rate RWE as having credible net-zero and 1.5°C pathways, lending external validation to the company's stated direction.

**Critical Substantiation Gaps**

However, three material gaps prevent a SUBSTANTIATED verdict:

1. **Missing Verified Emissions Baseline (EU ETS)**: Under EUR_LEX 2003/87/EC, verified EU ETS allowance surrender data is the legal ground truth for scope-1 emissions. RWE's EUTL installation identifiers are not registered in the Prasine access layer, preventing cross-verification of the claimed 2030 coal-exit baseline against audited allowance records. This is not a failure of the claim itself but a critical data unavailability that prevents independent verification.

2. **Lack of SBTi Coal-Exit Certification**: The claim specifies a 2030 coal-exit milestone. SBTi validates RWE's net-zero pathway at 1.5°C but does not explicitly certify a 2030 coal-exit intermediate target. CA100+ notes Capex Alignment and Short-term targets as "Partial," indicating incomplete documentation of transition capex roadmap by asset class.

3. **EmpCo Directive Compliance Deficiency**: Under EU 2024/825 (in force since March 2024), the claim lacks explicit statement of: (a) baseline year; (b) scope designation (Scope 1/2/3); (c) methodology for "socially responsible manner" (aspirational language unsupported by measurable criteria). This creates potential non-compliance with UCPD Article 6 substantiation standard.

**Lobbying Alignment — Inconclusive**

No documented active lobbying by RWE against coal-exit legislation is on record. However, InfluenceMap's "C" grade (neutral/mixed) indicates absence of affirmative evidence of RWE advocacy for coal-phase-out policy. EU Transparency Register confirms RWE is an active lobbyist but does not disclose policy positions. This dimension is ambiguous, not contradictory.

**Baseline Consistency**

Urgewald's listing of RWE with 5.8 GW coal capacity is not a contradiction of the 2030 exit claim — it establishes the baseline from which RWE must exit. The "expansion status unclear" classification does not confirm expansion or failure to phase out, making this a qualifying rather than directly contradicting signal.

**Company History Context**

RWE has 8 prior Prasine Index assessments with an average score of 49.8 and a "IMPROVING" trend. No prior equivalent 2030 coal-exit claims are detected, so this is not a repeat claim. The company's historical improvement trajectory (49.8 average, prior worst score 64.0) suggests partial credibility but incomplete substantiation relative to legal standards.

## Key Finding

RWE's 2030 coal-exit claim is supported by genuine emissions reductions (47% decline 2007–2024 per E-PRTR) and investor validation (CA100+, TPI, SBTi), but cannot be independently verified as substantiated because: (1) verified EU ETS baseline data is unavailable; (2) the 2030 coal-exit target lacks SBTi certification; and (3) the claim lacks explicit baseline year, scope definition, and methodology as required by EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825). This is not greenwashing (no documented lobbying opposition to coal exit), but unresolved material gaps prevent confidence sufficient for legal citation.

## Data Gaps

- **EU ETS Installation Registry (EUTL)**: RWE's installation IDs are not registered. This prevents access to verified allowance surrender data under Regulation 601/2012, the gold-standard baseline for scope-1 emissions auditing. Without this, the rate of emissions reduction necessary to reach full coal exit by 2030 cannot be verified against a legal ground truth.

- **SBTi Coal-Exit Target Certification**: SBTi records validate RWE's net-zero and 1.5°C pathway but do not explicitly list a 2030 coal-exit intermediate target as a separately validated milestone. This gap prevents third-party validation of the specific 2030 date.

- **Transition Capex Roadmap by Asset Class**: CA100+ rates Capex Alignment as "Partial," indicating that detailed capex allocation to coal retirement vs. renewable/storage investment is not publicly documented in accessible investor records. This prevents independent assessment of whether capex commitments match the 2030 timeline.

- **EmpCo Directive Baseline and Scope Disclosure**: The claim does not explicitly state baseline year or scope (Scope 1/2/3) as required by EU 2024/825. Without this, compliance with UCPD Article 6 substantiation standard cannot be confirmed.

These gaps reduce confidence from 62% to what it would be with full data availability (estimated 78–85%). **The confidence score of 62% reflects moderate uncertainty due to data unavailability, not inherent implausibility of the claim.**

The Italian AGCM (Autorità Garante della Concorrenza e del Mercato) issued a binding enforcement ruling in 2023 (AGCM PS12469) fining Eni €5 million for misleading environmental claims. The AGCM found that Eni's claim that HVO (hydrotreated vegetable oil) diesel was "green" and had a "carbon footprint close to zero" was unsubstantiated because feedstock sourcing and lifecycle methodology did not support the claims. This was the largest greenwashing fine in Italian regulatory history at the time of the ruling and establishes a documented pattern of environmental claim substantiation failure by the same company.

*Source: AGCM PS12469 enforcement ruling, 2023, https://www.agcm.it/dotcmsdoc/allegati-news/PS12469%20provvedimento.pdf*

**[8] Legislative Requirement — EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825)**

The EU Corporate Sustainability Directive Amendments (EmpCo Directive, EU 2024/825, in force March 2024) amend the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive to blacklist unsubstantiated environmental claims. Net-zero claims must demonstrate that residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals, not offset credits. Generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are automatically classified as unfair commercial practices.

Eni's claim fails substantiation under this standard because:
- No credible abatement/removal split disclosed
- No verified transition plan showing how Scope 3 (upstream production) will reach zero
- No baseline year or interim targets disclosed in the claim itself
- Relies on generic net-zero framing without demonstrating permanent carbon removal for residual emissions

*Source: [32024L0825] EmpCo Directive, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**[9] Mandatory Reporting Requirement — CSRD**

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD, 2022/2464, applicable FY2024 onwards) requires large companies to disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 standard. A net-zero claim that contradicts or is absent from mandatory CSRD disclosure constitutes material inconsistency.

*Source: [32022L2464] CSRD, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

## Assessment

Eni SpA's net-zero 2050 claim is **confirmed greenwashing** under three independent regulatory and business logic standards, each individually sufficient to trigger this verdict.

### I. Prior Regulatory Enforcement Ruling (CONFIRMED TRIGGER)

The AGCM 2023 enforcement action (AGCM PS12469) fined Eni €5 million for claiming HVO diesel was "green" and had a "carbon footprint close to zero" without verifiable lifecycle evidence. This ruling establishes that Eni has a documented pattern of making unsubstantiated environmental claims. Under the Prasine Index framework, a prior court or regulatory ruling against an equivalent claim type (environmental substantiation failure on a fossil fuel transition pathway claim) confirms that current claims by the same company cannot be trusted. The AGCM explicitly found that Eni's feedstock sourcing and lifecycle methodology did not support its green claims — a substantiation failure directly analogous to the current net-zero claim's lack of disclosed removal mechanisms and transition pathway detail.

**Confidence: 0.95** (regulatory ruling is definitive and independent of subsequent data gaps)

### II. Lobbying Obstruction Contradicts Net-Zero Pledge (CONFIRMED TRIGGER)

LobbyMap assigns Eni a **D+ score**, indicating active opposition to or delay of climate legislation. This is irreconcilable with a credible net-zero commitment. A company cannot simultaneously pledge net-zero achievement while lobbying against the regulatory framework required to deliver that pledge. Under the assessment framework, a D+ LobbyMap rating is a sufficient standalone trigger for CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING; no additional evidence of specific legislation opposed is required.

The contradiction is structural: Eni's stated climate commitments are negated by the company's own documented lobbying activity. This is a primary greenwashing indicator.

**Confidence: 0.85** (LobbyMap methodology is transparent and independently verified; D+ classification is definitive)

### III. Active Fossil Fuel Expansion Directly Contradicts Net-Zero (CONFIRMED TRIGGER)

GOGET verified data (March 2026) confirms Eni has **9 oil and gas fields with Final Investment Decisions (FID)**, including major developments in Libya, United Arab Emirates, Netherlands, and Congo. FID-stage projects lock in decades of upstream production, cash flows, and operational carbon emissions. Simultaneously, Eni operates 144 active producing fields and holds 20 pre-FID discoveries likely entering development.

This is direct, irreconcilable business contradiction: an oil major expanding FID-stage extraction projects cannot credibly claim net-zero leadership by 2050. The capital deployed to these fields will generate cumulative emissions far exceeding any abatement achieved through operational efficiency or renewable energy investment.

**Confidence: 0.87** (GOGET data is field-by-field verified; FID status is binding capital commitment)

### IV. Claim-Specific Substantiation Failures

The claim contains internal contradictions and greenwashing-typical pattern shifts:

**Scope 1+2 measured in absolute terms (2030/2035)** vs. **Scope 1, 2, 3 measured in intensity (2050)**

This pivot from absolute to intensity measurement is a classic greenwashing technique. Intensity metrics (emissions per unit of production) allow production volume to grow while per-unit emissions fall, enabling a company to claim net-zero while maintaining or increasing absolute emissions and production. The claim does not disclose expected production volume in 2050, making intensity targets unverifiable.

Example: If Eni increases upstream production by 30% by 2050 but reduces per-barrel emissions by 35%, it can claim 5% intensity improvement while absolute emissions remain flat or grow. This is not net-zero; it is reduction in carbon intensity.

**No baseline, removal mechanism, or transition pathway disclosed**

The claim lacks:
- Stated baseline year (2005? 2020? 2023?)
- Split between abatement (emissions avoided) and removal (certified carbon sequestration)
- Methodology for achieving Scope 3 net-zero (upstream production is Scope 3 for Eni's customers)
- Interim milestones between 2035 and 2050

Under EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), this constitutes automatic breach of substantiation requirements for net-zero claims.

### V. Historical Emissions Reductions Do Not Support Future Claims

E-PRTR data shows 77% reduction in regulated industrial emissions from 2007 to 2024. However, this reduction reflects facility decommissioning and divestment, not operational transformation. Critically, E-PRTR covers only regulated industrial installations, not upstream extraction emissions (Scope 3), which represent 80–90% of an oil/gas company's total footprint.

The fact that Eni has reduced emissions from *divested assets* does not support a claim that it will reduce Scope 3 emissions from *operating upstream fields* while simultaneously investing in 9 new FID projects. This is historical reduction from portfolio exit, not credible forward-looking transition.

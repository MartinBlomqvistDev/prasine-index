### Assessment

**Emissions Progress vs. Forward Contradiction**

Eni's historical record shows genuine operational decarbonisation. E-PRTR data confirm regulated emissions fell 77% from 2007 to 2024, with a 49% year-on-year reduction from 2023 to 2024. This establishes Eni is not fabricating emissions reduction capability. CA100+ and TPI benchmark assessments by major investor coalitions provide credible third-party endorsement of strategic intent and Paris-alignment.

However, the forward claim (2030 upstream net-zero) is directly contradicted by verified asset data. GOGET documents 9 extraction fields with Final Investment Decision — meaning capital is already committed to decades of new fossil fuel production. Named examples include Bahr Es Salam (Libya), Dalma Gas Field (UAE), Ghasha (UAE), L10-11 (Netherlands), and Litchendjili Phase 3 (Congo), plus 20 additional discovered fields likely entering development. A company cannot simultaneously achieve net-zero upstream emissions in 2030 (7 years from publication) while locking in new production capacity that will operate for 15–30 years. The physics of the claim is irreconcilable with the portfolio.

**Regulatory Greenwashing Precedent**

Eni's 2023 €5 million AGCM fine for the HVOlution campaign establishes a regulatory pattern: the company has made unsubstantiated green claims before. AGCM found Eni falsely claimed HVO diesel was "green" and had a "carbon footprint close to zero" without verifiable lifecycle evidence. This is the largest greenwashing fine in Italian regulatory history. The current net-zero pledge, published in parallel with continued upstream expansion (FID fields), follows the same pattern: aspirational climate language coupled with contradictory capex commitments.

**Lobbying Obstruction**

LobbyMap rates Eni D+ — "obstructive engagement" — indicating the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation while making green claims. This directly undermines the credibility of a 2030/2035 net-zero pledge. The company is betting on policy failure, not operational transformation. A company that lobbies against climate legislation cannot credibly claim commitment to net-zero in 7 years.

**Substantiation Gaps Under EU Law**

The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825, in force March 2024) blacklists net-zero claims that lack a "credible, verifiable transition plan." The claim text provides no baseline year, no disclosed methodology for measuring Scope 1+2 emissions (whether inclusive of all production or selective), no capex allocation to zero-carbon assets, and no decommissioning timeline. The company's CSRD disclosure (mandatory from FY2024 for large EU-listed companies) is not referenced in the record, creating material inconsistency between the marketing claim and mandatory sustainability reporting obligations.

**Why This Is Greenwashing, Not Confirmed Greenwashing (74/100, not 81+)**

The verdict is GREENWASHING (61–80 band) rather than CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100) because:

1. The E-PRTR emissions reduction is genuine and verified — the company is not fabricating historical progress.
2. CA100+ and TPI provide credible third-party endorsement of strategic intent (not false corporate self-dealing).
3. No enforcement ruling has yet been issued on this specific claim under EmpCo or CSRD.
4. The claim is forward-looking and dependent on future technology/policy; if Eni exits FID projects and sustains current emissions trajectory, it could be reclassified.

However, the verdict rises to GREENWASHING because:

1. **GOGET asset data (conf=0.87)** directly contradict the 2030 upstream target: 9 FID fields with committed capital mean Eni is actively expanding fossil fuel production now.
2. **ENFORCEMENT ruling (conf=0.95)** establishes a regulatory precedent of unsubstantiated green claims by this company.
3. **LobbyMap D+ (conf=0.85)** shows active obstruction of climate legislation — incompatible with a credible net-zero pledge.
4. **Substantiation gaps** under EmpCo Directive and CSRD create material inconsistency.

### Key Finding

Eni claims to achieve upstream net-zero by 2030 while simultaneously committing capital to 9 new extraction fields (with 20 additional discovered fields entering development) that will operate for decades — making the 2030 target physically impossible to achieve. Combined with the company's 2023 €5 million greenwashing fine, its D+ lobbying obstruction rating, and undisclosed transition methodology, the pledge lacks credibility despite genuine historical emissions reductions.

## The Claim

> "The Science Based Target initiative validated this Inter IKEA Group target in June 2024. In accordance with the Net-Zero Standard, by FY50 we will reduce the absolute GHG emissions from the IKEA value chain by at least 90% compared to FY16, with residual emissions neutralized by removing and storing carbon within our own value chain."

*Source: IKEA Group corporate website, https://www.ikea.com/global/en/our-business/sustainability/our-climate-agenda, 2026*

## Evidence

**[1] SBTi Registry Record**
The Science Based Targets initiative public database lists IKEA Group's net-zero target status as "unknown" with "not classified" temperature alignment and no validation date on file. The SBTi registry does not confirm the June 2024 validation cited in the claim.
*Source: SBTi, target record, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action, 2026*

**[2] E-PRTR Regulated Emissions Trend**
IKEA Group's reported regulated industrial emissions (E-PRTR scope) to air show the following trajectory: 2020: 323.2 t; 2021: 1,430.0 t; 2022: 1,599.5 t; 2023: 1,242.0 t; 2024: 110,243.0 t. Excluding the 2024 figure (flagged as likely a first-disclosure event for a new facility or pollutant category), regulated emissions rose 902% from 2010 to 2023. The confidence in the 2024 data is low; the pre-2024 trend contradicts the trajectory implied by a 90% absolute reduction commitment.
*Source: E-PRTR (European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register), pollution record 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/, 2026*

**[3] EU Lobbying Registration**
IKEA Group is actively registered in the EU Transparency Register as a direct corporate lobbyist (Netherlands HQ, registration no. 774019931221-41). Registration confirms active engagement with EU institutions; the substantive direction of lobbying activity (climate-supportive or climate-hostile) is not specified by the register entry alone.
*Source: EU Transparency Register, lobbying record, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do, 2026*

**[4] InfluenceMap Climate Policy Engagement Assessment**
InfluenceMap assigns IKEA Group a B-band climate policy engagement score, indicating supportive alignment with public climate commitments. No contradiction between lobbying activity and stated net-zero target has been detected.
*Source: InfluenceMap, lobbying assessment, https://influencemap.org/company-responses, 2026*

**[5] EU Green Claims Directive Legislative Framework**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) mandates that science-based target claims must be accompanied by publicly disclosed methodology and independent validation (e.g., SBTi approval). Claims referencing SBTi without confirmation of current validation status may be misleading under the Directive.
*Source: EUR-LEX, legislative record [52023PC0166], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166, 2023*

## Assessment

IKEA Group claims that the Science Based Targets initiative validated a net-zero target in June 2024, committing to 90% absolute GHG emissions reduction by FY50 (2050) relative to FY16 (2016), with residual emissions neutralized through carbon removal. Assessment reveals three material substantiation gaps:

**SBTi Validation Status Unconfirmed**

The claim explicitly states: "The Science Based Target initiative validated this...target in June 2024." However, the SBTi public registry (the authoritative source for validation status) shows IKEA's net-zero target as "unknown" with no validation date recorded. This creates direct tension between the company's assertion and the SBTi database record. While a recent validation may not yet be indexed in the SBTi system (confidence in the SBTi record is 0.65, reflecting possible timing lags), the absence of any validation timestamp in SBTi's public record is a substantiation failure under the Green Claims Directive, which requires that science-based claims be "independently validated." A claimed validation that does not appear in the validator's own registry cannot be said to be publicly confirmed.

**Baseline Emissions Data Not Verified**

The 90% reduction claim references FY16 as the baseline year. No audited absolute GHG inventory for FY16 or current year has been located in publicly available sources. Without access to IKEA's detailed GHG accounting (Scope 1, 2, and 3 boundaries; value-chain inclusions; baseline methodology), the percentage reduction claim cannot be independently verified. E-PRTR data—the only available regulated emissions source—captures only a narrow subset of IKEA's total value-chain footprint (direct industrial facilities subject to air quality reporting) and is therefore not a reliable proxy for the full scope of the company's claimed reduction target.

**Regulated Emissions Trend Does Not Yet Reflect Reduction Path**

E-PRTR regulated emissions from IKEA's operations rose 902% from 2010 to 2023 (excluding the anomalous 2024 spike). This observed upward trend in the company's direct operational footprint contradicts the spirit of a "90% reduction by 2050" commitment and suggests that emissions-reduction execution is not yet evident in the measured data, even accounting for the fact that E-PRTR captures only a small fraction of the company's total value-chain GHG footprint. The rising trend may reflect increased manufacturing output, facility additions, or improved reporting rather than emissions intensification, but the data does not show proof of a reduction path underway.

**Supporting Evidence**

InfluenceMap assigns IKEA a B-band climate policy engagement score, indicating supportive alignment with climate commitments. No contradiction between the company's lobbying activity and its stated net-zero target has been detected. EU Transparency Register data confirms IKEA is an active corporate lobbyist; the direction is supportive, not obstructive. This supports the plausibility of the commitment but does not validate the emissions claim itself.

**Target Parameters Are Science-Aligned**

The stated target—90% absolute reduction by 2050 with residuals neutralized through carbon removal—is consistent with 1.5°C science-based pathways and exceeds the IPCC minimum expectation (>80% reduction by 2050 for industrial and retail sectors). Reliance on carbon removal for the final 10% of emissions is acceptable within SBTi's Net-Zero Standard methodology. The target is credible and appropriately ambitious.

**Critical Data Gap: No Full GHG Inventory Available**

No EU ETS installations are registered to IKEA Group; the company operates no installations under the Emissions Trading System. Neither E-PRTR nor EU ETS captures IKEA's full value-chain GHG footprint (supply chain, operations, use phase, end-of-life). Without access to the company's published GHG inventory (or a regulatory mandate to disclose it), the claim cannot be verified against a baseline or cross-checked against independent audits.

## Key Finding

The SBTi validation cited in the claim is not recorded in the Science Based Targets initiative's public registry; as of the evidence available, IKEA's net-zero target shows "unknown" validation status. Combined with the absence of verified baseline (FY16) absolute emissions data, this prevents independent confirmation that the claimed 90% reduction target has been formally validated by SBTi as required by the EU Green Claims Directive. The company's regulated emissions trend (E-PRTR) has risen steeply through 2023, contradicting evidence of reduction path execution, though this covers only a small subset of the company's total value-chain scope.

## Data Gaps

1. **SBTi Validation Date**: The SBTi public registry does not record a June 2024 validation date for IKEA's net-zero target. This may reflect a timing lag between the company's internal validation and SBTi's public database update. However, as of 2026-05-05, no validation is confirmed in the official record. Confidence in the SBTi record is 0.65, meaning a recent indexing delay cannot be ruled out, but the absence of evidence is a substantiation gap.

2. **Baseline GHG Inventory (FY16)**: No audited absolute GHG emissions data for IKEA Group's FY16 baseline year is available in public regulatory sources (E-PRTR, EU ETS) or accessed via web search. The company's sustainability reports or GHG disclosure statements (if published) were not queried. Without this baseline, the 90% reduction claim cannot be verified against a documented starting point.

3. **Current-Year GHG Inventory**: No verified total GHG footprint (Scope 1 + 2 + 3) for IKEA Group's current financial year is available in public sources. Internal company reports may exist but were not accessed.

4. **Value-Chain Scope Definition**: The claim specifies "IKEA value chain" but does not provide a detailed accounting of scope boundaries (Scope 1, 2, 3 inclusions; supply chain tier coverage; use-phase and end-of-life assumptions). Without this specification, the scope of the 90% reduction cannot be independently assessed.

5. **EU ETS Data**: IKEA Group has no registered EU ETS installations. This is not a data gap in the adversarial sense—the company simply does not fall under the ETS—but it means emissions-trading data cannot serve as a verification source.

These gaps result in a confidence level of 65% (moderate). The SBTi validation gap is the primary substantiation issue; the baseline data gap prevents quantitative verification. If IKEA's SBTi validation were confirmed in the official registry and the company published an audited GHG inventory with clear scope boundaries, the assessment could be upgraded to SUBSTANTIATED (60+).

## Assessment

### Claim Structure and Substantiation Failure

The claim comprises three forward-looking commitments: (1) setting a "new world standard for mining," (2) developing "carbon-free processes and products," and (3) extracting critical minerals. None is accompanied by explicit timelines, measurable baselines, quantified interim targets, or recognised scientific validation (SBTi, ISO 14064, or equivalent).

Under the Green Claims Directive Article 3(1), this claim fails substantiation because:
- **"Set a new world standard"** is unquantified and unmeasurable. No metric, no comparison baseline, no deadline.
- **"Develop carbon-free processes and products"** lacks a baseline year, interim milestones, or certified pathway. The claim does not specify whether this applies to all products, all operations, or a subset. No science-based target validation is cited.
- **"Extract critical minerals"** omits the energy-intensity and carbon-intensity implications of mineral extraction, which contradicts the credibility of the "carbon-free" commitment. The claim does not address how extraction will be decoupled from fossil fuel inputs.

The claim is aspirational and non-binding, structured to avoid falsifiability while creating an impression of environmental leadership. **Verdict on substantiation: fails Article 3(1) test.**

### Emissions Accuracy: Recent Improvement Contradicted by Long-Term Deterioration

E-PRTR data reveals a contradictory emissions trajectory:

- **Recent improvement (2020–2024):** LKAB's regulated emissions fell 25% over four years, from 1,326,063 to 993,354 tonnes. If this 5% annual decline rate persists, the company would reach approximately 50% reduction by 2050—not net-zero.

- **Long-term deterioration (2007–2024):** Despite decades of sustainability commitments, LKAB's emissions have risen 19% since 2007 (834,047 → 993,354 t), a period covering multiple EU climate targets and corporate pledges. This indicates historical operational expansion without proportionate decarbonisation.

- **Absolute emissions remain 1 Mt/year.** The claim promises "carbon-free processes and products." At 993 kt CO2e annually, the gap between current state and true carbon-free operations is vast, unquantified, and unexplained. No interim 2030 or 2040 target is disclosed.

**Interpretation:** The recent 25% reduction is meaningful evidence of genuine effort (confidence 0.55 due to inconclusive long-term signal). However, it does not support the credibility of an imminent or near-future "carbon-free" transition. The claim exaggerates readiness by omitting: (a) the 19-year history of rising absolute emissions; (b) the 1 Mt/year baseline; (c) the required pace of future decarbonisation.

### Historical Consistency: Pattern of MISLEADING Claims

LKAB's prior assessment record (7 claims, average score 56.0) reveals a consistent pattern of aspirational, unsubstantiated environmental commitments. The stable trend (no material improvement in claim quality over time) suggests systematic over-statement rather than learning. This is the first assessment of the specific claim under review (not a repeat), but it is consistent with the company's historical greenwashing profile.

### Target Credibility: Unquantified, Non-Binding, Unfalsifiable

The claim provides no explicit target year for "carbon-free" status. This creates two credibility problems:

1. **Unfalsifiability:** Without a deadline, the claim cannot be audited or falsified. It functions as aspirational messaging rather than a commitment.
2. **Implausible trajectory:** Mining is energy-intensive. Primary copper, iron ore, and rare earth production require significant thermal and electrical input. Absolute decarbonisation to near-zero by 2050 is theoretically possible (via process electrification, renewable power contracts, and redesign), but requires capex reallocation on an unprecedented scale. LKAB has not published a quantified decarbonisation pathway, interim milestones, or science-based target validation.

The 25% reduction over 4 years is not evidence of trajectory toward "carbon-free." It reflects operational improvements and incremental efficiency gains—not the systemic transformation required for zero-carbon mining.

## Key Finding

LKAB's claim to "develop carbon-free processes and products" fails substantiation under the EU Green Claims Directive because it provides no baseline, timeline, or interim milestones; meanwhile, E-PRTR data shows the company's absolute emissions have risen 19% since 2007 and remain at ~1 Mt CO2e/year in 2024, contradicting the credibility of an imminent transition to carbon-free operations. The claim exaggerates readiness through strategic omission of quantified targets and timelines.

## Data Gaps

**EU ETS Registration Status:** No LKAB installation IDs were found in the EU Emissions Trading System registry. This is a critical gap. LKAB is a major mining and mineral processing company; the absence of EU ETS coverage suggests either: (a) LKAB facilities fall below the 10 MW heat input threshold and are not mandatory EU ETS participants, or (b) the company reports exclusively through the E-PRTR voluntary route. 

**Impact on confidence:** Without EU ETS data, we cannot cross-verify emissions claims against the EU's most robust, real-time emissions registry. This limits our ability to independently audit scope 1 + 2 emissions and validate any claims about power source (renewable vs. fossil). Confidence is reduced from ~75% to 58% as a result. E-PRTR data remains the primary source and is reliable, but dual-source confirmation is unavailable.

**Missing Evidence:**
- No Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) validation or approved target document found.
- No CA100+ or InfluenceMap lobbying record available for cross-reference.
- No published decarbonisation pathway or interim milestones document located.
- No lifecycle assessment or Scope 3 (supply chain) emissions boundary disclosed.

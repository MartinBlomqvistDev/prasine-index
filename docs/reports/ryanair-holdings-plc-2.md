## Evidence

**[1] ASA Enforcement Ruling — Misleading Emissions Claim (2020)**
The UK Advertising Standards Authority ruled on ASA complaint A20-529462 that Ryanair's claim to have the "lowest carbon emissions" was **misleading**. The company could not substantiate that it had lower CO2 per passenger than other European airlines when evaluated on the same basis. This is a regulatory finding by a court-equivalent consumer protection authority.

*Source: ASA (UK), 2020, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/ryanair-ltd-g21-1080990.html*

**[2] European Commission Investigation — Airline Greenwashing Sweep (2024)**
The European Commission and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation under CPC Regulation 2017/2394 into Ryanair's environmental claims about "carbon emissions, offsetting, and sustainability credentials," as part of a broader airline greenwashing sweep covering Ryanair, Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa.

*Source: European Commission, 2024, https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_2057*

**[3] Urgewald Global Coal Exit List — Active Coal Expansion (Year undated)**
Ryanair Holdings plc is listed on the Global Coal Exit List as **actively expanding coal capacity** with mining expansion status recorded as "Yes." The GCEL is the standard coal screening tool used by 400+ financial institutions (GFANZ, PAII).

*Source: Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list*

**[4] Green Claims Directive — Substantiation Standard (2023)**
The proposed Green Claims Directive [52023PC0166] establishes that net-zero claims must demonstrate: (a) explicit baseline year and reduction pathway, (b) permanent removal of residual emissions through **certified carbon removals** (not offset credits), and (c) substantiation meeting Article 3 standard. Article 7 explicitly prohibits claims "based solely on carbon offsetting schemes."

Ryanair's published claim provides: no baseline year, no disclosed methodology for permanent carbon removal, no distinction between absolute emissions reduction and offsets.

*Source: EUR-LEX [52023PC0166], 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**[5] InfluenceMap — Obstructive Climate Policy Engagement (Rating: D+)**
InfluenceMap rates Ryanair Holdings plc as **D+** — indicating obstructive engagement with climate legislation. This score represents active opposition to or delay of climate policy while simultaneously making climate commitments, the canonical definition of greenwashing.

*Source: InfluenceMap, https://influencemap.org/company-responses*

**[6] SBTi — No Validated Net-Zero Target**
SBTi records Ryanair as having **no validated net-zero target** and status listed as "unknown." The company has not submitted its 2050 pathway to independent science-based validation.

*Source: Science Based Targets initiative, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

**[7] TPI Assessment — Below 2°C Compatibility (2022)**
The Transition Pathway Initiative (used by 150+ investors with $80tn AUM) rated Ryanair's 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" (Paris-compatible), with Management Quality Level 2 (Acknowledging). This is the sole independent supporting assessment of the 2050 claim. However, this rating is dated 2022 and predates the 2024 EC investigation, the InfluenceMap D+ classification, and the GCEL coal expansion listing.

*Source: Transition Pathway Initiative, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*

**[8] EU Transparency Register — Active Lobbyist Registration**
Ryanair is registered as an active EU lobbyist (direct corporate lobbyist, reg. no. 002977215945-85). Registration confirms policy engagement activity; direction of lobbying position verified via InfluenceMap data (see [5]).

*Source: EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do*

**[9] Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive — Mandatory Emissions Disclosure (2022)**
The CSRD [32022L2464] requires large companies to disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024. A net-zero claim that contradicts or remains absent from mandatory CSRD disclosure constitutes material inconsistency.

*Source: EUR-LEX [32022L2464], 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

## Assessment

Ryanair's claim to have "developed a pathway to achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2050, which aligns with the Paris Agreement" receives a **GREENWASHING** verdict. The score of 78/100 reflects material contradictions across multiple independent evidence sources, partially mitigated by one supporting investor assessment, but insufficient to reach CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING status.

### Critical Contradictions

**Regulatory Enforcement History (Highest Weight)**
Ryanair was found to have made **misleading emissions claims** by the UK ASA in 2020 (ASA A20-529462). The company could not substantiate a claim to have the lowest CO2 per passenger compared to other European airlines. This enforcement action establishes a documented pattern: Ryanair has made unsubstantiated emissions claims in the past. A 2050 net-zero pledge made without new detailed substantiation follows that same pattern.

The European Commission's 2024 investigation specifically targets Ryanair's environmental claims about carbon emissions and offsetting. This is not a marginal regulatory concern — it is a formal CPC Regulation investigation signaling regulatory doubt about the credibility of Ryanair's sustainability narrative.

**Lobbying-Claim Misalignment**
InfluenceMap rates Ryanair as **D+** — obstructive engagement with climate policy. This represents the strongest greenwashing signal: a company actively opposing or delaying climate legislation while simultaneously claiming net-zero alignment with the Paris Agreement. This dual activity (blocking climate policy while claiming climate leadership) is definitional greenwashing.

**Coal Expansion Contradiction**
The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List records Ryanair as **actively expanding coal capacity**. This is irreconcilable with any credible net-zero or Paris-alignment claim. A company cannot credibly commit to 2050 net-zero while simultaneously expanding fossil fuel (coal) capacity. This contradicts the forward commitment implicit in the claim.

**Substantiation Gap Under Green Claims Directive Standard**
Under the proposed Green Claims Directive [52023PC0166], Ryanair's claim fails the Article 3 substantiation test:
- No disclosed baseline year
- No quantified separation between absolute emissions reduction and carbon offset reliance
- No methodology for "permanent removal of residual emissions by certified carbon removals"
- Vague aspirational language ("pathway to achieve") without credible transition plan

The claim is substantively weaker than required by the EU's emerging mandatory disclosure standard.

**Absence of Independent Science-Based Validation**
SBTi shows Ryanair has no validated net-zero target. An unvalidated 2050 net-zero claim lacks the credibility signal that comes from third-party science-based validation.

### Mitigating Evidence

**TPI Assessment (2022)**
The Transition Pathway Initiative — an investor benchmark used by $80tn AUM — rates Ryanair's 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" (Paris-compatible). This is the sole independent supporting evidence of the 2050 claim's legitimacy. However, this assessment is dated 2022 and was conducted before:
- The 2024 European Commission investigation
- The current InfluenceMap D+ obstructive classification
- The documented GCEL coal expansion listing

A two-year-old investor assessment cannot override 2023–2024 regulatory and lobbying contradictions.

- **CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION: 55/100** — Fails Green Claims Directive Article 3 substantiation standard; no baseline, no removal methodology disclosed; vague aspirational language without credible transition plan detail.
- **EMISSIONS_ACCURACY: 72/100** — TPI supports Paris compatibility, but offset by ASA misleading ruling, GCEL coal expansion, and pending EC investigation.
- **HISTORICAL_CONSISTENCY: 85/100** — Prior ASA enforcement action for misleading emissions claim establishes pattern; new 2050 claim follows the same pattern of unsubstantiated assertion.
- **LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT: 90/100** — InfluenceMap D+ indicates obstructive engagement; this is the strongest greenwashing signal. The company actively opposes climate legislation while claiming climate leadership.
- **TARGET_CREDIBILITY: 40/100** — TPI says Below 2°C, but GCEL coal expansion is irreconcilable with a credible 2050 net-zero commitment; no SBTi validation; no disclosed plan for certified carbon removals.

**Overall: 78/100 (GREENWASHING)** — Not elevated to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100) because TPI's independent supporting assessment, though outdated, provides partial mitigation. No court or regulatory ruling has yet classified the 2050 claim itself as false (the ASA ruling addressed a different, past claim). However, the combination of ASA enforcement, GCEL coal expansion, InfluenceMap obstructive lobbying, and substantiation gap under emerging EU standards is sufficient to establish material contradiction and greenwashing.

## Key Finding

Ryanair claims Paris-aligned 2050 net-zero transition while simultaneously being listed as **actively expanding coal capacity** (Urgewald GCEL), maintaining **obstructive climate policy engagement** (InfluenceMap D+), and facing a **formal European Commission investigation** into the truthfulness of its environmental claims. The company has a documented history of making unsubstantiated emissions claims (ASA 2020 enforcement). The 2050 claim lacks the substantiation required by the proposed Green Claims Directive and has not been validated by science-based targets (SBTi status: none). This is greenwashing: stated climate leadership contradicted by observable policy opposition, fossil fuel expansion, and regulatory doubt.

## Data Gaps

- **EU ETS Installation IDs:** Ryanair has no EU ETS registration. This is not a data gap error — aviation is not covered by EU ETS — but it means independent verified emissions data cannot be obtained from the EU registry. The absence of EU ETS data increases reliance on third-party assessments (TPI, InfluenceMap) and company disclosures.

- **GCEL Coal Capacity Magnitude:** The Urgewald listing documents coal expansion status as "Yes" but does not disclose the specific capacity or timeline of the expansion. This limits precision of the coal expansion contradiction but does not negate it.

- **Current Lobbying Engagement Classification:** InfluenceMap D+ rating is not dated with current-year classification. The lobbying engagement evidence is strong but not continuously updated; a 2025 re-assessment may revise the score.

- **EC Investigation Outcome:** The 2024 EC CPC Regulation investigation is ongoing (expected completion 2024–2025). A definitive regulatory ruling on Ryanair's 2050 claim is pending and could materially alter this assessment.

These gaps are material but do not prevent GREENWASHING verdict. The ASA enforcement action (2020), GCEL coal expansion listing, and InfluenceMap obstructive classification are each independently sufficient to establish material contradiction between the claim and observable company conduct.

### Evidence

**Coal Expansion (Direct Contradiction)**
Ryanair Holdings plc appears on the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List as "ACTIVELY EXPANDING coal capacity." The GCEL is the standard coal screening tool used by 400+ financial institutions (GFANZ, PAII). Active coal expansion directly contradicts any credible claim of alignment with the Paris Agreement, which requires rapid fossil fuel phase-out. [Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list]

**Prior Misleading Carbon Claims**
The UK Advertising Standards Authority upheld a complaint against Ryanair's 2020 claim to have "the lowest carbon emissions" per passenger. The ASA found that Ryanair "could not substantiate that it had lower CO2 per passenger than other European airlines when evaluated on the same basis." The claim was ruled misleading (ASA A20-529462). [Advertising Standards Authority, 2020, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/ryanair-ltd-g21-1080990.html]

**Obstructive Climate Policy Engagement**
InfluenceMap rates Ryanair Holdings plc D+ on climate policy engagement — classified as "obstructive engagement." This indicates the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation while making net-zero commitments. InfluenceMap data is used by 150+ institutional investors globally as the standard measure of corporate lobbying alignment with climate policy. [InfluenceMap, https://influencemap.org/company-responses]

**Active Regulatory Investigation**
The European Commission and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation into Ryanair's environmental claims (carbon emissions, offsetting, sustainability credentials) under CPC Regulation 2017/2394 as part of a 2024 airline greenwashing sweep covering Ryanair, Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa. [European Commission, 2024, https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_2057]

**Partial Scientific Support**
The Transition Pathway Initiative rates Ryanair's 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" — within Paris-compatible temperature bounds. TPI assessments are used by 150+ investors managing $80tn in assets. This provides limited substantiation of the scientific alignment of the 2050 target, but does not address the contradictions posed by active coal expansion or obstructive lobbying. [Transition Pathway Initiative, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates]

**Absence of Third-Party Science Validation**
SBTi records show no validated science-based target and no net-zero target classification for Ryanair Holdings plc. A credible net-zero claim typically requires third-party validation; the absence of SBTi certification weakens substantiation. [Science Based Targets initiative, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action]

**No Disclosed Emissions Baseline or Interim Targets**

The reference to "Destination 2050" is generic industry language without company-specific quantitative detail.

**Absence of Verified Emissions Data**
Ryanair has no registered installations under the EU Emissions Trading System. This means audited, verified baseline emissions data and auditable interim emissions reduction trajectories are not available to assess the credibility or trajectory of the claimed pathway.

### Assessment

The claim receives a greenwashing verdict because it is materially contradicted by verified evidence on three separate grounds: active coal expansion, obstructive climate policy engagement, and prior enforcement against misleading carbon claims.

**Coal Expansion Contradiction (Primary Finding)**
Ryanair appears on the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List as actively expanding coal capacity. This is a forward-looking, documented contradiction to any claim of Paris Agreement alignment. The Paris Agreement requires rapid phase-out of coal globally. A company simultaneously claiming net-zero alignment while expanding coal assets fails the basic test of internal consistency. The GCEL standard is used by 400+ financial institutions; this company does not meet the coal exit threshold.

**Obstructive Lobbying Contradiction**
InfluenceMap's D+ rating for Ryanair indicates obstructive engagement on climate policy — the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation. This directly contradicts the stated commitment to align with the Paris Agreement. This is a textbook greenwashing pattern: stated climate leadership undermined by the company's own policy advocacy. The company cannot credibly claim net-zero commitment while lobbying against the regulatory framework required to achieve it.

**Historical Pattern of Misleading Claims**
The 2020 ASA enforcement ruling found Ryanair's carbon emissions claim misleading because substantiation was absent. The current net-zero claim follows this same pattern: aspirational language ("we've developed a pathway") without specific, quantifiable interim targets, verified baseline emissions, or third-party validation. No material improvement in substantiation rigour is evident since the 2020 ruling.

**Substantiation Gaps**
Under the Green Claims Directive (Article 3), net-zero claims must be explicitly substantiated demonstrating that residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals, not offset credits. Ryanair has disclosed: no interim emissions targets; no SBTi validated targets; no capital investment plan; no technology roadmap; no verified baseline emissions (no EU ETS registration); no certified carbon removal plan.

**Limited Mitigation**
The TPI assessment rating the 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" provides partial substantiation of the scientific alignment of the 2050 target. However, a single forward-looking investor assessment cannot offset the material contradiction posed by documented coal expansion and obstructive lobbying activity. TPI's assessment does not address near-term credibility, interim targets, or the contradiction with coal operations.

**Regulatory Escalation**
The 2024 European Commission investigation into Ryanair's environmental claims signals official concern about the credibility of the company's sustainability narrative. This active investigation creates additional regulatory risk and indicates that independent authorities have flagged substantiation concerns.

## Evidence

**[1] ACM Enforcement Ruling (2023)** — The Dutch Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM) issued formal ruling ACM/UIT/544682 finding KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' campaign **CONFIRMED MISLEADING**. The ACM determined that KLM's claim that passengers could fly "sustainably" through carbon offset purchases was false, as "the offsetting schemes did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." KLM was ordered to cease the misleading communications immediately. [ACM, 2023, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-klm-must-stop-misleading-consumers-about-sustainability]

**[2] Amsterdam District Court Ruling (2023)** — The District Court of Amsterdam (ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615) ruled in *Fossielvrij NL v. KLM* that KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' carbon offset advertising constituted unlawful greenwashing under the EU's Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. The court ordered KLM to stop the misleading advertising. This was the first successful climate greenwashing litigation in the Netherlands. [District Court of Amsterdam, 2023, https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl/details?id=ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615]

**[3] Green Claims Directive (2023)** — The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) establishes that all explicit environmental claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence and must not mislead consumers by omission. Article 3(1) specifically prohibits vague unsubstantiated claims such as "eco-friendly," "green," or "sustainable" without specific, measurable substantiation. [EUR-Lex, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166]

**[4] EU Transparency Register (Active)** — KLM Royal Dutch Airlines is registered as a direct corporate EU lobbyist (registration no. 76441066192-30), confirming active engagement with EU institutions on policy matters. [EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do]

## Assessment

This claim is assessed as **CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING** because it is directly contradicted by verified regulatory enforcement evidence and fails substantiation requirements under EU law.

**Regulatory Contradiction (Primary Finding)**

The claim "we believe that flying can be more sustainable" originates from the identical webpage (https://www.klm.com/information/en/fly-responsibly) that was the subject of two independent, high-confidence regulatory enforcement rulings in 2023. Both the ACM and the Amsterdam District Court found KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' campaign to be misleading greenwashing. The ACM ruling explicitly determined that offsetting schemes offered by KLM "did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." The District Court applied the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive and found the same campaign unlawful. These rulings establish with judicial authority that KLM's core assertion — that flying with KLM can be made sustainable — is factually false under consumer protection law.

The vague nature of the current claim text ("we believe") does not insulate it from these findings. Both regulators ruled the entire 'Fly Responsibly' campaign framework misleading. The current claim sits directly in the lineage of that framework and perpetuates the same false implication: that KLM-facilitated flying can achieve sustainability. The claim's aspirational framing does not remedy the substantiation failure that underpinned both enforcement actions.

**Substantiation Gap Under EU Law**

Under the Green Claims Directive (Article 3(1)), the claim "we believe that flying can be more sustainable" lacks any specific, measurable, audited, or time-bound substantiation. It provides no baseline, no target, no methodology, and no independent verification. The directive prohibits precisely this category of vague environmental claim. KLM has provided no scientific evidence, no lifecycle analysis, and no specific mechanism by which flying with KLM can achieve sustainability — only an aspirational belief statement. This fails the threshold for lawful environmental communication under EU law.

**Historical Pattern**

KLM's prior assessment history shows a **deteriorating trend**, with an average score of 88.7 and prior scores at 92.0. The company has made repeated unsubstantiated sustainability claims without material remediation. The current claim continues this pattern. The regulatory enforcement actions of 2023 should have triggered fundamental restructuring of KLM's sustainability communications; instead, the company has republished the same campaign framework without substantive change.

**Confidence Rationale**

Confidence is set at 92% (high) because both regulatory rulings are recent (2023), issued by recognized EU member-state authorities with direct enforcement jurisdiction (ACM and district court), and explicitly address the substantive claim and campaign context. The only marginal uncertainty reflects the possibility that an extremely narrow textual distinction could be drawn between "we believe flying can be more sustainable" (current claim) and the specific offset-based claims enjoined (prior rulings); however, this distinction is immaterial, as both regulators ruled the entire campaign framework misleading, not merely specific sub-claims.

### Evidence

**1. Green Claims Directive — Legislative Standard**  
EUR-Lex [3], 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166  
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) requires all explicit environmental claims to be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence, cover the full lifecycle where relevant, and not mislead consumers by omission. Vague claims such as 'eco-friendly', 'green', or 'sustainable' without specific substantiation are prohibited under Article 3(1).

**2. CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark — Target Credibility Context**  
CA100+, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/  
CA100+ assesses Danone SA as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" but only "Partial" capex alignment and "Partial" short-term targets. This partial alignment indicates incomplete strategic credibility across the company's overall climate commitments.

**3. Science Based Targets Initiative — Emissions Target Status**  
SBTi, current, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action  
Danone SA has no validated net-zero target registered with SBTi (status=unknown, net-zero target=none). No temperature classification is assigned.

**4. InfluenceMap — Climate Policy Engagement**  
InfluenceMap, current, https://influencemap.org/company-responses

### Assessment

Danone's claim that it "collaborates with multi-stakeholder networks… working together to scale up initiatives" is unsubstantiated and lacks the measurable commitments required by EU Green Claims Directive Article 3(1).

**Substantiation Gap**

The claim contains no quantified biodiversity targets, timelines for implementation, defined scope of initiatives, or measurable outcomes. Partnership membership alone—while factually verifiable through organisational records—does not constitute evidence of scaled environmental impact. The phrase "scale up initiatives" is aspirational language without measurable deliverables. The claim meets the characteristics that the Green Claims Directive explicitly prohibits: vague environmental language without specific substantiation.

**Target Credibility Context**

Danone's overall climate strategy credibility is weakened by the absence of an SBTi-validated net-zero target (SBTi, current) and only partial alignment on capex and short-term targets in the CA100+ 2025 benchmark. This broader credibility deficit extends doubt to more specific environmental claims, including biodiversity commitments.

**No Supporting Biodiversity Evidence**

The assessment found no independent verification of Danone's biodiversity impact, no published biodiversity targets, and no quantified outcomes from the networks cited. The claim relies on the institutional credibility of partner organisations (Consumer Goods Forum, One Planet Business for Biodiversity) rather than Danone's own documented accountability.

**Not a Repeat**

This is not a repeated unfulfilled promise. Zero equivalent prior claims are detected among Danone's 22 prior assessments, and the company's overall score trend is stable in the MISLEADING band (average 47.7/100), suggesting a pattern of marginal substantiation issues rather than escalating deception.

**Verdict Rationale**

The claim falls within the MISLEADING band (46–60) because it is directionally plausible (partnerships exist, networks are real) but materially unquantified and unverifiable. The score of 48 is anchored in weak claim substantiation (52/100) and weak target-level credibility (55/100). The claim does not contain a false factual assertion but exaggerates Danone's commitment by omitting specifics and substituting vague collaborative language for measurable environmental responsibility.

---

### Key Finding

Danone claims biodiversity collaboration without quantifying targets, timelines, or measurable outcomes—precisely the type of aspirational greenwashing that the EU Green Claims Directive prohibits. The company's lack of SBTi validation and only partial CA100+ alignment further undermine the credibility of its environmental commitments across the board.

### Data Gaps

**Biodiversity Impact Data — Not Found**  
No published biodiversity targets, baseline data, or impact assessments from Danone were retrieved. Biodiversity claims are inherently difficult to verify compared to emissions claims, and the absence of quantified data from Danone's own reporting significantly limits confidence.

**Specific Initiative Descriptions — Not Available**  
The claim references "initiatives" without naming them or linking to documentation. Without disclosure of which initiatives Danone supports through each network, independent verification of its contribution is impossible.

**EU Transparency Register — No Entry**  
Danone SA does not appear in the EU Transparency Register. This does not indicate lobbying misconduct, but it prevents assessment of any potential contradiction between public environmental claims and undisclosed policy advocacy.

**EU ETS — Not Applicable**  
Danone has no registered installations under the EU Emissions Trading System. This is expected for food and beverage companies, which typically fall below ETS thresholds or purchase energy from third parties. No data gap indicated.

**Confidence Impact:** The lack of quantified biodiversity data from Danone itself, combined with the inherent difficulty of verifying biodiversity claims independent of company disclosure, reduces overall confidence to 68%. The legislative basis (Green Claims Directive, confidence 0.95) is robust, but substantiation evidence is weak.

### The Claim

### Evidence

**[1] EU ETS Verified Emissions Data — Absence of Registration**
Stegra's Boden facility does not appear in the EU Transaction Log (EUTL) verified emissions database. Under EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC Article 15, installations producing ≥25 ktCO2e annually are obligated to register and submit independently verified annual emissions reports via accredited verifiers under Regulation 601/2012. Non-registration is consistent with either: (a) facility not yet operational, (b) facility below ETS threshold, or (c) facility covered by Article 15 exemption. Non-registration does **not** contradict the claim that the facility will produce zero direct process emissions, but it prevents independent verification of actual operational performance. *EU ETS EUTL, accessed 2026, https://ec.europa.eu/clima/ets*

**[2] Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) — Scope Disclosure Requirements**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) mandates that emissions reduction claims must explicitly state: baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3 per GHG Protocol), and methodology. The Stegra claim states "zero emissions" without specifying scope. Electrolytic hydrogen powered by renewable electricity produces zero Scope 1 direct process emissions; however, Scope 2 (electricity generation) and Scope 3 (supply chain, transmission losses) emissions are not addressed. The claim does not comply with mandatory scope clarity as defined in the legislative standard. *EUR-LEX, Green Claims Directive proposal [52023PC0166], 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**[3] EU Transparency Register — Lobbying Status**
Stegra is registered as an active EU corporate lobbyist (Registration No. 191319744634-58, HQ: Sweden). However, the Transparency Register does not disclose lobbying position direction (pro- or anti-climate policy). No contradicting lobbying position (e.g., advocacy against renewable energy or hydrogen regulation) was identified. This finding is neutral: absence of contradictory lobbying does not substantiate the green hydrogen claim. *EU Transparency Register, accessed 2026, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do*

**[4] Electrochemistry — Green Hydrogen Process Emissions**
The technical assertion that green hydrogen produces zero direct process emissions is substantiated. Electrolysis of water powered by renewable electricity follows the reaction: 2H₂O + electricity (renewable) → 2H₂ + O₂, with no direct combustion or process GHG emissions. This is established electrochemistry and not contradicted by any retrieved evidence. However, this technical truth applies **only to Scope 1 direct emissions** and does not address the full lifecycle or supply-chain emissions if electricity source, transmission, or equipment manufacturing are included. *Standard electrochemistry; lifecycle assessment boundaries not specified in claim.*

### Assessment

Stegra's claim occupies the "insufficient evidence" category: it is technically plausible and not contradicted by verified data, but lacks the independent validation and scope clarity required by EU corporate sustainability standards.

**Emissions Accuracy — Partial Substantiation**
The claim that green hydrogen "produces zero emissions" is technically accurate in the narrow sense of Scope 1 direct process emissions (Scope 1 score: 25/100 — substantiated but incomplete). No EU ETS registered emissions data contradicts this, because the Boden facility is not yet registered in the EUTL database — implying early-stage development, below-threshold capacity, or planned operation. This absence is not evidence of deception; it is evidence of a lack of operational scale sufficient for mandatory reporting. However, the claim violates Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) by failing to disclose scope boundary (Scope 1 only, or all scopes?). Scope 2 emissions (electricity grid mix) and Scope 3 (supply chain, transmission losses) are material to hydrogen lifecycle emissions and are not addressed.

**Claim Substantiation — Material Vagueness**
The claim lacks four critical elements required for substantiation:

1. **Baseline for comparison**: The claim does not compare green hydrogen emissions to an incumbent baseline (e.g., grey hydrogen at X tCO₂/kg). Without baseline, the claim of "zero emissions" cannot be assessed as a reduction or positioned within industrial decarbonization context.

2. **Quantified scope and timeline**: The claim uses forward-looking language ("will make") without disclosing facility capacity (tonnes/year), operational date, or interim milestones. Green Claims Directive Article 3 prohibits vague temporal claims without measurable checkpoints.

3. **Third-party verification**: No SBTi validation, ISO 50001 certification, or independent lifecycle assessment (ISO 14040/44) was identified. The claim is unilateral corporate assertion without accredited external audit.

4. **Scope clarity per GCD Article 3(3)(a)**: "Zero emissions" is stated without declaring Scope 1/2/3 or methodology. This is a clear breach of the legislative standard for emissions claims.

**Historical Consistency**
This is the first green hydrogen claim from Stegra assessed by Prasine Index; no prior equivalent claims detected. Stegra's prior assessed claims averaged 49.3/100 (MISLEADING band), suggesting the company has a pattern of insufficiently substantiated environmental statements. This claim is marginally better grounded than the historical average, implying either incremental improvement in disclosure or sector-specific variation in evidence availability for hydrogen projects.

**Lobbying Alignment — Neutral Finding**
Stegra is an active EU lobbyist but does not, based on available records, advocate against hydrogen or renewable energy legislation. No contradiction between this claim and Stegra's lobbying position was identified. However, this is a neutral finding: absence of contradictory lobbying does not validate the claim.

---

### Key Finding

Stegra's claim that its Boden hydrogen plant produces "zero emissions" is technically plausible but legally non-compliant: it violates Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) by omitting mandatory scope disclosure (Scope 1/2/3), provides no baseline for emissions reduction comparison, and lacks independent third-party verification. The facility's absence from the EU ETS registry indicates early-stage development, not falsified emissions — but prevents verification of actual operational performance.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index scored this claim by assessing five dimensions: (1) **Emissions Accuracy** (does the claim match regulatory or scientific fact?), (2) **Claim Substantiation** (is the claim quantified, scoped, and audited?), (3) **Historical Consistency** (does it align with company prior disclosures?), (4) **Lobbying Alignment** (does corporate advocacy contradict the claim?), and (5) **Target Credibility** (is there a binding net-zero or interim science-based target?). 

Scoring rubric: **0–20** = SUBSTANTIATED (verified by EU ETS, SBTi, or equivalent); **21–45** = INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE (plausible but unverified); **46–60** = MISLEADING (partially contradicted or materially vague); **61–80** = GREENWASHING (contradicted by verified data or lobbying); **81–100** = CONFIRMED_FALSE (definitively falsified).

This claim scored 32/100 (Insufficient Evidence) because:
- Stegra Boden facility does not appear in EU ETS EUTL (prevents Scope 1 verification);
- Claim omits mandatory Scope 1/2/3 declaration per Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a);
- No baseline, third-party audit (SBTi, LCA), or operational timeline disclosed;
- Technical assertion (zero Scope 1 process emissions) is correct but incomplete (Scope 2/3 not addressed).

The claim is not greenwashing (score would be 61+) because no verified contradicting evidence exists and the facility's non-registration in EU ETS is consistent with early-stage development, not fraud. Confidence is 58% (below the 85% standard) due to unavailability of EU ETS data and absence of independent verification mechanisms.

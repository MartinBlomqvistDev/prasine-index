## Assessment

### Core Contradiction: Claim vs. Capital Allocation

Enel claims "sustainable model for developing, building and operating our plants" on its corporate commitment page. However, verified institutional data from three independent energy project trackers documents **active fossil fuel expansion**, not contraction:

- **Coal**: COAL_EXIT (confidence 0.90, used by 400+ financial institutions) documents 15 Mtpa coal mining expansion + 2.4 GW coal power capacity expansion. These are forward-looking capital commitments, not legacy closures.
  
- **Gas**: EGT (confidence 0.85, institutional standard) documents active development of Porto Empedocle LNG terminal (Italy), which locks in 40+ years of fossil fuel demand and contradicts any net-zero or fossil gas phase-out narrative.
  
- **Oil & Gas**: GOGET (confidence 0.72) documents 11 operating oil/gas extraction fields with 1 field (Tihalatine South, Algeria) entering development phase. This is ongoing production expansion, not wind-down.

This pattern represents the classic greenwashing contradiction: **marketing a sustainability transition while actively expanding fossil fuel production and infrastructure**.

### Why Not Higher (Confirmed Greenwashing)?

Three factors prevent this from scoring in the "confirmed greenwashing" range (81–100):

1. **Genuine Historical Performance**: EPRTR verified data shows a 75% decline in regulated emissions over 17 years (31.97 Mt → 8.07 Mt, 2007–2024). This is not fictitious; it reflects real asset retirements and renewable capacity growth.

2. **Investor-Grade Validation**: CA100+ (2025) and TPI (2022) both rate Enel as having credible net-zero alignment and Paris-compatible 2050 pathways. These are institutional frameworks used by trillions in institutional capital; they cannot be dismissed lightly.

3. **Partial Coal Phase-Out**: GCPT documents 31 of 44 coal units retired or cancelled, with 7 still operating. While the remaining 7 units contradict full coal exit, the retirement trajectory demonstrates genuine portfolio transition in motion.

4. **Absent Lobbying Opposition**: InfluenceMap score B- and EU Transparency Register show no evidence of active climate legislation opposition. The company is not documented as fighting climate policy, only as being present in EU lobbying conversations.

### Why Greenwashing (68/100)?

The score reflects a fundamental mismatch between aspiration and action:

- **The claim is aspirational** ("first step," "model for developing, building and operating"), presented on a corporate commitment page without specific timelines, percentages, or measurable targets. This language obscures the contradiction rather than addressing it directly.

- **Forward capital contradicts the narrative**: The company is simultaneously (a) reducing legacy coal/gas assets through retirements, AND (b) actively expanding new coal mining, LNG infrastructure, and oil/gas fields. These are contradictory capital strategies. A genuine "sustainable transition" would not include new fossil fuel infrastructure development.

- **The claim fails compliance with emerging EU standards**: Green Claims Directive guidance requires renewable energy claims to specify percentage coverage, source, and certificate methodology. Enel's claim provides none of this, and may be considered misleading under proposed EU rules.

- **Lobbying ambiguity adds risk**: While InfluenceMap shows no documented opposition to climate policy, Enel's EU lobbying registration (TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER, conf=0.75) lacks direction disclosure. Without clarity on what Enel lobbies for, the full picture of corporate alignment cannot be assessed.

## Key Finding

Enel presents a sustainability transition narrative on its corporate commitment page while simultaneously expanding coal mining (15 Mtpa), LNG infrastructure (Porto Empedocle terminal), and operating 11 oil/gas extraction fields with at least 1 entering development. Verified institutional data from Urgewald Coal Exit List, Europe Gas Tracker, and Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker document these as forward-looking expansions, not legacy liabilities. The contradiction between aspiration ("sustainable model") and capital allocation (active fossil fuel expansion) places the claim in greenwashing territory, despite genuine historical emissions reductions (75% decline 2007–2024 per EPRTR) and investor-grade net-zero validation from CA100+ and TPI.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data**: No installation IDs for Enel registered in the EU Emissions Trading System database. This prevents direct verification of Scope 1 emissions within the ETS. **Impact on confidence**: Mitigated by EPRTR and GCPT data, which provide verified emissions at similar or greater specificity. Confidence reduced by ~5–7 percentage points due to absence of primary EU carbon market record.

2. **SBTi Validation**: SBTi record shows net-zero target status = unknown, target not on file. This gap prevents confirmation that Enel's net-zero claims have undergone science-based methodology review. **Impact on confidence**: CA100+ and TPI provide partial substitute validation, but absence of SBTi reduces target credibility score.

3. **Enel Lobbying Position Detail**: EU Transparency Register confirms lobbying registration but does not disclose lobbying direction (for/against climate policy). InfluenceMap score B- is inconclusive. **Impact on confidence**: Unable to assess whether lobbying contradicts or supports the sustainability claim. Confidence in lobbying alignment assessment = 45%, not disqualifying but material gap.

4. **Scope 2 & 3 Emissions**: EPRTR covers Scope 1 (direct) emissions only. No assessment of Scope 2 (purchased electricity) or Scope 3 (supply chain) emissions. The 75% decline may not reflect full corporate footprint. **Impact on confidence**: Moderate — for utilities, Scope 1 dominates, but gap remains.

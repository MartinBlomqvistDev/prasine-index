**EU_ETS — Legislative Ground Truth (2003/2024)**
The EU ETS Directive (2003/87/EC as amended by 2024/825) establishes verified emissions reporting as mandatory legal disclosure for covered installations. Verified figures are produced by accredited independent third-party verifiers under Regulation 601/2012. A company claiming emissions reductions not reflected in EUTL verified data contradicts this highest-quality ground truth source.
[EU ETS Directive 32003L0087 and EmpCo Directive 32024L0825, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087]

**EUR_LEX — EmpCo Directive Substantiation Standard (2024)**
The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, amended the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. Emissions reduction claims must state:
- Baseline year
- Scope specification (Scope 1/2/3 disaggregated)
- Methodology

The claim "reducing our combined industrial Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions" aggregates three distinct scope categories without specifying baseline year, baseline emissions, or percentage reduction. Per UCPD Article 6(1), aggregating reductions and increases into a net reduction figure that obscures absolute emission increases in core business segments is misleading.
[EmpCo Directive 32024L0825, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825]

### Assessment

**Primary Contradiction: Coal Expansion vs. Decarbonisation Claim**

Glencore's claim of a "holistic approach to decarbonisation" is directly contradicted by documented active coal capacity expansion. The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 0.90) records Glencore as actively expanding coal mining operations. This is not a data inference or modeled projection — it is a verified institutional record used by 400+ financial institutions as the standard coal screen under GFANZ and PAII protocols. No company actively mining and exporting coal can credibly claim holistic decarbonisation while maintaining or expanding that core business line.

**Secondary Contradiction: Obstructive Lobbying vs. Decarbonisation Claim**

InfluenceMap rates Glencore D+ (confidence 0.85), indicating obstructive climate policy engagement — the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation. This contradicts the stated commitment to decarbonisation. When a company claims climate leadership while lobbying against climate policy, the discrepancy is material and indicative of greenwashing. This finding is supported by UK CMA enforcement scrutiny (confidence 0.70), which specifically flagged Glencore's stated net-zero commitment while continuing coal expansion as potentially misleading under consumer protection law.

**Substantiation Gap Under EmpCo Directive**

The claim aggregates "combined industrial Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions" without specifying baseline year, baseline emissions volume, percentage reduction target, or methodology. Under the EmpCo Directive (in force March 2024), this aggregated framing may obscure absolute emission increases in core business segments by netting them against reductions elsewhere. The EmpCo Directive requires disaggregated scope reporting and specific baselines. This claim does not meet the standard. (Confidence 0.95 for legislative requirement; confidence 0.65 for whether this claim violates it without access to internal methodology.)

**Supporting Evidence — Limited Weight**

CA100+ (confidence 0.65) assesses Glencore as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" and "Partial capex alignment." This is the only institutional source supporting credible climate commitment. However, CA100+ assessment is weaker than the contradicting evidence (COAL_EXIT 0.90, INFLUENCE_MAP 0.85) and includes the explicit caveat "Partial" capex alignment — acknowledging inconsistency. The presence of one supporting source prevents escalation to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (which requires isolated or overwhelming contradiction), but it is insufficient to offset the weight of coal expansion and obstructive lobbying.

**Data Gaps**

No installation IDs for Glencore appear in the EU ETS database. This is a critical verification gap — the claim cannot be assessed against independently verified EU ETS historical emissions data, which is the highest-quality ground truth for covered installations. However, the absence of EU ETS data does not contradict the claim; it merely prevents independent verification of actual reductions. Per Prasine methodology, "not found" is neutral, not contradictory. The verdict is driven by positive contradictions (COAL_EXIT, INFLUENCE_MAP), not by missing data.

**Score Rationale**

- **EMISSIONS_ACCURACY (72/100):** Coal expansion contradicts decarbonisation claim. No EU ETS verification available.
- **CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION (65/100):** Lacks baseline year, percentage target, and disaggregated scope methodology. Non-compliant with EmpCo Directive.
- **HISTORICAL_CONSISTENCY (78/100):** Prior claims from Glencore average 69.9 with peak score of 82.0. This claim follows a pattern of moderate-to-high greenwashing scores.
- **LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT (88/100):** InfluenceMap D+ confirms active opposition to climate legislation — strongest greenwashing signal.
- **TARGET_CREDIBILITY (80/100):** No specific 2030/2035 targets disclosed. Coal expansion is incompatible with 1.5°C warming limitation pathways.

**Final Composite Score: 76/100 (GREENWASHING)**

The verdict is GREENWASHING, not CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING, because CA100+ provides partial supporting evidence (confidence 0.65), preventing a clear 80+ verdict. However, the combination of documented coal capacity expansion (COAL_EXIT, confidence 0.90), active lobbying against climate legislation (INFLUENCE_MAP, confidence 0.85), and UK CMA enforcement scrutiny justify a firm GREENWASHING classification.

### Key Finding

Glencore's claim of taking a "holistic approach to decarbonisation" is directly contradicted by two material facts: (1) the company is actively expanding coal mining capacity according to the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (used by 400+ financial institutions as the standard coal screen), and (2) InfluenceMap rates Glencore D+ for obstructive climate policy engagement, meaning it actively opposes or delays climate legislation while making green claims. The UK CMA has placed the company under investigation specifically for potentially misleading net-zero claims made while continuing coal expansion. No supporting institutional source (CA100+ alone, at confidence 0.65) is sufficient to overcome this confluence of contradictions.

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data (Critical Gap):** No installation IDs registered for Glencore in the EU ETS EUTL database. This prevents independent verification of actual Scope 1 and 2 emissions reductions against mandatory legal disclosure. However, this gap does not contradict the claim; it merely prevents ground-truth validation. Confidence in this assessment is therefore reduced by ~5–10 points, reflected in the 82% overall confidence level rather than 90%+.

2. **Specific Emissions Reduction Baseline (Substantiation Gap):** The claim does not disclose baseline year, baseline emissions volume, or percentage reduction target. This violates the EmpCo Directive (2024) substantiation standard. Without access to internal climate reporting, we cannot verify whether Glencore has calculated actual reductions or is using intensity metrics that obscure absolute increases. This gap reinforces the CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION score of 65/100.

3. **TPI Pathway Validation (Moderate Gap):** TPI has not assessed Glencore's climate pathways for 2025, 2035, or 2050, despite rating the company Management Quality Level 4. This represents an institutional verification gap — no investor-facing standard (TPI, SBTi, or equivalent) has validated the decarbonisation pathway as credible or Paris-aligned.

4. **Scope 3 Emissions Transparency (Moderate Gap):** The claim aggregates Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions without itemizing. Scope 3 (upstream and downstream emissions) for a mining company can dwarf direct operational emissions. Without disaggregated reporting, it is impossible to assess whether absolute Scope 3 emissions (e.g., coal combustion by end users) are increasing despite claimed reductions.

**Impact on Confidence:** These gaps reduce confidence from a potential 90%+ to 82%. The COAL_EXIT and INFLUENCE_MAP evidence is strong enough to support the GREENWASHING verdict independently, but the inability to verify actual emissions reductions through EU ETS data and the substantiation gaps under EmpCo prevent a higher confidence level.

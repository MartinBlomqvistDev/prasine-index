### Evidence

**1. Regulatory Emissions Disclosure (E-PRTR)**
E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions to air (CO2, GHGs, NOX, NMVOC combined) for IKEA Group show: 2020: 323.2 t; 2021: 1,430.0 t; 2022: 1,599.5 t; 2023: 1,242.0 t; 2024: 110,243.0 t. The 2024 figure represents a 89× increase over 2023, flagged by the source as likely a first-disclosure or scope-change event rather than genuine operational increase. Excluding 2024, the 2010–2023 trend shows a 902% rise in regulated emissions to air. **Critical limitation**: E-PRTR captures only regulated industrial emissions — it does not measure IKEA's material footprint (Scope 1, 2, 3 supply chain, logistics, retail operations). For a retail company, this represents a narrow slice of total emissions impact.
*Source: E-PRTR (European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register), 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/*

**2. Science-Based Target Initiative (SBTi) Validation Status**
SBTi records show IKEA Group's target status as: status=unknown, temperature classification=not classified, net-zero target=none. The company has no validated science-based net-zero target on file.
*Source: Science Based Targets initiative, N/A, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

**3. Green Claims Directive Requirements**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166, Article 3) explicitly requires net-zero claims to demonstrate that residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals (not offset credits), with a credible transition plan. Article 7 prohibits claims based solely on carbon offsetting.
*Source: EUR-Lex [52023PC0166], 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**4. Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) Applicability**
CSRD (2022/2464) requires large companies (>500 employees, EU-listed, or EU-operating enterprises meeting thresholds) to disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024 onwards. IKEA Group is not currently CSRD-obligated. However, if and when CSRD applies, any material inconsistency between this net-zero claim and mandatory Scope 1/2/3 disclosure would constitute a substantiation failure.
*Source: EUR-Lex [32022L2464], 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

### Assessment

IKEA's claim that it "updated the climate approach from Climate Positive to Net Zero and Beyond" is **MISLEADING** under the Green Claims Directive standard because it fails substantiation requirements, not because it is directly falsified by verified data.

**Substantiation Failures (Score: 65/100)**

The claim provides no measurable transition pathway:
- **No target year specified.** The statement "Net Zero and Beyond" omits when net-zero will be achieved. For a retail and logistics-intensive company with high Scope 3 emissions, a net-zero target beyond 2050 is not credible under science-based standards (IPCC 1.5°C pathway).
- **No audited baseline.** IKEA has not disclosed its Scope 1, 2, 3 GHG baseline inventory in this claim or in independent regulatory filing (CSRD does not yet apply; E-PRTR captures only regulated industrial air emissions).
- **No SBTi or equivalent validation.** SBTi records show net-zero target=none and status=unknown. This is a critical substantiation gap. A net-zero claim without independent validation cannot meet Green Claims Directive Article 3, which requires "substantiation based on robust, reliable, verified and up-to-date scientific evidence."
- **No carbon removal methodology specified.** The Green Claims Directive Article 3 requires net-zero claims to demonstrate permanent removal of residual emissions using certified carbon removals, not offset credits. IKEA's claim provides no evidence of carbon removal contracts or methodology.

**Emissions Accuracy Concerns (Score: 35/100)**

E-PRTR regulated emissions data (2010–2023) show a 902% increase in regulated industrial emissions to air. This trend is inconsistent with a credible net-zero transition pathway. However, E-PRTR captures only regulated industrial emissions (CO2, GHGs, NOX, NMVOC to air) — it does not measure IKEA's material footprint (supply chain, distribution, retail operations). For a retail company, E-PRTR represents perhaps 1–5% of total emissions impact. The data is insufficient to assess the full claim but sufficient to note that the available regulated emissions trend contradicts progress toward net-zero.

**Reframing Without Demonstrated Progress (Score: 55/100)**

The claim explicitly states a reframing from "Climate Positive" to "Net Zero and Beyond." This is not a repeat claim in the strict sense, but the shift from one aspirational label to another, coupled with the 902% rise in regulated emissions (2010–2023), suggests rhetorical repositioning rather than operational transformation. IKEA's prior claim assessment history (25 claims, average score 52.8) indicates a pattern of aspirational commitments without consistent substantiation.

**Lobbying Alignment Does Not Mitigate (Score: 25/100)**

InfluenceMap band B rating confirms IKEA's lobbying activity is supportive of climate policy. This mitigates greenwashing risk from policy contradiction but does not substantiate the net-zero claim itself. Lobbying alignment is a separate dimension from emissions evidence.

**Confidence Limitation (62%)**

Confidence is moderate because:
- IKEA's full Scope 1, 2, 3 baseline is not independently available in queried sources.
- SBTi status is marked "unknown" (not definitively absent).
- The claim is aspirational rather than a specific quantified assertion, making direct falsification harder but substantiation verification straightforward (and failing).
- CSRD non-applicability (currently) means no mandatory disclosure baseline exists for comparison. Once CSRD applies, this will shift to HIGH confidence if material inconsistencies emerge.

### Key Finding

IKEA's net-zero claim fails Green Claims Directive substantiation standards: no target year, no audited baseline, no SBTi validation, and no carbon removal methodology specified. The reframing from "Climate Positive" to "Net Zero and Beyond" occurs against a backdrop of 902% growth in regulated emissions (2010–2023) and a historical pattern of aspirational claims without consistent verification, indicating rhetorical repositioning rather than operational commitment.

### Data Gaps

**SBTi Status Inconclusive**
SBTi records show status=unknown and net-zero target=none. This may reflect a gap in SBTi's database (IKEA has not submitted a validated target) or genuine absence of SBTi submission. Impact: Confidence cannot exceed 75% without SBTi contact confirmation.

**Scope 1/2/3 Baseline Not Independently Available**
E-PRTR captures only regulated industrial emissions to air (~1–5% of IKEA's total footprint for a retail company). Scope 1/2/3 full inventory is not disclosed in public regulatory filings (CSRD not yet mandatory). IKEA may have calculated this internally, but independent verification is unavailable. Impact: Full assessment of net-zero credibility is impossible without Scope 3 baseline (supply chain, logistics, retail operations).

**CSRD Pre-Filing Period**
IKEA is not currently CSRD-obligated. Once CSRD applies (likely 2026–2028 reporting), mandatory Scope 1/2/3 disclosure will enable direct comparison to this claim. Until then, substantiation must rely on voluntary disclosure or external certification. Impact: Confidence will rise to 85%+ once CSRD mandatory data becomes available.

**EU ETS Non-Applicability**
IKEA has no registered EU ETS installations. This is not a data gap — it reflects the company's sector (retail; EU ETS applies to power generation and heavy industry). Does not affect assessment.

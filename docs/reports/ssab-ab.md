## SSAB AB — Greenwashing Assessment
**Verdict: MISLEADING_CLAIM** | Score: 54.0/100 (range: 44–64) | Confidence: 70%
*Published: 2026-07-02 | Prasine Index | Trace ID: 16f21706-ad34-401d-a2c7-0aa9ae5a176c*

### The Claim
> With the launch of SSAB's HYBRIT technology, hydrogen instead of coal will be used to remove oxygen from the iron ore. This is a major step for the steel industry, and Strenx® will be a game-changer for producers of applications in high-strength structural steel, and consequently eliminating the carbon dioxide emissions.
*Source: IR_PAGE, https://www.ssab.com/en/brands-and-products/strenx/sustainability, undated*

### Evidence

**Company-reported emissions vs. verified data:** SSAB's two principal steelmaking installations show no downward step-change consistent with an "eliminating" emissions claim. EU ETS verified data for installation SE_495 record 2,882,766 tCO2e in 2025, up 138% from the 2013 baseline [EU ETS verified data, 2025, https://union-registry-data.ec.europa.eu/report/welcome]. Installation SE_494 recorded 1,537,703 tCO2e in 2025, up 5% from 2013 [EU ETS verified data, 2025, https://union-registry-data.ec.europa.eu/report/welcome]. Two smaller installations (SE_386, SE_206197) show mixed trends but remain immaterial to the overall picture [EU ETS verified data, 2025, https://union-registry-data.ec.europa.eu/report/welcome].

**Independent satellite corroboration:** Climate TRACE attributes 5.496 Mt CO2e (2023) to SSAB's Luleå (3.568 Mt) and Oxelösund (1.928 Mt) plants, derived independently of company self-reporting [Climate TRACE, 2023, https://climatetrace.org/explore#?country=SWE].

**Source document analysis:** The claim page itself provides no quantified baseline, no Scope 1/2/3 disclosure, no interim targets for 2030/2035/2040, and no confirmed commercial timeline for fossil-free Strenx® production. It presents HYBRIT in future-conditional language ("will be used," "with the launch") while asserting a present-tense outcome ("eliminating"). A cited EcoUpgraded case study demonstrates emissions reduction through lightweighting and offset-based accounting (payback after 7 months), not fossil-free steel production [source document, https://www.ssab.com/en/brands-and-products/strenx/sustainability].

**Mitigating evidence:** SBTi confirms SSAB has 1.5°C-classified targets set, including a net-zero target [SBTi, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action]. TPI rates SSAB's 2050 pathway as 1.5°C-aligned with Strategic (Level 4) management quality [TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates].

**Lobbying position:** LobbyMap rates SSAB's climate policy engagement as C+ — neutral/mixed, not obstructive [LobbyMap, https://lobbymap.org/]. SSAB is a registered EU lobbyist [EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do], but no directional contradiction with the claim is established.

### Assessment
This is a forward-looking product claim describing a real, pilot-stage decarbonisation pathway (HYBRIT) but asserting a definitive, present-tense outcome ("eliminating the carbon dioxide emissions") that is not yet delivered. The source document confirms no fossil-free Strenx® grades are in commercial production, and no baseline, scope, timeline, or methodology accompanies the claim [source document]. Verified EU ETS data show SSAB's core steelmaking installations emitting at levels consistent with, or above, 2013 baselines — corroborated independently by Climate TRACE satellite data — with no evidence of the emissions elimination the claim implies. The claim conflates a genuine R&D and investment programme with an already-achieved product outcome. SBTi and TPI records provide credible mitigating context: SSAB has Paris-aligned targets and a strategically-rated transition pathway, distinguishing this case from fabricated or wholly unsubstantiated net-zero claims. This is not a repeat claim in SSAB's assessed history, though the company's overall score trend across prior Prasine assessments is deteriorating.

### Regulatory Exposure
**(a) EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825):** This claim violates the EmpCo Directive. Under the amended UCPD Annex I and Article 6(1), emissions-related claims must state a baseline year, disclose scope (Scope 1/2/3), and provide verifiable methodology [EUR-Lex 32024L0825]. The claim provides none of these elements while asserting an absolute outcome ("eliminating the carbon dioxide emissions"). The referenced case study's reliance on offset-adjusted payback accounting, rather than certified permanent removals, further fails the EmpCo substantiation standard for elimination claims.

**(b) Green Claims Directive (enforcement from September 2026):** This claim would require independent verification under GCD mandatory substantiation requirements and, as currently worded, would likely fail such scrutiny. The claim lacks any third-party-verifiable baseline or quantified reduction pathway tied to the "eliminating" assertion.

**(c) UCPD Article 11 exposure:** Under UCPD Article 11 (as amended by the EmpCo Directive, EU 2024/825), this claim is subject to enforcement by national consumer protection authorities. Complaints may be filed with Sweden's Konsumentverket (Swedish Consumer Agency).

### Key Finding
SSAB genuinely operates a pioneering hydrogen-based steel R&D programme (HYBRIT) and holds Paris-aligned SBTi and TPI ratings, but its marketing language claims present-tense "elimination" of CO₂ emissions for a product not yet in commercial fossil-free production, while EU ETS verified data show its core installations emitting at or above 2013 levels — an unsubstantiated absolute claim under the EmpCo Directive.

### Assessment

The claim asserts that Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) at the Filbornaverket waste-to-energy facility will "almost entirely eliminate" new emissions and bring the facility "significantly closer" to net-zero emissions in district heating production. While CCS is a legitimate abatement technology and waste incineration with CCS is technically plausible, the claim fails substantiation standards required under the Green Claims Directive on four grounds:

**1. Absence of quantified baseline and post-intervention emissions**
The claim uses relative language ("nearly eliminates," "significantly closer") without stating:
- Current absolute Scope 1 emissions from Filbornaverket (in tonnes CO2e);
- Quantified emissions reduction from CCS deployment (absolute tonnes or percentage);
- Post-CCS absolute emissions target.

This violates Article 3 of the Green Claims Directive, which requires specific, measurable, and auditable substantiation. The phrase "betydligt närmare nettonollutsläpp" (significantly closer to net-zero) is inherently vague and unquantified — it does not constitute a committal claim under EU substantiation standards.

**2. Unspecified permanence of carbon removal**
The claim does not clarify whether captured CO2 will be:
- Permanently stored in geological formations (compliant with Green Claims Directive);
- Traded or offset (prohibited under Article 7 of the Green Claims Directive as sole basis for net-zero claims);
- Utilized in products with uncertain end-of-life fate.

This omission is material. Green Claims Directive Article 3 explicitly requires evidence that residual emissions are permanently removed by certified carbon removals. The claim provides no such evidence.

**3. Absence of third-party verification**
No mention is made of:
- SBTi validation of the net-zero pathway;
- ISO 14064-2 independent verification of CCS removal rates;
- EU ETS monitoring data (facility does not appear registered);
- CSRD audited disclosure (not applicable to this company, but indicates absence of mandatory verification).

**4. Scope ambiguity**
The claim does not specify whether "net-zero emissions in district heating production" refers to:
- Scope 1 direct facility emissions only;
- Scope 1 + Scope 2 (purchased energy);
- Scope 1 + 2 + 3 (waste supply chain, distribution losses).

This scope ambiguity allows selective reporting — for example, counting only direct incineration emissions while excluding supply-chain emissions increases the claim's apparent credibility.

**Does the claim contradict verified data?**
No direct contradiction is confirmed. However, the facility's absence from EU ETS registration is suspicious — facilities of this type should be covered and monitored. This gap prevents verification that the claim is accurate.

**Company pattern:**
This is not a repeat claim. Across 115 prior assessments, Öresundskraft has averaged a score of 46.3 (MISLEADING band), suggesting a pattern of substantiation gaps and vague commitments, though no systematic greenwashing has been confirmed.

**Why MISLEADING, not GREENWASHING?**
The claim does not rise to GREENWASHING (61+) because:
- No verified emissions data directly contradicts the assertion;
- No confirmed lobbying against climate legislation has been identified;
- Waste-to-energy plus district heating, when coupled with CCS, is not inherently inconsistent with decarbonization.

The claim is MISLEADING (52/100) because it exploits regulatory gaps and vague language to suggest decarbonization progress without providing the audited baseline, permanence verification, or timeline required by the Green Claims Directive. The absence of these substantiations is not accidental — it enables the company to claim "nearly eliminates emissions" without defining what the starting point was or when net-zero will be achieved.

### Key Finding

Öresundskraft's claim that CCS will bring Filbornaverket "significantly closer to net-zero emissions" violates the Green Claims Directive's substantiation standard (Article 3) by providing no quantified baseline emissions, post-CCS absolute emissions target, certification of permanent carbon removal, or timeline. The vague language masks the absence of auditable commitments. Facility-level emissions verification is blocked by the facility's non-registration in the EU ETS, which should apply to waste incineration facilities of this capacity.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS facility registration**
Filbornaverket does not appear in the EUTL database. If the facility operates ≥3 MW thermal input, it should be registered under Phase 4. Absence of registration prevents cross-check against verified emissions monitoring data and raises questions about applicability of the claim. Resolution would require: (a) confirmation of facility thermal capacity; (b) verification of regulatory exemption status; or (c) confirmation of future registration timeline.

**Facility-level emissions baseline**
No peer-reviewed or CSRD-audited GHG inventory for Filbornaverket is publicly available. The company is not CSRD-obligated. Without this baseline, the claim's assertion of emissions reduction cannot be quantified or verified independently.

**CCS permanence specification**
No independent verification (ISO 14064-2, SBTi review, or geological storage certification) is cited. The claim does not specify whether CO2 is permanently sequestered or traded. This is critical for Green Claims Directive compliance and cannot be resolved without company disclosure.

**Lobbying direction**
EU Transparency Register confirms Öresundskraft is an active EU lobbyist but does not specify whether advocacy supports or opposes climate regulation. InfluenceMap data would clarify whether the company's public decarbonization claims are aligned with its policy positions, but this cross-check was not available.

**Confidence impact**: These gaps reduce confidence from 0.85 to 0.65. The Green Claims Directive substantiation standard remains clear regardless of absolute emissions magnitude, but the inability to verify facility-level baselines limits the precision of this assessment.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index scored this claim using the Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166, Articles 3 and 7) as the primary normative standard. The assessment required verification of: (1) quantified baseline emissions; (2) post-intervention absolute emissions or reduction percentage; (3) permanence certification of carbon removal; (4) third-party verification (SBTi, ISO 14064-2, or CSRD); (5) scope clarity (Scopes 1, 2, 3); and (6) timeline specificity. A claim scores GREENWASHING (61–100) if verified data contradict the assertion or confirmed lobbying contradicts it. It scores MISLEADING (46–60) if it exploits vague language, scope ambiguity, or regulatory gaps to suggest progress without auditable substantiation. It scores CREDIBLE (31–45) if substantiation is incomplete but directionally consistent with available data. It scores DECEPTIVE (0–30) if multiple material omissions or false statements are confirmed. This claim scored 52/100 (MISLEADING) because vague relative language ("nearly eliminates," "significantly closer") combined with absent baselines, permanence specifications, and timelines violates Article 3 substantiation requirements, even though no verified contradictory data exist.

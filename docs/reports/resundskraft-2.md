### Assessment

Öresundskraft's claim that it will capture 200,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually from 2029 onwards meets the threshold definition of **MISLEADING** under Prasine Index methodology because it fails three critical substantiation requirements while appearing to assert a measurable emissions reduction commitment.

**Substantiation Failure (Primary Finding)**

The claim violates Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) on three grounds:

1. **No baseline year stated.** The claim does not reference a baseline year (e.g., 2024, 2025, or 2028) against which the 200 kt/year reduction is measured. This prevents third parties from verifying whether the claim represents absolute emissions reductions or merely aspirational technology deployment.

2. **No scope definition.** The claim does not explicitly state whether the 200,000 tonnes refers to Scope 1 (direct process emissions), Scope 2 (purchased energy), or Scope 3 (supply chain) emissions. Context suggests Scope 1 process emissions from industrial operations, but this is not stated.

3. **No verification methodology disclosed.** The claim does not explain whether the 200 kt/year figure represents: (a) CO₂ captured and permanently sequestered (CCUS/geological storage), (b) CO₂ captured and used in products (CCU), or (c) a theoretical maximum capture rate. Permanent sequestration is mandatory for emissions reduction claims; capture without permanent storage is not an emissions reduction.

**Emissions Baseline Gap (Critical Data Limitation)**

Öresundskraft is not registered in the EU ETS Union Registry. This creates an insurmountable verification problem: without access to verified baseline Scope 1 emissions (either from EU ETS or voluntary disclosure), Prasine Index cannot assess whether 200 kt/year represents material progress or immaterial relative to the company's actual operations. 

For example:
- If Öresundskraft's actual Scope 1 emissions are 300 kt/year, a 200 kt capture target would require capturing two-thirds of all emissions by 2029—a high-risk, aggressive timeline with no disclosed capex or interim milestones.
- If the company's emissions are de minimis (<50 kt/year), the 200 kt claim is non-credible and materially misleading.

Neither scenario can be ruled out without current emissions disclosure. This is a substantiation failure: the claim cannot be independently verified ex post.

**Credibility Uncertainties**

- **Timeline plausibility.** 2029 is only three years away and represents a realistic pilot-to-deployment horizon for CCS technology in Nordic utilities. However, the absence of disclosed capex allocation, interim milestones, or partnership agreements undermines credibility.
- **Permanence and additionality.** The claim does not state whether the 200 kt/year represents net new reductions (additionality) or offset-style accounting that could mask underlying emissions increases in other scopes.
- **Risk factors.** No discussion of technological, regulatory, or cost risks that could delay deployment beyond 2029.

**Mitigating Factor: Genuine Technology Engagement**

The reference to "Innozhero" on Öresundskraft's website indicates genuine engagement with CCS technology partnerships. This partially distinguishes the claim from confirmed greenwashing (where a company makes emissions claims wholly unsupported by technology investment). However, partnership announcements do not constitute verification of the 200 kt/year claim or compliance with Green Claims Directive standards.

**Scoring Rationale**

- **Claim Substantiation: 68/100.** The claim is specific in quantity (200,000 tonnes) and timing (2029) but entirely vague in baseline, scope, and methodology. It reads as aspirational rather than as a measured commitment grounded in current emissions data.
- **Emissions Accuracy: 45/100.** No verified baseline emissions are available to assess accuracy. The claim cannot be cross-checked against EU ETS verified data or equivalent third-party reporting.
- **Target Credibility: 48/100.** The 2029 timeline is plausible for CCS pilots but lacks supporting evidence (capex, interim targets, SBTi validation, partnership detail) to establish credibility as a binding commitment.

**Overall verdict: MISLEADING (52/100).** The claim is not yet GREENWASHING because no verified data directly contradicts the 200 kt target—only an absence of supporting substantiation. However, as published, the claim fails to meet mandatory EU climate accountability standards and would not survive legal challenge under the Green Claims Directive. A journalist or regulator reading this claim would reasonably conclude that Öresundskraft has not yet committed to measurable emissions reductions.

### Key Finding

Öresundskraft claims it will capture 200,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually from 2029 onwards but fails to disclose: (1) a baseline year and current emissions inventory, (2) whether the claim refers to Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions, or (3) whether captured CO₂ will be permanently sequestered or merely used. Under the Green Claims Directive, these are mandatory disclosures; their absence means the claim cannot be independently verified and does not constitute a substantiated emissions reduction commitment. The company is not registered in the EU ETS, preventing cross-check against verified baseline emissions data.

### The Claim
> "Stegra is building the world's first large-scale green steel plant, producing fossil-free steel using green hydrogen and renewable electricity, with zero CO2 emissions from the steelmaking process."

*Source: Investor Relations page, https://www.stegra.com, date unknown*

### Evidence

**EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC (as amended)**
EU ETS verified emissions data (Article 15, Regulation 601/2012) constitute the legally mandated ground truth for corporate greenhouse gas claims under EU law. Verified figures are produced by independent third-party accredited verifiers. Stegra has no EU ETS installation IDs registered in the EU Transaction Log. This means the facility either does not yet exist, is not yet operational, or remains exempt from mandatory EU ETS reporting. Absence from EUTL prevents independent verification of the "zero CO2 emissions" assertion.
*Source: EUR_LEX [32003L0087], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087*

**Green Claims Directive Proposal (COM/2023/0166)**
Article 3(3)(a) requires emissions reduction or emissions intensity claims to specify: (i) baseline year; (ii) scope (Scope 1, 2, or 3); (iii) methodology. The claim states "zero CO2 emissions from the steelmaking process" — a narrow system boundary (Scope 1, at-plant combustion only) — but does not clarify: whether upstream hydrogen production emissions are included; whether renewable electricity's lifecycle emissions (manufacturing, land use, grid losses) are accounted; whether the "zero" claim applies to capital equipment or only operational energy. Per Article 5, per-unit or intensity claims must not obscure underlying scope boundaries.
*Source: EUR_LEX [52023PC0166], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**Competitive Landscape Check**
Multiple green steel projects are operational or under construction globally, including H2GreenSteel (Sweden, partial operations as of 2024) and HYBRIT (Sweden/Finland joint venture). The claim of "world's first large-scale" is not independently verified and may conflate first-mover status with pre-operational marketing positioning.
*Source: Industry research, multiple vendors, 2024–2026*

**Transparency Register Search**
Stegra does not appear in the EU Transparency Register. No registered lobbying activity is recorded. This is not contradicting evidence but indicates the company has not disclosed political engagement related to this claim.
*Source: EU Transparency Register query, 2026*

### Assessment

Stegra's claim is assessed as **MISLEADING** because it is directionally plausible — hydrogen-based steelmaking with renewable electricity can produce near-zero-emission steel — but fails to meet current EU legal and substantiation standards in four material respects.

**1. Substantiation Failure (No Verified Baseline)**

The absence of any EU ETS installation ID registered under Stegra's name is a critical data gap. EU ETS EUTL verified emissions data represent the legal ground truth for GHG claims under Directive 2003/87/EC. Stegra has no such data. This means:
- The facility is either not yet operational, under construction, or exempt from mandatory EU ETS coverage (e.g., <25 MW thermal input, or non-stationary).
- No baseline year, historical emissions trajectory, or operational verification exists.
- Independent third-party verification of actual emissions performance is impossible.

This is not a contradiction (which would raise the verdict to GREENWASHING or CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING); it is an absence of supporting evidence. A company cannot substantiate a claim of "zero CO2 emissions" with no verified operational data.

**2. Non-Compliance with Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a)**

The claim as stated violates the Green Claims Directive's core requirement (Article 3(3)(a)): it does not specify baseline year, scope, or methodology. The phrase "zero CO2 emissions from the steelmaking process" implies Scope 1 (direct process emissions), but leaves three critical questions unanswered:
- Are upstream hydrogen production emissions included or excluded? (Hydrogen from electrolysis powered by coal-fired grid electricity, or from natural gas reforming, would have substantial embedded emissions.)
- Are renewable electricity's lifecycle emissions accounted for? (Solar panel or wind turbine manufacturing has non-zero carbon cost.)
- Does "steelmaking process" exclude capital equipment, land use, or decommissioning?

Without specification of these boundaries, the "zero" claim is either exaggerated (if boundaries are narrow) or unsupported (if boundaries are comprehensive).

**3. "Zero Emissions" Claim Is Implausibly Absolute**

Even best-case hydrogen + renewable electricity steel production involves lifecycle emissions: hydrogen production infrastructure, electricity grid losses, capital equipment manufacturing, and supply-chain transport. A credible claim would state "near-zero," "carbon-neutral" (if offsets are used), or provide a quantified intensity metric (e.g., "less than 0.5 tonnes CO2 per tonne steel"). The assertion of absolute "zero" without defined scope boundaries is exaggerated under the principle articulated in EUR_LEX [52023PC0166] that per-unit claims must not obscure underlying increases in absolute emissions or scope boundaries.

**4. "World's First Large-Scale" Is Unverified**

Multiple green steel projects exist globally (H2GreenSteel, HYBRIT). Stegra's claim to be "world's first" is not independently verified and may conflate first-mover status with actual operational primacy. The "large-scale" descriptor is also unsubstantiated; no capacity figures or timelines are provided. Marketing language of this type, without supporting data, raises credibility concerns.

**Why Not GREENWASHING (61–80)?**

GREENWASHING applies to claims actively contradicted by verified evidence (e.g., EU ETS data showing rising emissions, or a regulatory ruling). Stegra's facility has no EU ETS record, so no affirmative contradiction exists. The absence of data is a substantiation failure, not a contradiction.

**Why Not SUBSTANTIATED (0–20)?**

SUBSTANTIATED applies to claims supported by audited baselines, third-party verification, or regulatory compliance records. Stegra provides none of these.

**Placement at MISLEADING (48/100):**

The claim is substantively possible (hydrogen + renewables can produce near-zero steel) and is backed by a named technology and facility location, which differentiates it from pure aspirational net-zero pledges. However, the absence of baseline data, scope specification, and operational verification, combined with exaggerated "zero emissions" language and unverified "world's first" framing, places it in the MISLEADING range.

---

### Key Finding

Stegra claims zero-emission steel production without providing audited baseline data, EU ETS verification, or methodological scope definition required under the Green Claims Directive. The facility has no registered EU ETS installation ID, meaning independent verification of emissions performance is impossible. The "zero emissions" claim is exaggerated without defined lifecycle boundaries and is likely non-compliant with Article 3(3)(a) of the Green Claims Directive proposal.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index assessed this claim using the Greenwashing Severity Scale (0–100), which maps verdicts as follows: SUBSTANTIATED (0–20), COMPLIANT (21–45), MISLEADING (46–60), GREENWASHING (61–80), CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100). Evidence sources include EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC (Regulation 601/2012 verification standards) as the legal ground truth for emissions claims, the Green Claims Directive proposal (COM/2023/0166, Article 3(3)(a)) for emissions claim substantiation requirements, EU Transparency Register queries for lobbying alignment checks, and industry research on competing green steel projects. 

The MISLEADING verdict reflects that the claim is directionally plausible but falls materially short of EU legal and substantiation standards. The claim would be upgraded to GREENWASHING (61–80) upon publication of EU ETS data showing Stegra's actual emissions, or downgraded to SUBSTANTIATED (0–20) upon provision of audited baselines and third-party verification.

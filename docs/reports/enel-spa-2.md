### Assessment

Enel claims "Electricity for the decarbonisation of Europe" — an aspirational positioning statement linking the company's identity to the European decarbonisation agenda. The evidence presents a direct contradiction between historical emissions reductions and active forward-looking fossil fuel expansion.

**Historical Emissions Performance:**
E-PRTR verified data shows genuine 75% reduction in regulated industrial emissions from 2007 to 2024 (31.97 Mt → 8.07 Mt). This is unambiguous and material. Institutional investor assessments (CA100+, TPI) treat this as credible evidence of climate alignment. However, the claim scope is "electricity for decarbonisation of Europe" — not merely Enel's own regulated emissions reductions.

**Critical Contradiction — Active Fossil Fuel Expansion:**
The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence: 90%, used by 400+ financial institutions including GFANZ members) documents that Enel is **actively expanding** coal mining by 15 Mtpa and coal power by 2.4 GW. This is not historical data — it is forward-looking committed capacity. Simultaneously:
- EGT shows Enel is developing the Porto Empedocle LNG terminal in Italy (confidence: 85%, institutional database).
- GOGET shows Enel operates 11 oil & gas extraction fields with 1 more entering development (confidence: 72%, institutional database).

A company cannot claim "electricity for decarbonisation" while actively committing capital to expand coal mining, LNG infrastructure, and oil & gas production. These investments lock in decades of fossil fuel operations and directly contradict the decarbonisation positioning.

**Vagueness and Regulatory Non-Compliance:**
The claim contains no quantified target, baseline year, scope declaration, or methodology. Under the Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a), such claims require explicit baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology. This claim fails that standard. The vagueness allows aspirational language without measurable commitment, which is characteristic of greenwashing.

**Institutional Support vs. Verified Contradiction:**
CA100+ and TPI assessments predate or do not fully account for the 2026 COAL_EXIT and EGT expansions. These institutional frameworks assess published climate targets and capex alignment at a strategic level, but the Global Coal Exit List and Europe Gas Tracker are operational databases updated continuously. The contradiction between investor assessment and operational reality indicates the company has not updated its stated targets to reflect current fossil fuel expansion decisions.

**Lobbying Alignment:**
EU Transparency Register confirms active lobbying; InfluenceMap rates engagement as B- (neutral/mixed, confidence: 50%). No confirmed evidence of active anti-climate-policy lobbying, which prevents a CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING verdict. However, the simultaneous climate positioning and fossil fuel expansion suggests a company seeking "license to operate" through climate rhetoric while protecting fossil fuel assets through political channels. This is greenwashing, but not with confirmed lobbying contradiction.

**Score Rationale (72/100):**
- **Emissions Accuracy (25/100)**: Genuine historical regulated emissions reduction (75%, E-PRTR) is overshadowed by active coal mining and gas infrastructure expansion. Score reflects real progress undermined by forward-looking contradiction.
- **Claim Substantiation (45/100)**: Supporting sources (CA100+, TPI) validate credible climate commitments, preventing a lower score. However, vagueness and lack of specific baseline/scope/methodology violate Green Claims Directive Article 3. Score reflects: credible investor support but no measurable substantiation.
- **Historical Consistency (70/100)**: This is not a repeat claim, but company has prior history of scoring in greenwashing band (69.9 average, worst = 72.0). Current claim is consistent in tone but evidential contradictions are escalating. Score reflects pattern of positioning without proportionate action.
- **Lobbying Alignment (55/100)**: Registered lobbyist (EU_TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER); InfluenceMap B- rating (inconclusive). No confirmed anti-climate lobbying detected, which prevents higher greenwashing score. Score reflects suggestive but unconfirmed misalignment.
- **Target Credibility (65/100)**: CA100+ and TPI rate 2050 pathways as Paris-compatible, but these targets are distant. No SBTi validation found. Current capex decisions (coal, LNG, O&G) lock in fossil fuel operations across decades, contradicting 2050 targets. Score reflects: distant targets that do not constrain near-term portfolio expansion.

### Key Finding

Enel claims "electricity for decarbonisation of Europe" while simultaneously expanding coal mining by 15 Mtpa, developing an LNG terminal in Italy, and operating 11 oil & gas extraction fields. The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (used by 400+ financial institutions) documents this active fossil fuel expansion, directly contradicting the decarbonisation positioning. Historical regulated emissions reductions (75% since 2007, verified by E-PRTR) provide partial credibility, but forward-looking capex commitments contradict the claim at the strategic level.

Prevents confirmation of lobbying contradiction, though corporate fossil fuel expansion + climate positioning pattern suggests likely misalignment.

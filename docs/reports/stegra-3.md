**[2] EU ETS Database — No Registered Installation Found**  
Stegra has no registered installation in the EU Emissions Trading System (EUTL) database. Iron ore reduction plants with thermal capacity ≥20 MW or melting capacity ≥50 tonnes/day are mandatory reporters under EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC as amended (Article 3, Regulation 2019/1042). Absence of registration means either: (a) the plant is not operational; (b) the facility operates below mandatory reporting thresholds; or (c) the claim refers to a planned facility not yet in operation.  
*Source:* EU ETS EUTL verified data, 2024–2025, https://ec.europa.eu/clima/ets

**[3] Green Claims Directive — Substantiation Requirements (Proposed)**  
COM/2023/0166, Article 3(3)(a) requires emissions reduction claims to disclose: baseline year, emissions scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology for calculation. The Stegra claim provides:
- ✗ No baseline year
- ✗ No quantified reduction percentage or absolute target
- ✗ No disclosed scope (Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions included)
- ✗ No methodology for measuring or verifying reduction
- ✓ Identifies coal as the counterfactual, but provides no supporting data

*Source:* European Commission, COM/2023/0166, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166

**[4] Iron Reduction Chemistry and Process Emissions**  
Green hydrogen reduction of iron oxide (2Fe₂O₃ + 6H₂ → 4Fe + 6H₂O) is technically sound and produces water vapour as a direct reduction byproduct. However, the claim "just plain water" omits material process emissions: (a) Scope 2 emissions from hydrogen production (electrolysis requires grid or nuclear electricity); (b) indirect emissions from feedstock transport and plant operations; (c) hydrogen production is not specified as 100% renewable- or nuclear-sourced, a prerequisite for "green hydrogen" classification per ISO 14068-1.  
*Source:* Technical chemistry (public domain); hydrogen production classification, ISO 14068-1:2023

## Assessment

Stegra's claim receives a MISLEADING verdict because it fails substantiation standards under the proposed Green Claims Directive and exaggerates through material omission.

### Substantiation Failure

The claim provides no baseline year, quantified reduction target, scope definition, or methodology. Under Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a), these are mandatory disclosures for any emissions reduction claim. The statement "Instead of massive CO2 emissions from reducing iron with coal, we will be emitting steam" is qualitative comparison without numerical support. No audited baseline emissions data, no target year, and no verified reduction methodology are provided. This is aspirational marketing language, not compliant claim substantiation.

### Exaggeration Through Omission

The assertion that emissions will be "just plain water" is directionally accurate for the direct reduction chemistry (Fe₂O₃ + H₂ → Fe + H₂O), but materially incomplete. It omits:
- **Scope 2 emissions** from hydrogen electrolysis, which requires substantial grid electricity or nuclear heat. These emissions are not negligible unless hydrogen is sourced from 100% renewable generation, which is not specified or verified.
- **Process and operational emissions** (equipment manufacture, transport, plant operations).

A genuinely "green" iron reduction claim would need to disclose total lifecycle emissions including hydrogen production pathway and grid carbon intensity. The claim is incomplete and misleading.

### Unverifiable Operational Status

The absence of EU ETS registration is a material red flag. If Stegra operates an iron reduction plant at claimed scale (sufficient to serve as "our plant" in investor communications), and if that plant exceeds mandatory reporting thresholds (≥20 MW thermal or ≥50 tonnes/day melting capacity), then **non-registration would constitute a compliance violation under Directive 2003/87/EC**. 

Three interpretations remain possible:
1. The plant is not yet operational (claim refers to planned future capacity).
2. The plant operates below regulatory thresholds (sub-scale pilot or development facility).
3. The claim misrepresents operational status (compliance failure).

Without independent verification of facility status, confidence in the claim cannot exceed 58%.

### Company History Pattern

Stegra has issued 3 prior claims with an average greenwashing score of 50.0 (worst prior score: 52.0). This claim (48.0) represents a deteriorating trend. No prior claim directly repeats this assertion, but the pattern of unsubstantiated or borderline claims suggests a systematic approach to marketing green capabilities without corresponding regulatory verification.

### Lobbying Context

Stegra is registered as an active EU lobbyist. The direction of its lobbying activity (support for or obstruction of climate policy) could not be determined from registration data alone. If subsequent evidence shows lobbying against EU ETS stringency, hydrogen sustainability standards, or green claims enforcement, the verdict would escalate to GREENWASHING (61–80). For this assessment, lobbying alignment remains neutral.

## Key Finding

Stegra's claim fails substantiation under the Green Claims Directive (no baseline, scope, or methodology disclosed), is incomplete by omitting Scope 2 hydrogen production emissions, and cannot be verified against EU ETS data because the company has no registered installation. The claim is unsubstantiated marketing language misrepresenting operational capability or emissions impact.

Cannot verify claimed emissions baseline or actual reductions without this ground-truth data.

Without this data, cannot verify "green" status of input feedstock.

- **Facility Commissioning Date and Capacity**: No public disclosure of plant commissioning date, thermal capacity (MW), or melting capacity (tonnes/day) found. Cannot confirm whether facility is operational, planned, or sub-threshold for EU ETS mandatory reporting.

These gaps reduce confidence to 58%. If Stegra publishes EU ETS registration data or a third-party lifecycle assessment of hydrogen-based iron reduction, verdict confidence would increase and score may improve.

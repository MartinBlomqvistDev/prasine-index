## The Claim

> "As of the end of 2025, about a third of our industrial sites were in water-stressed areas. We have finalised the assessment of material water-related risks and set local targets for our industrial assets located in water-stressed areas and we are implementing actions to reduce impacts and drive performance against these targets."

## Assessment

### Substantiation of the Water Claim

The claim makes four specific assertions: (1) approximately one-third of industrial sites are in water-stressed areas; (2) material water-related risks have been assessed and finalised; (3) local targets have been set for water-stressed asset locations; and (4) actions are being implemented.

The claim lacks quantified baseline data, audited methodology for water-stress classification, measurable reduction targets, and timelines. Glencore does not disclose its Scope 3 water management data, which would be required under CSRD ESRS E1-6 for supply chain substantiation (Glencore is not CSRD-obligated but makes supply-chain-adjacent claims). Under the Green Claims Directive Article 3, such claims require independent verification; none is provided. This constitutes a substantiation gap rated at 58/100.

### Core Greenwashing Contradiction: Business Trajectory vs. Environmental Narrative

The material contradiction does not lie in the water claim itself, which is narrow and site-specific. Rather, it lies in the company's corporate narrative contradiction:

**Glencore simultaneously:**
- Claims environmental stewardship and material water-risk management (this claim)
- **Actively expands coal mining capacity** (Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, confidence 0.90)
- **Actively opposes or delays climate legislation** (InfluenceMap D+, confidence 0.85)
- **Faces regulatory investigation** for potentially misleading environmental claims (CMA, 2023, confidence 0.70)

Under the Prasine Index CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING criterion (b), obstructive lobbying that contradicts stated environmental commitments is a primary greenwashing indicator. The InfluenceMap D+ score + EU Transparency Register confirmation (confidence 0.75) + CMA investigation together establish documented contradiction between stated environmental commitments and actual corporate behaviour.

This is not a case of a company making an unachievable commitment or overshooting a timeline. This is documented active opposition to climate legislation while simultaneously claiming environmental stewardship — a pattern of narrative contradiction rather than performance gap.

### Why the Verdict is GREENWASHING rather than CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING

The score of 72/100 reflects the following calibration:

1. **The specific water claim is not directly falsified.** The coal expansion and obstructive lobbying contradict the company's broader environmental narrative, not the narrow water-management statement itself. A regulator or court could not definitively prove the water-stress assessment claim is false based on evidence that the company expands coal capacity. The contradiction is at the level of corporate credibility, not specific claim falsification.

2. **One supporting source (CA100+) provides partial mitigation.** CA100+ rates the company as having Net Zero Ambition and Partial Capex Alignment (confidence 0.65), which provides some investor-facing validation, though this does not override the coal-screening failure or lobbying contradiction.

3. **The claim is operationally specific rather than aspirational.** The water claim references completed assessments, set targets, and implementation — not forward-facing net-zero pledges. This higher level of specificity in the claim itself allows some separation from the company's documented contradictions at the corporate-strategy level.

**If the claim had been:** "Glencore is committed to net-zero emissions by 2050" or "We are aligned with the Paris Agreement," the verdict would be CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100). The company's documented coal expansion directly falsifies such claims.

**Because the claim is:** site-level water-risk management with demonstrable local action, the verdict is GREENWASHING (61–80), reflecting documented corporate narrative contradiction without direct claim falsification.

### Lobbying Alignment as the Strongest Greenwashing Signal

The highest individual score on the Prasine Index matrix is **LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT at 82/100**. This reflects:

- InfluenceMap D+ score (obstructive climate policy engagement): confidence 0.85
- EU Transparency Register confirmation of active lobbying: confidence 0.75
- CMA regulatory flagging of misleading environmental claims (2023): confidence 0.70

The company publicly claims environmental stewardship while documented evidence shows active opposition to climate legislation. This is greenwashing by definition: the company's lobbying posture contradicts its stated environmental commitments. However, this dimension applies to the company's broader narrative, not to the specific water claim in isolation.

### Data Gaps and Confidence Adjustment

- **EU ETS installation IDs:** Could not be retrieved. This does not affect the water claim assessment but prevents direct historical emissions verification via the official EU emissions database. This gap is neutral — the coal expansion contradiction is verified through GCEL.
- **Glencore's internal water-stress assessment methodology:** Not disclosed. Without access to the company's classification criteria, the water-stress baseline cannot be independently verified. This contributes to the substantiation gap (CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION: 58/100).
- **Scope 3 water management data:** Not publicly disclosed. The claim references "industrial assets" and "water-stressed areas," implying supply chain relevance. Under CSRD ESRS E1-6, this should be substantiated; absence of Scope 3 data is a material gap under Article 3 of the Green Claims Directive.

The confidence level of 78% reflects strong evidence on the coal expansion and lobbying contradictions (combined confidence ~0.85–0.90) balanced against the narrowness of the water claim itself (which reduces the definitiveness of the greenwashing verdict) and the presence of partial supporting evidence (CA100+, confidence 0.65).

## Key Finding

Glencore's water-risk management claim is unsubstantiated (no audited targets, no Scope 3 data), but the material greenwashing signal is corporate-level: the company simultaneously claims environmental stewardship while actively expanding coal capacity and actively opposing climate legislation, as documented by the Urgewald Coal Exit List (0.90 confidence), InfluenceMap D+ rating (0.85 confidence), and CMA investigation (2023). The water claim is narrow enough to avoid direct falsification, but it sits within a documented pattern of environmental narrative contradiction at the corporate strategy level.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS verified emission data (installations):** Installation identifiers required to cross-reference Glencore's operations with the EU Emissions Trading System database. Not retrieved. *Impact:* Neutral — coal expansion is verified through independent GCEL source. Does not reduce confidence in greenwashing verdict.

2. **Glencore's internal water-stress classification methodology:** The company's criteria for identifying water-stressed areas are not publicly disclosed. Without methodological transparency, the baseline claim ("about a third of sites") cannot be independently verified. *Impact:* Contributes to CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION score of 58/100. Reduces confidence in the water claim's accuracy from ~85% to ~70%.

3. **Scope 3 water management and supply chain baseline data:** The claim references "water-related risks" which may include supply chain impacts. CSRD ESRS E1-6 requires Scope 3 disclosure; Glencore does not provide quantified Scope 3 water metrics. *Impact:* Substantiation gap under Green Claims Directive Article 3. Confidence in supply-chain substantiation: 40%.

4. **Local targets — quantified reduction metrics and timeline:** The claim states "local targets" are set but provides no percentage reduction, absolute water-use baselines, or implementation timelines. *Impact:* Contributes to TARGET_CREDIBILITY score of 68/100.

## Methodology Note

This assessment applies the Prasine Index scoring matrix across five dimensions: CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION (evidence quality and completeness), EMISSIONS_ACCURACY (contradiction by verified emissions data), HISTORICAL_CONSISTENCY (pattern analysis of prior claims by the same company), LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT (contradiction between stated commitments and documented policy engagement), and TARGET_CREDIBILITY (measurability and science-basis of stated targets). 

Scores are weighted as follows: Lobbying contradictions (30%) receive the highest weight as the primary greenwashing indicator under the EU Green Claims Directive and Plasine Index CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING criteria. Substantiation failures (25%) and emissions accuracy contradictions (25%) follow. Historical consistency (10%) and target credibility (10%) provide supporting context.

The verdict GREENWASHING (61–80) is assigned when documented contradictions between stated environmental commitments and actual corporate behaviour are verified at confidence ≥0.70 by independent sources, but the specific claim assessed is not directly falsified. CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100) is assigned when the specific claim text is contradicted by verified evidence at confidence ≥0.80, or when multiple high-confidence contradictions (≥0.85) across coal expansion, lobbying, and emissions data are combined.

Full Prasine Index methodology is published at [https://prasine.org/methodology]. Data sources cited in this report (Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, InfluenceMap, EU Transparency Register, CMA case files, CSRD Directive text) are institutional standards used by regulators, major financial institutions, and investigative media.

## Assessment

Enel's claim that it is "enabling the energy system of the future through growth in renewables" is **materially contradicted by verified data on active fossil fuel expansion**.

**The Core Contradiction:**

The claim is not aspirational language ("we are committed to") but a factual assertion about current behaviour: *"We ARE ENABLING...through GROWTH IN RENEWABLES."* This is a specific claim about the company's current strategic direction and capital allocation.

Three independent institutional databases—all used by major financial institutions and investors—document **active expansion** of coal, oil/gas, and LNG assets:

- **COAL_EXIT** (confidence 0.90): 15 Mtpa coal mining expansion + 2.4 GW coal power expansion. Used by 400+ financial institutions as the standard coal screening tool.
- **GOGET** (confidence 0.72): 12 operating oil/gas extraction fields with 1 additional field in pre-development stage. 11 fields actively producing.
- **EGT** (confidence 0.85): Porto Empedocle LNG terminal in active development—a 40+ year fossil fuel demand lock-in.

These are not historical legacies. The data comes from 2026 records and represents **forward-looking capital deployment decisions made between 2024–2026**.

**Why Supporting Evidence Does Not Neutralise the Contradiction:**

Enel has achieved genuine progress on emissions reduction (EPRTR: 75% decline in regulated emissions 2007–2024) and holds credible investor-facing climate commitments (CA100+ Net Zero Ambition; TPI Below 2°C pathway). However, these achievements do not falsify the greenwashing verdict because:

1. **The claim is about energy *mix* transition**, not absolute emissions reduction. A utility can reduce emissions from its *existing* fossil fuel assets while simultaneously *expanding* its fossil fuel portfolio—which is exactly what Enel is doing.

2. **The expansion is active, not legacy.** If Enel were winding down coal and gas while maintaining historical stranded assets, the claim could be partially credible despite the legacy carbon. Instead, COAL_EXIT and GOGET document *growth* in extraction and generation capacity.

3. **Investor standards explicitly reject this position.** The GCEL (used by GFANZ) screens companies for coal expansion *precisely because* expanding coal contradicts net-zero transition claims, regardless of parallel renewable deployment or legacy asset retirement.

**Compliance with Green Claims Directive:**

Under the Green Claims Directive (Article 3), the claim "growth in renewables" requires:
- Specific percentage of total energy consumption covered
- Verified renewable source
- Measurable baselines and timelines

The claim provides none of these. It is a vague directional assertion unsupported by quantified targets.

**Score Rationale: 72/100 (Upper GREENWASHING band, 61–80):**

The score reflects genuine tension in Enel's corporate strategy:

- **Downward pressure (toward CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING, 81+):** Three high-confidence sources (0.90, 0.72, 0.85) document active fossil fuel expansion. The contradiction is institutional standard-level (GCEL used by 400+ institutions).

- **Upward mitigation (preventing CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING):** EPRTR's 75% emissions reduction is verified and substantial. CA100+ and TPI provide investor-credible climate alignment. The claim itself is directionally possible (renewables can grow even with fossil fuel expansion), making the falsification less absolute than a "net-zero by 2030" claim contradicted by flat emissions.

- **Result:** 72 places the claim in GREENWASHING territory. The company is simultaneously deploying renewables *and* expanding coal/gas—this is a mixed energy strategy marketed as a clean energy transition, contradicted by verified forward-looking fossil fuel capital allocation.

## Key Finding

**Enel SpA claims to enable "the energy system of the future through growth in renewables" while simultaneously expanding coal mining (15 Mtpa), coal power (2.4 GW), and LNG terminal infrastructure (Porto Empedocle, Italy)—contradictions documented by Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (GCEL: used by 400+ financial institutions), Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker (11 producing oil/gas fields), and Europe Gas Tracker. The company's 75% verified emissions reduction (EPRTR 2007–2024) represents genuine progress on legacy asset decarbonisation but does not offset the forward-looking fossil fuel expansion that makes the "enabling the energy system of the future" claim irreconcilable with institutional climate transition standards.**

## Data Gaps

1. **EU_ETS Installation IDs**: Enel has no registered installations in the EU Emissions Trading System database. This is unusual for a major utility and limits independent verification of emissions claims via the primary EU carbon accounting system. 
   - **Mitigation**: E-PRTR (European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register) provides ground-truth regulated emissions data for 2007–2024 and substitutes adequately for this gap. Confidence in emissions assessment remains at 0.75.

2. **SBTi Classification Status**: SBTi records for Enel show "status=unknown" and "net-zero target=none." It is unclear whether the company has submitted targets to SBTi, declined to do so, or is in evaluation. 
   - **Impact**: Cannot independently verify whether short-term (2030/2035) decarbonisation targets meet 1.5°C alignment. This is a minor gap, as CA100+ and TPI provide investor-level assessment of climate credibility.

3. **Lobbying Position Detail**: EU Transparency Register confirms Enel is an active EU lobbyist, but the register does not disclose the substance of lobbying positions (for/against climate policy, renewable subsidies, carbon pricing, etc.). InfluenceMap provides a B- engagement rating (neutral/mixed) but does not detail specific policy contradictions.
   - **Impact**: Cannot assess whether Enel's lobbying contradicts its renewable growth claims. Confidence in lobbying assessment reduced to 0.50 for this reason.

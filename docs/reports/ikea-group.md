### Evidence

**[1] EU E-PRTR regulated emissions to air (2010–2023)**
E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions recorded for IKEA Group rose 902% from 2010 to 2023, excluding the exceptional 2024 spike. The pre-2024 trend shows: 2020: 323.2 t; 2021: 1,430.0 t; 2022: 1,599.5 t; 2023: 1,242.0 t. The 2024 figure (110,243.0 t) is likely a first-disclosure event and should be treated with caution. The three-year rise from 2020–2023 (4× increase) contradicts the direction implied by a climate-focused commitment. However, E-PRTR captures only regulated air emissions to a subset of IKEA facilities and does not encompass Scope 1–3 greenhouse gas emissions or supply-chain impacts.
*Source: E-PRTR, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/*

**[2] Green Claims Directive — Article 3(1) substantiation requirement**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) requires all explicit environmental claims to be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence, cover the full lifecycle where relevant, and must not mislead by omission. Vague claims such as 'eco-friendly', 'green', or 'sustainable' without specific substantiation are prohibited. IKEA's claim names three domains (climate, nature, circularity) but provides no quantified targets, baselines, timelines, or independent verification.
*Source: EUR-LEX, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

SBTi record for IKEA Group shows no validated science-based target, no net-zero target, and status unknown. This represents a material substantiation gap for any claim focused on "addressing climate holistically".
*Source: SBTi, N/A, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

### Assessment

IKEA's claim that it is "working to address" climate, nature and circularity "holistically" fails to meet the substantiation standards required under the EU's Green Claims Directive and provides no measurable basis for verification.

**Substantiation failure (primary finding):** The claim is vague and aspirational. It names three interconnected environmental domains but specifies no quantified targets, baselines, delivery timelines, or metrics. Under Green Claims Directive Article 3(1) [2], such unsubstantiated claims are prohibited; they exaggerate through omission. A credible claim to address multiple environmental challenges holistically requires demonstrable targets and independent verification. IKEA provides neither.

**Emissions trend contradiction (secondary finding):** E-PRTR data [1] shows regulated air emissions from IKEA facilities rose 902% from 2010 to 2023. The recent trajectory (2020–2023) shows a 4× increase: 323.2 t to 1,242.0 t. This upward trend is inconsistent with a claim of "working to address climate". However, this evidence is qualified: E-PRTR captures only regulated air emissions at a subset of IKEA's operations and does not represent full Scope 1–3 greenhouse gas inventory. The contradiction is therefore material but incomplete.

**Target credibility absent (tertiary finding):** SBTi [3] shows no validated science-based target and no net-zero commitment. Without third-party verification or recognisable targets, IKEA's implicit commitment to holistic environmental action has no independent credibility benchmark. This is a substantiation failure for any climate-focused claim.

**Historical pattern consistency:** IKEA's prior assessment portfolio (22 claims, average score 54.3) demonstrates a consistent pattern of aspirational claims lacking measurable substantiation. The current claim is typical of this pattern but does not constitute a detected repeat.

**Lobbying alignment:** InfluenceMap's B-band rating [4] indicates no active lobbying against climate policy. This does not substantiate the claim but prevents a higher (GREENWASHING) score by removing evidence of direct contradiction between public claims and private advocacy.

**Score justification: 48/100 (MISLEADING, not GREENWASHING):** The claim scores as MISLEADING rather than GREENWASHING because the core defect is substantiation failure (absence of measurable targets and verification), not a direct, verifiable contradiction of a specific factual assertion. The claim is intentionally vague ("working to address them holistically") and therefore cannot be falsified by a single data point. The E-PRTR upward trend is material but does not capture full GHG scope. A GREENWASHING verdict requires proven deception of a specific quantifiable claim; this claim avoids specificity and therefore occupies the MISLEADING band (46–60).

### Key Finding

IKEA's claim lacks substantiation under EU Green Claims Directive standards and provides no measurable targets, timelines, or independent verification. Regulated emissions data shows an upward trend inconsistent with climate commitment, though this is limited to air emissions and does not represent full greenhouse gas scope. The company has no validated science-based targets (SBTi status unknown).

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS:** IKEA has no registered EU ETS installations; no verified greenhouse gas emissions baseline available from this source. This limits confidence in full emissions assessment.

**SBTi:** Status is unknown; no validated target or net-zero commitment on record. This is a material gap for any claim focused on climate action.

**EU Transparency Register:** IKEA not found; lobbying activity at EU level remains unverified. Confidence in the lobbying assessment is therefore qualified.

**Scope 1–3 emissions inventory:** No comprehensive greenhouse gas data (Scope 1, 2, 3 supply-chain, financed emissions) available. E-PRTR captures only regulated air emissions, a narrow subset of actual climate impact.

**Lifecycle assessment / nature / circularity metrics:** No independent data on biodiversity impact, circular economy performance, or product lifecycle emissions available. The claim addresses three domains; substantiation can be verified for none.

These gaps reduce confidence from 0.75 to 0.58. The verdict (MISLEADING) is not reversed by these gaps, but confidence remains moderate because the claim itself is intentionally vague and the primary defect is substantiation absence rather than a single, measurable contradiction.

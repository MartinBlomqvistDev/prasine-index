## IKEA Group — Greenwashing Assessment
**Verdict: MISLEADING CLAIM** | Score: 46.0/100 (range: 38–58) | Confidence: 60%
*Published: 2026-06-29 | Prasine Index | Trace ID: 805832f2-8533-49c3-8156-358b23003250*

### The Claim
> "Our Net Zero Transition journey"

*Source: Investor relations / corporate sustainability webpage, https://www.ikea.com/us/en/this-is-ikea/climate-environment/the-ikea-sustainability-strategy-pubfea4c210, retrieved 2026-06-29*

### Evidence

**Self-reported disclosure on the assessed page.** The webpage presents "Our Net Zero Transition journey" as a focus area under "Climate, nature & circularity" but contains no substantive climate or net-zero disclosure. Specifically absent: no baseline year for net-zero; no interim reduction targets for 2030, 2035, or 2040; no absolute emissions figures (Scope 1, 2, or 3) for any year; no abatement vs. removal split; no certified carbon removal mechanisms; no verified transition plan. The only emissions-related data points are non-target operational achievements: 100% renewable electricity in IKEA-operated factories (FY21) and 55.8% renewable materials sourced (2021). The page lacks all seven EmpCo Directive substantiation elements. (SOURCE_DOCUMENT, self-reported disclosure, https://www.ikea.com/us/en/this-is-ikea/climate-environment/the-ikea-sustainability-strategy-pubfea4c210)

**Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi).** SBTi records for IKEA Group show status = "Targets set", temperature classification = 1.5°C, and net-zero target = "Targets set". This indicates an externally validated net-zero target framework exists independent of the assessed page. (SBTi, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action; confidence 0.70)

**LobbyMap climate policy engagement.** IKEA Group is rated Band B, indicating supportive engagement broadly consistent with its public climate commitments. This is not the D/D+ obstruction band. (LobbyMap, https://lobbymap.org/; confidence 0.75)

**Regulatory context (non-company-specific).** The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, amends UCPD Annex I to blacklist environmental claims not substantiated by recognised scientific evidence; generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are automatically unfair commercial practices. (EUR-Lex, 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825) The CSRD (2022/2464) requires large companies to disclose Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions under ESRS E1. (EUR-Lex, 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464)

### Assessment

The claim "Our Net Zero Transition journey" is a vague, aspirational headline rather than a falsifiable assertion. On the assessed page it constitutes a substantiation failure: the page presents the net-zero framing while disclosing no baseline year, no interim 2030/2035/2040 targets, no absolute Scope 1/2/3 figures, no abatement-versus-removal split, no certified removal mechanism, and no verified transition plan. Measured against the EmpCo Directive standard (EUR-Lex 32024L0825), the page fails all seven substantiation elements (SOURCE_DOCUMENT).

This page-level failure is materially mitigated by two independent supporting sources. SBTi records a net-zero target as "set" with a 1.5°C classification, demonstrating that IKEA's net-zero ambition rests on an externally validated science-based framework rather than pure invention. LobbyMap rates IKEA Band B (supportive), indicating its policy engagement is broadly consistent with its stated climate commitments — and is not in the obstruction band.

No confirmed greenwashing trigger is met. LobbyMap is Band B, not D/D+, so there is no lobbying contradiction. IKEA's core business (furniture retail and manufacturing) presents no irreconcilable contradiction with a net-zero claim. No prior regulatory ruling against IKEA's climate claims is on file, and this claim is not a repeat. The accumulated evidence comprises one company-specific contradiction (the inadequate page) against two supporting sources at or above the 0.70 mitigating threshold; there are not three independent contradicting sources at ≥0.85. The score is therefore capped below the confirmed-greenwashing band and placed in the MISLEADING range (41–60).

The claim is directionally plausible and partially externally corroborated, but the specific disclosure assessed exaggerates through omission and fails the EU substantiation standard. The score sits at the lower end of the MISLEADING band, at 46.

### Key Finding

IKEA holds an SBTi-validated, 1.5°C-aligned net-zero target, but the specific webpage carrying its "Net Zero Transition journey" headline contains no baseline, no interim targets, no absolute emissions figures and no transition plan — failing all seven EmpCo Directive substantiation elements. The ambition is real; the disclosure on this page is not.

There is therefore no independent ground-truth emissions dataset to corroborate or contradict the claim. This is the primary driver of the moderate confidence rating.
- **Supporting source confidence:** the SBTi record (0.70) and LobbyMap record (0.75) are moderate-confidence sources, and both carry no stated data year, limiting temporal precision.
- **CSRD obligation:** the claim is recorded as not CSRD-obligated; CSRD is therefore treated as contextual rather than as a binding disclosure benchmark for this assessment.

These gaps place overall confidence at 60%.

**[2] Green Claims Directive (EU 2023/2161), 2023**
Article 3(1) requires renewable energy transformation claims to specify: (a) percentage of total energy consumption covered by renewables; (b) the energy source (wind, solar, etc.); (c) whether renewable energy certificates (RECs) or guarantees of origin (GOs) are used. Unbundled certificate-based claims claiming "100% renewable" without physical supply contracts are explicitly identified as potentially non-compliant.
*Source: EUR-LEX, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166, confidence 0.95*

### Assessment

Ørsted's claim to have "transformed from one of Europe's most coal-intensive utilities to a global leader in renewable energy" is directionally plausible but cannot be independently verified as substantiated.

**Emissions Trajectory (Score: 25/100)**

The claim rests on two factual assertions: historical coal intensity and current renewable energy leadership. The absence of Ørsted from current EU ETS registrations is consistent with divestment of coal-fired generation—the company's publicly documented portfolio shift over 15+ years supports this interpretation. However, EU ETS data unavailability prevents verification of: (a) baseline emissions when the company was "most coal-intensive," (b) the timeline of reduction, (c) current emissions intensity relative to renewable energy output. No third-party verified emissions inventory, Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) validation, or CDP climate disclosure was provided. This critical data gap prevents quantified assessment of the transformation's scale.

**Claim Substantiation (Score: 48/100 — MISLEADING risk)**

Under the Green Claims Directive (EUR-LEX, 2023), the claim fails substantiation standards. It is framed as a narrative ("have transformed") with no measurable baseline, no percentage of renewable energy in total energy mix, no specification of renewable sources, and no disclosure of certificate types (RECs/GOs vs. physical power purchase agreements). This vagueness creates compliance risk: Article 3(1) classifies such unspecified renewable claims as potentially misleading. A compliant claim would read: "We have increased renewable energy to 85% of generation by 2026, divested all coal assets by 2023, and procure 100% through guarantees of origin and physical power purchase agreements." The claim as stated does not meet this standard.

**Historical Consistency (Score: 15/100)**

Prasine Index records show one prior assessment of Ørsted (score: 35, INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE); no repeat claims detected. No evidence of prior broken commitments or contradictory historical claims. This dimension reflects absence of corroborating data rather than contradictory history.

**Lobbying Alignment (Score: 18/100)**

InfluenceMap records supportive engagement (B-band). No contradictory lobbying activity documented. This aligns with rather than contradicts the renewable energy transition claim. However, absence of active climate leadership lobbying (e.g., support for stronger EU climate targets) limits the positive score.

**Data Gaps**

The following sources could not be queried or returned insufficient data:
- **EU ETS**: No installation IDs found. This prevents emissions baseline and trajectory verification for EU-regulated operations. Does not disprove the claim but prevents quantification.
- **SBTi validation status**: Not provided. No Science Based Targets initiative validation found; cannot verify net-zero alignment.
- **CA100+ engagement**: Not assessed.
- **Company sustainability/annual reports**: Claim source does not link to detailed reports with quantified renewable energy percentages, capacity figures, or divestment dates.
- **Third-party assurance**: No independent audit referenced.

This absence of granular data is material: the claim concerns a major utilities sector transition and should be supported by verified capacity data, audited emissions inventories, and regulatory filings.

### Key Finding

Ørsted's transformation claim is consistent with documented sector knowledge and supportive lobbying alignment, but cannot be verified as substantiated because: (1) EU ETS data gap prevents independent emissions verification; (2) the claim lacks the specificity (renewable percentage, source, certificate type) required by the Green Claims Directive and risks classification as misleading under EU 2023/2161. Company must provide quantified renewable energy percentages, verified coal asset divestment dates, and third-party audited emissions baselines.

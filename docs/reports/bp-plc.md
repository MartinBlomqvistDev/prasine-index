### Evidence

**[1] Global Oil and Gas Exit List (GOGEL) — Fossil fuel expansion record**
GOGEL lists BP plc as actively expanding oil and gas capacity, recording production of 2,860,055 boe/d and proven reserves of 2,996,140,000 boe. Both upstream expansion and LNG expansion are confirmed. GOGEL is used by over 400 financial institutions within GFANZ and PAII frameworks as the standard oil and gas screen.
*Urgewald Global Oil and Gas Exit List, 2026, https://gogel.org/*

**[2] Global Oil and Gas Extraction Tracker (GOGET) — FID-committed extraction fields**
GOGET documents 163 oil and gas extraction fields operated by BP across 21 countries. Ten fields are classified as in development with Final Investment Decision (FID) confirmed — meaning capital has already been committed to new fossil fuel extraction. Fields include CLOV 3 Oil Phase (Angola), Dalia 3 Oil Phase (Angola), Ginger Gas Field (Trinidad and Tobago), Guadalupe (KC010) (United States), and Harmattan Gas Field (Egypt). A further 17 discovered pre-FID fields are recorded as likely to enter development, including Achmelvich (United Kingdom), BirAllah (Mauritania), and Bumerangue (Brazil). 131 fields are currently operating. Each FID-committed field locks in decades of upstream fossil fuel production.
*Global Oil and Gas Extraction Tracker, March 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/*

**[3] Europe Gas Tracker (EGT) — Gas infrastructure in active development**
EGT records 10 European gas assets associated with BP plc: 9 pipelines and 1 LNG terminal. Three assets are in active development, including the Trans-Adriatic Gas Pipeline (Türkiye, Greece, Albania, Italy) and the Trans-Anatolian Gas Pipeline (Georgia, Greece, Türkiye). Gas infrastructure of this type locks in 40 or more years of fossil gas demand. Five assets are operating and two are retiring or cancelled.
*Europe Gas Tracker, March 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/europe-gas-tracker/*

**[4] Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) — Paris alignment assessment**
TPI rates BP plc as Not Aligned with the Paris Agreement at all three benchmark checkpoints: CP 2025, CP 2035, and CP 2050. BP's disclosed emissions trajectory exceeds the TPI sector benchmark for the Oil and Gas sector at every horizon, including 2050. TPI Centre assessments are used by over 150 investors with combined AUM of $80 trillion as the standard investor-facing measure of corporate climate transition quality. BP's management quality is rated Level 4 (Strategic+), indicating awareness of the issue at board level, which makes the alignment failure on the carbon performance dimension more — not less — significant.
*Transition Pathway Initiative, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*

**[5] ASA Ruling — Prior enforcement for equivalent environmental advertising**
The UK Advertising Standards Authority upheld complaints against BP advertisements in July 2019 (ASA ruling G19-1020800). The advertisements featured flowers, wind turbines, and solar panels promoting BP as an environmentally responsible company. The ASA found that the ads selectively presented BP's most favourable low-carbon activities, which represented a fraction of BP's overall business, creating a misleading overall impression. This constitutes a prior regulatory ruling confirming a documented pattern of misleading green claims.
*ASA (UK), 2019, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/bp-plc-g19-1020800.html*

**[6] BP Sustainability Report (Source Document) — Substantiation record**
BP's 2025 Sustainability Report discloses the assessed claim verbatim and reports a 7% reduction in the average lifecycle carbon intensity of energy products sold against the 2019 baseline (6% for 2024). The document does not provide: interim reduction targets for 2030, 2035, or 2040; a breakdown between emissions abatement and certified permanent carbon removals; a certified removal plan; or a verified transition plan. On this document's own figures, the claimed trajectory has advanced 7% of the required distance over six years, with no mechanistic pathway disclosed for the remaining 93%.
*BP plc 2025 Sustainability Report, 2025, https://www.bp.com/about-us/sustainability/sustainability-report*

**[7] EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) — Applicable legal standard**
Directive EU 2024/825, in force since March 2024, amends Annex I of the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive to blacklist generic net-zero claims that are not substantiated by recognised scientific evidence and that do not demonstrate residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals. Generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are automatically classified as unfair commercial practices.
*EUR-Lex [32024L0825], 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**[8] Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD, EU 2022/2464) — Disclosure standard**
CSRD requires large companies and those listed on EU-regulated markets to disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024 reporting onward. A net-zero claim that is unsupported by or inconsistent with mandatory CSRD disclosures constitutes a material inconsistency under this framework.
*EUR-Lex [32022L2464], 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

### Assessment

Three independent CONFIRMED GREENWASHING triggers are established by the evidence record. No supporting source at any confidence level was identified.

**Core business contradiction.** BP's claim of a net-zero trajectory for the lifecycle carbon intensity of its sold energy products is directly contradicted by its documented expansion of fossil fuel production capacity. GOGEL (confidence 0.90) records BP as actively expanding both upstream oil and gas capacity and LNG infrastructure. GOGET (confidence 0.87) documents 10 extraction fields with FID already committed — meaning capital expenditure has been legally and financially locked in for new fossil fuel extraction across Angola, Trinidad and Tobago, Egypt, and the United States, among others — alongside 17 pre-FID fields likely to enter development. EGT (confidence 0.85) confirms three European gas infrastructure assets in active development, including two Trans-Anatolian Gas Pipeline entries and the Trans-Adriatic Gas Pipeline, locking in 40 or more years of fossil gas demand. The combination of FID-committed extraction fields, LNG expansion, and long-lived pipeline infrastructure constitutes a structural, capital-committed contradiction of any credible net-zero sales trajectory. This is not a future risk: the capital decisions have been made.

**Prior regulatory ruling.** The ASA's 2019 ruling (confidence 0.90) established that BP's selective presentation of its most favourable environmental activities creates a misleading overall impression of its business — precisely the mechanism operating in the assessed claim, which presents a 2050 net-zero ambition without disclosing the active fossil fuel expansion documented above. This is a precedent-establishing finding by a competent authority against BP on an equivalent environmental claim type.

**Accumulated contradicting evidence with zero support.** Six evidence sources contradict the claim; four carry confidence ratings of 0.85 or above (GOGEL 0.90, ASA 0.90, SOURCE_DOCUMENT 0.95, TPI 0.85, GOGET 0.87, EGT 0.85). TPI (confidence 0.85) rates BP as Not Aligned with Paris at CP 2025, CP 2035, and CP 2050 — a direct, quantitative contradiction of BP's net-zero trajectory claim at every relevant time horizon. This rating is used by investors representing $80 trillion in AUM. No source supports the claim.

**Substantiation failure.** BP's own Sustainability Report (confidence 0.95) — the source document for the claim — confirms that after six years of this stated aim, the reported progress is a 7% intensity reduction against the 2019 baseline. The document discloses no interim targets, no certified removal plan, no abatement-versus-removal breakdown, and no verified transition plan. A 7% intensity improvement over six years, with no mechanistic pathway for the remaining 93%, does not constitute substantiation of a 2050 net-zero claim. The intensity metric used (average lifecycle carbon intensity of sold products) also masks continued absolute fossil fuel production growth, as a per-unit measure can decline while total emissions volume rises.

**Lobbying.** LobbyMap rates BP C- (neutral/mixed, confidence 0.50). This is insufficient to establish a lobbying-obstruction finding, and the LOBBYING_CONTRADICTION sub-score is correspondingly low (40/100). This is the only dimension in which the evidence does not clearly confirm greenwashing.

### Regulatory Exposure

**(a) EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825, in force March 2024)**
This claim violates the EmpCo Directive. Directive EU 2024/825 amends Annex I of the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive to blacklist net-zero claims that are not substantiated by recognised scientific evidence and that do not demonstrate residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals rather than offset credits. BP's Sustainability Report (SOURCE_DOCUMENT, confidence 0.95) confirms that no certified removal plan, no removal-versus-abatement breakdown, and no verified transition plan have been disclosed. The claim therefore lacks all required elements of EmpCo substantiation. It is not a borderline case: the disclosure record contains none of the required components.

Under EmpCo Directive Article 6 (as amended), in conjunction with Annex I of the UCPD as amended, this claim is subject to enforcement by national consumer protection authorities. BP plc is incorporated in the United Kingdom but markets to and operates within EU member state jurisdictions. Complaints may be filed with national consumer authorities in member states where BP's sustainability communications are directed at consumers, including the Autorité de la concurrence (France), the Autoriteit Consument en Markt (Netherlands), and the Bundeskartellamt / Verbraucherzentralen (Germany), as well as with the European Commission's Consumer Protection Cooperation network.

**(b) Green Claims Directive (enforcement begins September 2026)**
The assessed claim would fail Green Claims Directive scrutiny. Under the GCD's mandatory substantiation requirements, environmental claims must be verified by an accredited independent third party before publication. BP's claim is not accompanied by: verified interim targets; a certified removal methodology; an independent audit of the transition plan; or a quantified and time-bound pathway from the current 7% reduction to net zero. This claim is not ready for GCD scrutiny and would require substantial redevelopment — including independent verification of interim milestones and a certified removal plan — before it could be lawfully made in the EU under the GCD regime commencing September 2026.

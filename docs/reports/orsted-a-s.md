### Assessment

The claim is a specific, quantified, past-tense assertion: a 98% reduction in scope 1 and 2 emissions **intensity** by end-2025. Four sources — LobbyMap, SBTi, GCPT, and EGT — are directionally consistent with Ørsted's documented pivot away from fossil generation (coal fully retiring, most gas pipeline assets retiring, no adverse lobbying signal, 1.5°C-classified SBTi targets). None of these sources contradicts the claim.

However, this assessment could not draw on the decisive primary source: EU ETS verified emissions (EUTL). Ørsted operates numerous EU ETS installations (its former DONG Energy power plants), but Prasine had not yet mapped those installation IDs to the company at assessment time, so the EUTL record was not queried in this run. This is a Prasine data-mapping gap, not a statement that Ørsted lacks EU ETS installations — and it is the central limitation of this assessment.

The only absolute emissions dataset available, E-PRTR, shows a different pattern than the intensity claim implies: combined regulated emissions rose from 1.64 Mt (2020) to 4.15 Mt (2024), including a near-doubling between 2023 and 2024, even as the 2007–2024 comparison shows a 36% decline. An intensity reduction is mathematically compatible with rising absolute emissions if output (e.g. MWh generated) grew faster than emissions — plausible for an expanding energy company — but this tension is exactly what the EmpCo Directive identifies as a risk area for misleading claims. Without the underlying denominator (output/production basis), baseline year, or methodology, the 98% figure cannot be independently checked.

Compounding this, the source webpage returned an HTTP 403 error, preventing verification of the baseline year, methodology, or scope boundaries underpinning the claim as published.

This is not a repeat claim, and no prior enforcement rulings against Ørsted were found — the company does not carry a pattern of substantiation failures. The absence of contradiction, combined with the absence of verification, places this claim squarely in the UNVERIFIABLE_CLAIM category rather than MISLEADING or CONFIRMED.

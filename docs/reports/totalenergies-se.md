### Evidence

**[1] Dutch ACM Enforcement Action (2021)**
The Netherlands Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM) issued a formal enforcement warning in 2021 naming TotalEnergies among energy companies making greenwashing claims. ACM found that "net-zero and sustainability claims lacked the required substantiation under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive as transposed into Dutch law." This is a regulatory body's formal determination that the company has made unsubstantiated environmental claims. Confidence: 0.80. [ACM enforcement ruling, 2021, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-warns-companies-about-misleading-green-claims]

**[2] E-PRTR Regulated Emissions Data (2024)**
European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register data for TotalEnergies SE shows regulated industrial emissions to air (CO2, GHGs, NOx, NMVOC combined):
- 2020: 14,790,789.5 tonnes
- 2021: 13,022,761.4 tonnes
- 2022: 16,443,668.8 tonnes (+26% spike from 2021)
- 2023: 13,958,317.4 tonnes
- 2024: 12,156,022.6 tonnes

Long-term reduction: 30% from 2007 (17,395,562.1 t) to 2024 (12,156,022.6 t). The directional trend supports emissions reduction, but the 2022 spike contradicts a steady "less emissions" narrative. Confidence: 0.75. [E-PRTR data, 2024, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/]

**[3] Green Claims Directive Substantiation Requirements (2023)**
The EU Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) Article 3(3)(a) requires that "emissions reduction claims must state the baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology." Article 5 prohibits aggregating reductions and increases to manufacture net reduction figures. The claim "More Energy, Less Emissions" provides none of these required elements: no baseline year, no scope definition, no methodology, no measurable targets. This is a compliance failure under EU law. Confidence: 0.95. [EUR-Lex legislative record, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166]

**[4] EU ETS Verified Emissions Data — Unavailable**
The EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC establishes that verified EUTL emissions data "represent the highest-quality legal ground truth for actual GHG emissions" under Regulation 601/2012. No installation IDs for TotalEnergies are registered in the available EUTL database, preventing access to verified third-party audited GHG emissions data. For a major energy producer with substantial EU assets, this is a significant gap. Confidence: 0.95 (for the legal standard; 0.00 for data availability). [EUR-Lex legislative record, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087]

**[5] CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark (2025)**
The Climate Action 100+ investor benchmark assesses TotalEnergies as having a credible net-zero ambition with "Partial" capex alignment and "Partial" short-term targets. This provides directional support for the company's emissions reduction trajectory, though with noted qualifications and moderate investor confidence. Confidence: 0.65. [CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/]

**[6] InfluenceMap Climate Policy Engagement Score**
InfluenceMap rates TotalEnergies as C- on climate policy engagement, indicating mixed or inconclusive engagement. Active engagement classification is not assessed in available records. This does not provide clear evidence of lobbying contradicting the claim. Confidence: 0.50. [InfluenceMap, https://influencemap.org/company-responses/]

### Assessment

The claim "More Energy, Less Emissions" receives a **GREENWASHING** verdict based on regulatory contradiction, substantiation failure under EU law, and a documented pattern of unsubstantiated environmental claims by this company.

**Regulatory Contradiction — ACM Enforcement (Lead Finding)**

The Dutch ACM enforcement action of 2021 directly contradicts the credibility of this claim. ACM explicitly named TotalEnergies in a greenwashing enforcement warning, finding that the company's "net-zero and sustainability claims lacked the required substantiation" under Dutch law. This is not an allegation or media criticism—it is a formal regulatory ruling by a competition and consumer authority. The current claim repeats the identical pattern: vague aspirational language ("More Energy, Less Emissions") without measurable commitments, baseline years, scope definitions, or methodologies. The company has a documented regulatory history of making unsubstantiated environmental claims, and this claim exhibits the same structural defects.

**Substantiation Failure Under Green Claims Directive**

The claim violates Article 3(3)(a) of the EU Green Claims Directive, which requires emissions reduction claims to state:
- Baseline year: *not stated*
- Scope (Scope 1/2/3): *not stated*
- Methodology: *not stated*
- Comparison period or target: *not stated*

The claim also implies that energy production is increasing ("More Energy") while emissions are falling ("Less Emissions"), but provides no figures to substantiate either assertion. This is a vague aspirational claim that cannot be falsified because it contains no measurable commitments—a classic greenwashing structure.

**Emissions Data: Directional Support with Critical Gaps**

E-PRTR regulated emissions data supports a long-term downward trend (30% reduction from 2007 to 2024), which provides some factual basis for the "less emissions" component. However:

1. **E-PRTR scope is limited**: E-PRTR covers only regulated industrial emissions to air; it does not capture TotalEnergies' full Scope 1 and Scope 3 emissions across global operations. The claim is global, but the supporting data is partial.

2. **Recent volatility undermines "less emissions" narrative**: The 2022 spike (+26% from 2021) contradicts the implication of steady downward progress.

3. **"More Energy" is unverified**: The claim asserts both increased energy production and decreased emissions, but E-PRTR data does not specify energy output. This assertion is unsubstantiated.

4. **EU ETS verified data unavailable**: The absence of EU ETS verified emissions data from EUTL is particularly significant for a major energy producer. Verified EUTL data is the legally mandated ground truth under EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC. The unavailability of this data prevents verification of the most authoritative emissions metric available under EU law.

**Historical Pattern Confirms Greenwashing, Not Isolated Incident**

TotalEnergies' prior three assessments by Prasine Index show an average greenwashing score of 63.7 (range: worst 67.0) with a deteriorating trend. Combined with the 2021 ACM enforcement finding, this establishes a documented pattern: the company repeatedly makes vague environmental claims without rigorous substantiation. This new claim is not an isolated error but consistent with the company's historical practice.

**Mitigating Evidence: Limited and Qualified**

CA100+ rates TotalEnergies as having "credible net-zero alignment" with "Partial" capex and short-term target alignment (2025). This provides directional support for the company's climate strategy, but investor confidence is moderate (0.65) and qualified by noted gaps. The long-term E-PRTR trend (30% reduction) is also directionally supportive, but E-PRTR scope is limited to regulated air emissions and does not constitute comprehensive GHG accounting.

### Key Finding

The Dutch ACM formally ruled in 2021 that TotalEnergies' environmental claims "lacked the required substantiation," and the current claim "More Energy, Less Emissions" repeats the identical pattern of vagueness—no baseline year, scope, methodology, or measurable targets. Coupled with this company's documented regulatory history of unsubstantiated environmental claims, this constitutes greenwashing.

InfluenceMap provides only a C- score with no specific active engagement assessment. This limits assessment of lobbying alignment but does not contradict the greenwashing finding.

3. **Energy Output Data — Unavailable**: The claim asserts "More Energy" but provides no figures for energy production or output. E-PRTR data does not track energy production. This assertion remains unverified and unsubstantiated.

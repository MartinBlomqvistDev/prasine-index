## Enel SpA — Greenwashing Assessment
**Verdict: LIKELY_GREENWASHING** | Score: 65.0/100 (range: 56–72) | Confidence: 70%
*Published: 2026-06-29 | Prasine Index | Trace ID: 3475a029-e0d8-4767-9ff9-fd38cf9d6529*

### The Claim
> "At the heart of Enel's commitment is the zero-emissions ambition for 2040. This is according to a roadmap that's in line with the Paris goals to limit the average global temperature increase to below 1.5°C compared to pre-industrial levels and with targets certified by the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi), which covers both direct emissions generated by the Group's plants and indirect emissions produced upstream by suppliers and downstream by customers."

*Source: Investor Relations page, https://www.enel.com/investors/sustainability/strategy-sustainable-progress/sustainability-plan, retrieved 2026-06-29*

### Evidence

**Contradicting the claim:**

- Enel is listed as **actively expanding oil and gas capacity**, with confirmed LNG expansion (upstream expansion: no; LNG expansion: yes). The list is used by 400+ financial institutions (GFANZ, PAII) as the standard oil and gas screen. *Urgewald Global Oil and Gas Exit List (GOGEL), n.d., https://gogel.org/*
- Enel holds 2 European gas assets, with 1 asset in active development: the **Porto Empedocle LNG Terminal** (Italy, Proposed). European gas infrastructure locks in 40+ years of fossil fuel demand. *Europe Gas Tracker (EGT), 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/europe-gas-tracker/*
- Enel holds **12 oil and gas extraction fields across 4 countries**; 11 operating and 1 pre-FID (Tihalatine South Oil and Gas Field, Algeria). *Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker (GOGET), 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/*
- The claim "lacks substantive climate data: it does not disclose an explicit baseline year, scope 1/2/3 absolute emissions figures, interim reduction targets for 2030/2035/2040, any distinction between emissions abatement and certified permanent carbon removals, or details of a verified transition plan." It does not meet EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) net-zero substantiation requirements. *Source document (self-reported disclosure), 2023, https://www.enel.com/investors/sustainability/strategy-sustainable-progress/sustainability-plan*

**Supporting the claim:**

- SBTi target status: **status = Targets set; temperature classification = 1.5°C; net-zero target = Targets set.** *Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi), n.d., https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*
- TPI assessment (Electricity Utilities, Italy): **Management Quality Level 4 (Strategic); Carbon Performance 2025 = 1.5°C; 2035 = 1.5°C; 2050 = Below 2°C.** TPI assessments are used by 150+ investors ($80tn AUM). *Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI), 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*
- Coal fleet: 44 units across 8 countries, of which only **7 are operating (863 MW)** and **31 are retiring or cancelled.** *Global Coal Plant Tracker (GCPT), 2026, https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-coal-plant-tracker/*
- Regulated industrial emissions to air fell 72% from 2007 to 2014 (931,074.2 → 262,322.0 tonnes); most recent year (2014): 262,322.0 tonnes. *E-PRTR, 2014, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/*

**Regulatory and lobbying context:**

- Enel is actively registered as an EU lobbyist (reg. no. 6256831207-27, HQ Italy); registration alone does not indicate the direction of lobbying. *EU Transparency Register, n.d., https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do*
- LobbyMap climate policy engagement score: **B-**, indicating neutral or mixed engagement — inconclusive for greenwashing assessment. *LobbyMap, n.d., https://lobbymap.org/*
- The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, blacklists generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan; residual emissions must be addressed by certified carbon removals, not offsets. *EUR-Lex [32024L0825], 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*
- The CSRD (2022/2464) requires disclosure of Scope 1, 2 and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1; a net-zero claim absent from or contradicting mandatory disclosure constitutes a material inconsistency. *EUR-Lex [32022L2464], 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464*

### Assessment

Enel's "zero-emissions ambition for 2040" is contradicted by its verified, ongoing fossil-gas and oil expansion. The Global Oil and Gas Exit List records Enel as actively expanding oil and gas capacity with confirmed LNG expansion, and the Europe Gas Tracker confirms the Porto Empedocle LNG Terminal is in active development — infrastructure that locks in decades of fossil fuel demand. The Global Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker documents 12 extraction fields, 11 operating and one pre-FID field in Algeria. A forward commitment to "zero-emissions" by 2040, asserted as Paris 1.5°C-aligned, is inconsistent with this current expansion trajectory. The decisive contradicting factor is the pursuit of new LNG capacity while claiming net-zero alignment.

This contradiction is compounded by a substantiation failure. The source document fails EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) requirements: it discloses no baseline year, no absolute Scope 1/2/3 figures, no interim 2030/2035 targets, and no distinction between emissions abatement and certified permanent carbon removals.

These findings are weighed against substantial mitigating evidence. SBTi records 1.5°C-classified targets and a net-zero target as "Targets set." TPI rates Enel's management quality "Strategic" (Level 4) and its near-term carbon performance pathway as 1.5°C-compatible. The Global Coal Plant Tracker shows a genuine coal phase-out, with 31 of 44 units retiring or cancelled.

No CONFIRMED greenwashing trigger is cleanly met. LobbyMap returned a B- (neutral/mixed) score, so no lobbying-obstruction trigger applies. There is no prior regulatory ruling against Enel's claims. The accumulated-weight trigger is not met because substantial mitigating evidence exists (SBTi at 0.90, E-PRTR and TPI at 0.75). Enel's core business is electricity generation transitioning toward renewables rather than fossil-fuel extraction, so the irreconcilable core-business contradiction is not met. The claim is therefore capped below the CONFIRMED threshold. This is not a repeat claim.

### Key Finding

Enel's "zero-emissions ambition for 2040," asserted as 1.5°C-aligned, is directly contradicted by verified, active oil-and-gas and LNG expansion — including the Porto Empedocle LNG Terminal and a pre-FID extraction field in Algeria — while the underlying disclosure fails EU EmpCo Directive substantiation requirements (no baseline, no interim targets, no abatement/removal split). Set against active SBTi 1.5°C targets and a documented coal phase-out, the result is LIKELY_GREENWASHING rather than confirmed.

- **SBTi validation status:** SBTi status is "Targets set" rather than fully "validated/approved net-zero," introducing ambiguity reflected in the score range.
- **Lobbying direction:** LobbyMap returned a B- (mixed/inconclusive) score, and the EU Transparency Register confirms registration but not direction; the direction of Enel's climate lobbying could not be determined.

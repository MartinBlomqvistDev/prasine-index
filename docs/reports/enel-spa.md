Year-on-year trajectory 2020–2024: 15.00 Mt → 16.71 Mt → 14.62 Mt → 11.84 Mt → 8.07 Mt. Demonstrates consistent, long-term downward emissions reduction consistent with renewable energy deployment as transition mechanism. URL: https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

### Assessment

Enel's statement is a directional assertion about the role of renewable electricity in energy transition, not a specific quantified commitment about company emissions or renewable capacity. This limits greenwashing severity compared to false assertions of carbon neutrality or net-zero status.

However, the claim is **materially contradicted by verified evidence of active coal expansion**. The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 90%) documents two concurrent expansions: 2.4 GW of new coal power capacity and 15 Mtpa of coal mining capacity. A company simultaneously claiming that "renewable electricity is the most efficient solution for energy transition" while expanding coal capacity cannot credibly hold both positions. This is not aspirational misalignment — it is documented, forward-looking strategic contradiction.

The E-PRTR data (confidence 75%) provides genuine mitigating evidence: Enel has achieved 75% historical emissions reduction from 2007 to 2024, with consistent year-on-year declines across the five most recent years. CA100+ validation (confidence 75%) confirms institutional assessment of net-zero alignment. These findings prevent classification as "CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING," which would require clearer evidence of core business contradiction or active regulatory misalignment.

However, the coal expansion is irreconcilable. A company cannot deploy renewable electricity at scale (as E-PRTR reduction shows) while simultaneously expanding coal mining and coal generation (as GCEL verifies). The claim's generality — "represents the most efficient solution" rather than "we are implementing this solution" — does not excuse the strategic contradiction embodied in active coal expansion.

The score of 72 reflects:
- Strong contradicting evidence (coal expansion, conf. 90%) pushes toward CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING territory
- Substantive supporting evidence (E-PRTR 75% reduction, CA100+ validation, conf. 75%) provides mitigation
- Claim is aspirational/general rather than false assertion → prevents highest greenwashing classification
- Forward coal expansion directly contradicts energy transition narrative → prevents higher credibility scores

This assessment scores across five dimensions:
- **Emissions Accuracy (15/100):** Historical reduction is strong; forward coal expansion creates material contradiction.
- **Claim Substantiation (65/100):** Claim lacks specificity (no percentage, timeline, or baseline); Green Claims Directive requires such detail but claim does not provide it.
- **Historical Consistency (20/100):** No prior equivalent claims detected; E-PRTR shows consistent emissions decline but forward-looking coal expansion is inconsistent with transition narrative.
- **Lobbying Alignment (55/100):** InfluenceMap data inconclusive (B- rating); no EU Transparency Register record found.
- **Target Credibility (70/100):** No SBTi-validated net-zero target on file; CA100+ capex alignment is partial; coal expansion contradicts any credible 1.5°C pathway.

### Key Finding

Enel cannot credibly claim that "renewable electricity is the most efficient solution for driving the energy transition" while simultaneously expanding coal capacity by 2.4 GW and coal mining by 15 Mtpa, as verified by the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 90%). This forward-looking strategic contradiction directly undermines the claim's credibility, despite genuine supporting evidence of 75% historical emissions reduction (E-PRTR, 2007–2024).

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data:** No Enel installation IDs registered in EU ETS database. This does not indicate absence of emissions — Enel likely operates under different registry or outside EU ETS perimeter. E-PRTR data (which covers all regulated industrial emissions) partially fills this gap.

2. **SBTi Validation Status:** SBTi target status marked "unknown" with confidence 50%. Cannot verify whether Enel maintains validated science-based net-zero pathway. This affects TARGET_CREDIBILITY scoring but does not contradict the claim.

3. **Lobbying Registry Coverage:** Enel not found in EU Transparency Register. InfluenceMap score (B-, confidence 50%) is only available source for policy engagement assessment. Full lobbying contradiction assessment is incomplete.

4. **Renewable Capacity Baseline:** Claim does not specify percentage of Enel's electricity generation currently sourced from renewables, nor does the assessment identify published capacity data for comparison against the Green Claims Directive substantiation standard.

These gaps reduce confidence from potential 85% to confirmed 78%, but do not alter the primary finding: coal expansion evidence (confidence 90%) is sufficient to establish material contradiction.

## Assessment

### Core Contradiction: Genuine Emissions Decline vs. Active Fossil Expansion

Enel's claim that "renewable sources represent the most efficient and cost-effective solution for driving the energy transition" is contradicted by institutional-standard evidence of simultaneous fossil fuel expansion across three fuel types:

- **Coal**: GCEL (used by 400+ financial institutions as the authoritative coal screen) lists Enel as actively expanding coal mining (15 Mtpa) and coal power (2.4 GW). This is a binary fail condition under GFANZ climate alignment standards.
- **Gas**: Europe Gas Tracker documents active LNG terminal development (Porto Empedocle), which locks in multi-decade fossil fuel demand.
- **Oil**: GOGET records 12 active oil and gas extraction fields.

However, this contradiction occurs **alongside documented genuine emissions decline**. E-PRTR verified data shows absolute regulated emissions fell 75% from 2007 to 2024. GCPT confirms 70% of coal units (31 of 44) have been retired or cancelled. Both CA100+ and TPI rate the company as net-zero aligned and Paris-compatible.

### Why This Is Greenwashing, Not Confirmed Greenwashing

The claim reaches **GREENWASHING** (72/100) rather than **CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING** (81–100) for two reasons:

1. **No documented anti-climate lobbying**: InfluenceMap rates Enel B- (mixed/neutral), not adversarial. The EU Transparency Register confirms lobbying activity but provides no evidence of opposition to climate legislation. A confirmed finding of lobbying *against* climate policy would push the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

2. **Material but not irreconcilable contradiction**: Enel is not a pure coal or fossil company expanding its core business. It is an energy utility with a mixed renewable–fossil asset base undergoing genuine historical transition. Absolute emissions are declining; most coal units are being retired; institutional investors assess the company as Paris-aligned. The active expansion of specific fossil assets (coal mining, LNG) contradicts the claim's implication of renewable-led transition, but does not negate the historical emissions decline.

### Substantiation Gap Under Green Claims Directive

The claim makes no specific commitment: no renewable energy percentage target, no timeline, no baseline. Under EU Green Claims Directive Article 3, claims of "most efficient and cost-effective solution" must be substantiated with quantified evidence. The claim lacks measurable commitments and therefore fails substantiation requirements.

## Key Finding

Enel SpA has achieved a documented 75% reduction in absolute regulated emissions from 2007 to 2024 (E-PRTR, 2024), and institutional investors (CA100+, TPI) assess it as Paris-aligned. However, the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 90%, institutional standard for 400+ financial institutions) records Enel as **actively expanding coal mining and coal power capacity**, while the Europe Gas Tracker documents ongoing LNG terminal development. This creates a material contradiction: the company claims renewable energy is "the solution" to energy transition while simultaneously expanding coal extraction and gas infrastructure, signalling that the energy transition narrative is aspirational rather than operationally aligned.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data**: Enel SpA is not directly registered in the EU ETS registry. Emissions are likely consolidated at subsidiary level, preventing cross-check with the regulated ETS baseline. This reduces ability to verify scope and scale of covered emissions against independent regulatory baseline.

2. **Lobbying Direction**: EU Transparency Register confirms Enel is an active lobbyist but does not specify policy positions. InfluenceMap provides only a B- (inconclusive) rating. Unable to confirm or refute whether Enel is actively lobbying against climate legislation — a factor that would definitively move the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

3. **SBTi Validation**: No validated Science Based Targets initiative submission. Absence of third-party emissions target validation reduces confidence in medium-term decarbonisation pathway credibility.

These gaps reduce confidence by 5–10 percentage points but do not prevent a GREENWASHING verdict given the high-confidence institutional evidence from GCEL (90%) and E-PRTR (75%).

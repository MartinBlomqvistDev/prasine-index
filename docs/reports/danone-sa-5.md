### Assessment

Danone's claim is **specific and quantified** (30.3% reduction, 2030 target date, 2020 baseline, Scope 3 FLAG defined). However, it is **not independently verifiable** through publicly accessible, audited sources and **lacks the substantiation mechanisms required by the Green Claims Directive**.

**Substantiation Failures:**

1. **No independent baseline audit**: The company's web page provides no linked methodology document, third-party verification statement, or audited baseline report for the 2020 Scope 3 FLAG baseline. The Green Claims Directive Article 3(1) requires claims to be substantiated by "verifiable, reliable and representative data." A 2020 baseline self-declared by Danone without independent audit does not meet this standard.

2. **No published measurement methodology**: Scope 3 FLAG emissions are complex to measure (requires supply chain data from dairy farmers, ingredient suppliers, and land-use accounting). Danone does not disclose how it defines or measures "FLAG emissions" in the claim, making external verification impossible.

3. **No SBTi validation**: SBTi Target Database shows no validated target for Danone. If this 2030 reduction claim were credible and 1.5°C-aligned, SBTi validation would be expected and publicly verifiable. The absence of validation weakens credibility.

4. **No TPI climate pathway assessment**: TPI has not assessed a climate pathway for Danone, indicating the company has not provided institutional investors with sufficient credible, verifiable transition data to enable independent assessment against climate science.

5. **No interim progress disclosure**: Danone provides no audited progress data for 2021–2024 to validate whether the claimed trajectory toward a 2030 target is on track. This prevents independent validation.

**Mitigating Factors:**

- CA100+ assessment (confidence 0.65) provides directional support for the company's net-zero ambition, preventing a higher greenwashing score. However, CA100+ support does not substitute for the independent baseline audit, methodology disclosure, and SBTi validation required by the Green Claims Directive.
- No verified source directly contradicts the claim (e.g., showing Scope 3 FLAG emissions increased or baseline was inflated). The verdict is MISLEADING rather than GREENWASHING because the claim lacks substantiation rather than being affirmatively false.

**Pattern Analysis:**

This claim exemplifies the MISLEADING category (46–60/100): a specific, quantified target that exaggerates credibility through precision and apparent ambition while omitting the independent verification, third-party baseline audit, and published methodology that the Green Claims Directive mandates. Danone's 27 prior assessments show a consistent historical pattern (average score 47.8, range 40–58) of moderate misleading claims lacking full substantiation, consistent with this score.

### Key Finding

Danone claims a specific 30.3% Scope 3 FLAG reduction by 2030 but provides no independent audit of the 2020 baseline, no published measurement methodology, and no SBTi validation or TPI pathway assessment—all mechanisms the Green Claims Directive requires to substantiate emissions reduction claims. The company's web page contains no link to verification documentation, methodology, or audited baseline, making the claim unverifiable by external parties including journalists, regulators, and investors.

---

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS verified emissions data**: Danone SA has no registered EU ETS installations. This means the company's Scope 1/2 baseline and reductions are not verified under mandatory EU law. For a company claiming major Scope 3 reductions without concurrent Scope 1/2 verified data, this creates a material substantiation gap. Cannot be resolved without Danone's voluntary submission to third-party GHG verification.

2. **Independent baseline verification**: No third-party audit report or verification statement for the 2020 Scope 3 FLAG baseline is publicly available or linked from the claim source. Cannot be resolved without Danone publishing or submitting the baseline audit to an independent verifier.

3. **SBTi target validation**: SBTi database shows no validated target for this claim. Cannot be resolved without Danone submitting the target to SBTi for science-based validation.

4. **TPI climate pathway assessment**: TPI has no assessed climate pathway for Danone. Cannot be resolved without Danone providing transition data to TPI.

5. **Interim progress data**: No audited Scope 3 FLAG reduction figures disclosed for 2021–2024. Cannot be verified without Danone publishing annual audited Scope 3 data.

6. **E-PRTR trend data**: Only 2008 E-PRTR data available (188 tonnes). Too old and limited to assess current baseline. Not directly relevant to Scope 3 claim, but indicates limited historical verified emissions data in public domain.

**Impact on Confidence:** These gaps do not reduce confidence in the MISLEADING verdict; rather, they reinforce it. The absence of these standard verification mechanisms is itself a substantiation failure under the Green Claims Directive. Confidence is 62% (not higher) only because CA100+ provides partial mitigating evidence; without that, the verdict would be higher confidence.

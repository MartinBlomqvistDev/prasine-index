## The Claim

> "We support the goals of the Paris Agreement to limit the increase in the global average temperature to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels. Beyond supporting the energy transition overall through the supply of metals, we take a holistic approach to decarbonisation, focusing on reducing our combined industrial Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions."

*Source: Corporate website, https://www.glencore.com/sustainability/esg-a-z, accessed 2026-05-06*

The GCEL is the institutional standard used by 400+ financial institutions (GFANZ, PAII) for coal-screen divestment decisions. Any company listed as actively expanding coal capacity fails to meet Paris Agreement alignment criteria (well-below-2°C or 1.5°C pathways). *Source: COAL_EXIT, Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list*

**[2] Obstructive Climate Lobbying**
InfluenceMap assigns Glencore an engagement score of D+, indicating "active opposition to or delay of climate legislation." This classification means the company simultaneously makes Paris Agreement support claims while actively lobbying against climate policy. EU Transparency Register confirms Glencore is actively registered as a corporate EU lobbyist (registration no. 869729025581-78). *Source: INFLUENCE_MAP, InfluenceMap climate policy engagement score, https://influencemap.org/company-responses; EU_TRANSPARENCY_REGISTER, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do*

**[3] Regulatory Enforcement Flagging**
UK Competition and Markets Authority (2023) placed Glencore under investigation as part of its inquiry into green claims by fossil fuel producers. The CMA specifically flagged "Glencore's net-zero commitment while continuing coal mining expansion" as potentially misleading under consumer protection law. The CMA notes Glencore as "the world's largest coal exporter." *Source: ENFORCEMENT, CMA investigation (UK), 2023, https://www.gov.uk/cma-cases/greenwashing-claims*

**[4] Claim Substantiation Failures**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166, Article 3) requires that emissions reduction claims state a baseline year, specify scope (Scope 1/2/3), and disclose methodology. The claim omits all three. Article 5 prohibits intensity-based reductions that obscure absolute emission increases. The claim describes a "holistic approach to decarbonisation" without specifying timebound, measurable targets or distinguishing between absolute and intensity-based reductions. *Source: EUR_LEX, [52023PC0166] Green Claims Directive proposal, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**[5] Supporting Evidence: Investor Engagement Benchmark**
CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark (2025) assigns Glencore "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" with "Capex Alignment = Partial." CA100+ uses investor engagement frameworks and self-reported company data. The "Partial" capex alignment explicitly acknowledges incomplete alignment with net-zero trajectories. *Source: CA100, CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/*

**[6] Data Gap: EU ETS Verified Emissions**
No EU ETS installation IDs are registered for Glencore plc in the EUTL database. Verified emissions data under Regulation 601/2012 represents the highest-quality legal ground truth for GHG emissions disclosure. This absence prevents direct verification of claimed emissions reductions via mandatory regulatory reporting. ## Assessment

Glencore's claim of Paris Agreement alignment and "holistic" emissions reductions is contradicted by three institutional sources of high evidential weight:

**Primary Contradiction: Coal Expansion**
The Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 0.90) documents Glencore as actively expanding coal mining capacity. This is a forward-looking, documented contradiction of any credible claim to support "well-below-2°C" warming limits. The IPCC AR6 assessment models no new coal mine development in 1.5°C or well-below-2°C scenarios. Glencore's status as the world's largest coal exporter, combined with documented capacity expansion, is inherently incompatible with the stated Paris commitment.

**Secondary Confirmation: Obstructive Lobbying**
InfluenceMap's D+ engagement score (confidence 0.85) indicates Glencore actively opposes or delays climate legislation. This is a canonical greenwashing signal per Prasine Index methodology: the company makes climate commitments in public corporate communications while simultaneously using its lobbying capacity to obstruct the policy framework necessary to achieve those commitments. EU Transparency Register confirmation (confidence 0.75) validates active lobbying presence.

**Tertiary Confirmation: Regulatory Enforcement**
The UK CMA's 2023 investigation specifically flagged Glencore's coal-expansion contradiction as "potentially misleading" under consumer protection law (confidence 0.70). This represents institutional validation that the claim meets the legal definition of greenwashing in the UK market.

**Substantiation Failures**
The claim fails the prospective Green Claims Directive standard: it provides no baseline year, methodology, or clarity on absolute vs. intensity-based reductions. Phrases like "take a holistic approach" are aspirational, not measurable. The claim cannot be independently verified without these disclosures.

**Single Supporting Source Cannot Counter Multiple Verified Contradictions**
CA100+ assigns "Partial capex alignment" (confidence 0.65), acknowledging incomplete net-zero trajectory alignment. One supporting source (using investor engagement data and self-reported company submissions) cannot credibly override two high-confidence regulatory/institutional contradictions (COAL_EXIT 0.90, INFLUENCE_MAP 0.85) and one CMA enforcement action (0.70).

**Data Gap Does Not Rescue the Claim**
The absence of EU ETS verified emissions data prevents quantitative confirmation of the claim via the highest-quality regulatory source. However, COAL_EXIT and CMA enforcement provide sufficient verified contradiction independent of EU ETS data. The data gap reduces confidence from 90%+ to 87%, but does not change the verdict.

The company simultaneously makes Paris commitments while actively expanding coal capacity and lobbying against climate legislation.

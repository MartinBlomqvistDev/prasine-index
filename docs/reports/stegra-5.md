**[2] EU ETS Verified Emissions Data (EUTL)**
No installation IDs for Stegra were found in the EU Emissions Trading System registry. This indicates either: (a) Stegra operates below the EU ETS threshold (20 MW thermal input); (b) Stegra is not a direct industrial operator subject to EU ETS (e.g., a hydrogen producer or service provider, not a steel mill operator); or (c) Stegra does not yet own or operate steel-production installations. For a claim about "decarbonizing hard-to-abate industries – starting with steel," the absence of verified baseline emissions data prevents independent verification of current emissions or future progress.
*EU ETS EUTL verified data, 2026, https://ec.europa.eu/clima/ets*

**[3] Green Claims Directive (2023/2427)**
Article 3(3)(a) requires substantiation of environmental improvement claims. Emissions reduction claims must explicitly state: (i) baseline year; (ii) scope (Scope 1, 2, or 3); and (iii) methodology for measurement. The claim makes a forward commitment ("will use green hydrogen to decarbonize") but provides none of these required elements.
*EUR-Lex [52023PC0166], COM/2023/0166, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

**[4] Steel Sector Emissions Baseline**
Steel production accounts for approximately 24–26% of EU industrial process emissions (direct emissions from blast furnaces and converters), consistent with Stegra's claim of "25% of Europe's industrial emissions." This sector-level fact is accurate but is used to substantiate a company-specific commitment lacking quantified targets or baselines.
*Eurostat & European Steel Association data, 2023–2024*

## Assessment

The claim contains two distinct assertions:
1. **Sector-level truth claim:** "Green hydrogen plays a key role in fighting climate change" — this is directionally consistent with IEA and IPCC scenarios and is not disputed.
2. **Company-specific commitment claim:** "At Stegra, we will use green hydrogen to decarbonize hard-to-abate industries – starting with steel" — this claim requires substantiation under the Green Claims Directive.

**Substantiation Failure**

Stegra's company-specific commitment violates Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a). The claim provides:
- No baseline year
- No quantified reduction target (absolute or intensity-based)
- No scope definition (Scope 1, 2, or 3)
- No implementation timeline or methodology
- No independent verification (SBTi, CA100+, or third-party audit)

**Verified Emissions Baseline**

The absence of Stegra from the EU ETS EUTL registry is a material data gap. Under mandatory EU reporting, any facility producing steel with capacity ≥20 MW thermal input must report verified emissions. Stegra's absence from EUTL suggests it does not currently operate steel-production capacity. If the company intends to decarbonize steel production, it must eventually acquire or build such capacity — at which point verified emissions data will become available. Until then, the company cannot demonstrate a baseline against which to measure claimed reductions. This is not proof the claim is false, but it prevents verification under the highest legal standard (verified emissions data).

**Exaggeration Through Omission**

The claim conflates sector-level opportunity ("steel accounts for 25% of Europe's industrial emissions") with company-specific commitment ("we will use green hydrogen to decarbonize"). The sector fact is accurate and relevant, but it is used to manufacture credibility for a commitment that lacks measurable targets. This is exaggeration by association: positioning a vague future aspiration as a substantiated commitment.

**Score Explanation**

The claim scores 46/100 (MISLEADING) because:
- **CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION (55/100):** Forward commitment uses aspirational language ("will use") without measurable targets or timelines, violating Green Claims Directive substantiation requirements.
- **EMISSIONS_ACCURACY (40/100):** No verified baseline emissions data available. The company cannot demonstrate accuracy without EU ETS registration or alternative third-party verified emissions reporting.
- **TARGET_CREDIBILITY (50/100):** No specific target or deadline stated. Credibility against 1.5°C pathways cannot be assessed.

The claim is not rated CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100) because no verified data directly contradicts the claim, and Stegra appears to be a legitimate hydrogen/decarbonization company, not a fossil fuel operator making false retrospective claims.

The claim is not rated INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE (21–45) because the substantiation failure is material and deliberate: a company with no registered industrial emissions is claiming to decarbonize an industry without stating a baseline, target, or scope, in direct violation of the Green Claims Directive.

## Key Finding

Stegra's commitment to use green hydrogen to decarbonize steel production fails to meet Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) substantiation requirements: no baseline year, quantified target, scope, or methodology is provided. The company's absence from the EU ETS EUTL registry prevents independent verification of current emissions or future progress, making this claim unverifiable under the highest legal standard for corporate emissions data.

## Data Gaps

**EU ETS EUTL Registry**
Stegra has no registered installations. This is the critical substantiation gap. For a claim about "decarbonizing hard-to-abate industries – starting with steel," absence of verified baseline emissions data prevents independent verification of progress. This does not disprove the claim, but it means the company cannot currently satisfy the Green Claims Directive's requirement for "substantiation" (which implies verifiable, measurable data). Confidence is moderated to 68% due to this data gap; if Stegra publishes an SBTi-validated science-based target or third-party verified baseline, assessment should be revisited.

**InfluenceMap Lobbying Alignment**
No lobbying position intelligence available. Stegra is registered in the EU Transparency Register as an active lobbyist, but InfluenceMap returned no data on the company's positions on climate, energy, or industrial policy. If Stegra lobbies against EU ETS expansion or Green Claims Directive enforcement, this would be a material contradiction to its stated commitment. This gap prevents full assessment of LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT; confidence is accordingly reduced.

**[2] EUR-LEX [52023PC0166] — Green Claims Directive | 2023**
The Green Claims Directive proposal (COM/2023/0166) establishes substantiation standards for renewable energy claims. Article 3 requires that renewable energy percentages must specify the reference baseline, measurement methodology, the portion of total energy consumption covered, the source of renewable electricity, and whether renewable energy certificates (RECs) or guarantees of origin (GOs) are used. Claims lacking an audited baseline or verification pathway may be classified as potentially misleading.

### Assessment

This claim is a forward-looking regional renewable energy commitment with a 2035 target date. It is aspirational in framing—"should come" (*ska komma*) rather than "will be"—and makes no assertion about current performance or emissions reductions.

**Substantiation Status (Score: 35/100)**

The claim fails four of five substantiation tests required under the Green Claims Directive:

1. **No disclosed baseline:** The claim does not state what percentage of electricity in the Öresund region currently comes from local solar. Without a baseline, the ambition level cannot be verified and the trajectory cannot be audited.

2. **No measurement methodology:** The claim does not specify how "local solar cells" will be measured, accounted for, or verified. Nor does it clarify whether the 10% refers to installed capacity, energy output, or final consumption; how regional boundaries are defined; or whether renewable energy certificates will be used.

3. **Attribution ambiguity:** "Our region" is not formally bounded. The Öresund region spans Sweden and Denmark. It is unclear whether Öresundskraft claims direct delivery responsibility or reliance on regional grid operators or policy drivers outside its control.

4. **No interim milestones or binding mechanism:** The claim specifies only an end-date target with no intermediate checkpoints, investment commitments, or disclosed governance mechanism to ensure delivery.

The claim satisfies only the first test: it does specify a single percentage and end date, meeting minimal clarity requirements.

**Emissions Accuracy (Score: 50/100)**

This dimension is not directly assessable. Öresundskraft is not registered in the EU ETS (no installation IDs found); the company operates below the direct emissions reporting threshold or is unregulated under the scheme. The claim concerns regional solar deployment, not the company's direct GHG footprint. No baseline emissions pathway or interim reduction trajectory is disclosed. No verified data contradicts the solar target; the absence of baseline data indicates a substantiation gap, not falsity.

Score of 50 reflects neutrality—neither supporting nor contradicting evidence is available.

**Target Credibility (Score: 25/100)**

*Strengths:*
- The 2035 date is a recognized interim milestone for EU climate ambition, aligned with the intermediate phases of the Fit for 55 framework.
- Solar deployment is a physically and technically realistic pathway for regional energy utilities in Nordic climates.
- A 10% regional solar penetration target is achievable for the Öresund grid by 2035, based on comparable regional deployments in Denmark and southern Sweden.

*Weaknesses:*
- No reference to science-based climate targets, the EU Red IV directive, or the Paris Agreement. No comparison to sector benchmarks or alignment with official Swedish energy policy.
- The 10% target is not contextualized: Is it ambitious, baseline, or conservative for Öresund by 2035? No justification provided.
- Permissive framing ("should come") rather than binding commitment language reduces credibility as a material corporate pledge.
- No disclosure of what happens if the target is missed, or any accountability mechanism.

**Lobbying Alignment**

Öresundskraft is confirmed to be an active EU lobbyist (registration no. 883228750982-76). However, the EU Transparency Register does not disclose the company's substantive lobbying positions on climate, renewable energy, or solar policy. Without access to detailed lobbying stances, no contradiction between the solar claim and disclosed lobbying positions can be identified. This remains a data gap; no evidence of conflict is available.

Öresundskraft's 2035 local solar target lacks the baseline data, measurement methodology, and binding governance required under the Green Claims Directive—making it unverifiable. However, no independent data contradicts the claim's plausibility; the substantiation gap does not constitute evidence of falsity.

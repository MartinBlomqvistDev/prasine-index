# RWE AG — Greenwashing Assessment

**Verdict: GREENWASHING** | Score: 64/100 | Confidence: 68%
*Published: 2026-04-13 | Prasine Index | Trace ID: 13793a81-e9b2-41dc-9ebf-a342ea6ce55f*

---

### The Claim

> "We are investing EUR 50 billion in renewable energies, hydrogen and batteries by 2030."

*Source: Investor Relations webpage, https://www.rwe.com/en/the-group/our-strategy/, 2026*

---

### Evidence

**1. Coal Operations Remain Substantial (Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, 2025)**
RWE AG operates 44 Mtpa of coal mining capacity and 5.8 GW of coal power generation, according to the Global Coal Exit List. The company is classified as a major coal operator. Expansion status is unclear, but coal represents a significant proportion of the company's generation and extraction portfolio.

**2. Verified Emissions Reductions Show Genuine Progress (E-PRTR, 2024)**
E-PRTR regulated industrial emissions data shows emissions of 64.7 Mt in 2024, down from 94.7 Mt in 2020 (32% reduction in four years). Over the longer term (2007–2024), emissions fell 47% from 122.0 Mt to 64.7 Mt. This demonstrates substantial and verifiable progress in absolute emissions reductions.

**3. Institutional Recognition of Climate Ambition, But Qualified (CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark, 2025)**
CA100+ rates RWE as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes," "Capex Alignment = Partial," and "Short-term target = Partial." This indicates investor recognition of climate strategy, but the "Partial" qualifications signal that the transition pathway is incomplete or under-weighted relative to stated ambition.

**4. Green Claims Directive Compliance Gap (EUR-Lex COM/2023/0166, 2023)**
The Green Claims Directive proposal establishes that renewable energy claims must specify: (a) the percentage of total energy consumption covered, (b) the source, and (c) whether renewable energy certificates are used. The claim as stated—"investing EUR 50 billion in renewable energies, hydrogen and batteries by 2030"—provides none of these qualifiers. It states a financial commitment and timescale but gives no baseline, no target renewable energy percentage of total generation mix, and no clarity on whether investments produce physical renewable supply or merely financial asset acquisition.

**5. No Validated Science-Based Emissions Target (SBTi, status unknown)**
RWE holds no SBTi-validated science-based target and is not classified as net-zero by the Science Based Targets initiative. This creates a substantiation gap: the company lacks external validation of its emissions reduction pathway or alignment with 1.5°C pathways.

**6. Climate Policy Engagement: Inconclusive (InfluenceMap, 2025)**
InfluenceMap rates RWE as "C" for climate policy engagement (neutral/mixed). No evidence of active climate legislation obstruction was found in EU Transparency Register searches, so the company does not meet criteria for "Confirmed Greenwashing" classification based on lobbying contradiction.

---

### Assessment

RWE's claim is materially misleading because it presents an investment commitment in new renewable capacity while obscuring the company's continued major coal operations and the absence of a coal exit timeline.

**The contradiction:** A company operating 5.8 GW of coal power and extracting 44 Mtpa of coal cannot credibly claim to be executing a "renewable energy transition" without explicitly committing to coal phase-out. The EUR 50 billion investment target is a capex figure, not a binding emissions reduction target. It does not state what percentage of RWE's total energy generation will come from these investments by 2030, nor does it commit to retiring coal assets.

**Substantiation failure:** The claim breaches the Green Claims Directive standard (COM/2023/0166). It provides no baseline emissions or energy mix, no percentage target for renewable energy in total generation, and no transparency on certificate sourcing. A renewable energy investment claim that does not quantify the renewable energy share of total output is selective disclosure—it highlights new capacity while downplaying continued fossil fuel exposure.

**Mitigating factors:** E-PRTR data (confidence 0.75) confirms genuine historical emissions progress: 47% reduction since 2007 and 32% in the past four years. CA100+ recognizes climate ambition, albeit with partial capex and target alignment. These prevent classification as "Confirmed Greenwashing" (81–100 band), which would require either falsified data or clear policy obstruction.

**Trend concern:** RWE's prior assessment score was 62 (deteriorating trend). The company has made similar investment and transition claims in the past without demonstrating corresponding coal exit progress or renewable energy percentage targets. This repeat pattern of aspiration without specificity strengthens the greenwashing verdict.

---

### Key Finding

RWE AG's EUR 50 billion investment claim is greenwashing because it omits a coal exit timeline and renewable energy percentage target, and therefore misrepresents the pace and scope of the company's energy transition while it continues to operate 5.8 GW of coal capacity. The claim meets Green Claims Directive standards for misleading renewable energy claims due to lack of baseline, renewable energy share, and certificate sourcing disclosure.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation-Level Data (Not Retrieved)**
Prasine Index could not query EU ETS EUTL verified emissions data by installation ID. E-PRTR aggregated data substitutes for this, but EUTL would provide higher granularity (power plant and facility level). This reduction in source precision reduces confidence from 0.85+ to 0.68.

**SBTi and InfluenceMap Coverage (Partial/Inconclusive)**
SBTi status is unknown (not listed as target-setter or net-zero classified). InfluenceMap score is "C" (inconclusive). Neither source provides sufficient signal to confirm or rule out active policy obstruction. Absence of evidence of lobbying against climate legislation is not evidence of absence.

**COAL_EXIT Expansion Status (Unclear)**
The Urgewald list does not state whether RWE is actively expanding coal capacity or closing mines. This ambiguity prevents the coal contradiction from reaching 0.75+ confidence. A definitive statement of coal expansion plans would strengthen the greenwashing verdict to 75+/100.

**Capex Allocation by Technology (Not Published in Claim)**
RWE's EUR 50 billion investment target does not specify the allocation between renewables, hydrogen, and batteries. Without this breakdown, the claim cannot be assessed for technical feasibility or alignment with grid decarbonisation needs. This gap makes independent verification of the claim's credibility impossible.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index scored this claim using six evidence categories: (1) operational fossil fuel exposure (COAL_EXIT), (2) verified emissions trends (E-PRTR), (3) investor-recognized climate strategy (CA100+), (4) regulatory compliance with Green Claims Directive (EUR-Lex), (5) external target validation (SBTi), and (6) climate policy engagement (InfluenceMap). The GREENWASHING band (61–80) is assigned when claims are contradicted by material operational data or fail substantiation tests, but do not meet the CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING threshold (81–100), which requires either falsified data, SBTi invalidation, or active policy obstruction. Confidence of 68% reflects reliance on E-PRTR data (0.75) and COAL_EXIT (0.65) in place of higher-granularity EU ETS data, and ambiguity in coal expansion status.

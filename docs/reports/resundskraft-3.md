## Evidence

**[1] Green Claims Directive (EUR-LEX 52023PC0166), Legislative Record, 2023**
The EU legislative proposal establishes substantiation requirements for renewable energy claims. Article 3 explicitly states that claims of renewable electricity relying on unbundled certificates (Renewable Energy Certificates or Guarantees of Origin) without disclosure of physical supply contracts or clear distinction from physical generation are "potentially misleading." The directive requires that claims specify: (a) the percentage of total energy consumption covered; (b) the energy source; and (c) whether RECs/GOs are used or physical supply is guaranteed.

## Assessment

Öresundskraft's claim that 10% of regional electricity will come from local solar cells by 2035 is specific and measurable—both positive indicators. However, it fails the substantiation test required under the Green Claims Directive for three material reasons:

**Substantiation Failure (Primary Finding)**

The claim does not disclose whether the 10% target is based on:
- Physical electricity supply contracts backed by owned or contracted solar generation capacity, or
- Unbundled renewable energy certificates (RECs) or Guarantees of Origin (GOs) with no direct link to actual power flows.

This distinction is legally material under EUR_LEX 52023PC0166, Article 3. Without explicit clarification, the claim creates ambiguity about whether Öresundskraft is committing to actual local solar infrastructure development or merely purchasing renewable certificates that may originate elsewhere. The company's source material provides no evidence of owned solar assets, infrastructure investment plans, power purchase agreements, or interim targets that would substantiate the commitment as a genuine operational transformation rather than a financial offset strategy.

**Baseline and Feasibility Assessment Missing**

The claim omits the current baseline of solar electricity in the region, making it impossible to assess ambition or verify progress. No interim milestones (2028, 2030) are specified. No detail is provided on whether the 10% is within Öresundskraft's direct operational control or depends on third parties (municipal authorities, private developers, customers).

**Regulatory Compliance Gap**

Under the Green Claims Directive (applicable to EU companies from 2026), Öresundskraft's claim as stated does not meet substantiation standards. The claim would require one of two formats to be compliant:
1. Explicit statement that the 10% is backed by physical generation capacity with named assets and timelines, or
2. Clear disclosure that RECs/GOs will be used, with explanation of additionality.

Neither is present in the quoted claim text.

**Historical Context**

This is not a repeat claim (0 prior identical claims detected). However, Öresundskraft's portfolio median score of 46.3 (MISLEADING) indicates a pattern of substantiation shortfalls across its sustainability communications. This claim is consistent with that pattern.

## Key Finding

Öresundskraft's 10% local solar target by 2035 lacks the disclosure required under the Green Claims Directive: it does not clarify whether the commitment is backed by physical generation infrastructure or renewable energy certificates, and provides no baseline, interim milestones, or evidence of operational control—making verification impossible and compliance non-certain.

## Data Gaps

Öresundskraft is registered as a regional electricity supplier/distributor, not as an EU ETS installation operator. No direct emissions data available under the EU Emissions Trading System. This gap does not contradict the claim but means the target cannot be benchmarked against regulated emissions baselines.

**Independent Verification Records (Not Located)**
No evidence found that the 10% target has been submitted to Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi), third-party audit, or regulatory certification. The company is not listed in EU Transparency Register for lobbying disclosures (typically applies to larger regulated entities; small regional suppliers often exempt).

**Baseline Data (Not Disclosed)**
Current percentage of local solar in Öresundskraft's regional energy mix: not stated in source material or publicly available sources queried. This prevents assessment of ambition level or interim progress measurement.

**Infrastructure Investment Plans (Not Disclosed)**
No evidence located of owned solar capacity, contracted development pipelines, or power purchase agreements that would substantiate the 10% claim as operationally grounded.

These gaps lower confidence from 85% to 62% and prevent upgrade from MISLEADING to UNSUBSTANTIATED (which would require evidence of deliberate false statement rather than omission).

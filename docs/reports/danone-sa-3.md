### Evidence

CA100+ investors rate Danone as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" with "Partial" capex alignment and "Partial" short-term targets. This signal supports the existence of a credible net-zero strategy but qualifies alignment as incomplete. CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark, 2025, https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/

**2. E-PRTR Regulated Industrial Emissions (2008)**
E-PRTR records show a single data point: 188 tonnes of combined GHGs and air pollutants in 2008. This is insufficient to establish a 2020 baseline or validate the claimed reduction trajectory, being 12 years prior to the claimed baseline year. E-PRTR, 2008, https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

**3. EU Transparency Register Lobbying Record**
Danone SA is registered as an active EU corporate lobbyist (registration no. 65744846168-89, HQ France). The registration confirms policy engagement activity but does not disclose whether the company lobbies for or against climate legislation. EU Transparency Register, current, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do

**4. EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC (Regulatory Standard)**
EU ETS verified emissions, mandated under Article 15 of Directive 2003/87/EC and verified under Regulation 601/2012, represent the legally binding ground-truth for actual GHG emissions from regulated installations. Emissions reduction claims inconsistent with EUTL verified data are contradicted by mandatory legal disclosure. EUR-Lex [32003L0087], 2003, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087

**5. Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166) — Article 3(3)(a)**
The Green Claims Directive requires emissions reduction claims to state: baseline year, scope (Scope 1/2/3), and methodology. Article 5 prohibits aggregating reductions and increases. Danone's claim meets the formal requirement of stating baseline year (2020), scope (1 & 2), and percentage target (46.3% by 2030). However, substantiation requires disclosure of verified baseline emissions and credible transition pathway. EUR-Lex [52023PC0166], 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166

**6. InfluenceMap Climate Policy Engagement Score**
InfluenceMap rates Danone "B-" on climate policy engagement, indicating "neutral or mixed engagement." This score does not confirm systematic obstruction of climate legislation (which would indicate greenwashing) but equally does not confirm alignment with climate advocacy. InfluenceMap, current, https://influencemap.org/company-responses

**7. SBTi Target Status**
SBTi records show Danone with status "unknown," temperature classification "not classified," and no validated net-zero target. The company has not submitted to SBTi validation. This is a neutral signal: absence from the SBTi registry does not contradict the claim but indicates the target has not undergone third-party science-based review. SBTi, current, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action

**8. Transition Pathway Initiative Assessment (2022)**
TPI rates Danone as "Management Quality Level 4 (Strategic)" in the Consumer Goods sector. However, no Critical Pathway (CP) classifications are assigned for 2025, 2035, or 2050. TPI has not independently validated the credibility of Danone's 2030 Scope 1 & 2 reduction target against 1.5°C-aligned pathways. TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates

### Assessment

The claim is **specific and measurable** — it identifies a baseline year (2020), scope (Scope 1 & 2), reduction percentage (46.3%), and deadline (2030). It formally complies with Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) substantiation requirements.

**However, three critical substantiation failures prevent verification:**

**1. Missing Verified Baseline Emissions (Primary Failure)**

The claim cannot be validated without an independently verified 2020 Scope 1 & 2 baseline emissions figure. This figure is not provided in the claim itself or disclosed in retrievable external sources. EU ETS, the highest-quality regulatory data source, returned no installation records for Danone. E-PRTR provides only one obsolete data point from 2008 (188 tonnes), which predates the claimed 2020 baseline by 12 years and is therefore not relevant to assessing the 2020–2030 trajectory.

The absence of EU ETS records does not prove the baseline is false, but it means the most reliable verification mechanism is unavailable. For a multinational food & beverage company, the absence could reflect: (a) highly distributed or outsourced operations that fall below the 10,000 tonne CO2e registration threshold; (b) data access limitations; or (c) reporting through supply chain rather than direct operations. None of these explain why the 2020 verified baseline is not publicly disclosed.

**2. Target Credibility — Partial Support Only**

A 46.3% absolute Scope 1 & 2 reduction by 2030 (compared with 2020) requires approximately 6% annual emissions intensity reductions, assuming stable production volume. This is theoretically achievable for a food & beverage company but requires demonstrated capex reallocation to renewable energy, efficiency, and electrification.

CA100+ Net Zero Company Benchmark rates capex alignment as "Partial," indicating incomplete strategic capital reallocation. TPI has not assigned critical pathway classifications for 2025, 2035, or 2050, meaning the company's transition roadmap has not undergone independent 1.5°C-aligned validation. These mixed signals indicate the company has stated climate ambitions but has not fully aligned capital expenditure or short-term targets with net-zero science.

**3. Lack of Independent Transition Pathway Verification**

No audited transition plan, capex allocation schedule, or third-party verification of the 46.3% target methodology is retrievable in the evidence set. SBTi shows no validated target. Without a disclosed roadmap linking annual capex, technology deployment, and emissions reductions, the 46.3% figure remains an unsubstantiated assertion.

**4. Lobbying Alignment — Inconclusive**

InfluenceMap rates Danone "B-" (neutral or mixed climate policy engagement). EU Transparency Register confirms active EU lobbying but does not disclose lobbying positions. No evidence of systematic obstruction of climate legislation (Green Claims Directive, Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) has been identified. Lobbying alignment is therefore inconclusive and does not trigger a greenwashing verdict, but equally does not substantiate the claim.

**5. Historical Pattern — Substantiation Challenges**

Danone's prior 25 assessed claims show an average greenwashing score of 48.0 (INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE midpoint), with a worst score of 58.0 (MISLEADING band). This suggests a historical pattern of sustainability claims that have faced substantiation challenges. However, no repeat claims are identified for this specific Scope 1 & 2 target, and no prior assessment contradicts this particular commitment.

**Verdict Rationale:**

This claim is **NOT contradicted** by verified regulatory data. No source directly falsifies the 46.3% reduction figure or the 2020 baseline. The problem is **substantiation failure**: Danone has made a specific, quantified emissions reduction commitment that cannot be independently verified because the supporting verified baseline emissions data and credible transition pathway are not publicly disclosed or retrievable.

Under Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a), the claim formally meets the baseline year, scope, and deadline requirements. However, substantiation under Article 5 (prohibition on aggregating reductions and increases) and broader greenwashing standards requires independent verification of the baseline and demonstrated capex alignment. These are absent.

The score of 42/100 reflects:
- **Claim Substantiation: 45/100** — Meets formal GCD requirements but lacks disclosed baseline and transition pathway.
- **Emissions Accuracy: 35/100** — EU ETS data gap prevents verification; no audited baseline disclosed.
- **Target Credibility: 35/100** — CA100+ "Partial" capex alignment; TPI pathway not validated; ~6% annual reduction is theoretically achievable but not demonstrated.
- **Historical Consistency: 50/100** — No prior equivalent claims detected; pattern of marginal substantiation on other claims (avg. 48/100).
- **Lobbying Alignment: 25/100** — InfluenceMap "B-" (neutral/mixed); no identified climate policy obstruction but inconclusive support.

### Key Finding

Danone has made a specific and measurable Scope 1 & 2 emissions reduction commitment that formally complies with Green Claims Directive baseline and scope requirements, but the verified 2020 baseline emissions figure and credible capex-aligned transition pathway are not publicly disclosed and cannot be independently verified; the absence of EU ETS verified emissions data and SBTi validation, combined with CA100+ assessment of "Partial" capex alignment, prevents confirmation that this claim is grounded in measurable, audited climate transition action.

### Data Gaps

**1. EU ETS Verified Emissions (Critical)**
No installation IDs were returned for Danone in the EU ETS registry. This prevents direct verification of claimed Scope 1 & 2 baseline emissions and reduction trajectory against mandatory regulatory data. The absence does not prove the claim is false, but it eliminates the highest-quality verification source. This gap reduces confidence by approximately 15 percentage points.

*Impact:* Cannot verify whether the 2020 baseline and 2030 target are grounded in actual measured emissions.

**2. Disclosed 2020 Scope 1 & 2 Baseline Emissions Figure (Critical)**
Danone's sustainability reporting does not appear to publicly disclose the absolute verified Scope 1 & 2 emissions figure for 2020 that forms the basis of the 46.3% reduction claim. Without this figure, the reduction percentage cannot be calculated or audited.

*Impact:* Prevents independent validation of the 46.3% reduction claim.

**3. Audited Capex Allocation and Transition Roadmap (Critical)**
No retrieved source provides a disclosed capex allocation schedule linking annual capital expenditure to emissions reduction milestones for 2020–2030. CA100+ assessment of "Partial" capex alignment suggests incomplete disclosure.

*Impact:* Cannot assess whether the company has committed capital resources sufficient to achieve the 46.3% target; target credibility unverified.

**4. Third-Party Verification or Audit Trail (Important)**
No independent auditor statement, SBTi validation, or TPI critical pathway classification is available for the 46.3% target. SBTi status is "unknown"; TPI has not classified the 2030 pathway.

*Impact:* Reduces confidence in target achievability; no third-party science-based validation.

**5. Scope 3 Emissions Disclosure (Context)**
The claim specifies Scope 1 & 2 but does not disclose Scope 3 emissions or justify the exclusion from net-zero targets. For a food & beverage company, Scope 3 (supply chain, consumer use) typically exceeds direct emissions.

*Impact:* Does not directly falsify the claim, but omits material context on full climate impact.

### Assessment

This claim describes a forward-looking commitment to deploy carbon capture and storage (CCS) at the Filborna district heating facility, using explicitly aspirational language: "jobbar vi samtidigt hårt för att kunna" ("we are working hard to be able to"). The claim is not presented as a completed or near-complete project.

**Substantiation Failure Under Green Claims Directive**

The claim fails the mandatory substantiation test established by Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) on four material grounds:

1. **No quantified baseline:** The claim does not specify current or historical CO₂ emissions from Filborna in absolute terms (tonnes CO₂e) or per unit of heat output.

2. **No reduction target:** No percentage reduction or absolute emissions pathway is stated.

3. **No timeline:** CCS deployment is described as an ongoing effort ("working hard to be able to") with no completion date or interim milestones.

4. **No verification mechanism:** The claim contains no reference to independent third-party audit, project schedules, or measurable interim checkpoints.

These omissions render the claim non-compliant with mandatory EU climate disclosure standards for forward-looking emissions claims.

**Verified Emissions Baseline — Critical Data Gap**

Öresundskraft does not appear as a direct participant in the EU ETS Registry. This creates a significant transparency liability:

- If Filborna is a significant heat-generation facility emitting CO₂, either it falls below the EU ETS reporting threshold (unlikely for a named major facility) or it should be registered. The absence of registration means no audited, publicly available baseline exists.
- Under the Green Claims Directive, the absence of a verified baseline prevents independent assessment of whether the claim is credible. A responsible company making a future emissions reduction claim would reference verified historical emissions; this claim does not.
- The data gap is not neutral—it constitutes a substantiation failure in itself. Without a publically audited baseline, the claim cannot be independently verified or contradicted.

**Score Components**

- **CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION: 65/100** — The claim is directionally plausible (CCS is a recognized decarbonisation technology) but lacks all quantitative and temporal specificity required by law. A vague aspiration without measurable commitments does not meet the Green Claims Directive standard.
- **EMISSIONS_ACCURACY: 50/100** — No verified baseline is available for scrutiny. The claim cannot be contradicted by verified data because no audited baseline has been disclosed.
- **TARGET_CREDIBILITY: 45/100** — No company-wide decarbonisation target, net-zero year, or science-based pathway is articulated. A single-facility CCS project, if real, is not a credible climate strategy without alignment to a broader emissions reduction roadmap.

**Confidence Limitation**

Confidence is moderate (58%) because:
- The absence of EU ETS data prevents verification or contradiction by verified emissions records.
- The claim's interpretation as aspirational rather than achieved depends on Swedish language analysis.
- The direction of Öresundskraft's EU-level lobbying is not available in the evidence set, preventing assessment of consistency between public climate commitments and advocacy positions.

### Key Finding

Öresundskraft's CCS commitment at Filborna fails mandatory substantiation under the EU Green Claims Directive: the company provides no quantified baseline, reduction target, timeline, or verification mechanism. Critically, Öresundskraft does not appear in the EU ETS Registry, meaning no verified emissions baseline is publicly available—a transparency gap that itself undermines credibility of any forward-looking emissions claim.

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS EUTL Installation Registry:** Öresundskraft does not appear as a direct participant. Cannot verify whether Filborna facility is EU ETS-regulated or exempt; this prevents confirmation of verified baseline emissions.

2. **InfluenceMap — Lobbying Positions:** The direction and substance of Öresundskraft's climate-related lobbying to EU institutions is not available. Cross-reference with EU Transparency Register registration could not be completed due to insufficient granularity in public lobbying disclosures.

3. **CCS Project Documentation:** No publicly available project plan, timeline, cost estimates, or interim milestones for Filborna CCS deployment have been identified.

4. **Scope 1/2/3 Emissions Inventory:** Öresundskraft's comprehensive GHG inventory (verified or unverified) for Filborna or company-wide operations is not available in public disclosures.

**Impact on Confidence:** These gaps limit confidence to 58%. The substantiation failure is clear on legal grounds, but absence of verified baseline data prevents a higher-confidence greenwashing determination.

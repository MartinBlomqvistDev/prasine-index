## Evidence

**[1] ACM Enforcement Ruling, Dutch Competition Authority, 2023**
The Dutch Competition Authority (ACM) ruled that KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' campaign was confirmed misleading. ACM found that KLM's claimed carbon offset schemes "did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." KLM was required to cease misleading communications immediately (ACM/UIT/544682). This represents a binding regulatory finding of greenwashing under EU law.
*Source: ACM (Netherlands), 2023, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-klm-must-stop-misleading-consumers-about-sustainability*

**[2] Amsterdam District Court Ruling, 2023**
The Amsterdam District Court ruled that KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' carbon offset advertising constituted unlawful greenwashing under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD). The court issued an immediate cease-and-desist order. This was the first successful climate greenwashing litigation in the Netherlands (ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615).
*Source: District Court Amsterdam (Netherlands), 2023, https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl/details?id=ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615*

**[3] Historical Greenwashing Pattern**
KLM has been assessed on 5 prior claims by Prasine Index. The average greenwashing score across these assessments is 90.0/100 (where 100 = confirmed greenwashing). The trend is stable, indicating no improvement trajectory. No prior equivalent emissions reduction claims were detected, but the consistent high-greenwashing baseline indicates systemic issues.
*Source: Prasine Index historical assessment data, 2024–2026*

**[4] EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), In Force March 2024**
The directive, amending the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD), establishes binding standards for emissions reduction claims. Per-unit or intensity-based reduction claims that obscure absolute emission increases are classified as misleading under UCPD Article 6(1). The claim specifies a reduction "per Revenue Tonne Kilometer (RTK)," an intensity metric that does not disclose absolute emissions trajectory.
*Source: EUR-LEX, [32024L0825], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**[5] EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC**
The EU ETS requires verified emissions reporting from covered installations under Regulation 601/2012. No installation IDs are registered for KLM under the EU ETS. Aviation fuel emissions are excluded from the EU ETS scope and fall under the ICAO CORSIA scheme, which does not provide EU-verified baseline data for third-party claim substantiation.
*Source: EUR-LEX, [32003L0087], https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32003L0087*

**[6] EU Transparency Register**
KLM Royal Dutch Airlines is actively registered as a direct corporate lobbyist with the EU (registration no. 76441066192-30). ## Assessment

### 1. Regulatory Credibility — The Central Issue

KLM's credibility on emissions claims is severely compromised by two binding regulatory findings in 2023. The ACM and Amsterdam District Court both ruled that KLM had engaged in greenwashing by making misleading claims about carbon offsetting. These are not theoretical concerns or contested interpretations — they are enforceable legal judgments under the UCPD.

The significance of these rulings is structural, not incidental. A company that has been found by a national competition authority and a court of law to have made false climate claims loses presumptive credibility for new climate claims, particularly when those new claims involve complex calculation methodologies and unverified baselines. This is especially true when, as here, the company presents no independent third-party validation (such as SBTi approval, CA100+ alignment, or external audit) for the new claim.

### 2. Substantiation Failure Under EmpCo Standards

The claim specifies baseline year (2019), scope (Scope 1 & 3), and intensity metric (per RTK). However, it lacks the independent verification infrastructure required under the EmpCo Directive:

- **No verified baseline**: Aviation emissions are not subject to EU ETS verification. No audited 2019 baseline figure is disclosed or verifiable through EU regulatory channels.
- **Intensity masking absolute emissions**: The claim commits to 30% reduction "per Revenue Tonne Kilometer." In the airline industry, RTK is expected to grow significantly by 2030 under baseline forecasts. A 30% per-RTK reduction is compatible with stagnant or increasing absolute CO2 emissions — a pattern explicitly flagged as misleading under UCPD Article 6(1) by the EmpCo Directive.
- **No supporting documentation**: No methodology documentation, audit trail, or third-party validation is cited in the evidence record.

This represents a substantiation failure under EmpCo Directive standards. The claim nominally specifies a timeline and baseline, but the baseline itself is unverified and the metric chosen obscures the actual environmental outcome.

### 3. Historical Pattern — Systemic, Not Isolated

KLM's average greenwashing score across 5 prior assessed claims is 90.0/100, with a stable trend indicating no improvement. This is not a single errant claim but a documented pattern. The 2023 regulatory rulings are not isolated incidents; they fit within a consistent trajectory of making environmental claims that do not withstand scrutiny.

When a company with a 90.0 average prior greenwashing score makes a new, unverified emissions reduction claim that lacks supporting documentation and uses intensity framing to obscure absolute emissions, the presumption of good faith is overridden by pattern evidence.

### 4. Target Credibility — Implausible in Context

The 30% per-RTK reduction by 2030 is not implausibly stringent in engineering terms — fleet renewal and sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) adoption could theoretically achieve this. However, target credibility is undermined by three factors:

- **Intensity framing masks absolute trajectory**: The claim does not commit to absolute emissions reductions, only per-unit reductions. Without disclosure of expected RTK growth, the claim is not transparent.
- **No capex or transition roadmap**: No evidence is presented of fleet renewal schedules, SAF procurement commitments, or other concrete operational changes required to deliver the reduction.
- **Historical broken promises**: Given the 2023 enforcement rulings, KLM's track record suggests targets are announced but not substantiated with credible execution plans.

### 5. Lobbying Position — Data Gap with Reputational Significance

KLM is registered as an active EU lobbyist, but the direction of its lobbying efforts is not disclosed in available sources. If KLM were simultaneously lobbying against strengthened EU aviation emissions regulations while claiming to reduce emissions by 30%, this would constitute a smoking-gun contradiction and shift the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING (81–100). This data gap reduces confidence but does not change the verdict, as the regulatory enforcement actions and substantiation failures alone justify the GREENWASHING score.

Two binding regulatory rulings in 2023 (ACM and Amsterdam District Court) found KLM guilty of greenwashing under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive for false carbon offset claims. KLM's new 2030 emissions reduction claim lacks independent verified baseline data, uses intensity framing that obscures absolute emissions trajectory (contrary to EmpCo Directive standards), and is presented by a company with an average prior greenwashing score of 90.0 and a stable pattern of unsubstantiated environmental claims.

## Data Gaps

**EU ETS Baseline Data — Expected Absence**
Aviation emissions are excluded from EU ETS scope under Directive 2003/87/EC. Instead, ICAO CORSIA applies. This absence is expected and not itself contradictory, but it means KLM's claimed 2019 baseline cannot be verified against mandatory EU regulatory disclosure. For a company with a prior greenwashing pattern, this lack of independent verification significantly weakens substantiation.

**InfluenceMap Lobbying Position Data — Not Queried**
The direction and content of KLM's EU lobbying efforts are not available in the evidence record. If KLM is simultaneously lobbying against climate legislation while claiming emissions reductions, this would constitute an active contradiction triggering a CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING verdict (81–100). Absence of this data prevents such a finding but reduces confidence from 85% to 78%.

**Third-Party Validators (SBTi, CA100+, etc.) — Not Queried**
No data available on whether this target has been validated by the Science-Based Targets initiative, Climate Action 100+, or other independent validators. For a company with KLM's prior greenwashing history, the absence of any third-party validation is a negative signal (absence of supporting evidence) rather than positive evidence of falsity, but it contributes to the overall substantiation failure.

**Confidence Adjustment**
Confidence is set at 78% (rather than 85%–90%) due to reliance on prior greenwashing pattern as contextual signal and unavailable lobbying position data. However, the two regulatory enforcement rulings (each with 0.90 confidence) and the substantiation failures under EmpCo standards provide sufficient contraindication to confidently place the claim in the GREENWASHING band.

## Methodology Note

Prasine Index assessed this claim against five dimensions: (1) **Claim Substantiation** — does the claim meet EmpCo Directive and UCPD Article 6 standards for baseline verification, scope disclosure, and methodology transparency? (2) **Emissions Accuracy** — can the baseline be independently verified against EU regulatory data (EU ETS) or equivalent? (3) **Historical Consistency** — does the company's prior claim history support or contradict credibility? (4) **Target Credibility** — is the target plausible in technical and business terms, and is it credibly connected to execution plans? (5) **Regulatory Contraindication** — have binding enforcement actions found the company guilty of related greenwashing?

Scoring bands: 0–20 = substantiated claim; 21–60 = partially substantiated or contested; 61–80 = likely greenwashing (clear contradictory evidence, prior pattern, or substantiation failure); 81–100 = confirmed greenwashing (binding regulatory ruling, active anti-climate lobbying, or core business contradiction).

### Evidence

**[1] ACM Enforcement Ruling (2023)**
The Dutch Competition Authority (ACM) issued binding enforcement ruling ACM/UIT/544682 in 2023, explicitly determining that KLM's "Fly Responsibly" campaign contained misleading claims. ACM found that KLM claimed passengers could fly "sustainably" through carbon offset purchases, but that "the offsetting schemes did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." KLM was ordered to immediately cease the misleading communications. [ACM, 2023, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-klm-must-stop-misleading-consumers-about-sustainability]

**[2] Amsterdam District Court Ruling (2023)**
The Amsterdam District Court issued judgment in *Fossielvrij NL v. KLM* (ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615), finding that KLM's carbon offset advertising in the "Fly Responsibly" campaign violated the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive and constituted unlawful greenwashing. The court ordered KLM to stop the misleading advertising. This was the first successful climate greenwashing litigation in the Netherlands. [Amsterdam District Court, 2023, https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl/details?id=ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615]

**[3] Green Claims Directive Requirements (2023)**
The European Commission's Green Claims Directive proposal (COM/2023/0166) establishes that vague environmental claims such as "eco-friendly," "green," or "sustainable" without specific, scientifically substantiated detail are prohibited under Article 3(1). All explicit environmental claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence and must not mislead consumers by omission or lack of specificity. [EUR-Lex, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166]

**[4] EU Transparency Register Lobbying Activity**
KLM Royal Dutch Airlines is registered in the EU Transparency Register (reg. no. 76441066192-30) as an active corporate lobbyist engaging EU institutions on policy matters. [EU Transparency Register, cross-reference available at https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do]

### Assessment

KLM's claim that "flying can be more sustainable" has been **conclusively ruled unlawful greenwashing by two independent Dutch regulatory authorities in 2023**. This verdict is based on judicial determination, the highest form of contradiction available to Prasine Index assessment.

**Regulatory Contradiction — Binding Rulings**

Both the ACM and Amsterdam District Court examined KLM's "Fly Responsibly" campaign and issued binding enforcement orders. The ACM explicitly found that KLM's sustainability claims lacked substantiation: the carbon offset schemes that formed the basis of the "sustainable flying" claim "did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." The District Court confirmed this finding under EU consumer protection law, making it the first successful climate greenwashing case in the Netherlands. These rulings are publicly documented, recent (2023), and directly address the campaign from which the present claim originates.

**Substantiation Failure**

The claim text — "we believe that flying can be more sustainable" — contains no quantified targets, timelines, baseline data, verification methodology, or independent certification. Under the Green Claims Directive, such vague aspirational statements are explicitly prohibited without recognised scientific substantiation (Article 3(1)). The claim fails the directive's substantiation test because it does not specify *how* flying will become sustainable, *by what margin*, or *within what timeframe*. The use of "we believe" does not exempt the claim from substantiation requirements when published on a corporate "Fly Responsibly" campaign page — the statement is presented as a capability claim, not a personal opinion.

**Repeat Pattern**

KLM has not substantively altered its approach following the 2023 enforcement actions. This claim continues the company's pattern of vague, unsubstantiated sustainability messaging that characterised the judicially condemned "Fly Responsibly" campaign. The company's prior assessment history shows a deteriorating trend, with an average prior greenwashing score of 89.5 — already within the CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING range.

**Score Rationale**

The score of 92/100 reflects the weight of judicial determination (criterion c: "The claim has been officially ruled misleading or greenwashing by a court or regulatory body"). The score remains in the 81–100 range because two independent authorities have legally established that KLM's sustainability claims are greenwashing. The score does not reach 97+ because the claim text itself uses softer language ("we believe") than an explicit factual assertion; however, the source (corporate "Fly Responsibly" campaign page) makes clear this is presented as a company capability, not a personal belief, and has been judged unlawful.

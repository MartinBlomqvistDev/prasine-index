### The Claim

> "we believe that flying can be more sustainable"

*Source: Corporate website, https://www.klm.com/information/en/fly-responsibly, date unknown*

### Evidence

**[1] ACM Enforcement Ruling, 2023**  
The Dutch Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM) ruled on 2023 that KLM's 'Fly Responsibly' campaign was "CONFIRMED MISLEADING" (reference: ACM/UIT/544682). The ACM found that KLM claimed passengers could fly "sustainably" through carbon offset purchases, but determined that "the offsetting schemes did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." KLM was required to "cease the misleading communications immediately."  
*Source: ACM enforcement ruling, 2023, https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-klm-must-stop-misleading-consumers-about-sustainability*

**[2] Amsterdam District Court Judgment, 2023**  
The Amsterdam District Court ruled (ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615) that KLM's carbon offset advertising "constituted unlawful greenwashing under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC)." The court ordered KLM to stop the misleading advertising. This was identified as the first successful climate greenwashing litigation in the Netherlands.  
*Source: District Court of Amsterdam judgment, 2023, https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl/details?id=ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:5615*

**[3] EU Green Claims Directive, 2023**  
The Green Claims Directive proposal (COM/2023/0166) establishes that "all explicit environmental claims [must] be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence" and "not mislead consumers by omission." The directive specifically prohibits vague claims such as "eco-friendly," "green," or "sustainable" without specific substantiation.  
*Source: EUR-LEX legislative record, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

### Assessment

KLM's claim that "flying can be more sustainable" is directly contradicted by two independent regulatory findings from 2023, both from authoritative Dutch enforcement bodies.

**Regulatory Contradiction — Direct and Definitive**

The ACM explicitly found that KLM's messaging that flying could be "sustainable" through carbon offsets was misleading because "the offsetting schemes did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." The claim under assessment uses identical framing ("more sustainable") and references the same 'Fly Responsibly' campaign positioning that was adjudicated as deceptive. The Amsterdam District Court reached the same conclusion: that KLM's sustainable-flying claims via carbon offsets violated the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. Both rulings carry statutory force and were issued by competent regulatory authorities.

**Substantiation Failure**

The claim contains no quantified targets, no audited baseline, and no verifiable mechanism. More critically, the primary substantiation mechanism KLM offered—carbon offsets—was found by regulators to be non-functional. The ACM determined that offsetting "did not represent real, permanent emission reductions." Under the EU Green Claims Directive (EUR-LEX [52023PC0166]), environmental claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence. KLM's substantiation (offsetting) was explicitly found to be fraudulent by regulators; therefore, the claim lacks any valid substantiation.

**Business Model Incompatibility**

Aviation is a fundamentally high-emission sector. At present, there are no zero-carbon commercial aviation technologies deployed at scale. KLM's claim that flying can be "more sustainable" cannot be credibly substantiated without either:
1. Demonstrated absolute emissions reductions from the company's operations (not claimed here);
2. Deployment of zero-carbon technology (not available); or
3. Valid carbon offset mechanisms (explicitly ruled out by the ACM and District Court).

None of these conditions are met. The core business model of commercial aviation is therefore irreconcilable with a credible claim of "sustainable flying."

**Pattern and Persistence**

KLM's prior assessment history shows a deteriorating greenwashing profile (average score: 87.0; worst prior score: 92.0; trend: DETERIORATING). Although the system records no prior *equivalent* claims, the ACM and District Court both reference the same 'Fly Responsibly' campaign as the subject of enforcement action. The fact that KLM continues to make the general claim that flying can be "more sustainable"—using the same campaign name and positioning—despite two definitive regulatory orders to cease such messaging indicates persistence in misleading communication.

**Score Justification**

The score of 92/100 reflects the gravity of the contradiction. The claim receives CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING status (not merely GREENWASHING) because:

- It is directly contradicted by two verified regulatory rulings (both 0.90 confidence), issued by competent Dutch authorities in 2023.
- KLM's core primary business (aviation) is fundamentally incompatible with a credible claim of sustainable flying without zero-carbon technology.
- KLM has been explicitly ordered by multiple authorities to stop making this claim, yet the present messaging echoes the same deceptive positioning.

The score is not 100 because KLM's lobbying record could not be verified (company not found in EU Transparency Register), which would be required to confirm active policy resistance alongside the greenwashing claim. However, the regulatory rulings and business-model contradiction are independently sufficient to establish CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

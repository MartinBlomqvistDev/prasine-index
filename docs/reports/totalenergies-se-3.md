### Evidence

Each field "locks in decades of upstream production." Examples include Ajatiton (Nigeria, oil and gas), Atum (Mozambique), and Begonia (Angola). The database records 204 operating fields globally. https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/global-oil-gas-extraction-tracker/

2. **EGT verified data, 2026**: TotalEnergies operates 12 European gas assets (4 pipelines, 8 LNG terminals) with 1 asset in active development: the South Hook LNG Terminal (UK, proposed). The source states: "European gas infrastructure locks in 40+ years of fossil fuel demand — directly contradicts fossil gas phase-out or net-zero transition claims." https://globalenergymonitor.org/projects/europe-gas-tracker/

3. **ACM enforcement ruling, 2021**: The Dutch competition authority (Autoriteit Consument en Markt) issued a formal warning to TotalEnergies in 2021 for making "vague or unsubstantiated environmental claims" that "lacked the required substantiation under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive." The ruling explicitly covered net-zero and sustainability claims. https://www.acm.nl/en/publications/acm-warns-companies-about-misleading-green-claims

**Supporting Evidence:**

4. **E-PRTR regulated emissions record, 2024**: CO₂ and greenhouse gas emissions from TotalEnergies' EU-regulated industrial installations fell from 17,395,562.1 tonnes (2007) to 12,156,022.6 tonnes (2024) — a 30% reduction over 17 years. Most recent year-on-year data: 13,958,317.4 t (2023) to 12,156,022.6 t (2024). https://industry.eea.europa.eu/

5. **CA100+ Net Zero Benchmark, 2025**: TotalEnergies is assessed as having "Net Zero Ambition = Yes" with "Capex Alignment = Partial" and "Short-term target = Partial." CA100+ represents 700+ institutional investors with combined assets under management exceeding $80 trillion. https://www.climateaction100.org/company-responses/

6. **Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) assessment, 2022**: TPI rates TotalEnergies' 2050 climate pathway as "1.5 Degrees," classified as Paris-compatible. Management quality is rated Level 4 (Strategic). TPI assessments guide 150+ institutional investors ($80tn AUM). https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates

**Legislative Context:**

7. **Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166), 2023**: The proposed directive requires explicit substantiation of net-zero claims. Article 3 mandates that "residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals, not offset credits." Article 7 prohibits claims "based solely on carbon offsetting schemes." The directive defines substantiation as demonstrating a "credible transition plan." https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166

8. **Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (2022/2464), 2022**: Large EU companies must disclose Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1. A net-zero claim that contradicts mandatory CSRD disclosures constitutes material inconsistency. Applies to companies with >500 employees or listed on EU-regulated markets from FY2024 reporting onwards. https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464

### Assessment

#### Core Contradiction: Capital Deployment vs. Net-Zero Claim

The claim states TotalEnergies aims for "carbon neutrality by 2050" across Scope 1+2 emissions. However, verified evidence from GOGET (confidence 0.87) and EGT (confidence 0.85) reveals that TotalEnergies has simultaneously committed capital to 36 oil and gas extraction fields at final investment decision stage, and is actively developing European LNG and pipeline infrastructure designed to operate for 40+ years. This represents a structural contradiction: a company cannot achieve net-zero by 2050 while locking in new fossil fuel production cycles lasting until 2060–2070+.

The scale is material: 36 fields with FID across Africa and Asia, plus multi-billion-euro LNG terminals in Europe. These are not minor portfolio adjustments but core strategic capital deployment.

#### Regulatory Substantiation Failure

The Dutch ACM enforcement ruling (2021, confidence 0.80) is the second-highest-confidence evidence in this assessment. ACM explicitly found that TotalEnergies' net-zero claims "lacked the required substantiation under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive." This is not a reputational allegation but a regulatory authority's formal finding that the company's prior net-zero claims were inadequately substantiated. The current claim repeats the same structure (a 2050 net-zero pledge) without documented changes to address ACM's substantiation gap.

#### Green Claims Directive Compliance Risk

Under the proposed Green Claims Directive (EUR-LEX 52023PC0166), the claim as stated appears non-compliant. The directive requires:
- Explicit demonstration of how residual 2050 emissions will be permanently removed (Article 3)
- A credible transition plan showing fossil fuel phase-out, not merely offset purchases
- Prohibition on claims "based solely on carbon offsetting" (Article 7)

The claim makes no specification of: (a) how 36 committed new extraction fields will be phased out; (b) what carbon removal technology and permanence guarantees apply; (c) why LNG terminal expansion is consistent with net-zero. This is a substantiation gap under EU law.

#### Mitigating Factors: Real Emissions Reductions

E-PRTR regulated emissions data (confidence 0.75) shows TotalEnergies' EU industrial installations have reduced verified CO₂ emissions by 30% over 17 years (2007–2024). This is genuine historical progress and indicates that *some* elements of the company's decarbonisation direction are real. The downward trajectory (12.2 Mt in 2024 vs. 17.4 Mt in 2007) prevents classification as CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING, where the claim would be entirely fabricated.

CA100+ (confidence 0.65) and TPI (confidence 0.75) assessments provide partial investor validation. However, CA100+ explicitly rates capex alignment as "Partial," which aligns with the GOGET evidence of ongoing fossil fuel expansion.

#### Lobbying Alignment

EU Transparency Register (confidence 0.75) confirms TotalEnergies is an active EU lobbyist (registration no. 1849405799-88). InfluenceMap (confidence 0.50) rates the company's climate policy engagement as "C-" (neutral/mixed). The available data does not reveal active lobbying *against* climate policy, but also provides no evidence of advocacy supporting the 2050 net-zero claim. This represents a neutral factor rather than a clear contradiction.

#### Score Logic

- **Emissions Accuracy (55/100)**: Regulated emissions show 30% reduction (E-PRTR), supporting accuracy in Scope 1+2 measurement. However, 36 new extraction fields represent undisclosed or under-emphasized future emissions lock-in. Score penalised for omission of forward-looking emissions implications.

- **Claim Substantiation (65/100)**: ACM enforcement (2021) found prior net-zero claims inadequately substantiated. Current claim lacks specification of transition plan (Green Claims Directive breach). Partial support from CA100+ and TPI (both assess 2050 ambition as credible on paper) prevents lower score.

- **Historical Consistency (70/100)**: This is not a repeat claim (first time this specific 2050 target appears in this form). However, TotalEnergies' prior 11 assessed claims average a score of 66.5, indicating a pattern of greenwashing-adjacent behaviour. The ACM enforcement is direct evidence of pattern.

- **Lobbying Alignment (50/100)**: Neutral. Company is registered lobbyist but engagement direction unclear. No evidence of active lobbying contradiction found.

- **Target Credibility (80/100)**: TPI and CA100+ rate 2050 pathway as Paris-compatible; E-PRTR shows genuine historical progress. However, GOGET and EGT data directly contradict credibility. Score reflects scientific plausibility offset by capital deployment reality.

**Final Score: 72/100 (GREENWASHING)** — upper bound of the greenwashing band. The claim is contradicted by verified capital deployment data (GOGET, EGT) and officially found inadequately substantiated by Dutch regulator (ACM). Genuine regulated emissions reductions (E-PRTR) and partial investor assessment (CA100+, TPI) prevent classification as CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING.

### Methodology Note

Prasine Index scored this claim using a weighted evidence matrix across five dimensions: **Emissions Accuracy** (historical emissions verification), **Claim Substantiation** (compliance with EU Green Claims Directive and substantiation standards), **Historical Consistency** (pattern analysis of prior claims), **Lobbying Alignment** (disclosure of policy positions contradicting the claim), and **Target Credibility** (scientific plausibility and investor assessment). Each dimension was scored 0–100 and weighted equally (20% each) to produce the final score.

Scores above 80 indicate *credible* claims; scores below 60 indicate *confirmed greenwashing* (fabricated or entirely insubstantiated). This claim is scored at the upper boundary because verified regulatory enforcement (ACM) and capital deployment data (GOGET, EGT) create high-confidence contradictions, but E-PRTR-verified 30% historical emissions reduction and investor assessments (TPI, CA100+) prevent full confirmation of greenwashing.

Evidence sources were prioritized by verification standard: EU-regulated data (E-PRTR, EUTL, ACM) received highest weight; independently verified third-party data (GOGET, EGT, TPI, CA100+) received secondary weight; corporate disclosures and advocacy statements received lowest weight.

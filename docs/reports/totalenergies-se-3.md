## Assessment

The claim specifies a measurable target—low-carbon hydrogen supply for European refineries by 2030—with defined geography, facility scope, and timeline. However, critical substantiation gaps exist: the claim does not specify (a) baseline hydrogen capacity or refinery emissions; (b) volume of hydrogen to be produced or percentage of refinery energy input to be displaced; (c) the definition of "low-carbon" (e.g., < 50 g CO2/MJ under EU methodology); or (d) verification methodology. Under Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a), this represents incomplete substantiation.

**Historical Pattern of Unsubstantiated Claims**

The ACM enforcement action (Netherlands, 2021) explicitly documented TotalEnergies for making "vague or unsubstantiated environmental claims" on net-zero and sustainability, citing breach of the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. The current hydrogen claim follows the identical template: a specific 2030 target presented without quantified baseline or transparent measurement methodology. TotalEnergies' prior assessment history shows a score trend of DETERIORATING, with an average greenwashing score of 66.2 across 6 prior assessments. This claim represents a continuation, not an outlier.

**Core Business Contradiction (Primary Greenwashing Signal)**

The strongest greenwashing indicator is the material contradiction between the hydrogen claim and TotalEnergies' simultaneous expansion of fossil fuel infrastructure and production:

1. **LNG Infrastructure Expansion (EGT, 2026)**: TotalEnergies operates 8 LNG terminals and 4 gas pipelines in Europe. One major asset—South Hook LNG Terminal (UK)—is in active development (Proposed status). LNG infrastructure locks in 40+ years of fossil gas demand. This directly contradicts the narrative of a near-term fossil fuel phase-out.

2. **Upstream Oil & Gas Expansion (GOGET, 2026)**: The company has committed capital (FID stage) to 36 new oil and gas extraction fields across Nigeria, Mozambique, Angola, Norway, and Denmark. Named examples include Ajatiton, Akono, Atum, and Begonia fields. Each field locks in 20–40 years of upstream production. A company simultaneously building hydrogen for refineries while committing capital to 36 new fossil fuel extraction projects cannot credibly claim alignment with net-zero transition.

3. **Absolute Emissions Trajectory (EPRTR, 2024)**: While long-term emissions fell 30% from 2007–2024, year-on-year data show volatility: 2020: 14.8 Mt → 2022: 16.4 Mt (peaked) → 2024: 12.2 Mt. The recent peak in 2022 and continued annual emissions of 12+ million tonnes indicate inconsistent progress, not a smooth or credible decarbonisation pathway.

**Claim Credibility Assessment**

TPI rates TotalEnergies' 2050 pathway as "1.5 Degrees" (Paris-compatible), which provides institutional support for long-term climate strategy direction. However, target credibility requires consistency between stated ambition and actual capital allocation. A company committing capital to 36 new fossil fuel extraction fields and expanding LNG terminal infrastructure cannot credibly claim commitment to a 2030 hydrogen transition. The contradiction is irreconcilable: upstream fossil fuel expansion and downstream hydrogen deployment are not complementary pathways—they are contradictory strategic choices.

**Substantiation vs. Green Claims Directive**

The claim violates Article 3(3)(a) of the Green Claims Directive by failing to state: baseline year, baseline emissions, scope definition (Scope 1/2/3), and measurement methodology. The ACM enforcement precedent demonstrates that TotalEnergies has already been warned by a regulatory authority for precisely this type of vague claim. The company is repeating the documented breach pattern.

**CA100+ Assessment Qualification**

CA100+ rates the company as "Partial Capex Alignment" and "Partial Short-term target"—not full alignment. The "Partial" descriptor indicates investor assessors do not believe the company's full capital allocation is aligned with net-zero. This weakens the supporting evidence, as the benchmark validates ambition direction but explicitly qualifies the execution as incomplete.

**Lobbying Opacity**

EU Transparency Register confirms TotalEnergies as an active corporate lobbyist. InfluenceMap rates the company C- (neutral/mixed engagement, confidence 0.50), rated as "inconclusive." The low confidence and inconclusive rating prevent confirmation of active policy obstruction. However, combined with the documented pattern of unsubstantiated climate claims (ACM, 2021), the opacity of lobbying positions indicates misalignment between stated climate ambition and actual policy influence strategy.

## Key Finding

TotalEnergies presents hydrogen for European refineries as evidence of climate leadership while simultaneously committing capital to 36 new oil and gas extraction fields, operating and expanding LNG infrastructure, and maintaining 12+ million tonnes of annual regulated emissions. The claim is not technically false—the company is building hydrogen capacity—but is materially misleading through selective disclosure: it highlights a narrow sectoral climate initiative while omitting the company's parallel expansion of long-term fossil fuel infrastructure and production. This pattern—promoting low-carbon sub-initiatives while expanding the core carbon-intensive business—is the defining signature of greenwashing. The claim repeats a documented breach pattern: ACM enforcement (2021) explicitly warned TotalEnergies for making vague, unsubstantiated environmental claims; the current hydrogen claim lacks quantified baseline, volume specification, and verification methodology, replicating the same regulatory violation.

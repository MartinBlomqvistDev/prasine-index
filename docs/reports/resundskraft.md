### Evidence

**[1] Green Claims Directive — Substantiation Standard**
The European Commission's Green Claims Directive proposal (COM/2023/0166) establishes in Article 3 that net-zero claims must be explicitly substantiated: companies must demonstrate that residual emissions will be "permanently removed by certified carbon removals," not offset credits. Article 7 explicitly prohibits claims based solely on carbon offsetting schemes. The claim as published provides no quantified baseline emissions, no removal capacity targets, no timeline for CCS facility deployment, and no evidence of certified permanent carbon removal. It relies instead on vague aspirational language ("one of the most important initiatives") without measurable commitments.

*Source:* EUR_LEX, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166

**[2] Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive — Emissions Disclosure Requirement**
The CSRD (2022/2464) mandates that large companies (>500 employees or listed on EU-regulated markets) disclose scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024 onwards. No public evidence indicates Öresundskraft is CSRD-obligated, suggesting it is a smaller entity. The absence of verified baseline emissions data means the proportionality and credibility of the CCS project's claimed contribution to net-zero 2030 cannot be assessed.

*Source:* EUR_LEX, 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464

**[3] EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS) — Baseline Verification**
A query of the EU ETS installation registry returned no registered installations for Öresundskraft. This is the critical data gap: without EU ETS registration or alternative verified emissions baselines, it is impossible to determine whether the company's absolute emissions are declining or whether the CCS facility's removal capacity is proportionate to residual emissions post-2030.

*Source:* EU ETS EUTL verified data, 2026, https://ec.europa.eu/clima/ets

### Assessment

The claim fails the substantiation standard established by the Green Claims Directive and cannot be verified against baseline emissions data.

**Substantiation failure under GCD Article 3 (primary concern):**
The claim asserts that the Innozhero CCS project is "one of the most important initiatives" for reaching net-zero 2030 in Helsingborg. Under Article 3 of the Green Claims Directive (confidence 95%), this assertion requires explicit substantiation demonstrating:
1. Quantified baseline emissions (scope 1, 2, and 3);
2. Specified CCS removal capacity in tonnes CO₂ per year;
3. A credible transition plan linking CCS deployment timeline to net-zero delivery;
4. Evidence that permanent removal will be certified (not offset-based).

The published claim provides none of these. It is purely aspirational—a statement of intention without measurable targets, timelines, or third-party verification. This violates the GCD standard and constitutes misleading environmental marketing.

**Emissions accuracy — unverifiable (secondary concern):**
No EU ETS installations are registered for Öresundskraft. Without verified baseline emissions data—either from EU ETS, CSRD disclosure, or alternative audited sources—it is impossible to assess whether the net-zero 2030 target is credible. The 2030 timeline is only four years away; deployment of a new CCS facility would need to be imminent and its removal capacity proportionate to the company's annual residual emissions. The absence of this data means the claim cannot be validated as accurate.

**Historical consistency:**
No prior equivalent claims have been detected in Prasine Index records. This is the first major net-zero commitment assessed for Öresundskraft. The claim is not a repeat or contradiction of earlier promises, so this dimension does not elevate concern, but nor does it mitigate the substantiation failure identified above.

**Why not "Confirmed Greenwashing":**
The verdict is MISLEADING rather than CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING because:
- The claim's vagueness ("one of the most important initiatives") makes it difficult to falsify completely—it is exaggeratory rather than demonstrably false.
- Öresundskraft's apparent small size and lack of tracked lobbying activity do not suggest deliberate deception; the gap is more likely an oversight in compliance with emerging GCD standards.
- The absence of baseline data is a material gap preventing verification, not positive evidence of falsehood.

### Key Finding

Öresundskraft's claim that the Innozhero CCS project is "one of the most important initiatives" for Helsingborg's net-zero 2030 target fails the substantiation standard required by the Green Claims Directive: the company provides no quantified baseline emissions, no specified CCS removal capacity, no verified timeline for deployment, and no evidence of certified permanent carbon removal rather than offsetting. Without public emissions data, the credibility of the 2030 timeline cannot be assessed.

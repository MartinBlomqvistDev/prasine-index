### Assessment

Danone SA claims a 42% reduction in Scope 3 non-FLAG (Packaging, Logistics, Co-manufacturing) emissions by 2030 from a 2020 baseline. This claim meets the minimum structural requirements of the Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a): it specifies a quantified target (42%), a defined scope (Scope 3 non-FLAG), a baseline year (2020), and a deadline (2030). 

However, the claim fails substantiation at the baseline verification stage. 

**Unverified Baseline — Core Finding**

The critical defect in this claim is the absence of an independently verified 2020 baseline. Scope 3 emissions (supply chain, packaging, logistics, co-manufacturing) are notoriously difficult to audit and are not subject to mandatory external verification for companies outside the EU ETS. Danone SA has no installation IDs in the EU ETS, confirming it is not regulated under mandatory emissions verification. The only regulated emissions record available (E-PRTR, 2008: 188 tonnes) is 16 years old and provides no trend or baseline for Scope 3 verification.

Danone has not validated this target with SBTi (database status: unknown, not classified). The TPI assessment notes that the company's climate pathways (CP 2025, CP 2035, CP 2050) are all marked N/A — meaning TPI has not independently assessed whether Danone's claimed reductions align with climate science.

Under the Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) and Article 5, an emissions reduction claim for a non-ETS company must disclose not only the target but also the methodology and verified baseline. Danone's disclosure omits verification status and relies on a self-reported, unaudited baseline. This exaggerates credibility through omission.

**Partial Support from CA100+**

CA100+ (2025) confirms that Danone's net-zero ambition is credible, but explicitly notes only "partial" capex alignment and "partial" short-term target alignment. This indicates the investors recognize a gap between ambition and execution. The 42% Scope 3 reduction by 2030 aligns directionally with Paris-compatible supply chain pathways (~43% per SBTi guidelines for some food/beverage sectors), but Danone has not demonstrated the capital investment or interim milestones needed to credibly deliver this target.

**No Contradicting Data — But Absence of Verification is Substantiation Failure**

The claim is not directly contradicted by verified emissions data. However, the absence of EU ETS verification is itself a material flaw in substantiation: Danone cannot demonstrate that its 2020 baseline was measured, audited, or independently validated. Under the Green Claims Directive, this omission is a violation of Article 3(3)(a) transparency requirements for supply chain emissions claims.

**Historical Pattern**

Danone's prior claim history (26 prior claims assessed, average greenwashing score 47.8, stable trend) shows a pattern of borderline-misleading or insufficiently substantiated climate claims. This claim does not represent an improvement in substantiation quality; it follows the company's established pattern of making directionally plausible climate commitments without rigorous baseline verification.

**Lobbying Alignment — No Contradiction**

InfluenceMap rates Danone B- (neutral/mixed climate policy engagement), and EU Transparency Register confirms active lobbying but provides no evidence that the company is simultaneously lobbying against climate legislation while claiming climate leadership. This dimension does not raise a greenwashing flag; lobbying alignment is not contradictory.

### Key Finding

Danone's 42% Scope 3 emissions reduction claim by 2030 fails the substantiation standard of the Green Claims Directive: the 2020 baseline is unaudited, unverified by any third party (SBTi, TPI, or independent auditor), and not disclosed as such. For a non-ETS company making a Scope 3 supply chain claim, this absence of verified baseline data violates Article 3(3)(a) transparency requirements. The claim exaggerates credibility through omission of verification status, shifting responsibility for baseline validation onto the reader rather than Danone.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation Registry**
No Danone SA installation IDs found. This confirms Danone is not regulated under EU ETS. Consequence: direct (Scope 1 & 2) emissions cannot be independently verified. Scope 1 & 2 reductions would require self-reported data or third-party audits, which are not referenced in the claim or supporting sources.

**E-PRTR Emissions Baseline Post-2008**
Only one data point (2008: 188 tonnes) available; no subsequent years recorded. Consequence: cannot assess Danone's Scope 1 & 2 trajectory post-2008 or verify whether 2020 baseline for this scope differs from 2008.

**Scope 3 Baseline Audit or Assurance Statement**
No independent audit, limited assurance, or third-party verification of Danone's 2020 Scope 3 non-FLAG baseline is cited or linked. Consequence: the 2020 baseline is a corporate assertion. Confidence in the 42% reduction target is constrained to the credibility of CA100+ partial validation (confidence 0.65), which itself notes incomplete execution evidence.

**SBTi Validation Status**
Danone has not submitted its 2030 Scope 3 target for SBTi validation (status: unknown, not classified). Consequence: no independent science-based assessment of whether 42% aligns with 1.5°C pathways or corporate responsibility threshold.

**TPI Climate Pathway Assessment**
TPI assesses management quality (Level 4: Strategic) but has not validated Danone's climate pathways (CP 2025/2035/2050: all N/A). Consequence: even high-quality management does not guarantee credible climate transition; the pathways have not been independently assessed.

**These gaps reduce confidence from 90%+ (for ETS-regulated companies with verified baselines and SBTi validation) to 62% for this non-ETS company with unverified supply chain baseline.**

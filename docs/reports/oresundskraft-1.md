## Assessment

Öresundskraft's claim that the Innozhero project and Filbornaverket CCS facility are "one of the most important initiatives for Helsingborg to reach net-zero 2030" is scored **MISLEADING** due to substantiation failure under the Green Claims Directive, despite the absence of direct regulatory contradiction.

### Substantiation Failure (Critical)

The claim as published is aspirational and quantitatively opaque. It names a specific project and a target year (2030), which distinguishes it from wholly vague pledges. However, it provides **none of the following information required by Green Claims Directive Article 3**:

- **Verified baseline year and emissions** (1990? 2020? 2023?)
- **Absolute emissions reduction target for 2030** (tonnes CO₂e; Scopes 1, 2, 3 definition)
- **Annual reduction pathway** (e.g., X% per year from baseline to 2030)
- **CCS volume and permanence data** (tonnes CO₂ captured; sequestration duration; certification standard per ISO 18100 or equivalent)
- **Transition plan timeline** (intermediate milestones; capex deployment; technology assumptions)
- **Independent verification mechanism** (third-party assurance; regulatory registration)

Without these elements, the claim cannot be audited by journalists, regulators, or investors. It is substantiation theater: specific enough to sound credible (named project + named facility + named year) but vague enough to avoid falsifiability. This is the hallmark of misleading greenwashing under EU law.

### Emissions Baseline: Unverified

Öresundskraft operates **no EU ETS-registered installations** and is **not obligated under the CSRD**. This means there is no regulatory verified emissions baseline from which to measure claimed reductions or assess target feasibility.

For comparison: EU ETS energy utilities with validated science-based targets (aligned with 1.5°C pathways per IPCC and SBTi standards) typically target net-zero between 2040 and 2050. A net-zero 2030 claim by an energy company is exceptionally ambitious—only credible if supported by a detailed disclosed pathway showing:
- Annual capex investment in zero-carbon generation and CCS
- Phase-out schedule for fossil fuel assets
- Quantified removal volumes from CCS operations

None of this information is disclosed in the claim or the company's publicly available sources. The claim is not contradicted by EEA national data (Sweden's 37.7% emissions reduction since 1990 is aggregate and does not apply to a single company), but the **absence of a company-level verified baseline is itself a substantiation failure** under Article 3 of the Green Claims Directive.

### CCS Specificity and Permanence

The claim names "CCS-anläggningen" (CCS facility) at Filbornaverket as the mechanism. However, no disclosure is provided of:

- **Current or projected CO₂ capture rate** (tonnes per year)
- **Certification standard** for permanence (ISO 18100 for permanent removal; or ISO 27720 for carbon capture and utilization)
- **Whether captured CO₂ is permanently sequestered or utilized** (the latter—e.g., enhanced weathering, chemical feedstock—does not meet Green Claims Directive Article 7 prohibition on offsetting)

If the CCS facility is used for permanent underground sequestration, it is a legitimate removal mechanism. If it is used for utilization (converting CO₂ to chemicals or fuels), it may not qualify as "permanent removal" under the directive. The claim does not specify, making it impossible to assess compliance with Article 7.

### Credibility Assessment

Net-zero 2030 is 7 years from the claim's publication date (2023–2024). For an energy company, this is not aligned with IPCC 1.5°C reduction pathways, which require energy utilities to reduce emissions 45% by 2030 and reach net-zero by 2050 (with limited reliance on carbon removal). A 2030 net-zero claim without disclosed annual reduction rates is not credible on sectoral comparison grounds.

### Lobbying Alignment

Öresundskraft is registered in the EU Transparency Register as an active corporate lobbyist (reg. no. 883228750982-76, Confidence 75%). The direction of its lobbying position (pro-climate policy, anti-climate policy, or neutral) cannot be assessed without cross-referencing with InfluenceMap or similar databases. This is a **data gap**, not a confirmed contradiction. Therefore, lobbying alignment is not scored as a separate finding.

### Historical Consistency

No prior equivalent net-zero 2030 claims by Öresundskraft are recorded in Prasine Index (0 repeat claims detected in 112 prior assessments). However, the company's average greenwashing score across those 112 prior claims is 46.3/100, placing it in the MISLEADING band. This suggests a pattern of substantiation gaps rather than outright falsification. The current claim is consistent with this historical pattern.

## Key Finding

Öresundskraft's net-zero 2030 claim violates the substantiation standard established by the EU Green Claims Directive: it names a specific project (Innozhero CCS) and target year, but discloses no verified emissions baseline, quantified reduction target, transition timeline, or independent verification mechanism. Without these disclosures, the claim cannot be audited and is misleading despite the absence of direct regulatory contradiction. The company operates no EU ETS-registered emissions sources and is not obligated under the CSRD, removing the primary regulatory verification layers for corporate net-zero claims in the EU.

**Verdict Rationale: Why MISLEADING (Not GREENWASHING or INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE)**

- **Not INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE (0–45):** The claim is not too vague to assess. It makes a specific, measurable promise (net-zero 2030 for Helsingborg via a named facility). Insufficient evidence verdicts apply to wholly unquantified pledges ("we are committed to sustainability") with no measurable target.

- **Not GREENWASHING (61–80):** Greenwashing verdicts require direct contradiction from verified data (e.g., EU ETS showing rising emissions while company claims reductions; CSRD disclosure contradicting the claimed 2030 target). This claim is vague enough to avoid direct falsification. It is not contradicted by external verified data; it is simply unsubstantiated.

- **MISLEADING (48–60):** The claim exaggerates through **omission**, not commission. It names a plausible mechanism (CCS is a real removal technology), a real facility (Filbornaverket exists), and a specific year (2030), creating an appearance of precision. But it omits the quantified baseline, target, transition plan, and verification mechanism required by the Green Claims Directive. This is the classic structure of misleading greenwashing: specific enough to sound credible, vague enough to avoid accountability.

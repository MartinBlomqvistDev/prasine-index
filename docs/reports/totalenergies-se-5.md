## Assessment

### The Core Contradiction: Strategic Misalignment

The hydrogen claim is **materially contradicted by verified data on TotalEnergies' simultaneous fossil fuel expansion.**

The claim asserts that low-carbon hydrogen deployment will "reduce direct CO2 emissions by up to 3 million tons a year by 2030" in European refineries. This is a specific, time-bound commitment to a defined geographic scope. In technical isolation, hydrogen decarbonization of refining is plausible: the technology exists, and hydrogen-based processes can replace carbonaceous fuels in refinery operations.

However, **this claim cannot be evaluated in isolation.** The company is simultaneously:

1. **Committing capital to 36 oil and gas extraction fields with Final Investment Decision** (GOGET, March 2026). Each FID locks in decades of upstream fossil fuel production. These commitments expand the volume of crude oil and gas flowing into refineries.

2. **Developing new European gas infrastructure**, including the South Hook LNG Terminal (EGT, March 2026). Gas infrastructure lockup extends 40+ years beyond the 2030 hydrogen transition target, creating structural incentives for refineries to maximize throughput to amortize supply costs.

This is not a case of a company maintaining modest legacy fossil operations while transitioning refinery emissions. It is a company making a *narrow refinery decarbonization claim* while aggressively expanding upstream fossil supply. The hydrogen claim functions as a **tactical, offsetting measure** to manage reputational risk from expanded extraction, not as evidence of genuine decarbonization strategy.

### Substantiation Deficits

The claim fails minimum disclosure standards under the Green Claims Directive:

- **No baseline year stated.** The claim specifies a target (3 Mt reduction) and timeline (2030) but does not anchor to a 2020, 2023, or other baseline. This violates Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a).

- **No methodology disclosed.** The claim does not explain how the 3 Mt figure was calculated, which refinery assets are included, or whether Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions are measured.

- **No third-party validation cited.** The claim is unaccompanied by Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) verification, independent audit, or regulatory commitment.

- **Qualified with "up to,"** introducing additional uncertainty beyond already-stated methodology gaps.

### Historical Pattern of Unsubstantiated Claims

ACM (Dutch regulator) formally found in 2021 that TotalEnergies made "vague or unsubstantiated environmental claims" in violation of the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive. This enforcement ruling predates the hydrogen claim and establishes institutional pattern: the company has a documented history of making climate commitments without adequate substantiation.

This hydrogen claim follows that established pattern. While the claim itself is new (not a repeat from prior assessment), it reflects the same risk profile: specific-sounding target, undefined baseline/methodology, lack of third-party audit.

### Emissions Plausibility vs. Strategic Credibility

The historical E-PRTR data shows TotalEnergies achieved a 30% emissions reduction from 2007 to 2024. This supports the *technical plausibility* of a 3 Mt refinery reduction by 2030.

However, plausibility of the hydrogen technology does not establish credibility of the claim within the company's actual strategy. The simultaneous expansion of 36 FID-approved extraction fields and new gas infrastructure demonstrates that corporate capital allocation is directed toward *increasing* fossil fuel throughput, not reducing it. A company cannot credibly claim refinery decarbonization while locking in expanded upstream supply.

## Key Finding

TotalEnergies' hydrogen decarbonization claim for European refineries is materially contradicted by verified data showing simultaneous expansion of 36 FID-approved fossil extraction fields and active development of new European gas infrastructure. While the hydrogen technology itself is plausible, the company's capital allocation demonstrates that fossil fuel supply is expanding faster than refinery emissions can be reduced, making the claim appear as a tactical, offsetting measure rather than credible decarbonization. This pattern repeats a 2021 ACM enforcement finding that the company made unsubstantiated environmental claims.

## Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS verified emissions (critical gap).** No installation IDs for TotalEnergies are registered in the EU Emissions Trading System database. Under EU ETS Directive 2003/87/EC Article 15, verified emissions from regulated installations represent the highest-quality ground truth for actual GHG reductions. Absence of EUTL data means the claim cannot be independently verified against mandatory legal disclosure. This gap reduces confidence from 0.85–0.90 to 0.72 but does not invalidate the assessment because GOGET/EGT verified data and ACM enforcement ruling provide substantial contradicting evidence from other regulatory sources.

2. **Baseline year not disclosed by company.** Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) requires baseline year; the claim does not state this. Unable to verify whether the 3 Mt target is measured against 2020, 2023, or other reference point.

3. **Methodology not disclosed.** Unable to verify whether 3 Mt figure includes Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions; which refinery assets are in scope; or whether calculation method complies with GRI, CDP, or other standard.

4. **Third-party validation absent.** No SBTi verification, independent audit, or regulatory commitment detected. Claim relies entirely on company self-reporting.

5. **South Hook LNG Terminal development timeline.** EGT data confirms terminal is "Proposed" as of March 2026, but exact FID date and commissioning timeline are not specified in available records. This does not change the assessment (infrastructure expansion is verified) but limits precision on lock-in duration.

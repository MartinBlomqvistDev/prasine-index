### Assessment

The narrow claim — that TotalEnergies launched a hydrogen tender for European refineries by 2030 — is factually specific and time-bound, and likely verifiable as a real initiative. However, the claim fails substantiation requirements under the EU Green Claims Directive when assessed in full business context.

**Substantiation Failure:**

The claim lacks the material disclosures required by Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) and Article 5:

- No baseline emissions scope (Scope 1, 2, or 3) stated for 2030 comparison
- No absolute CO2 equivalent reduction quantified (500 kt/year is a hydrogen volume, not an emissions metric)
- No reconciliation with simultaneous fossil fuel expansion (36 committed O&G fields with FID, plus active LNG terminal development)
- Green Claims Directive Article 5 explicitly prohibits aggregating low-carbon initiatives in one sector while expanding emissions-intensive activities in others to manufacture a net reduction claim

**Historical Pattern of Greenwashing:**

The 2021 ACM enforcement ruling is material to assessment. Dutch regulators found TotalEnergies had made climate claims that lacked required substantiation. The current hydrogen claim, while specific in volume and timeline, sits within a documented pattern of selective low-carbon messaging that omits context on fossil fuel expansion. This repetition of contextually incomplete claims, following formal regulatory sanction, elevates the MISLEADING classification.

**Emissions Trend Contradiction:**

E-PRTR data shows 30% historical emissions reduction (2007–2024), supporting general downward trajectory claims. However:
- This reduction is not causally attributed to the hydrogen initiative (which post-dates 2023)
- E-PRTR covers only regulated installations; Scope 3 emissions (the majority for an integrated oil & gas company) are not reflected
- The positive E-PRTR trend is contradicted by forward evidence: 36 new fossil fuel extraction fields with capital committed (GOGET) will generate decades of additional upstream emissions not captured in current E-PRTR reporting

**Score Components:**

- **CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION (72/100)**: The claim is specific and measurable on its face, but violates Green Claims Directive scope requirements by omitting absolute baseline, CO2 metrics, and full business emissions context.

- **EMISSIONS_ACCURACY (45/100)**: The claim addresses hydrogen sourcing, not direct emissions reduction. No verified emissions data exists for the hydrogen initiative itself. Historical E-PRTR reduction (+30%) is positive but unconnected to this specific commitment. Forward fossil fuel expansion (GOGET 36 fields, EGT LNG development) contradicts the claim's implied transition narrative.

- **HISTORICAL_CONSISTENCY (55/100)**: 2021 ACM enforcement ruling indicates prior unsubstantiated claims. No prior equivalent hydrogen claims detected in database, but enforcement history signals systematic pattern of selective, contextually incomplete green claims.

- **TARGET_CREDIBILITY (62/100)**: The 2030 timeline is near-term and credible. However, 500 kt/year hydrogen commitment lacks explicit CO2 abatement targets, SBTi validation, or independent audit. TPI 2050 pathway is 1.5°C-aligned (positive signal), but the 2030 hydrogen target is not independently verified.

- **LOBBYING_ALIGNMENT (40/100)**: EU Transparency Register confirms TotalEnergies is an active EU lobbyist (Reg. no. 1849405799-88). InfluenceMap score is C- (mixed engagement, confidence 0.50), providing insufficient data to assess whether lobbying positions contradict or support this climate claim. No confirmed climate-denial lobbying detected, but data gaps prevent definitive assessment.

**Key Finding:**

TotalEnergies simultaneously made a 500 kt/year hydrogen commitment for 2030 and invested in 36 new fossil fuel extraction fields with final investment decisions — a direct contradiction. The company was formally sanctioned in 2021 by Dutch regulators for unsubstantiated climate claims. The current hydrogen claim, while specific and time-bound, omits material context (fossil fuel expansion, absolute baseline emissions) required by the Green Claims Directive, misleading stakeholders about the company's actual transition trajectory.

### Key Finding

TotalEnergies' hydrogen claim is materially misleading because it omits simultaneous commitment to 36 new fossil fuel extraction fields (GOGET 2026) and LNG terminal expansion (EGT 2026). The company was formally sanctioned by Dutch regulators in 2021 for unsubstantiated climate claims, and the current selective low-carbon initiative lacks the absolute emissions baselines and full business scope required by the EU Green Claims Directive.

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Installation Data**: No EU ETS installation IDs returned for TotalEnergies hydrogen refinery initiative. The claim cannot be verified against mandatory EU ETS verified emissions disclosures (Regulation 601/2012). This limits confidence in the hydrogen commitment's actual implementation status.

2. **SBTi or Third-Party Validation**: No Science-Based Targets initiative (SBTi) validation or independent audit of the 500 kt/year hydrogen target detected. This prevents assessment of whether the commitment meets recognized climate science benchmarks.

3. **InfluenceMap Lobbying Direction**: InfluenceMap score (C-, confidence 0.50) does not specify direction of lobbying engagement (for or against climate policy). This prevents definitive assessment of whether EU-level lobbying contradicts or supports the hydrogen claim.

4. **Hydrogen Baseline and CO2 Metrics**: The claim provides volume (500 kt/year) but no baseline hydrogen consumption for European refineries, and no CO2 equivalent reduction quantified. Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a) requires baseline year and methodology — both absent.

5. **Scope 3 Emissions Data**: E-PRTR covers only Scope 1 regulated installations. Scope 3 (upstream oil & gas production, which dominates TotalEnergies' emissions) is not publicly verified. This limits assessment of absolute company-wide emissions trajectory.

**Impact on Confidence (68%)**: The strong verification of contradicting evidence (GOGET 0.87, EGT 0.85, ACM 0.80) supports MISLEADING verdict with high confidence in the contradiction itself. However, inability to verify the hydrogen claim's actual implementation status via EU ETS data, and gaps in InfluenceMap lobbying direction, reduce overall confidence to 68%. The core finding — that the claim omits material context on simultaneous fossil fuel expansion — remains highly confident (0.85+).

**2. Swedish National Emissions Context**

*Source: Implicit in claim analysis; Swedish emissions data from EU Member State inventory reports.*

**3. Green Claims Directive — Substantiation Requirements**
The Green Claims Directive (COM/2023/0166, Articles 3–5) requires emissions reduction claims to disclose:
- Baseline year (when the comparison period begins)
- Emissions scope (Scope 1, Scope 2, Scope 3, or combination)
- Quantified methodology for attribution

Article 3(3)(a) states: "Environmental claims relating to the future environmental performance of a product or organisation shall only be made where based on and substantiated by objective information."

Article 5 prohibits aggregating reductions and increases to conceal net emission rises.

The claim as published specifies **none of these required elements**.

*Source: European Commission Proposal for a Green Claims Directive, COM/2023/0166, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

### Assessment

The claim fails on four independent grounds:

**1. Substantiation Deficiency (Green Claims Directive Compliance)**

The claim omits mandatory disclosures required by Green Claims Directive Article 3(3)(a):
- No baseline year is stated (when does the comparison begin — 2020? 2007? 2030?)
- No emissions scope is specified (Scope 1 only? Does it include supply-chain [Scope 3] abatement?)
- No methodology is disclosed for how the 10% figure was calculated or attributed to LKAB

Without these elements, the claim cannot be verified or challenged independently. This meets the definition of "misleading" environmental claim under the Directive.

**2. Arithmetic Implausibility**

LKAB's regulated emissions in 2024 total 0.993 Mt CO2e. Sweden's national total is approximately 50–55 Mt CO2e annually. Even if LKAB reduced emissions to zero, the reduction would represent ~1.8–2% of Sweden's total emissions baseline, not the claimed "up to 10%."

For the claim to be arithmetically feasible, it would require LKAB to deliver indirect reductions (e.g., supply-chain transformation, Scope 3 abatement) of 5–5.5 Mt CO2e annually. No such mechanism is articulated in the claim, and no public evidence of LKAB's Scope 3 leverage or supply-chain influence supports this magnitude of impact.

**3. Historical Emissions Trajectory Contradicts Decarbonisation Narrative**

E-PRTR data shows LKAB's absolute emissions have risen 19% over the long term (2007–2024: 834,047 → 993,354 tonnes). While recent performance (2020–2024, −25.1%) is positive, the company's overall trajectory is one of expansion, not contraction. This historical pattern weakens credibility for a forward-looking claim of transformational emissions reduction.

**4. EU ETS Verification Gap**

LKAB's absence from the EU ETS registry prevents verification of the company's emissions baseline and reductions against the primary regulatory standard for GHG accountability. While this absence does not contradict the claim, it removes the possibility of independent third-party verification of the most material assertion.

**Verdict Basis:**

The claim is **GREENWASHING** because it:
- Lacks the substantive disclosures (baseline, scope, methodology) required by the Green Claims Directive
- Makes an arithmetically implausible assertion (10% of Sweden's total = 5.5 Mt; LKAB's baseline = 1 Mt)
- Contradicts the company's own historical emissions trajectory (long-run +19% absolute increase)
- Cannot be independently verified against EU-mandated regulatory data (EU ETS)

The verdict is **not** CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING because:
- The claim is prospective ("will reduce") and cannot be directly contradicted by current-year data alone
- LKAB does not appear in the EU Transparency Register, preventing analysis of simultaneous climate policy lobbying that would confirm active deception

### Key Finding

LKAB claims its transformation will reduce Sweden's total CO2 emissions by 10%, but this assertion is arithmetically implausible (LKAB's total regulated emissions are ~2% of Sweden's baseline, making a 10% national reduction impossible without undisclosed supply-chain mechanisms), lacks the baseline year, emissions scope, and methodology required by the Green Claims Directive, and contradicts the company's own long-term emissions trajectory (+19% from 2007–2024). The claim cannot be independently verified against EU ETS data, as LKAB is not registered in the system.

---

### Data Gaps

1. **EU ETS Verified Emissions Data:** LKAB is not registered in the EU Emissions Trading System (EUTL). This prevents verification of the company's emissions baseline and reduction claims against the legal ground-truth standard mandated under Regulation 601/2012. This gap reduces confidence by approximately 10 percentage points, as EU ETS data represents the most authoritative independent verification mechanism for GHG claims.

2. **LKAB Scope 3 (Supply Chain) Emissions:** The claim's implicit reliance on supply-chain influence to achieve a 10% Swedish reduction cannot be assessed without access to LKAB's Scope 3 emissions inventory and its documented leverage over suppliers. No public disclosure of such impact is available, creating a substantiation void.

3. **EU Transparency Register Entry:** LKAB does not appear in the EU Transparency Register, preventing analysis of the company's direct lobbying on climate policy. This prevents a determination of whether simultaneous climate policy opposition contradicts the company's stated transformation goals (which would elevate the verdict to CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING).

4. **Baseline Year and Methodological Documentation:** LKAB has not published the baseline year, scope definition, or calculation methodology underlying the "up to 10%" claim. Requests to LKAB for this documentation have not been included in this assessment. Availability of such documentation could materially alter the substantiation score.

**Impact on Confidence:** The combination of EU ETS absence and lack of Scope 3 disclosure reduces confidence from an achievable 80%+ to 68%. If Scope 3 documentation and EU Transparency Register data become available, confidence could rise to 85%+.

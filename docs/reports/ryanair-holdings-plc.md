### Evidence

**1. Prior Regulatory Ruling — ASA (UK, 2020)**
The UK Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) upheld a complaint against Ryanair's claim to have the "lowest carbon emissions" (ASA A20-529462). The ASA ruled that Ryanair could not substantiate that it had lower CO2 per passenger than other European airlines on a like-for-like basis, and declared the claim misleading. This is a binding enforcement decision against an equivalent prior environmental/emissions claim by the same company.

*Source: ASA (UK), 2020, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/ryanair-ltd-g21-1080990.html*

**2. Active Lobbying Obstruction — LobbyMap D+ Rating**
LobbyMap rates Ryanair Holdings plc as D+ (Obstructive) in climate policy engagement, with confidence 0.85. A D+ rating denotes active obstruction of climate legislation. This means the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation while simultaneously making green commitments — a direct contradiction between stated net-zero goals and actual policy engagement.

*Source: LobbyMap, 2026, https://lobbymap.org/*

**3. EU Transparency Register Confirmation**
Ryanair Holdings plc is actively registered as an EU corporate lobbyist (registration no. 002977215945-85, HQ: Ireland). This confirms direct engagement with EU institutions on policy matters, corroborating the LobbyMap obstruction classification.

*Source: EU Transparency Register, 2026, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do*

**4. EU Consumer Protection Investigation (2024)**
The European Commission and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation into Ryanair's environmental claims about carbon emissions, offsetting, and sustainability as part of the 2024 airline greenwashing sweep (EC CPC Regulation 2017/2394). This investigation covers Ryanair alongside Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa. The investigation remains open; no binding ruling has yet been issued.

*Source: European Commission, 2024, https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_2057*

**5. EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) — Binding Standard**
The EmpCo Directive, in force since March 2024, amends the UCPD to blacklist environmental claims that rely solely on carbon offsetting schemes rather than certified permanent removals. Net-zero claims must demonstrate that residual emissions will be permanently removed by recognised certified carbon removals, not offset credits. Generic net-zero pledges without credible, verifiable transition plans are automatically classified as unfair commercial practices under Annex I.

*Source: EUR-LEX [32024L0825], EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825*

**6. Source Document — Substantiation Analysis**
Ryanair's 2025 Sustainability Report discloses a net-zero 2050 target aligned with the Paris Agreement (1.5°C). The pathway comprises: 32% from technological/operational improvements, 34% from SAF, 10% from Single European Sky, and **24% from offsetting and other economic measures**. The document critically lacks: (1) an explicit baseline year for the 2050 target (inferred as 2019 from SBTi reference but not stated); (2) interim absolute or intensity reduction targets for 2035 or 2040; (3) a quantified split between emissions abatement and certified removals; (4) a certified permanent carbon removal plan; and (5) a verified transition plan meeting EmpCo requirements.

Notably, the company states that offsetting is "not a long-term solution." The reliance on offsets for 24% of the net-zero pathway, without certified removal mechanisms, directly contravenes the EmpCo Directive's requirements for net-zero claims.

*Source: Ryanair Sustainability Report 2025, September 2025, https://corporate.ryanair.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Ryanair-Sustainability-Report-2025.pdf*

**7. SBTi Record**
SBTi records that Ryanair has set targets conforming to 1.5°C classification for near-term objectives, but the record explicitly shows net-zero target = none. Per Prasine Index methodology, SBTi near-term/interim targets do not substantiate a net-zero 2050 claim. SBTi does not validate or support the net-zero 2050 commitment.

*Source: SBTi, 2026, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

**8. TPI Benchmark Assessment**
The Transition Pathway Initiative (TPI) rates Ryanair's 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" with management quality at Level 2 (Acknowledging). TPI assesses the company as Paris-compatible. However, "Below 2 Degrees" is weaker than the 1.5°C net-zero alignment implied by the claim, and "Acknowledging" is the lowest management quality level indicating policy awareness without substantive implementation. This provides modest mitigation only.

*Source: TPI (Airlines, Ireland), 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates*

### Assessment

Ryanair's net-zero 2050 claim triggers **two independent CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING conditions**, each individually sufficient under Prasine Index scoring rules to sustain a verdict above the 81-point threshold:

**Trigger 1: Lobbying Obstruction (LobbyMap D+)**

LobbyMap assigns Ryanair a D+ (Obstructive) rating with 85% confidence. Per the Prasine Index methodology, a D or D+ LobbyMap rating alone constitutes a confirmed greenwashing trigger: a company actively obstructing climate legislation while simultaneously pledging net-zero emissions is irreconcilably contradicting its own stated commitment. A net-zero 2050 target requires sustained, accelerating climate policy support (aviation fuel standards, emissions trading expansion, SAF mandates). Active obstruction of such policies is incompatible with genuine net-zero commitment. The EU Transparency Register confirms Ryanair's active EU lobbying registration, corroborating direct policy engagement.

**Trigger 2: Prior Regulatory Ruling (ASA 2020)**

The ASA's 2020 binding ruling against Ryanair's "lowest carbon emissions" claim establishes a documented pattern of unsubstantiated environmental claims by this company. The ASA explicitly found Ryanair unable to substantiate lower CO2 per passenger on a like-for-like basis — precisely the type of emissions comparison required for credible net-zero claims. Prasine Index methodology states that ASA rulings qualify as prior violations and need not target the exact sentence under assessment. This is a binding enforcement decision establishing pattern and precedent.

**Substantiation Failure — EmpCo Non-Compliance**

The source document (Ryanair's 2025 Sustainability Report) confirms that the net-zero pathway relies on **24% offsetting** with no certified permanent carbon removal plan. The company itself admits offsetting is "not a long-term solution." The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, explicitly blacklists net-zero claims relying solely on carbon offset credits rather than certified permanent removals as automatically unfair commercial practices. Ryanair's claim therefore fails this binding EU consumer protection standard.

The claim also lacks: (1) an explicit baseline year; (2) interim 2035/2040 targets; (3) a quantified abatement vs. removals split; and (4) a verified transition plan meeting EmpCo substantiation requirements.

**Corroborating Signal: EC CPC Investigation**

The European Commission's 2024 coordinated investigation into Ryanair's environmental claims (EC CPC airline greenwashing sweep) provides additional corroborating evidence of concern at the enforcement level, though no binding ruling has yet been issued. This investigation is ongoing.

**Mitigating Evidence (Within-Band Position Only)**

TPI rates the 2050 pathway as "Below 2 Degrees" (Paris-compatible), which provides modest mitigation. However, under Prasine Index methodology, mitigating evidence cannot lower the score below the threshold established by confirmed triggers. TPI's "Acknowledging" management quality rating (Level 2, the lowest) further limits the strength of this mitigation. SBTi records no net-zero target for Ryanair, so SBTi provides no support.

**Score Rationale**

The score of 86/100 reflects: (1) two confirmed triggers (lobbying obstruction and prior regulatory ruling) pushing the verdict firmly into CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING territory; (2) substantiation failure under binding EmpCo standards; (3) corroborating enforcement investigation; (4) modest mitigation from TPI within-band positioning; and (5) confidence reduced to 86% rather than 90+ because EU ETS ground-truth emissions data was unavailable and the EC CPC investigation has not yet issued a binding EU-wide ruling on this specific net-zero claim.

### Key Finding

Ryanair's net-zero 2050 claim is confirmed greenwashing on two independent grounds: the company actively obstructs climate legislation (LobbyMap D+ rating) while pledging net-zero, and it has a documented pattern of unsubstantiated emissions claims (ASA 2020 binding ruling). Additionally, the company's stated pathway relies on offsetting for 24% of emissions abatement with no certified permanent removal plan, directly violating the EmpCo Directive's binding prohibition on net-zero claims unsupported by certified carbon removals.

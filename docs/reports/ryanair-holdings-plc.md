## Ryanair Holdings plc — Greenwashing Assessment
**Verdict: CONFIRMED GREENWASHING** | Score: 86.0/100 (range: 82–90) | Confidence: 86%
*Published: 2026-06-26 | Prasine Index | Trace ID: af429cf9-6269-40a7-b75b-a81dc2190433*

### The Claim
> "We've developed a pathway to achieve our net-zero carbon emissions goal by 2050, which aligns with the Paris Agreement and the aviation industry's Destination 2050 initiative."
*Source: Investor relations / corporate sustainability page, https://corporate.ryanair.com/sustainability/pathway-to-net-zero, retrieved 2026-06-26*

### Evidence

**Lobbying engagement.** LobbyMap rates Ryanair Holdings plc **D+**, classified as obstructive engagement — actively opposing or delaying climate legislation while making green claims. LobbyMap, https://lobbymap.org/ (confidence 0.85). This is corroborated by the EU Transparency Register, which confirms Ryanair is an actively registered direct corporate lobbyist (reg. no. 002977215945-85, HQ Ireland). EU Transparency Register, https://ec.europa.eu/transparencyregister/public/consultation/search.do (confidence 0.75).

**Prior regulatory ruling.** The UK Advertising Standards Authority upheld a complaint against Ryanair's claim to have the "lowest carbon emissions," finding it **CONFIRMED MISLEADING** because Ryanair could not substantiate lower CO2 per passenger than other European airlines on a like-for-like basis. ASA ruling A20-529462, 2020, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/ryanair-ltd-g21-1080990.html (confidence 0.90).

**Ongoing investigation.** The European Commission and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation under the CPC Regulation 2017/2394 into Ryanair's environmental claims concerning carbon emissions, offsetting, and sustainability credentials, as part of the 2024 airline greenwashing sweep. European Commission, 2024, https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_2057 (confidence 0.70).

**Source-document substantiation.** Ryanair's own pathway document discloses four reduction mechanisms: 32% from technological/operational improvements, 34% from SAF (target >10% by 2030, currently at the EU-mandated 2%), 10% from Single European Sky, and **24% from "offsetting and other economic measures."** Ryanair self-reported disclosure, 2024, https://corporate.ryanair.com/sustainability/pathway-to-net-zero (confidence 0.95). The document states no explicit baseline year, no 2035 or 2040 interim targets, no split between emissions abatement and certified permanent removals, no certified removal mechanism, and no verified transition plan. It also states it will "Prioritize carbon reduction over carbon offsetting" while allocating 24% of the pathway to offsetting.

**Applicable law.** The EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825), in force since March 2024, amends UCPD Annex I to require that net-zero claims demonstrate residual emissions will be permanently removed by certified carbon removals, not offset credits; claims based solely on carbon offsetting are blacklisted, and generic net-zero pledges without a credible, verifiable transition plan are automatically unfair commercial practices. EUR-Lex CELEX:32024L0825, 2024, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024L0825 (confidence 0.95). The CSRD (2022/2464) requires mandatory disclosure of scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions under ESRS E1. EUR-Lex CELEX:32022L2464, 2022, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2464 (confidence 0.95).

**Mitigating evidence.** SBTi records status "Targets set," temperature classification 1.5°C, but **net-zero target = none**. SBTi, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action (confidence 0.70). TPI rates the 2050 pathway "Below 2 Degrees" with Management Quality Level 2 (Acknowledging). TPI, 2022, https://www.transitionpathwayinitiative.org/corporates (confidence 0.75).

### Assessment

The decisive finding is the contradiction between Ryanair's net-zero pledge and its own lobbying conduct. Ryanair's LobbyMap rating of D+ classifies it as obstructive — actively opposing or delaying the climate legislation required to deliver any net-zero pathway. A company obstructing the very policy framework on which its 2050 pledge depends cannot reconcile that pledge with its conduct. The EU Transparency Register confirms this engagement is operationalised at the institutional level. Under Prasine Index scoring policy, a D/D+ LobbyMap rating is alone sufficient to confirm greenwashing.

Two further independent triggers reinforce the verdict. First, the ASA found an equivalent prior emissions claim by Ryanair — its "lowest carbon emissions" claim — confirmed misleading in 2020 (A20-529462), establishing a documented pattern within the same claim category; the 2024 European Commission CPC investigation into Ryanair's carbon, offsetting and sustainability claims adds substantial corroborating weight. Second, the substantiation failure is severe: Ryanair's own pathway document fails the requirements of the amended UCPD Annex I under the EmpCo Directive. It states no baseline year, no 2035/2040 interim targets, no abatement-versus-certified-removal split, and relies on 24% offsetting and "economic measures" — a basis expressly blacklisted for net-zero claims. The document is internally inconsistent, prioritising reduction over offsetting on its face while assigning nearly a quarter of the pathway to offsets. The 34% SAF component currently stands at the EU-mandated 2% against a >10% 2030 target, indicating the trajectory is materially behind what the pathway requires.

The supporting evidence does not displace the confirmed floor. SBTi records interim targets but **no net-zero validation**, which is neutral for a net-zero claim. TPI rates the pathway only "Below 2 Degrees" — within Paris bounds but not 1.5°C-aligned — with weak management quality (Acknowledging). These two moderate supporters place the score in the lower-to-mid confirmed band rather than higher. This claim is not a repeat: it is the first assessment of this specific net-zero claim, though Ryanair has 24 prior claims assessed with a stable score trend.

### Key Finding

Ryanair holds a D+ obstructive climate-lobbying rating from LobbyMap, meaning it actively opposes or delays the climate legislation its own net-zero pledge depends on — and its self-published pathway relies on 24% carbon offsetting, a basis expressly blacklisted for net-zero claims under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825).

## The Claim

> "Ryanair is Europe's greenest airline."

*Source: Corporate website, https://www.ryanair.com/gb/en/plan-trip/environment, accessed 2026-04-17*

---

## Evidence

**[1] ASA Enforcement Ruling, 2020**
The UK Advertising Standards Authority ruled Ryanair's claim to have the "lowest carbon emissions" misleading and unsubstantiated. The ASA found that Ryanair could not demonstrate on a consistent per-passenger basis that it had lower CO2 emissions than other European airlines. The current claim ("Europe's greenest airline") is a direct functional equivalent — a comparative environmental leadership assertion that repeats the falsified 2020 claim in broader language.

*Source: Advertising Standards Authority, A20-529462, 2020, https://www.asa.org.uk/rulings/ryanair-ltd-g21-1080990.html*

---

**[2] European Commission Investigation, 2024**
The European Commission and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation into Ryanair's environmental claims about carbon emissions, offsetting, and sustainability credentials under CPC Regulation 2017/2394. The investigation is part of a 2024 airline-sector greenwashing sweep covering Ryanair, Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa.

*Source: European Commission, CPC Regulation 2017/2394, 2024, https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_2057*

---

**[3] Global Coal Exit List, Urgewald**
Ryanair Holdings plc is listed on the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List as actively expanding coal capacity. The GCEL is the standard coal-sector screen used by 400+ financial institutions (GFANZ, PAII) for divestment and engagement decisions. Inclusion on the GCEL is incompatible with any credible claim of environmental or clean energy leadership.

*Source: Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, https://www.urgewald.org/en/themen/global-coal-exit-list*

---

**[4] InfluenceMap Climate Policy Engagement Score**
InfluenceMap rates Ryanair Holdings plc as D+ — the lowest credibility tier, indicating obstructive engagement with climate legislation. The company actively opposes or delays climate policy while making green claims. This classification documents a direct contradiction between stated environmental leadership and actual corporate policy activity.

*Source: InfluenceMap, climate policy engagement score, https://influencemap.org/company-responses*

---

**[5] Green Claims Directive, 2023**
Under EU directive COM/2023/0166, all explicit environmental claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence, cover full lifecycle where relevant, and not mislead by omission. Vague claims such as "green" or "sustainable" without specific substantiation are prohibited under Article 3(1). Ryanair's environmental claims page provides no audited carbon baseline, no lifecycle coverage, no quantified metrics, and no comparative baseline.

*Source: EUR_LEX, COM/2023/0166, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

---

**[6] SBTi Target Status**
Science Based Targets initiative shows Ryanair with status=unknown, no validated net-zero target, and no temperature classification. The company has not committed to a 1.5°C-aligned emissions pathway.

*Source: Science Based Targets initiative, https://sciencebasedtargets.org/companies-taking-action*

## Assessment

This claim meets the threshold for CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING under at least three independent criteria: (1) verified factual contradiction by regulatory authority; (2) documented coal-sector expansion listed on institutional divestment standards; (3) active corporate lobbying misaligned with stated climate commitments.

**Regulatory Contradiction**
The ASA enforcement ruling (2020) found Ryanair's equivalent claim — "lowest carbon emissions" — to be misleading because the company could not substantiate lower per-passenger CO2 than peers. The current claim ("Europe's greenest airline") is a direct semantic repeat and generalisation of this falsified assertion. Both claims make an absolute comparative environmental leadership statement; both lack substantiation. The 2024 European Commission investigation indicates ongoing concern that Ryanair continues to make unsubstantiated environmental claims post-2020 ASA ruling, suggesting no remediation has occurred.

**Coal Expansion Contradiction**
Ryanair appears on the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List as actively expanding coal capacity. This GCEL record is used by 400+ financial institutions as the standard coal-sector screen. Coal expansion is irreconcilable with any credible claim of environmental leadership, clean energy transition, or Paris alignment, regardless of the airline sector's relative efficiency gains. This is a core institutional contradiction: the company's documented business conduct directly contradicts the marketing claim.

**Lobbying-Commitment Misalignment**
InfluenceMap rates Ryanair D+ — obstructive engagement with climate legislation. The company actively opposes or delays climate policy while marketing environmental leadership. This is the defining greenwashing pattern: stated climate commitments contradicted by actual institutional behaviour. A D+ rating places Ryanair in the lowest credibility tier for climate policy alignment.

**Substantiation Failure**
Under the Green Claims Directive, the claim "Europe's greenest airline" lacks: (1) specific quantified metrics defining "greenest"; (2) comparative baseline against which airlines; (3) lifecycle coverage (fuel, manufacturing, operations); (4) recognised scientific substantiation. Ryanair's environmental claims page provides no audited carbon baseline, no SBTi-validated target, and no transparent measurement methodology. The ASA's 2020 ruling explicitly confirmed this substantiation gap.

**Target Credibility**
SBTi records show no validated science-based target (status=unknown, net-zero target=none). Without SBTi validation, any environmental leadership claim is unanchored to 1.5°C-aligned pathways. Combined with the coal expansion listing, this indicates the company has made no credible institutional commitment to emissions reduction.

**Confidence Level**
Confidence remains high (92%) because the three highest-weight sources — ASA enforcement ruling, GCEL coal listing, and InfluenceMap lobbying record — are all institutional records with published methodologies and publicly verifiable data. The EU ETS data gap (no installation IDs registered) is noted but does not reduce confidence: the regulatory ruling, coal listing, and lobbying score are independently sufficient to confirm greenwashing.

Ryanair's claim "Europe's greenest airline" is a repeated and unsubstantiated assertion contradicted by: (1) an ASA enforcement ruling (2020) finding an equivalent claim misleading; (2) the company's active listing on the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List; and (3) an InfluenceMap D+ rating for obstructive climate policy engagement. The company's documented business conduct—coal expansion and climate policy obstruction—is irreconcilable with stated environmental leadership.

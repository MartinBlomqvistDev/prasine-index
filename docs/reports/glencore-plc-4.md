### Evidence

**[1] CLAIM_SUBSTANTIATION FAILURE**  
The claim provides specific percentage achievements (39%, 6%, 55%) against an exact date (31 December 2025) but fails to identify what targets are being reported. No baseline metrics, measurement methodology, scope definition, or third-party verification is provided. Under the Green Claims Directive Article 3(1), environmental claims must be substantiated by recognised scientific evidence and cannot mislead by omission. A percentage claim without defined targets, units, or verification method does not meet this standard.  
*Source: EUR_LEX [52023PC0166] Green Claims Directive proposal, 2023, https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:52023PC0166*

### Assessment

The claim "39% of the targets have already been achieved; 6% of the targets were not met; and 55% are scheduled to be completed by the applicable targeted deadline" presents numerical precision while concealing substantive vagueness. It is classified as MISLEADING rather than GREENWASHING because the claim itself avoids making a specific, falsifiable assertion—it reports abstract percentages against unnamed targets. However, it is misleading on two grounds:

**First: Substantiation Failure**  
The claim violates the Green Claims Directive (EUR_LEX, 2023) by providing percentages without identifying what targets are being measured, their baselines, measurement methodology, or verification status. A claim that provides numerical progress without defining the numerator and denominator fails the substantiation requirement for explicit environmental claims (Article 3(1)). The appearance of precision masks an absence of verifiable content.

**Second: Contextual Contradiction — Active Coal Expansion**  
The claim appears within Glencore's water-management sustainability materials. However, the company is simultaneously listed as "ACTIVELY EXPANDING coal capacity" by the Urgewald Global Coal Exit List (confidence 0.90), a screening tool used by 400+ financial institutions. Coal mining is a major driver of water stress globally—it requires high volumes of freshwater for extraction and processing, often in water-stressed regions. A water-management achievement claim from an actively expanding coal producer is contradicted by the material fact of coal expansion, which the claim does not disclose. This constitutes misleading by omission under the Green Claims Directive.

**Third: Regulatory Pattern**  
The UK CMA's 2023 investigation of Glencore for potentially misleading sustainability claims, specifically flagging the net-zero commitment / coal expansion contradiction, establishes this as a pattern. The current water-management claim follows the same structure: claimed progress in one environmental domain while material contradictions (coal expansion) are omitted.

**Fourth: Obstructive Lobbying**  
InfluenceMap assigns Glencore a D+ score for climate policy engagement, indicating obstructive engagement—the company actively opposes or delays climate legislation while making public green claims. The company is registered in the EU Transparency Register as an active EU lobbyist. This creates a structural contradiction: water-management progress is claimed while the company simultaneously lobbies against climate policies that would incentivise water-efficient commodity production.

**Why MISLEADING (46–60) rather than GREENWASHING (61–80)?**  
A GREENWASHING classification requires a specific, falsifiable factual claim directly contradicted by verified data (e.g., "we have reduced water consumption by 25%" + data showing the opposite). This claim avoids falsifiability by not defining which targets, what baselines, or what measurement standards apply. The claim is substantively empty. It is classified as MISLEADING because it uses numerical precision to create a false impression of transparency while concealing the absence of defined targets, scope, or verification—and while omitting the material fact of active coal expansion that contextually contradicts any water-management sustainability narrative.

### Key Finding

Glencore claims water-management progress (39% of targets achieved) without identifying which targets, how they are measured, or who verified them, violating substantiation requirements under the Green Claims Directive. The claim is contextually contradicted by the company's active coal capacity expansion (Urgewald Global Coal Exit List, 0.90 confidence) and obstructive climate lobbying (InfluenceMap D+ score), and follows a documented regulatory pattern: the UK CMA investigated the company in 2023 for similar potentially misleading claims pairing net-zero commitments with coal mining expansion.

### Data Gaps

**EU ETS Installation Data — Not Available**  
Glencore has no registered EU ETS installations in available emissions trading registries. This is unusual for a major commodity trading and mining company with operational presence across the EU. The absence of EU ETS data prevents direct verification of Scope 1/2 emissions trajectory for EU operations. This gap does not support the claim but raises the question of whether emissions are structured to avoid EU reporting requirements. However, the GCEL listing (Urgewald, confidence 0.90) provides independent high-confidence evidence of coal expansion regardless of EU ETS registration status.

**Target Definition — Not Provided**  
The claim references "targets" but does not name, define, or link them. Without knowing which targets are being reported against, full independent verification of the 39%/6%/55% percentages is impossible. This is a critical substantiation gap under the Green Claims Directive: a claim to progress toward environmental goals must identify the goals explicitly.

**Measurement Methodology — Not Disclosed**  
The claim provides no methodology for how target achievement was measured, audited, or verified. No third-party assurance, certification body, or measurement standard is referenced. This prevents independent verification of the percentages claimed.

**Impact on Confidence Assessment**  
These gaps reduce confidence from a potential 0.80+ (if multiple independent sources converged on verifying the 39%/6%/55% figures) to 0.70. However, confidence remains above 0.65 because:
- Multiple independent sources (GCEL 0.90, InfluenceMap 0.85, CMA 2023, 0.70) converge on the finding that Glencore makes sustainability claims while contradicting them through coal expansion and obstructive lobbying.

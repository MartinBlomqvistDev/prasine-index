Architecture

Seven agents. One evidence chain.

Each agent has a single responsibility. Data flows through typed Pydantic models at every boundary — no raw strings, no untyped dicts between steps.

1
Discovery Agent
Monitors EU company IR pages, press releases, and CSRD reports for new content via SHA-256 change detection.
2
Extraction Agent
Extracts every green claim verbatim with forced tool use. Returns structured List[Claim].
Raw Anthropic SDK
3
Context Agent
Queries PostgreSQL for historical claims, aggregate scores, and semantically similar prior claims via pgvector.
PostgreSQL + pgvector
4
Verification Agent
Queries 22 open data sources in parallel — including the claim's own source document, fetched and analysed via the Anthropic document API. Partial failures are tolerated.
LangGraph
5
Lobbying Agent
Cross-references the company against the EU Transparency Register. A company lobbying against climate legislation while claiming green leadership is flagged explicitly.
6
Judge Agent
LLM-as-judge. Produces a calibrated 0–100 score with per-dimension breakdown and full chain-of-thought reasoning preserved verbatim for audit.
Raw Anthropic SDK
7
Report Agent
Generates a publication-ready Markdown report. Every factual assertion is cited. Every data gap is disclosed.
Raw Anthropic SDK
Why LangGraph only at step 4? The Verification Agent queries 22 independent data sources concurrently and must handle partial failures gracefully — exactly the fan-out / partial-state-merge problem LangGraph's StateGraph with operator.add reducers solves. The other six agents are single-step operations where raw SDK gives better prompt control and cleaner code. This is when to use a framework, not just that frameworks exist.

Key finding

Evidence

LobbyMap D+ (Obstructive) + EU Transparency Register

LobbyMap rates Ryanair D+ (Obstructive) — actively opposing or delaying climate legislation while making green claims (confidence 0.85). Corroborated by the EU Transparency Register: Ryanair is an actively registered direct corporate lobbyist (reg. no. 002977215945-85, HQ Ireland, confidence 0.75). Under Prasine scoring, a D/D+ LobbyMap rating alone triggers CONFIRMED status.

Independent trigger · confidence 0.85

UK ASA Ruling G20-1089921 (2020)

The Advertising Standards Authority found Ryanair's "lowest carbon emissions" claim CONFIRMED MISLEADING — Ryanair could not substantiate lower CO₂ per passenger than comparable airlines on a like-for-like basis. Establishes a documented prior violation within the same claim category. Under the Prasine framework, a prior ruling against an equivalent claim type independently triggers CONFIRMED status.

Independent trigger · confidence 0.90

Ryanair Source Document — Substantiation Failure

Analysis of the claim's source document confirms the net-zero pledge lacks: a stated baseline year, interim 2035/2040 targets, a disclosed abatement-versus-certified-removal split, a certified permanent removal plan, and a verified transition plan. The cited research supports only a 43% emissions reduction via a 50% SAF blend on specific routes — an operational measure, not net-zero substantiation.

Substantiation failure · confidence 0.95

4

European Commission CPC Investigation (2024)

The EC and national consumer protection authorities opened a coordinated sweep under CPC Regulation 2017/2394 into Ryanair's environmental claims regarding carbon emissions, offsetting, and sustainability credentials, alongside Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa. Not yet a binding ruling.

Open investigation · confidence 0.70

Legislative framework

5

EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) — Blacklisted Claim Type

In force since March 2024, the EmpCo Directive amends UCPD Annex I to require that net-zero claims demonstrate: a baseline emissions year, interim targets, a split between abatement and certified permanent removal, and a verified transition plan. Claims based on carbon offsetting are expressly blacklisted. Ryanair's disclosure fails every requirement. EUR-Lex CELEX:32024L0825.

Substantiation failure · confidence 0.95

6

CSRD (EU 2022/2464) — Mandatory Emissions Disclosure

CSRD requires mandatory disclosure of scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024. A net-zero claim that cannot be cross-referenced against a CSRD-compliant baseline constitutes a material substantiation gap. EUR-Lex CELEX:32022L2464.

Data gap · confidence 0.95

TPI rates the 2050 pathway "Below 2 Degrees" at Management Quality Level 2 (Acknowledging) — Paris-compatible but not 1.5°C-aligned. Neither displaces the three independent confirmed triggers.

Partial mitigation — insufficient to rebut · confidence 0.70–0.75

Assessment

The lead finding is the lobbying contradiction. LobbyMap rates Ryanair D+ (Obstructive) — actively opposing or delaying the climate legislation required to deliver any net-zero pathway — while the company publicises a Net Zero by 2050 target. The contradiction is corroborated, not merely alleged: the EU Transparency Register confirms Ryanair is an active, registered EU lobbyist (reg. no. 002977215945-85). Under Prasine scoring, a D/D+ rating alone triggers CONFIRMED status.

Two further independent triggers reinforce the verdict. The ASA found an equivalent prior emissions claim misleading (G20-1089921, 2020), establishing a documented pattern within the same claim category. And the claim's own source document fails every EmpCo Directive substantiation requirement: no baseline year, no interim 2035/2040 targets, no abatement-versus-certified-removal split, no certified removal plan, no verified transition plan. The cited SAF research supports an operational efficiency measure, not net-zero substantiation.

Mitigating evidence — SBTi "Targets set" (net-zero validation: none) and TPI's "Below 2 Degrees" pathway rating — is addressed on its merits but cannot override three independently sufficient confirmed triggers. These signals place the score in the lower-to-mid confirmed band (82–91) rather than higher. The 2024 EC CPC coordinated investigation remains open and is weighted as a live enforcement concern, not a concluded ruling.

Score logic

Three independent confirmed triggers, each individually sufficient under Prasine methodology: the LobbyMap D+ obstruction rating, the 2020 ASA prior ruling, and the source-document substantiation failure (confidence 0.95). Emissions discrepancy is capped at moderate confidence (55/100) because Ryanair has no EU ETS installations to verify against — a disclosed data gap that lowers confidence to 85% but does not affect the CONFIRMED floor.

Architecture decisions

Build what earns its keep.

Every non-obvious choice in this codebase was made for a specific reason. These are the three that matter most.

No LangChain — except where justified.
Six of the seven agents are raw Anthropic SDK — single-step operations where the SDK gives better prompt control, cleaner error handling, and no hidden abstraction overhead. LangGraph is used exactly once: the Verification Agent's parallel fan-out across 22 data sources, with partial-failure tolerance via operator.add reducers. That is the problem LangGraph was designed to solve. No others warranted it.
Pydantic v2 at every boundary — typed or nothing.
All agent inputs and outputs are typed Pydantic models. Claim, VerificationResult, ScoreBreakdown, PipelineResult — no raw dicts cross any boundary. Validation failures are loud and immediate. This is what let a 7-agent pipeline be debugged at the boundary level rather than by tracing state through six layers of untyped output.
Claim quality filter before Opus spend.
A two-pass extraction model: run --extract-only on Haiku (~$0.01) to preview discovered claims before committing the full Opus judge and report (~$1.15/company). Claims are ranked by a heuristic priority score — category weight, specificity signals (numbers, years, percentages), compound bonus for targets with baseline years — and claims below the minimum threshold are filtered out before verification begins. This makes the pipeline safe to run on unknown URLs without wasting budget on pages that only have generic copy.
Evaluation

An 80% CI gate on every commit.

A golden eval dataset of 20 known greenwashing cases with ground-truth verdicts. The pipeline runs against all 20 before any production run. CI fails below 80% pass rate. Calibrated Haiku judge for fast eval; Opus judge for publication output.

Architecture

Seven agents. One evidence chain.

Each agent has a single responsibility. Data flows through typed Pydantic models at every boundary — no raw strings, no untyped dicts between steps.

1
Discovery Agent
Monitors EU company IR pages, press releases, and CSRD reports for new content via SHA-256 change detection.
2
Extraction Agent
Extracts every green claim verbatim with forced tool use. Returns structured List[Claim].
Raw Anthropic SDK
3
Context Agent
Queries PostgreSQL for historical claims, aggregate scores, and semantically similar prior claims via pgvector.
PostgreSQL + pgvector
4
Verification Agent
Queries 22 open data sources in parallel — including the claim's own source document, fetched and analysed via the Anthropic document API. Partial failures are tolerated.
LangGraph
5
Lobbying Agent
Cross-references the company against the EU Transparency Register. A company lobbying against climate legislation while claiming green leadership is flagged explicitly.
6
Judge Agent
LLM-as-judge. Produces a calibrated 0–100 score with per-dimension breakdown and full chain-of-thought reasoning preserved verbatim for audit.
Raw Anthropic SDK
7
Report Agent
Generates a publication-ready Markdown report. Every factual assertion is cited. Every data gap is disclosed.
Raw Anthropic SDK
Why LangGraph only at step 4? The Verification Agent queries 22 independent data sources concurrently and must handle partial failures gracefully — exactly the fan-out / partial-state-merge problem LangGraph's StateGraph with operator.add reducers solves. The other six agents are single-step operations where raw SDK gives better prompt control and cleaner code. This is when to use a framework, not just that frameworks exist.

Key finding

Ryanair holds a D+ obstructive climate-lobbying rating from LobbyMap, meaning it actively opposes or delays the climate legislation its own net-zero pledge depends on — and its self-published pathway relies on 24% carbon offsetting, a basis expressly blacklisted for net-zero claims under the EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825).

Evidence

Contradicting evidence

1

LobbyMap D+ (Obstructive) + EU Transparency Register

LobbyMap rates Ryanair D+ (Obstructive) — actively opposing or delaying climate legislation while making green claims (confidence 0.85). Corroborated by the EU Transparency Register: Ryanair is an actively registered direct corporate lobbyist (reg. no. 002977215945-85, HQ Ireland, confidence 0.75). A company obstructing the policy framework its own 2050 pledge depends on cannot reconcile that pledge with its conduct. Under Prasine scoring, a D/D+ LobbyMap rating alone triggers CONFIRMED status.

Independent trigger · confidence 0.85

2

UK ASA Ruling A20-529462 (2020)

The Advertising Standards Authority found Ryanair's "lowest carbon emissions" claim CONFIRMED MISLEADING — Ryanair could not substantiate lower CO₂ per passenger than comparable airlines on a like-for-like basis. Establishes a documented prior violation within the same claim category. Under the Prasine framework, a prior ruling against an equivalent claim type independently triggers CONFIRMED status.

Independent trigger · confidence 0.90

3

Ryanair Self-Disclosure — Pathway to Net Zero (2024)

Ryanair's own pathway document reveals that 24% of its net-zero plan relies on "offsetting and other economic measures" — expressly blacklisted under the EmpCo Directive. The document states no explicit baseline year, no 2035/2040 interim targets, no abatement-versus-certified-removal split, and no verified transition plan. The 34% SAF component currently sits at the EU-mandated 2% against a >10% 2030 target.

Substantiation failure · confidence 0.95

4

European Commission CPC Investigation (2024)

The EC and national consumer protection authorities launched a coordinated investigation under CPC Regulation 2017/2394 into Ryanair's environmental claims. On 6 November 2025, Ryanair was among 21 airlines that reached a formal settlement with the CPC Network, committing to remove or revise misleading environmental claims. A concluded regulatory determination.

Regulatory settlement · confidence 0.70

Legislative framework

5

EmpCo Directive (EU 2024/825) — Blacklisted Claim Type

In force since March 2024, the EmpCo Directive amends UCPD Annex I to require that net-zero claims demonstrate: a baseline emissions year, interim targets, a split between abatement and certified permanent removal, and a verified transition plan. Claims based on carbon offsetting are expressly blacklisted. Ryanair's pathway fails every requirement. EUR-Lex CELEX:32024L0825.

Substantiation failure · confidence 0.95

6

CSRD (EU 2022/2464) — Mandatory Emissions Disclosure

CSRD requires mandatory disclosure of scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions under ESRS E1 from FY2024. A net-zero claim that cannot be cross-referenced against a CSRD-compliant baseline constitutes a material substantiation gap. EUR-Lex CELEX:32022L2464.

Data gap · confidence 0.95

7

SBTi records interim reduction targets classified as 1.5°C but net-zero target: none — neutral for the assessed net-zero commitment (confidence 0.70). TPI rates the 2050 pathway "Below 2 Degrees" at Management Quality Level 2 (Acknowledging) — Paris-compatible but not 1.5°C-aligned. Neither displaces the three independent confirmed triggers.

Partial mitigation — insufficient to rebut · confidence 0.70–0.75

Assessment

The decisive finding is the contradiction between Ryanair's net-zero pledge and its own lobbying conduct. LobbyMap classifies Ryanair D+ (Obstructive) — actively opposing the climate legislation required to deliver any net-zero pathway. A company obstructing the policy framework its 2050 pledge depends on cannot reconcile that pledge with its conduct. Under Prasine scoring, this alone confirms greenwashing. The EU Transparency Register confirms this engagement is operationalised at the institutional level.

Two further independent triggers reinforce the verdict. The ASA found an equivalent prior emissions claim CONFIRMED MISLEADING (A20-529462, 2020), establishing a documented pattern. Ryanair's own pathway document then fails the EmpCo Directive on every substantiation requirement: no baseline year, no 2035/2040 interim targets, no abatement-versus-certified-removal split, and 24% reliance on offsetting — expressly blacklisted. The self-published pathway is internally inconsistent and materially behind its own 2030 SAF trajectory.

Mitigating evidence (SBTi interim targets, TPI Below 2°C rating) is genuine but cannot override three independent confirmed triggers. These signals place the score in the lower-to-mid confirmed band (82–90) rather than higher. The November 2025 CPC settlement — Ryanair among 21 airlines that committed to revise misleading environmental claims — adds a concluded regulatory determination on top of the existing finding.

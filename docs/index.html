Architecture

Seven agents. One evidence chain.

Each agent has a single responsibility. Data flows through typed Pydantic models at every boundary — no raw strings, no untyped dicts between steps.

1
Discovery Agent
Monitors EU company IR pages, press releases, and CSRD reports for new content via SHA-256 change detection.
2
Extraction Agent
Extracts every green claim verbatim with forced tool use. Returns structured List[Claim].
Raw Anthropic SDK
3
Context Agent
Queries PostgreSQL for historical claims, aggregate scores, and semantically similar prior claims via pgvector.
PostgreSQL + pgvector
4
Verification Agent
Queries 21 open data sources in parallel. Partial failures are tolerated — if one source is down, the pipeline continues with what was retrieved.
LangGraph
5
Lobbying Agent
Cross-references the company against the EU Transparency Register. A company lobbying against climate legislation while claiming green leadership is flagged explicitly.
6
Judge Agent
LLM-as-judge. Produces a calibrated 0–100 score with per-dimension breakdown and full chain-of-thought reasoning preserved verbatim for audit.
Raw Anthropic SDK
7
Report Agent
Generates a publication-ready Markdown report. Every factual assertion is cited. Every data gap is disclosed.
Raw Anthropic SDK
Why LangGraph only at step 4? The Verification Agent queries 21 independent data sources concurrently and must handle partial failures gracefully — exactly the fan-out / partial-state-merge problem LangGraph's StateGraph with operator.add reducers solves. The other six agents are single-step operations where raw SDK gives better prompt control and cleaner code. This is when to use a framework, not just that frameworks exist.

Data Sources

21 open data sources.

All EU open data. No proprietary APIs. Every source is independently authoritative.

EU ETS / EUTL
Verified annual CO₂ emissions per installation, 2005–present. Third-party verified under EU Regulation 601/2012.
Active — daily snapshot
SBTi
Science-based targets — validated, committed, and removed. A removed target is a CONFIRMED_GREENWASHING signal at 0.95 confidence.
Active — quarterly
InfluenceMap
Corporate climate policy engagement scores A+ to F. A D/E/F company claiming climate leadership is textbook greenwashing.
Active — bulk CSV
Enforcement Rulings
ASA, ACM, AGCM, CMA, EC rulings and court judgments. A prior ruling is the strongest possible evidence — an authority has already decided.
Active — curated static
CA100+
Net-zero benchmark from 700+ investors ($68tn AUM) for the 170 largest emitters. "Not Aligned" contradicts net-zero claims.
Active — annual
E-PRTR
EEA-verified non-CO₂ GHG releases (CH₄, N₂O, HFCs) per facility — emissions not covered by EU ETS.
Active — annual
Banking on Climate Chaos
Fossil fuel financing by the 60 largest banks, 2016–present. Banks claiming net zero while financing hundreds of billions in fossil expansion.
Active — annual
Global Coal Exit List
~1,000 companies active in the coal value chain. Expanding coal capacity while claiming clean energy transition.
Active — annual
EU Transparency Register
Brussels lobbying declarations. Companies lobbying against climate legislation while claiming green leadership is flagged explicitly.
Active — XLSX bulk export
EEA National Totals
EEA-verified national GHG inventory totals per country, 1990-present. Official EU submission benchmarks for company percentage claims.
Active — annual
EU Innovation Fund
EU-funded decarbonisation projects (steel, cement, chemicals, hydrogen). Context for whether claimed green investments match the scale of emissions.
Active — annual
Urgewald GOGEL
~1,000 companies active in global oil and gas expansion. The O&G equivalent of GCEL — used by 400+ financial institutions under GFANZ.
Active — annual
Climate TRACE
Independent facility-level emissions estimates from satellite imagery and remote sensing — not self-reported, not government-submitted.
Active — v7 API
TPI (Transition Pathway Initiative)
Management quality and carbon performance benchmarks for 491 listed companies. "Not Aligned" by 2050 directly contradicts net-zero claims.
Active — annual CSV
GEM Coal Plant Tracker
Every coal power plant worldwide — status, capacity, and owner. A company expanding coal while claiming clean energy transition is directly contradicted.
Active — January 2026
GEM Europe Gas Tracker
Gas pipelines, LNG terminals, and gas plants across Europe. New gas infrastructure under construction contradicts Paris-aligned transition claims.
Active — March 2026
GEM Oil & Gas Extraction Tracker
Oil and gas extraction fields worldwide. Fields in active development (FID taken) mean capital committed to decades of upstream fossil fuel production.
Active — March 2026
EDGAR JRC
JRC independent GHG estimates for all countries, 1970-2024. Cross-checks Eurostat and EEA figures with the EU Commission's own atmospheric/activity data.
